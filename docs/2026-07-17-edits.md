## Manuscript

`manuscripts/PIFB2.tex` now contains the section “Beyond Mean Field: Correlated Gauge-VFE and the Effective Theory.” It distinguishes state-posterior factorization, configuration saddle selection, exchangeable large-population closure, and projected RG closure as separate approximations. The section confirms that PIFB2 already contained the central configuration meta-entropy Gibbs lift and the local RG fluctuation determinant, then extends the framework with a normalized two-level variational model, a nested state/configuration ELBO theorem, correlated state posterior families, local configuration generating and effective functionals, a quotient one-loop expansion, a proposed 2PI completion, and an exact-RG-to-tested-projection research program.

The revision separates established results from proposed structure. Tree Bethe inference is stated as exact, while generic loopy Bethe and Kikuchi pseudomarginals are stated to lack a generic evidence bound or monotone improvement guarantee. A globally normalized sparse block-Gaussian posterior retains ordinary variational-gap semantics. For noncompact gauge actions, entropy and integration use covariantly transformed or quasi-invariant reference measures with their Jacobians; direct quotient integration and redundant-field Faddeev–Popov gauge fixing are presented as alternative prescriptions. The local effective action is identified as background- and chart-dependent until quotient existence and overlap compatibility have been proved.

The live peer-KL sector remains outside the state-level fixed-joint ELBO. It is described as an engineered gauge-covariant consensus energy and, under a finite belief-configuration partition function, an exact generalized-Bayesian energy on that separate sample space. It is only a candidate consensus-operator truncation of the structural state-reduced action until an explicit posterior-to-summary map, fitted coefficients, and quantitative energy and force residuals are supplied. The closing claim is conditional: the configuration Gibbs/effective-action tier is part of the proposed completion when configuration thermodynamics, connected structural fluctuations, and scale-dependent coarse-graining are scientific targets, but it is not required merely to replace a mean-field state posterior with a correlated posterior.

`manuscripts/references.bib` now includes Yedidia, Freeman, and Weiss on region free energies; Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis on the 2PI effective action; Abbott on the background-field method; and Faddeev and Popov on gauge fixing.
