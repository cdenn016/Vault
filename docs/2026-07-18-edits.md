## VFE 4.0 white paper geometry revision

The VFE 4.0 white-paper revision replaces the causal-token-graph base with the established general geometry of agents as local statistical-bundle sections over a noumenal or contextual base manifold. The language-model construction is formulated as the zero-dimensional reduction in which all labeled token agents are stacked over one base point and the causal graph records internal probabilistic dependencies among them.

The revision distinguishes latent sample bundles from statistical bundles, defines the correlated population recognition law as the exact ELBO object, recovers persistent local agent beliefs as its pushforward marginals, distinguishes those marginals from full conditionals used by population updates, and treats mean-field as an optional block-diagonal restriction. Same-point frame comparisons and any optional discrete graph connection are separated from curvature of the smooth base. The exact core uses the frame coboundary \(\Omega_{tj}=U_tU_j^{-1}\); independent graph links \(\Lambda_{tj}\) belong only to a separately declared extension. The diagonal \(G\) action inherited from the principal bundle over the point is separated from the internal population-frame reparameterization group \(G^{T+1}\).

The normalized language model now conditions on deterministic \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{core}}\), uses one normalized causal joint and one normalized correlated recognition law, and retains categorical cross-entropy as the observation sector of the negative ELBO. Random frames, graph links, or base-connection variables require their own proper prior, reference measure, recognition entropy, and normalization audit. The coordinate-update chapter states guarantees only for the same complete ELBO and distinguishes those guarantees from finite gradient steps, truncated optimization, and a separately normalized posterior-consensus objective.

Natural Gaussian coordinates are \((h,-J/2)\), their Fisher-dual expectation coordinates are \((\mu,M)\) with \(M=\Sigma+\mu\mu^\top\), and \((\mu,\Sigma)\) remains a moment, sampling, and reporting view. Source marginalization is identified as a mixture operation, so a source-conditioned Gaussian component is never conflated with the complete source-marginal law.

The prediction protocol now requires a target-blind, prefix-measurable prior predictor. Recognition may see the target during training but cannot replace the prior predictor during held-out scoring. A finite leakage property-test set is paired with a static causal-mask and dataflow audit. Identity population frames are tested only as a change-of-variables oracle; they are not a predictive ablation. Empirical attribution to the equivariant internal-map restriction therefore requires a genuinely different, capacity-matched unconstrained-map control.

The graphical abstract, factor graph, bundle-and-coordinate diagram, and verification ladder were redrawn or revised in TikZ. The figures visibly place the agent population over one base point, type the state and model sample copies, distinguish fixed core data from random variables, and separate natural, expectation, and moment coordinates. ## Exact VFE 4.0 mean-field derivation

The ELBO chapter now derives the complete product-family theory obtained by factorizing the state, model, and categorical source coordinates of the fixed VFE 4.0 joint. The negative ELBO is written as initial complexity, expected categorical observation loss, two categorical source KL divergences, and source-weighted expected conditional transition KL divergences. Closed-form source-row coordinate updates and their log-sum-exp envelope are derived at the unit scale fixed by the ordinary ELBO.

The derivation retains the complete Markov blanket in the continuous state and model coordinate equations, including each receiving transition, the local observation, and all child or recoil transitions. The Gaussian specialization gives the full expected edge costs, including sender-covariance and model-covariance trace terms, and then expresses the conjugate transition sectors directly in natural information coordinates. The manuscript states that its categorical softmax emission remains nonconjugate, so no exact finite Gaussian information increment is claimed for that observation sector.

The comparison with V3 now separates three claims. The generic VFE 4.0 mean-field edge is an expected KL to a fixed conditional kernel and is not the live divergence between two moving recognition marginals. A restricted fixed-sender-covariance Gaussian choice recovers the V3 edge algebra up to the row constant $d/2$. A fixed-template row model also reproduces the unit-temperature V3 source-row decomposition exactly while its templates remain fixed, but refreshing those templates makes it a plug-in surrogate rather than coordinate ascent on one fixed global ELBO. A mixed-variation argument records the open-family obstruction, while the separate configuration-Gibbs model remains one exact construction that can house a fully live peer-consensus energy.
