# Blue opening — site-local moving-peer obstruction

Blue defended the finite theorem because equality of functionals on the open neighborhood would imply equality along every admissible site-local path. On each such path, the fixed-joint product-family derivative is zero, while the peer derivative contributes the stated positive variance. The simultaneous-site counterexample does not reach this theorem because the proof sums derivatives of separate paths.

Blue also defended the intended continuum scope as a dominated-differentiation result for a scalar base integral, not as a continuum ELBO, a measure on sections, or a continuum limit. Blue listed the support, positivity, mixture-openness, common-rectangle, finite-KL, bounded-ratio, domination, positive-variance, and zero-remainder hypotheses as falsification conditions.

At the opening stage, Blue treated the family and continuum assumptions charitably. Red’s rebuttal showed that two qualifications still needed to be written explicitly.
