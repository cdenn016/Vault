# Red opening — site-local moving-peer obstruction

Red could not falsify the finite derivative calculation under a genuinely design-point-factorized product family. Red agreed that summing separate site-local derivatives is sufficient: equality on the neighborhood would force equality along each path, so the corresponding sums must agree.

Red attacked two written scope defects at commit `9833a44`.

First, Chapter 6 permits dependence across design points inside its declared mean-field factors. The theorem varies one factor per design point. It therefore cannot be described as covering every family called “mean-field” elsewhere in the manuscript.

Second, the continuum corollary assumed a common rectangle for a supplied receiver tangent and then replaced that tangent by a centered choice. Red gave a measurable counterexample with base `(0,1]` and coefficient `b(c)=c^{-1/4}`: the integrated variance is finite and positive, but the centered receiver ratio is unbounded, so no common two-sided receiver interval preserves positivity.

Red stated that the attack would be falsified by naming the fine product family explicitly and by defining the centered continuum tangent before binding the common-rectangle and domination assumptions to that exact path.
