# Entropy and consciousness

A line of work proposing that the level and content of conscious experience track the entropy / informational diversity of brain dynamics. The 'entropic brain' hypothesis associates richer, more flexible conscious states (and psychedelic states) with higher entropy in neural activity, while integration measures balance this diversity against unified information. The conjecture is that consciousness corresponds to a regime that jointly maximizes integration and differentiation (diversity), making entropy a candidate marker of conscious level.
