# Relational Observables

Relational observables are gauge-invariant quantities defined not by absolute (coordinate or frame) values but by the value of one dynamical variable conditioned on, or correlated with, another. In gauge and constrained systems (and in quantum reference frames), absolute positions/orientations are unphysical; only relations between subsystems carry physical content. This is the conceptual core of the perspective-neutral and quantum-reference-frame programs, where physics is reconstructed from the perspective of an internal frame using relational, constraint-satisfying observables.
