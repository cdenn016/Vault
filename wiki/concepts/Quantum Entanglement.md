# Quantum Entanglement

Quantum entanglement is the nonseparability of a joint quantum state: the state of a composite system cannot be factored into independent subsystem states, so measurement outcomes exhibit correlations stronger than any classical (local hidden-variable) model allows (violating Bell inequalities). Entanglement entropy quantifies these correlations and, in holography, is conjectured to literally build spacetime geometry (Ryu-Takayanagi, ER=EPR). It is the central resource of quantum information and a key bridge between information and geometry in the participatory program.
