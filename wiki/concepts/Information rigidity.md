# Information rigidity

Information rigidity is the empirical macroeconomic phenomenon in which economic agents' forecasts adjust only sluggishly and incompletely to new information, producing predictable, persistent forecast errors. It is quantified by regressing forecast errors on past forecast revisions; a positive coefficient signals rigidity. The two leading micro-foundations are sticky information (agents update infrequently) and noisy/rational inattention (agents observe signals imperfectly). It is a direct economic analogue of the program's belief-inertia idea: beliefs carry momentum and resist instantaneous Bayesian updating.
