# Binding Problem

The binding problem is the cognitive-neuroscience question of how distributed neural representations of an object's separate features (color, shape, motion, location) are combined into a single coherent percept of one object. In Bayne and Chalmers's taxonomy it underlies 'objectual unity' (two states directed at the same object). It is distinct from the panpsychist combination problem (how micro-subjects compose a macro-subject) but related as another form of the unity-of-consciousness puzzle; in the VFE program it parallels how attention pools per-feature beliefs into a joint object-level belief.
