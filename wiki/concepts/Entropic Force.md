# Entropic Force

An entropic force is an emergent, effective force arising not from a fundamental microscopic interaction but from a system's statistical tendency to increase entropy (e.g. polymer elasticity, osmosis). In Verlinde's entropic-gravity proposal, gravity itself is reinterpreted as an entropic force tied to information on holographic screens, with Newton's law and Einstein's equations recovered from thermodynamics and the holographic principle. The concept connects information, entropy, and dynamics in a way resonant with the program's free-energy and information-theoretic themes.
