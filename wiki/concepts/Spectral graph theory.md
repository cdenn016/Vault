# Spectral graph theory

Spectral graph theory studies graphs through the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of associated matrices — the adjacency matrix and especially the graph Laplacian. Spectral quantities encode global structure: the algebraic connectivity (Fiedler value) bounds connectivity and mixing time, the spectral gap controls random-walk convergence and expansion, and Laplacian eigenvectors yield spectral clustering and partitioning. It is the mathematical backbone for analyzing diffusion, consensus, and community structure on social and attention networks.
