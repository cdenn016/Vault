# Misinformation and its persistence

Misinformation is false or misleading information that spreads through a population; its defining feature for opinion dynamics is the continued-influence effect — once a belief is encoded it persists and biases reasoning even after a clear, credible retraction. Lewandowsky et al. catalogue why corrections fail (familiarity backfire, mental-model gaps, worldview-protective rejection) and why misinformation propagates preferentially through homophilous, internally-reinforcing clusters. It is the substantive social phenomenon underlying echo chambers, polarization, and the persistence/inertia of belief.
