# Quantum superposition

The superposition principle states that any linear combination of valid quantum states is itself a valid state; quantum mechanics predicts it holds universally, yet macroscopic objects are never observed in superposition. The tension between universal superposition and apparent classicality drives both decoherence accounts (which preserve unitarity) and objective-collapse models (which modify it beyond some mass/complexity scale). Within the program it is a touchstone for participatory-realism discussions of measurement and the quantum-classical boundary.
