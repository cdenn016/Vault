# No-Signalling Principle

The no-signalling principle states that the marginal statistics of a local measurement cannot depend on the choice of distant measurement settings, so no faster-than-light communication is possible from correlations alone. It is a constraint weaker than locality that quantum correlations respect while still violating Bell inequalities, and it defines the no-signalling polytope of generalized probabilistic theories (which contains super-quantum PR boxes). It is a key axiom in information-theoretic reconstructions of quantum theory relevant to the participatory-realism / it-from-bit program.
