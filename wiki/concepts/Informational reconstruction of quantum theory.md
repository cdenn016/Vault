# Informational reconstruction of quantum theory

The *informational reconstruction* program asks a deflationary question about the most successful theory in physics: instead of postulating the Hilbert-space formalism — complex amplitudes, Hermitian operators, the trace rule for outcome probabilities — as an opaque mathematical given, can we *derive* it from transparent, operational principles about how an agent prepares systems, measures them, and updates the probabilities connecting the two? The program's organizing picture is the space of **generalized probabilistic theories (GPTs)**: a state is identified with the list of probabilities it assigns to some fiducial set of measurements, and a candidate theory is fixed by how those numbers relate to one another and to the number of perfectly distinguishable states. Classical probability theory and quantum theory then appear not as incommensurable rivals but as two points in one continuous landscape of conceivable theories, with more exotic alternatives (higher-order interference, super-quantum nonlocality) populating the rest. The reconstruction's goal is to identify the handful of operational axioms that pick out *exactly* the quantum point, thereby explaining *why* the formalism has the shape it does rather than merely asserting that it works.

The canonical instance is Hardy's "five reasonable axioms" ([[hardy-2001-five-axioms]]). Hardy parametrizes a GPT by two integers: $K$, the number of fiducial probabilities ("degrees of freedom") needed to fix a state, and $N$, the maximum number of states that can be reliably distinguished in a single shot. A simplicity relation $K = N^r$ is posited, and five operational axioms — concerning probabilities, simplicity, subspaces, the behaviour of composite systems, and crucially the existence of *continuous* reversible transformations between pure states — force $r = 2$. This $K = N^2$ is precisely the parameter count of the quantum density matrix on an $N$-dimensional Hilbert space, and the rest of the Hilbert-space machinery follows. The decisive observation is that four of the five axioms are shared with classical probability theory; only the continuity axiom is "quantum." Drop it and the theory collapses to the classical $K = N$ (a probability simplex with no continuous reversible mixing of pure states); keep it and one is forced up to quantum theory. Two complementary lines fill in the conceptual content of this skeleton. Spekkens' epistemic toy theory ([[spekkens-2007-toy-theory]]) shows from the *other* direction how much of the "quantum feel" survives in a manifestly classical model governed by a single **knowledge-balance principle** — that an agent in a state of maximal knowledge can answer at most half the questions about the underlying ontic state. From that one restriction emerge noncommutativity, interference, no-cloning, no-broadcasting, the multiplicity of mixed-state decompositions, teleportation, and mutually unbiased bases; the analogy fails exactly at Bell-inequality violation and contextuality, sharply demarcating the genuinely nonclassical residue. The QBist reconstruction of Fuchs and Schack ([[fuchs-schack-2013-bayesian-coherence]]) supplies a third, fully probabilistic recasting: using a symmetric informationally complete measurement (SIC-POVM) as the fiducial reference, the Born rule becomes a *single dimension-dependent deformation* of the classical law of total probability, $q(j) = \sum_i \left[(d+1)\,p(i) - \tfrac{1}{d}\right] r(j\,|\,i)$ — quantum theory written entirely as one coherence constraint among an agent's subjective credences, with no objective state appearing anywhere. Underlying all three is the relational reading of Rovelli ([[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]]), which frames the wavefunction as bookkeeping for the information one system holds about another and explicitly names the derivation of the formalism "from simple informational postulates" as the right interpretive target — prefiguring the whole reconstruction enterprise.

## Relevance to this research

This program is the methodological template — and the discipline — for the gauge-theoretic VFE project's central ambition: that geometric structure should *emerge* from requirements on inference rather than be imposed by fiat. The project treats an agent's belief as a variational posterior, a probability assignment revised to minimize [[Variational free energy]]; this is the inference-theoretic analogue of the GPT "state as a list of fiducial probabilities," and the representational dimension $K$ of belief space is the project's design counterpart to Hardy's $K = N^2$ counting. The aspiration to derive the [[Fisher information metric|Fisher metric]], the [[Quantum information geometry|information geometry]], and the [[Gauge transformation|gauge structure]] from axioms about how agents represent, update, and share information is exactly Hardy's "begin with bare informational primitives, then derive the rich geometry" arc, and his reconstruction sets the bar: if the project claims its geometry is the *natural* one, the standard is to show it follows from reasonable axioms about agent inference, not merely that it is convenient. Fuchs and Schack supply the foundational-physics image of the project's normative stance — that "probability is an agent's commitment and the formalism only constrains how those commitments cohere," the precise counterpart to treating a belief update as an agent-internal consistency condition rather than estimation of an external truth, and the basis on which [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] privileges no global belief state. Spekkens marks the project's *pre-consensus* regime: it tells us which features of agreement and disagreement among agents are explicable by ignorance-plus-coherence alone (and recoverable in a [[Belief inertia|belief-inertia]]/opinion-dynamics setting, cf. [[Opinion dynamics]]), and which demand the stronger relational/transport machinery of [[Parallel transport]] and [[Holonomy]]. Rovelli's "no view from nowhere" becomes the statement that there is no canonical global section over the agents' belief bundle. Reconstruction is thus both the inspiration and the unmet challenge of [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] within the project: the structural correspondences it asserts are, so far, analogies awaiting their own reconstruction theorem.
