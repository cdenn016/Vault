# Hidden metric space

The hidden-metric-space (or hyperbolic network geometry) framework posits that real complex networks are embeddings of nodes in an underlying latent metric space — typically hyperbolic — where connection probability decreases with metric distance. This single geometric assumption simultaneously reproduces the heavy-tailed degree distributions, strong clustering, small-world property, and self-similarity observed in real networks, and underlies geometric renormalization of networks. It explains network navigability and community structure as consequences of latent geometry.
