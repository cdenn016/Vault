# Bayesian reasoning

Bayesian reasoning is the normative model of belief revision in which an agent updates a prior over hypotheses to a posterior in proportion to the likelihood of observed evidence, via Bayes' rule. It serves as the rational benchmark against which empirical departures — confirmation bias, belief perseverance, motivated reasoning — are measured: a Bayesian reasoner weighs confirming and disconfirming evidence symmetrically and seeks maximally diagnostic tests, whereas human reasoners systematically deviate. In this program it is the idealized update rule that the belief-inertia and active-inference dynamics relax, recovering momentum and bias as deviations from instantaneous Bayesian conditioning.
