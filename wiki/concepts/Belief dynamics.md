# Belief dynamics

Belief dynamics studies how agents' beliefs (point estimates or full distributions) evolve over time under evidence, social influence, and interaction. It spans opinion-dynamics models (bounded-confidence, DeGroot, Friedkin-Johnsen) and the program's own inertial / second-order treatments where beliefs carry momentum and mass set by Fisher information. Belief traps, attractors, and hysteresis are characteristic phenomena, linking the social-physics and multi-agent VFE threads.
