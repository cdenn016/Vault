# Hamiltonian belief dynamics

## Definition

**Hamiltonian belief dynamics** is the second-order, momentum-carrying
regime of the gauge-theoretic multi-agent model, in which each agent's
mean belief $\mu_i$ is treated as the position coordinate of a mechanical
system whose potential is the [[Multi-agent variational free energy]]
$\mathcal{F}$ and whose inertia tensor is the four-term Hessian
[[Belief inertia|mass]] $\mathbf{M}$. With conjugate momenta
$\pi = (\pi^\mu, \Pi^\Sigma)$ the dynamics are generated by the
Hamiltonian

$$
H = \tfrac{1}{2}\langle \pi,\, \mathbf{M}^{-1} \pi\rangle + \mathcal{F}[\xi],
$$

where $\xi = (\mu_1,\dots,\mu_N,\Sigma_1,\dots,\Sigma_N)$ is the full
state vector. The equations of motion are Hamilton's equations
$\dot{\xi} = \mathbf{M}^{-1}\pi$ and
$\dot{\pi} = -\nabla\mathcal{F} - \tfrac{1}{2}\nabla_\xi\langle\pi,\mathbf{M}^{-1}\pi\rangle$.

This is explicitly adopted as a *Hamiltonian ansatz* rather than derived
from information geometry alone: the [[Fisher information metric]]
supplies the curvature of the free-energy landscape (a stiffness), and
reading that same precision tensor *also* as an inertial mass is the
additional postulate that turns dissipative gradient flow into
oscillatory mechanics. The model contrasts this regime with the
overdamped first-order [[Natural gradient|natural-gradient]] descent that
recovers classical opinion dynamics.

## Why it matters here

In the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]] the same free-energy
functional drives two qualitatively different integrators, selected by a
single config toggle:

- **Overdamped / first-order** (`natural_gradient`): Fisher-preconditioned
  descent $\dot\mu \propto -\Sigma\,\nabla_\mu\mathcal{F}$. In this limit
  the model reproduces standard Bayesian free-energy descent and the
  classical social-physics limits ([[degroot-1974-consensus|DeGroot]],
  [[friedkin-johnsen-1990|Friedkin-Johnsen]], bounded-confidence
  [[hegselmann-krause-2002|Hegselmann-Krause]] / [[deffuant-2000-bounded-confidence|Deffuant]]).
- **Underdamped / Hamiltonian** (`hamiltonian`): a symplectic leapfrog
  integrator that endows beliefs with momentum, producing oscillation,
  overshoot, resonance, and inter-agent momentum transfer absent from the
  purely dissipative treatment.

Hamiltonian belief dynamics is therefore what distinguishes this model
from first-order active inference and from classical opinion-dynamics
models: it is the regime where the precision-as-[[Mass as Fisher information|mass]]
identification has observable dynamical consequences. It is the
mechanism that reframes confirmation bias and belief perseverance as
geometric consequences of *epistemic inertia* rather than irrationality,
and it makes belief change frame-covariant through the [[Gauge transformation|gauge]]
structure of the transport operators $\Omega_{ij}$.

## Details

**Potential and mass.** The potential is the multi-agent free energy
$\mathcal{F} = \sum_i D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i\|p_i)
- \sum_i \mathbb{E}_{q_i}[\log p(o_i\mid c_i)]
+ \sum_{i,k}\beta_{ik}D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i\|\Omega_{ik}\cdot q_k)$,
with attention weights $\beta_{ik}$ in softmax form over the transported
KL divergences. The inertia tensor is the Hessian
$\mathbf{M} = \partial^2\mathcal{F}/\partial\xi\,\partial\xi^\top$, which
for the mean sector of agent $i$ is the four-term precision sum

$$
M_i = \underbrace{\bar{\Lambda}_{p_i}}_{\text{prior}}
+ \underbrace{\Lambda_{o_i}}_{\text{observation}}
+ \underbrace{\sum_k \beta_{ik}\tilde{\Lambda}_{q_k}}_{\text{incoming social}}
+ \underbrace{\sum_j \beta_{ji}\Lambda_{q_i}}_{\text{outgoing social}},
$$

where $\tilde{\Lambda}_{q_k} = (\Omega_{ik}\Sigma_{q_k}\Omega_{ik}^\top)^{-1}$
is neighbour $k$'s precision transported into agent $i$'s frame. The
off-diagonal blocks couple agents through the gauge transport,
$[\mathbf{M}^\mu]_{ik} = -\beta_{ik}\Omega_{ik}^{-\top}\Lambda_{q_k}
- \beta_{ki}\Lambda_{q_i}\Omega_{ki}^{-1}$.

**Kinetic energy and the covariance sector.** The kinetic term is
$T = \tfrac{1}{2}\dot\mu^\top \mathbf{M}^\mu \dot\mu
+ \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{tr}[\mathbf{M}^\Sigma[\dot\Sigma,\dot\Sigma]]$.
The covariance-sector mass
$[\mathbf{M}^\Sigma]_{ii} = \tfrac{1}{2}(\Lambda_{q_i}\otimes\Lambda_{q_i})
\cdot(1 + \sum_k\beta_{ik} + \sum_j\beta_{ji})$ is the affine-invariant
metric on the SPD cone; sensory precision enters the mean mass but *not*
the covariance mass because the sensory term is quadratic in $\mu$ but
only linear in $\Sigma$.

**Forces.** The potential force decomposes into prior-restoring,
consensus, reciprocal, and sensory-evidence terms, plus a *geodesic
force* $-\tfrac12\,\pi^\top(\partial\mathbf{M}^{-1}/\partial\mu_i)\,\pi$
that encodes manifold curvature.

**Damped reduction and regimes.** Adding dissipation gives
$M_i\ddot\mu_i + \gamma_i\dot\mu_i + \nabla_{\mu_i}\mathcal{F} = 0$.
Linearizing near equilibrium with stiffness $K_i = \nabla^2\mathcal{F}|_{\mu^*}$,
the discriminant $\Delta = \gamma_i^2 - 4K_iM_i$ sorts the dynamics into
overdamped ($\Delta>0$, monotonic Bayesian-style decay), critically
damped ($\Delta=0$, fastest equilibration), and underdamped ($\Delta<0$,
oscillatory overshoot). The strongly overdamped limit, where momentum
relaxes on a fast timescale and the position follows a slow manifold, is
the setting of [[Geometric singular perturbation theory]]: it makes
precise the reduction of the second-order Hamiltonian flow to the
first-order natural-gradient descent recovered in the dissipative limit. The harmonic (energy-conserving) regime predicts
an overshoot distance $d_{\text{overshoot}} = |\dot\mu_i|\sqrt{M_i/K_i}$,
i.e. overshoot scaling as the square root of epistemic mass.

**Symplecticity and the envelope convention.** Because $\beta$ depends on
$\mu$ through the softmax, a naive state-dependent reading of $\mathbf{M}$
would demand the $-\tfrac12\,p^\top(\partial\mathbf{M}^{-1}/\partial\mu)\,p$
geodesic term. The model adopts the envelope-theorem convention: $\beta$
(and $\Omega$) are detached and held at their softmax-equilibrium value
within an integration step, so $\mathbf{M}$ is locally constant and
leapfrog stays symplectic. Under asymmetric $\beta$ the flow is not the
gradient flow of a single conservative potential, so the integrator
symmetrizes $\beta \to \tfrac12(\beta+\beta^\top)$ before assembling the
mass to restore conservativity.

## In this work

This concept is developed in the manuscript [[belief-inertia]] (*The
Inertia of Belief*), which states the Hamiltonian $H = \tfrac12\langle\pi,
\mathbf{M}^{-1}\pi\rangle + \mathcal{F}$, the four-term mass formula,
Hamilton's equations, the damped-regime discriminant, and the
overshoot/oscillation/resonance predictions. It links tightly to
[[Belief inertia]] and [[Mass as Fisher information]], which supply the
precision-as-inertia identification, and to
[[Multi-agent variational free energy]], which is the potential being
descended.

In code it is implemented in `gauge_agent/dynamics.py` as the
`HamiltonianDynamics` class: a symplectic leapfrog integrator
($H = \tfrac12 p_\mu^\top M_{\mu\mu}^{-1} p_\mu
+ \tfrac12\langle p_\Sigma, M_{\Sigma\Sigma}^{-1} p_\Sigma\rangle + V$)
with a first half-kick, position drift, and second half-kick per step.
The inertia tensor is built by `MassMatrix` (`gauge_agent/mass.py`); the
covariance sector is opt-in via `integrate_covariance`. It is selected at
runtime by `CONFIG['dynamics']['dynamics'] = 'hamiltonian'`, in contrast
to the default `'natural_gradient'` overdamped path
(`NaturalGradientDynamics`). Knobs include `dt`, `damping`,
`mass_convention` (`'hessian'` four-term Hessian, or `'ouroboros'`
timescale-separation inertia), `symmetrize_attention`, `use_full_block`
(the $N\!\cdot\!K$ coupled mass), and `fixed_mass` (caches $\mathbf{M}^{-1}$
across the trajectory to restore a true conserved Hamiltonian with
$O(dt^2)$ energy drift). Runs emit a `<name>_energy.{pdf,png}` figure of
$T$, $V$, and $T+V$, and the verifier checks `energy_drift` for
Hamiltonian runs.

> [!note] Editorial: The manuscript explicitly flags the second-order
> dynamics as an *ansatz*, not a consequence of information geometry; the
> overdamped predictions are presented as geometrically necessary while
> the underdamped predictions depend on the ansatz and remain to be
> empirically established. The `belief_inertia.tex` source notes that the
> $\sqrt{M/K}$ overshoot law is not yet directly simulated.
