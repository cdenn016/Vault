# Agents as fibre-bundle sections

## Definition

In the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]] an *agent* is not a point but a **smooth section of associated fibre bundles** over a base manifold $\mathcal{C}$. The construction stacks three pieces:

1. a **base manifold** $\mathcal{C}$, a smooth $n$-dimensional "parameter / index space of contexts" — explicitly *not* spacetime, but a noumenal domain of inquiry (Dennis 2026, Def. Base Manifold);
2. a **principal $G$-bundle** $\pi : \mathcal{N} \to \mathcal{C}$ carrying the gauge freedom, with structure group $G = \mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ (the proof-of-concept simulations restrict to the compact $G = \mathrm{SO}(3)$);
3. two **associated bundles** $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{state}}$ and $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{model}}$ whose fibres are the statistical manifolds of Gaussian distributions over the latent state ($\mathbb{R}^K$) and over the generative-model parameters ($\mathbb{R}^M$).

An agent $\mathcal{A}^i$ at scale $s$ with support domain $\mathcal{U}_i \subseteq \mathcal{C}$ is then the data of two primitive sections, two derived sections, and a gauge-frame field:

$$
\begin{aligned}
q_i &: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{state}}|_{\mathcal{U}_i} && \text{(belief, primitive)}\\
s_i &: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{model}}|_{\mathcal{U}_i} && \text{(generative model, primitive)}\\
p_i &= \Omega_{i,I}\big[q_I^{(s+1)}\big] \in \Gamma\!\left(\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{state}}|_{\mathcal{U}_i}\right) && \text{(prior, derived)}\\
r_i &= \tilde\Omega_{i,I}\big[s_I^{(s+1)}\big] \in \Gamma\!\left(\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{model}}|_{\mathcal{U}_i}\right) && \text{(hyper-prior, derived)}\\
\phi_i &: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathfrak{g} && \text{(gauge frame field)}
\end{aligned}
$$

where $\mathfrak{g} = \mathrm{Lie}(G)$ and $I = \mathrm{pa}(i)$ indexes the level-$(s{+}1)$ meta-agent enveloping $i$, if any. The belief and model are *posited* fields; the prior and hyper-prior are *derived* by [[Parallel transport]] from the meta-agent one scale up (see [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]]).

## Why it matters here

This is the foundational ontological commitment of the model: it is what makes the framework "multi-agent" and "gauge-theoretic" at once. Several distinctive features of the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]] follow directly from agents being *sections* rather than vectors:

- **Spatial extension and overlap.** Because $q_i$ is a field $c \mapsto q_i(c)$ over $\mathcal{U}_i$, multiple agents can hold *different* beliefs about the *same* region of $\mathcal{C}$ wherever their supports overlap ($\mathcal{U}_i \cap \mathcal{U}_j$). Intersubjective structure — the model's formal correlate of objectivity — is generated by this multiplicity; a single agent cannot produce it.
- **Comparison requires transport, not subtraction.** Two sections live in different gauge frames; comparing them is only meaningful after gauge-covariant transport $\Omega_{ij} = U_i U_j^{-1}$ — taking the frame to be $U_i = \exp(\phi_i)$, this is exactly the $\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ form (equivalently $g_i g_j^{-1}$) used in [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] and [[Participatory realism and quantum foundations]] (see [[Gauge transformation]], [[Holonomy]]). This is why the consensus/alignment energy is the *transported* KL $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \,\|\, \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ rather than a naive distance.
- **The variational hierarchy is geometric.** The four statistical sections are organized as
$$
r_i \to s_i \to p_i \to q_i \to \text{observations},
$$
with each arrow realized cross-scale: $p_i$ regularizes $q_i$ *because* $p_i$ is the meta-agent's belief transported down. This converts hierarchical [[Variational free energy]] into a statement about sections of nested associated bundles.
- **Transformers as a limit.** When the base collapses to a point ($\dim\mathcal{C}=0$) the sections become single distributions per agent and standard attention is recovered — the section picture is the general object of which the transformer is the zero-dimensional shadow (developed in [[participatory-it-from-bit]] and [[gl-k-attention]]).

## Details

**Statistical fibres.** Each fibre is a Gaussian family with the Fisher-Rao metric (see [[Fisher information metric]]):

$$
\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{state}} = \{\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma): \mu\in\mathbb{R}^K,\ \Sigma\succ 0\},
\qquad
\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}} = \{\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma): \mu\in\mathbb{R}^M,\ \Sigma\succ 0\}.
$$

For Gaussian agents the sections specialize componentwise to $q_i(c) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_i(c),\Sigma_i(c))$, etc., with $\Sigma \in \mathbb{S}^K_{++}$, and the frame expands in a Lie-algebra basis $\phi_i(c) = \phi_i^a(c)\,G_a$.

**Associated-bundle gluing.** Gauge structure is attached to probability by the associated-bundle equivalence $(n\cdot g,\, b) \sim (n,\, \rho(g)b)$, where $\rho: G \to \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{B})$ is a representation. For Gaussians the representation acts by linear transport of the mean and **congruence** of the covariance:

$$
\rho_{\mathrm{state}}(g)\cdot \mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma) = \mathcal{N}(g\mu,\ g\Sigma g^{\top}).
$$

This is exactly the transport law implemented as $\mu' = \Omega_{ij}\mu_j$, $\Sigma' = \Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^{\top}$. Changing the gauge frame thus transforms an agent's belief *consistently*, which is what makes "the same belief in a different frame" a well-defined notion (cf. [[Group equivariance]]).

**Local trivializations.** A general principal bundle has no global section; the framework therefore works in local trivializing patches $\{\mathcal{U}_i\}$, each $\phi_i$ a local section over agent $i$'s domain. On overlaps the transports $\Omega_{ij}$ are transition functions satisfying the Čech cocycle condition $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk} = \Omega_{ik}$. Because each $U_i$ is defined on its whole patch, this Čech data is a coboundary, so the reconstructed bundle is canonically trivializable — the bundle language is organizational, and nontrivial bundle topology is out of scope in the present construction.

**Two roles of the frame.** The frame field $\phi_i$ does double duty. In its *transport role* (Role A) it is gauge-redundant: dynamics depend only on relative transports $\Omega_{ij}$, invariant under the global right-translation $U_i \mapsto U_i g$. In its *state role* (Role B) the per-agent frame is treated as an internal cognitive state contributing to ontology (distinct pulled-back geometries, distinct phenomenal character). Role-B quantities are invariant only under the constant-per-agent residual subgroup, not the full local gauge. The gauge-redundancy of Role A places agents-as-sections within [[Structural realism]] — what is real is the relational/transport structure, not the frame-dependent representatives — and the per-agent frames function as internal [[Quantum reference frames]] against which only relational quantities are physical.

> [!note] Editorial: The manuscript explicitly flags that the partition/single-tree hypotheses behind the derived sections $p_i,r_i$ are idealizations the reported emergence simulation does not satisfy; in the single-scale runs $p_i,r_i$ are primitive boundary conditions and the slow subsystem $(s_i,r_i)$ is held fixed ($\gamma_{ij}=0$).

## In this work

- **Manuscript.** The construction is developed in [[participatory-it-from-bit]] — §"Principal Bundles and Gauge Freedom", §"Associated Bundles", and §"Agents as Smooth Sections" (the Agent definition). The associated-bundle attachment of [[gl-k-attention]] is the same statistical-fibre-bundle picture specialized to the attention setting.
- **Code.** `gauge_agent/agents.py` defines `Agent` with the docstring "A single agent as a section of two associated bundles," carrying $q_i = \mathcal{N}(\mu_q,\Sigma_q)$ on the belief fibre and $s_i$ on the model fibre, and `MultiAgentSystem` as $\mathcal{M} = \{\mathcal{A}^i = (q_i,p_i,s_i,r_i,\Omega_i,\tilde\Omega_i)\}$. The base-manifold fibres $\mathcal{C}$ (Euclidean / Sphere / Hyperbolic / Torus, each with metric, volume form, exp/log maps) live in `gauge_agent/manifolds.py`. The gauge frame field $\Omega_i$, the transport operator $\Omega_{ij} = \Omega_i\,\Omega_j^{-1}$, and the gauge-aligned KL $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \,\|\, \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ are implemented in `gauge_agent/gauge_structure.py` (`GaugeFrame`, `TransportOperator.gauge_aligned_kl`).
- **Related concepts.** The transport of beliefs between sections is [[Parallel transport]] / [[Holonomy]]; the precision that anchors belief sections feeds [[Belief inertia]] and [[Mass as Fisher information]]; the derived priors realize [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]]; the configuration-counting over populations of belief sections defines [[Meta-entropy]].
