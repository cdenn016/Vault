# Surface charges

Surface charges (boundary charges) are the conserved quantities generated by gauge transformations that act non-trivially at the boundary of a subregion. When a gauge system is split into local subsystems, gauge invariance must be restored by introducing edge modes living on the entangling surface; their associated surface charges measure the residual boundary symmetry. This boundary symplectic/charge structure is central to gauge theory and gravity (e.g. holographic entropy and local subsystems) and motivates the program's treatment of agent boundaries and shared gauge degrees of freedom.
