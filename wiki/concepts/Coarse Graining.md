# Coarse Graining

Coarse graining is the procedure of integrating out or aggregating microscopic degrees of freedom to obtain an effective description at a larger scale, discarding detail while preserving relevant macroscopic behavior. It is the operational heart of statistical mechanics, the renormalization group, and emergent (entropic) descriptions of dynamics, and underlies the program's multi-agent/meta-agent hierarchy and renormalization-group flow of beliefs.
