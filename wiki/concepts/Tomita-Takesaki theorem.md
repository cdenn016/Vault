# Tomita-Takesaki theorem

The Tomita-Takesaki theorem (modular theory) is a result in the theory of von Neumann algebras: given a von Neumann algebra with a cyclic and separating vector, there exists a canonical one-parameter group of *-automorphisms (the modular automorphism group) intrinsic to the algebra-and-state pair. In the Connes-Rovelli thermal time hypothesis this modular flow is identified with a state-dependent, intrinsic notion of physical time, so that time emerges from the statistical state rather than being fundamental. It is the mathematical engine behind the emergence-of-time argument relevant to the participatory / emergent-spacetime theme.
