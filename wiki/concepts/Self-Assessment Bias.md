# Self-Assessment Bias

Self-assessment bias is the systematic tendency of individuals to inaccurately judge their own competence, performance, or knowledge — most famously the Dunning-Kruger effect, in which low-skill individuals overestimate their ability because the metacognitive skill needed to recognize incompetence is the same skill they lack. In a belief-dynamics framing, miscalibrated self-assessment couples directly to belief inertia and the precision (Fisher information) an agent assigns to its own estimates, biasing how it weights its private signal against social information.
