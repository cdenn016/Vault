# Modular automorphism group

The modular (Tomita-Takesaki) automorphism group is the canonical one-parameter group of automorphisms that a faithful normal state induces on a von Neumann algebra. Given such a state, Tomita-Takesaki theory produces a distinguished time-like flow intrinsic to the algebra-state pair, and the Cocycle Radon-Nikodym theorem shows the associated outer automorphism class is state-independent. In the thermal-time hypothesis of Connes and Rovelli this modular flow is postulated to be physical time, tying time's arrow to the thermodynamic state rather than a fixed background, which the program reads as a structural model of observer/state-relative dynamics.
