# Phenomenal Consciousness

Phenomenal consciousness (Ned Block's P-consciousness) is the qualitative, experiential 'what-it-is-likeness' of a mental state — its raw feel or qualia — as opposed to its functional role in reasoning, report, and behavioral control. It is the locus of the hard problem of consciousness and is what phenomenal unity (the total phenomenal state) unifies in the Bayne-Chalmers account. It is distinguished sharply from access consciousness, which concerns availability of content to global processing.
