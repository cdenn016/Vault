# Fixed Points

A fixed point of a transformation (map) is a state left invariant by it. In renormalization-group flow, fixed points of the coarse-graining transformation govern universal large-scale behavior: stable (attractive) fixed points draw in distributions (e.g. the Gaussian for the central limit theorem), while unstable ones mark critical points / phase boundaries; the relevant vs. irrelevant directions classify perturbations. The concept anchors the program's RG-style analysis of belief dynamics and coarse-grained, scale-invariant structure.
