# Diffeomorphism invariance

Diffeomorphism invariance is the requirement that the laws of physics be unchanged under arbitrary smooth, invertible coordinate transformations (diffeomorphisms) of the underlying manifold — the defining gauge symmetry of general relativity. It implies that points and coordinates carry no intrinsic physical meaning; only relational, coordinate-independent quantities are observable. As the spacetime analogue of the GL(k)/gauge reparametrization freedom in the VFE program, it is the symmetry whose local realization underlies horizon thermodynamics and the observer-relative, background-independent picture of physics.
