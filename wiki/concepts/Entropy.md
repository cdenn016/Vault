# Entropy

Shannon entropy H(p) = -sum p(x) log p(x) quantifies the average uncertainty / information content of a distribution, introduced by Shannon (1948) as the unique measure satisfying continuity, monotonicity, and additivity. It is the foundation for relative entropy (KL divergence), mutual information, and the variational free energy decomposition (energy minus entropy) that underlies the program. In the participatory and consciousness strands it appears as the entropic-brain measure and as the quantity maximized under the maximum-entropy principle.
