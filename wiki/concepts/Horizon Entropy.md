# Horizon Entropy

Horizon entropy is the entropy assigned to a causal horizon, proportional to its area: S = A/(4 G) (Bekenstein-Hawking). Treating local Rindler (causal) horizons as carrying area-entropy and the Unruh temperature, Jacobson derived the Einstein field equations from the Clausius relation delta Q = T dS, recasting gravity as an equation of state / thermodynamic phenomenon rather than a fundamental force. It is a cornerstone of the entropic / informational view of spacetime.
