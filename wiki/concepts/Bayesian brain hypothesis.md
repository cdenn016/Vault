# Bayesian brain hypothesis

The Bayesian brain hypothesis holds that the nervous system represents and processes information probabilistically, maintaining and updating a generative model of sensory causes via approximately Bayesian inference. Perception is cast as inverting this model to infer hidden causes, and learning as updating its parameters. It is the conceptual umbrella under which predictive coding and the free-energy principle sit, supplying the inferential framing that the gauge-theoretic VFE program inherits when it treats beliefs (mu, Sigma) as posterior sufficient statistics.
