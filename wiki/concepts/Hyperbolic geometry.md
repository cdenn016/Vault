# Hyperbolic geometry

Hyperbolic geometry is the non-Euclidean geometry of constant negative curvature, with the Poincare disk/ball as a standard model; volume grows exponentially with radius, making it the natural host for tree-like and hierarchical data. Nickel & Kiela (2017) embed hierarchies in the Poincare ball, achieving high-fidelity, low-dimensional representations of taxonomies; Garcia-Perez et al. (2018) use hyperbolic geometry for multiscale network structure. In the program it is the geometric counterpoint to the SPD/Fisher belief manifold and a candidate latent space for hierarchical belief and meta-agent structure.
