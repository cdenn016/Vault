# Dynamic functional connectivity

Dynamic functional connectivity (dFC) treats the statistical coupling between brain regions as time-varying rather than static, typically estimated with sliding-window or instantaneous-phase methods on fMRI/EEG signals. Its repertoire of recurring connectivity states and their transition dynamics are used as markers of cognitive flexibility and conscious level: richer, more diverse dFC repertoires correlate with wakeful conscious states, while reduced diversity accompanies loss of consciousness. It is a primary observable in entropy-and-consciousness and integration/diversity accounts.
