# Phase Transitions

A phase transition is a qualitative change in the macroscopic state of a many-body system as a control parameter (temperature, coupling, noise) crosses a critical value — e.g. the paramagnet-to-ferromagnet transition in the Ising model. Continuous (second-order) transitions exhibit diverging correlation length and power-law observables governed by critical exponents (see [[Critical Phenomena]]), classified into universality classes by the renormalization group. In the program, phase transitions model the onset of collective order — consensus or belief synchronization in multi-agent systems and opinion dynamics — and the regime boundaries of the VFE-minimization flow under varying coupling.
