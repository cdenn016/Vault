# Observer-dependent facts and Wigner's friend

## Definition

The **Wigner's-friend** scenario asks what happens when one observer (Wigner) treats another observer (the friend) as a quantum system. The friend, sealed in a lab, measures a superposed system and obtains a definite outcome; Wigner, outside, describes friend-plus-system by a unitarily evolving entangled state in which no outcome has yet occurred. Whether the friend's result is a *fact* therefore appears to depend on whose description is taken. **Extended Wigner's-friend** experiments — Frauchiger–Renner's nested-agent thought experiment and Brukner's no-go theorem — sharpen this into formal impossibility results. They show that a small set of intuitively appealing assumptions, jointly that measurement outcomes are **observer-independent facts** (single-world absoluteness), that agents may reason about one another's correct conclusions (consistency/self-reference), that records can be measured (universal validity of quantum mechanics), and locality/freedom-of-choice, cannot all hold. Either some measured outcomes are not absolute facts shared across all perspectives, or one of the reasoning principles fails. A **observer-dependent fact** is then an outcome that is real *relative to* a measuring agent's perspective without being a perspective-independent element of reality.

## Why it matters here

This debate is the sharpest physics-side test of the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] program that animates the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]]. The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] takes seriously the "observer-dependent reality" rung of Wheeler's table: different agents pull back *different* induced geometries from the same noumenal substrate through their own [[Gauge transformation|gauge]] frames, and there is no agent-independent "correct" geometry. The Wigner's-friend no-go theorems are precisely the argument that nature *forces* such observer-dependence at the quantum, scale-0 level. The model's distinctive move is its answer to the obvious worry such relativism raises: if facts are perspectival, where does shared, stable reality come from? The participatory answer is **consensus-as-objectivity**. Objectivity is not a frame-independent substrate that all observers read off, but an emergent structure built from inter-agent agreement — the regulated consensus / Haar-averaged metric over realised gauge frames, and the cross-scale loop in which agent statistics propagate upward into [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence|meta-agents]] and back down as shared priors via [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]]. Crucially for the analogy, the framework also forbids *total* consensus: complete agreement is the informational "heat death" with no perspective diversity left to drive dynamics, mirroring the no-go theorems' verdict that a single absolute fact-set is unattainable.

## Details

The repair on the physics side that the model most resembles is **relational quantum mechanics**' move to **cross-perspective links** ([[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]]; Adlam–Rovelli). Brukner's theorem leaves RQM exposed if facts are purely private to each observer; the cross-perspective-link postulate restores enough inter-observer structure — that an outcome recorded relative to one observer is recoverable when a second observer later measures the record — to block paradox without reinstating absolute facts. This is structurally the same gesture as consensus-as-objectivity: shared reality is reconstructed from links between perspectives rather than presupposed beneath them. Neighbouring interpretive options frame the same data differently. [[QBism]] reads quantum states as one agent's personal degrees of belief, dissolving the paradox by denying the friend's state has third-person content at all — a stance close to the model's pan-agentic, belief-first ontology. The reconstruction programs of [[hardy-2001-five-axioms|Hardy's five axioms]] and [[spekkens-2007-toy-theory|Spekkens' toy theory]] locate observer-dependence in an epistemic restriction on knowledge rather than in ontic facts, suggesting the perspectivalism is information-theoretic at root. [[Quantum reference frames]] make the frame-dependence literal and transformable, the closest quantum analogue of the model's gauge-frame-dependent pullback. Zurek's decoherence and einselection supply the complementary story of *how* robust, redundantly recorded outcomes (quantum Darwinism) become the candidate shared facts — the physical mechanism by which consensus could crystallise from many observers measuring the same environmental records.
