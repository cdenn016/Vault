# Superselection rules

A superselection rule forbids coherent superpositions across certain sectors of a quantum Hilbert space, so that observables connecting those sectors are unphysical (e.g. charge or particle-number superselection). Bartlett, Rudolph and Spekkens show that an apparent superselection rule can be understood as an artifact of lacking a shared reference frame: averaging (twirling) over the missing frame's symmetry group induces an effective restriction equivalent to the superselection rule, which is lifted once a suitable quantum reference frame is supplied.
