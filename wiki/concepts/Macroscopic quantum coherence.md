# Macroscopic quantum coherence

The persistence of quantum superposition and phase coherence in massive or macroscopic systems, normally destroyed rapidly by decoherence. Experimentally pursued via ground-state cooling of mechanical oscillators (e.g. levitated nanoparticles), it sets the stage for probing the quantum-classical boundary and proposals to test whether gravity can generate entanglement between massive objects.
