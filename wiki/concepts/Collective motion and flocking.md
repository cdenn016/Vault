# Collective motion and flocking

## Definition

**Collective motion** is the coherent, large-scale movement that emerges when many self-propelled agents — birds, fish, insects, cells, robots, pedestrians — each follow simple local interaction rules with no central controller. The canonical claim of the field is that lifelike flocks, herds, schools, swarms, and mills are not orchestrated from above but self-organize from the bottom up: global order is an emergent property of local coupling. The original distributed demonstration is Reynolds' **boids** model ([[reynolds-1987-boids]]), in which each simulated bird steers by three local urges evaluated over its visible neighbors — separation (avoid crowding), alignment (match average heading), and cohesion (move toward the local center of mass) — and lifelike flocking appears with no leader and no global plan. The minimal statistical-physics distillation is the **Vicsek model** ([[vicsek-1995-self-driven-particles]]): particles move at constant speed and at each step adopt the average heading of neighbors within a fixed radius plus an angular noise term, $\theta_i(t+1) = \langle\theta_j\rangle_{|\mathbf{x}_j-\mathbf{x}_i|<r} + \eta\xi_i$. As the noise $\eta$ falls below a critical $\eta_c$, the normalized average velocity $\varphi = \frac{1}{Nv_0}|\sum_i\mathbf{v}_i|$ rises continuously from zero — a genuine nonequilibrium phase transition from disorder to collective directed motion via spontaneous breaking of rotational symmetry. Couzin's **zonal model** ([[couzin-2002-collective-memory]]) reintroduces biological realism with three concentric response zones (repulsion, alignment, attraction); varying only the alignment-zone width drives abrupt, *hysteretic* transitions between swarm, torus (mill), and polarized states, so the group's current configuration encodes its history — a collective memory held by the population, not by any individual. The comprehensive map of the whole field, across every scale from molecular motors to human crowds, is the Vicsek–Zafeiris review ([[vicsek-zafeiris-2012-collective-motion]]), which organizes models by ingredient (self-propulsion, alignment, attraction/repulsion, noise, metric vs topological neighborhood) and by description level (agent-based, kinetic, hydrodynamic).

The field has three further pillars. **Coarse-graining** the discrete rules yields the Toner–Tu hydrodynamic field theory ([[toner-tu-1995-flocking-hydrodynamics]]), continuum equations for a density $\rho(\mathbf{r},t)$ and a polarization field $\mathbf{v}(\mathbf{r},t)$ whose advective nonlinearity $\lambda(\mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla)\mathbf{v}$ — absent in equilibrium XY models — lets a driven flock sustain true long-range orientational order even in two dimensions, in apparent defiance of the equilibrium Mermin–Wagner theorem, and places flocking in its own nonequilibrium universality class with anomalous scaling exponents extracted by dynamical renormalization group. **Empirical structure** comes from stereoscopic field measurements of real starling flocks: Ballerini et al. ([[ballerini-2008-topological-interaction]]) show that each bird coordinates with a roughly fixed *number* of nearest neighbors (about six or seven), so interaction is governed by topological rank rather than metric radius, which makes flocks robust to density fluctuations. **Inference** comes from Bialek et al. ([[bialek-2012-statistical-mechanics-flocks]]), who fit a maximum-entropy (Heisenberg-like) model constrained only by the measured pairwise direction correlations and reproduce flock-wide order and scale-free correlations with essentially no free parameters. Two further results sharpen the social reading: a vanishing fraction of informed, goal-biased leaders can steer an entire naive group with no signaling ([[couzin-2005-effective-leadership]]), and "swarmalators" ([[okeeffe-2017-swarmalators]]) couple each agent's spatial position to an internal Kuramoto-style phase, producing collective states — static and active phase waves — that neither pure synchronization nor pure swarming can reach.

## Why it matters here

Collective motion is the SocialPhysics program's nearest natural-systems sibling, and the honest summary is that it functions mostly as **phenomenological template and intellectual-neighborhood precedent**, with a few genuinely strong methodological bridges and one structural analogy that is unusually on-point — but it is *not*, with one partial exception, machinery that the [[belief-inertia]] variational free-energy functional actually contains. The strongest and most legitimate connection is the shared bottom-up emergence ethos: boids and Vicsek establish that global order can arise purely from local, neighbor-based rules with no central controller, which is exactly the stance of [[Multi-agent variational free energy]], where each agent minimizes its own VFE from local gauge-transported coupling and collective structure (consensus, factions, [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence|meta-agents]]) is emergent rather than imposed. The Vicsek order–disorder transition is the physical template for the program's consensus-versus-fragmentation transition: as idiosyncratic "noise" on beliefs rises past a threshold, collective alignment collapses, the same symmetry-breaking story [[Sociophysics]] tells with spins.

Several bridges are methodological and run deeper than analogy. Toner–Tu's coarse-graining of discrete alignment into an order-parameter field, with dynamical-RG scaling exponents, is the literal physics template for the program's [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]] — flowing from discrete coupled agents to a continuum belief field is the same operation, even though the advective hydrodynamics itself is not in the belief functional. Bialek's maximum-entropy reconstruction of a flock is the same inferential principle — least-structured distribution subject to measured constraints — that underlies treating a belief population as a statistical ensemble and grounds the configuration-counting of [[Meta-entropy]]; this is a shared inferential method, not shared spin couplings. Ballerini's topological-interaction finding supplies empirical justification for the program's sparse, fixed-rank coupling: attention already selects a small, content-determined partner set per agent ($\beta_{ij}=\mathrm{softmax}_j(-\mathrm{KL}(q_i\|\Omega_{ij}[q_j])/(\kappa\sqrt{K}))$) rather than everyone within a fixed divergence ball, so real flocks are the natural-systems precedent for that design choice and for the graph-Laplacian / [[Community detection and modularity|community-structure]] analyses built on it.

Two further links touch the program's two-regime claim. On the overdamped side, Couzin's informed-minority leadership ([[couzin-2005-effective-leadership]]) is a consensus-and-influence result whose goal-biased, anchored leaders are structurally the stubborn agents of Friedkin–Johnsen ([[Opinion dynamics]], [[Bounded confidence]]): the $\omega$-weighted blend of social direction and private goal is the same trade-off as anchoring a belief to a private prior while coupling to neighbors, which makes it a clean bridge between the collective-motion and opinion-dynamics framings. On the underdamped side, Couzin's hysteresis and collective memory ([[couzin-2002-collective-memory]]) are phenomenologically what momentum-carrying [[Belief inertia]] predicts — overshoot, lag, and path dependence so the population's state depends on its trajectory — but I should be plain that the mechanism there is multistability of a *first-order* zonal model, not literal inertial mass; it is a close behavioral analogue, not the second-order equation $M\ddot\mu + \gamma\dot\mu + \nabla F = 0$ of [[Hamiltonian belief dynamics]]. The single most on-point structural analogy is the swarmalator ([[okeeffe-2017-swarmalators]]): coupling an agent's internal phase to its spatial coupling is the toy-physics image of coupling a belief $(\mu,\Sigma)$ to its gauge frame $\phi$ through $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$, where phase-locking and value-coupling interact — a strong analogy for the joint sync-plus-coupling picture, still short of shared equations.

## Details

The models form a ladder of abstraction. At the agent-based bottom, Reynolds' boids fix the paradigm — a steering acceleration $\mathbf{a}_i = w_s\mathbf{a}^{\text{sep}}_i + w_a\mathbf{a}^{\text{align}}_i + w_c\mathbf{a}^{\text{coh}}_i$ over locally perceived neighbors with no global state ([[reynolds-1987-boids]]) — and Couzin's zonal model ([[couzin-2002-collective-memory]]) makes the three urges biologically explicit as concentric repulsion, alignment, and attraction zones, with a maximum turning rate and orientation noise. The Vicsek model ([[vicsek-1995-self-driven-particles]]) strips this to the single ingredient that produces order, alignment-plus-noise at fixed speed, and exposes the order parameter $\varphi$ and its continuous vanishing $\varphi\sim(\eta_c-\eta)^\beta$ at criticality, establishing collective motion as a nonequilibrium phase transition distinct from any equilibrium ferromagnet precisely because the particles move. Climbing the ladder, Toner–Tu ([[toner-tu-1995-flocking-hydrodynamics]]) coarse-grain to the continuum, $\partial_t\mathbf{v} + \lambda(\mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla)\mathbf{v} = \alpha\mathbf{v} - \beta|\mathbf{v}|^2\mathbf{v} - \nabla P + D\nabla^2\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{f}$ together with continuity $\partial_t\rho + \nabla\cdot(\rho\mathbf{v}) = 0$, where the $\alpha,\beta$ terms select a nonzero mean speed (broken rotational symmetry) and the advective term stabilizes 2D long-range order; the Vicsek–Zafeiris review ([[vicsek-zafeiris-2012-collective-motion]]) is the map that situates each of these by ingredient and description level.

The empirical and inferential pillars constrain which abstractions are right. Ballerini et al. ([[ballerini-2008-topological-interaction]]) measure the angular anisotropy of neighbor positions as a function of neighbor rank and find it persists to a characteristic rank $n_c\approx 6$–7 and then vanishes — coupling is set by topological rank, not metric distance — which is why flock cohesion survives large density changes. Bialek et al. ([[bialek-2012-statistical-mechanics-flocks]]) then build the maximum-entropy model consistent with the measured local alignment, a Heisenberg-like distribution $P(\{\mathbf{s}_i\}) = Z^{-1}\exp(\tfrac{1}{2}\sum_i J\sum_{j\in n_i}\mathbf{s}_i\cdot\mathbf{s}_j)$ in which each bird couples to a fixed number $n_c\approx 11$–21 of neighbors; fitting just the coupling $J$ and range $n_c$ reproduces the full correlation function, the four-point statistics, and the scale-free correlations of real flocks. The two findings are mutually consistent — both point to fixed-number coupling — and together they are the cleanest demonstration that maximum entropy plus local constraints captures emergent collective order, the same logic the program uses to treat a belief population as an ensemble.

The control and synchronization extensions matter for the social reading. Couzin et al. ([[couzin-2005-effective-leadership]]) give informed individuals a goal direction, $\mathbf{d}_i'(t+\tau) = (\mathbf{d}_i + \omega\mathbf{g}_i)/|\mathbf{d}_i + \omega\mathbf{g}_i|$, naive individuals $\omega=0$, and show the group's directional accuracy rises sharply with the informed fraction $p$ and saturates, with the fraction needed shrinking as group size grows — leadership and consensus from local coupling plus a small biased minority, the collective-motion image of stubborn-agent steering. O'Keeffe, Hong, and Strogatz ([[okeeffe-2017-swarmalators]]) close the loop between motion and phase: each swarmalator carries position $\mathbf{x}_i$ and phase $\theta_i$ that co-evolve, $\dot{\mathbf{x}}_i$ blending attraction modulated by phase similarity $(1+J\cos(\theta_j-\theta_i))$ with hard-core repulsion, and $\dot\theta_i = \tfrac{K}{N}\sum_j \sin(\theta_j-\theta_i)/|\mathbf{x}_j-\mathbf{x}_i|$ a distance-weighted Kuramoto term, yielding five collective states including static and active phase waves. Because phase drives motion and motion drives phase, swarmalators reach behavior neither synchronization nor swarming alone can — the cleanest existing precedent for the program's coupled belief-value and gauge-frame dynamics, where the transport $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ ties an internal frame to inter-agent coupling.

Taken together, this body of work sits within the broader theme of the [[Statistical physics of social systems and collective behavior]]: it is the animal-and-active-matter wing whose order parameters, symmetry-breaking transitions, coarse-graining, and maximum-entropy inference the belief-inertia program recapitulates in belief space — strongly as shared method (coarse-graining, max-ent, sparse coupling) and as honest analogy (hysteresis-as-memory, swarmalator-as-frame-coupling) rather than as imported equations.
