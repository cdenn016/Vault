# Black Hole Thermodynamics

Black hole thermodynamics is the correspondence between the laws of black hole mechanics and the laws of thermodynamics: a black hole carries an entropy proportional to its horizon area (Bekenstein-Hawking, S = A/4) and a temperature proportional to its surface gravity (Hawking radiation), with the area theorem playing the role of the second law. Because entropy scales with horizon area rather than enclosed volume, these results are the original motivation for the [[Emergent spacetime and holography|holographic principle]] and the [[Covariant Entropy Bound]]. In the program they anchor the participatory/holographic theme linking information, geometry, and observation. See [[bekenstein-1973-black-holes-entropy]] and [[bousso-2002-holographic]].
