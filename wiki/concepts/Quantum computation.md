# Quantum computation

Quantum computation is information processing that uses quantum states (superposition, entanglement, unitary evolution, measurement) as the computational substrate, with operations bounded by quantum speed limits such as the Margolus-Levitin theorem. In the participatory/physics-from-information thread it appears via Lloyd's framing of the universe itself as a quantum computer whose dynamics is computation, linking physical law, energy, and the maximum number of logical operations performable. It is a touchstone for the it-from-bit view that information is physically fundamental.
