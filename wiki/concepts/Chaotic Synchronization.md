# Chaotic Synchronization

Chaotic synchronization is the phenomenon whereby two or more coupled chaotic dynamical systems — despite sensitive dependence on initial conditions — converge onto a common or related trajectory. Variants include complete (identical) synchronization, generalized synchronization, phase synchronization, and lag synchronization, with stability typically diagnosed via the transverse (conditional) Lyapunov exponents or the master-stability function. Unlike Kuramoto phase locking of simple oscillators, the synchronized units here are themselves chaotic, making the emergent coherence a stronger form of collective order.
