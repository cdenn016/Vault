# Global neuronal workspace theory

The **global neuronal workspace** (GNW) theory is a neuroscientific account of *conscious access*: the claim that a sensory representation becomes conscious not when it is merely processed, but when it is amplified and made *globally available* to the brain's many otherwise-local specialized processors. On this view the cortex is a patchwork of modular subsystems — visual, auditory, motor, mnemonic, linguistic — each computing locally and largely in parallel. Most of their activity stays subliminal and confined to its module. A stimulus crosses the threshold into awareness only when its representation is recruited into a distributed fronto-parietal "workspace" that broadcasts it back out to all the modules at once, so that the content can be reported, held in working memory, and flexibly combined with other information. The theory is laid out as a general-audience synthesis by Dehaene ([[dehaene-2014-consciousness-brain]]) and stated in its contemporary primary-literature form by Mashour, Roelfsema, Changeux and Dehaene ([[mashour-2020-global-workspace]]).

## Ignition: the non-linear broadcast transition

The mechanistic core of GNW is **ignition**. Below a critical level of stimulus strength and attention, fronto-parietal activity remains low and the representation stays local and unconscious. Above it, recurrent (top-down and lateral) connections drive a sudden, self-amplifying, non-linear surge of sustained activity across the workspace network — the representation "ignites" and is broadcast globally ([[mashour-2020-global-workspace]]). The transition is *all-or-none* rather than graded: it has the character of a bifurcation, in which a small change in input or attentional gain flips the network from a quiescent local state to a globally coherent, self-sustaining one. Schematically, if $A$ is workspace activity driven by feedforward input $s$ and recurrent gain $g$, conscious access corresponds to the system crossing a threshold $A^\ast$ at which the recurrent term overcomes decay, $g\,\partial A > $ leak, so that activity latches into the high, broadcasting branch. Dehaene marshals the empirical *signatures* of this transition — a late ($\sim 300$ ms) all-or-none amplification, a P3b-like global event, sustained fronto-parietal firing, and a "global availability" of the content to report — as the experimental fingerprint distinguishing conscious from subliminal processing ([[dehaene-2014-consciousness-brain]]). The defining property of the ignited state is therefore not richer local representation but *coherent coupling*: many distributed processors transiently locked onto a single shared content.

## Relevance to this research

GNW matters to this program less as a brain theory than as a *structural template* for how local content becomes collectively available — the same problem the gauge-theoretic [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] model faces one level up. In the meta-agent tower, individual agents are local processors carrying belief states $q_i$, coupled to neighbours through transport that penalizes disagreement; coherent collective beliefs propagate across the coupled population in much the way an ignited representation propagates across cortical modules ([[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]]). The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] cites GNW precisely on this parallel: ignition-and-broadcast — a local fragment crossing a non-linear threshold into global availability — is structurally echoed by the emergence of a coherent meta-agent belief from many low-level agents. The analogy is sharpest at the bifurcation: just as ignition is an all-or-none latching driven by recurrent gain, the formation of a higher-scale "meta-agent" is a coarse-graining transition in which sufficiently strong coupling makes a population's beliefs cohere into a single shareable content rather than remain a heap of local opinions — the same threshold phenomenon studied as consensus formation in [[Opinion dynamics]], and conditioned by the resistance-to-change captured by [[Belief inertia]]. Within the broader landscape of [[Mathematical consciousness science]], GNW sits as the *access/broadcast* theory alongside integration-based ($\Phi$) and predictive-processing accounts; the project treats its broadcast mechanism as one face of [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]], where what is "real and available" to a system is the content its coupled internal frames jointly register. As flagged in [[Consciousness and the hard problem]], this correspondence is an *interpretive* alignment the program asserts, not a theorem it imports: GNW addresses Chalmers' *easy* problem of conscious access and reportability, and supplies the program a vivid mechanism for coherent belief propagation rather than a solution to the explanatory gap.
