# Mathematical consciousness science

## Definition

**Mathematical consciousness science** is the program of treating theories of consciousness not as informal verbal frameworks but as explicit mathematical objects — sets, maps, functors, irreducibility functionals — so that their claims can be proved, compared, and falsified rather than merely asserted. Its paradigm case is **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)**, which posits that the quantity and quality of an experience are fixed by a system's integrated information $\Phi$ and its associated cause–effect structure. Two complementary strands give IIT mathematical content. The first, Kleiner and Tull's reconstruction, strips IIT 3.0 down to a chain of well-defined maps — physical system and state $\to$ cause/effect repertoires $\to$ irreducible "concepts" found by minimizing over a partition lattice $\to$ a system-level cause–effect structure ("Q-shape") $\to$ $\Phi^{\max}$ under the exclusion postulate — and abstracts the distance measure, mechanism space, and partition scheme into parameters, yielding a *generalized* IIT of which classical and quantum IIT are special cases. The second, the Tsuchiya–Taguchi–Saigo proposal, supplies the missing structure-to-experience bridge using **category theory**: model qualia as objects whose morphisms encode experiential relations, and invoke the **Yoneda lemma** — an object is determined up to isomorphism by its complete profile of morphisms — to license the thesis that fixing all relations fixes the phenomenal character. This is phenomenal structuralism with a formal engine.

## Why it matters here

This concept is the formal backdrop against which the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] positions its own structuralism, and it sharpens two distinct PIFB threads. The Tsuchiya–Saigo Yoneda reading is a near-twin of PIFB's central move: PIFB relocates the [[Explanatory gap]] by holding that phenomenal character is fixed by *relational/structural* content rather than by an intrinsic substrate property, which is precisely the Yoneda claim that identity is the totality of relations. The correspondence is interpretive, not a borrowed theorem — PIFB does not import the categorical machinery — but the *shape* of the claim matches, and it maps cleanly onto the gauge-theoretic model where a belief is already fixed by its couplings and transports to other beliefs ([[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], [[Gauge transformation]], [[Holonomy]]). The IIT contrast runs the other way. IIT makes information *intrinsic* to a substrate and observer-independent; PIFB makes geometry and content *frame-relative* and participatory. PIFB registers this contrast rather than entering the IIT debate: Kleiner and Tull's formalized $\Phi$-structure is the rigorous object that lets the manuscript state its difference exactly — an intrinsic-information identity theory versus a frame-relative one — instead of arguing informally.

## Details

The two strands are siblings, not rivals: Kleiner–Tull formalize *what the structure is* (an irreducibility functional over a partition lattice, with exclusion as maximization), while Tsuchiya–Saigo formalize *how that structure could fix experience* (a Yoneda-style relational determination). Their IIT-shaped notion of emergence — a collective is a genuine higher-level unit only when irreducible to independent parts — parallels [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and the [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]] tower, except that the participatory program grounds emergence in [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] and [[Fisher information metric]] geometry rather than in $\Phi$. See [[tononi-2016-iit]] for the originating verbal theory the formalizations target.

IIT is only the most-formalized member of a broader landscape that mathematical consciousness science seeks to render precise and comparable. The **global neuronal workspace** theory (Dehaene's synthesis [[dehaene-2014-consciousness-brain]]; the primary statement Mashour et al. [[mashour-2020-global-workspace]]) locates conscious access in a non-linear "ignition" that broadcasts a representation for global availability — a candidate to be formalized as a dynamical bifurcation, and structurally echoed by the program's account of coherent collective belief propagation across coupled agents. The **free-energy / active-inference** route of Solms and Friston [[solms-friston-2018-how-consciousness-arises]] is the most directly formal rival to the program's own machinery: it makes a near-*identity* claim (feeling is what it is like to be a free-energy-minimizing, self-evidencing system), where PIFB instead treats free-energy structure as the dispositional role and the protophenomenal as its occupant. Information-theoretic *markers* supply candidate order parameters for a quantitative science: neural entropy as a correlate of conscious-state richness ([[carhart-harris-2014-entropic-brain]]) and a consciousness-specific balance of integration and functional diversity ([[luppi-2019-consciousness-integration-diversity]]), both of which connect to the program's [[Meta-entropy]] and integration/segregation trade-offs. Finally, Lahav and Neemeh's *relativistic* proposal ([[lahav-neemeh-2022-relativistic-consciousness]]) is the clearest case where the program *adds* missing formal content: they assert frame-dependence of consciousness but supply no transformation law, and the gauge transport $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ ([[Gauge transformation]], [[Holonomy]]) is offered as that law — turning a metaphor into a theory with a transformation group. The quantum-collapse route of Orch-OR ([[hameroff-penrose-1996-orch-or]]) rounds out the landscape as a formalized but contested foil the program does not adopt. The wider synthesis of how these theories relate is the [[Consciousness and the hard problem]] theme.
