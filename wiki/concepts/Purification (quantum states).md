# Purification (quantum states)

A purification of a mixed density operator rho on a Hilbert space H is a pure state |psi> on an enlarged space H tensor H_E whose partial trace over the ancilla E returns rho. Every mixed state admits purifications, and they are unique up to an isometry on the ancilla. Uhlmann's theorem expresses the quantum fidelity between two states as the maximal pure-state overlap over all joint purifications, which is the route by which Uhlmann (1976) derived the transition probability / Bures-Uhlmann fidelity. Purification underlies entanglement-based accounts of mixed-state geometry and the holographic 'thermofield-double' constructions.
