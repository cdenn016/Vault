# Attention and consciousness

The relationship between selective attention and conscious access. In global-workspace accounts (Dehaene, Baars) top-down attention gates which contents win the competition for global broadcast ('ignition') and thereby reach consciousness, but attention and consciousness are partially dissociable: stimuli can be attended without being consciously reported, and some conscious percepts arise with minimal attention. The distinction matters for the program's reading of attention mechanisms as precision-weighting / access-gating rather than as consciousness itself.
