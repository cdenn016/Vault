# Rindler observer

A Rindler observer is a uniformly accelerated observer in flat (Minkowski) spacetime, who perceives a causal horizon (the Rindler horizon) beyond which signals cannot reach them. By the Unruh effect such an observer sees the Minkowski vacuum as a thermal bath at temperature proportional to their acceleration, giving the horizon a temperature and entropy. Rindler observers make horizon thermodynamics observer-relative even without gravity, underpinning Padmanabhan's derivation of gravitational field equations from local horizon thermodynamics and exemplifying the observer-dependence of physical facts that motivates participatory realism.
