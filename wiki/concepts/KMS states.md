# KMS states

A KMS (Kubo-Martin-Schwinger) state is the operator-algebraic characterization of thermal equilibrium at inverse temperature beta: it is the analytic boundary condition relating the two orderings of a product of observables evolved under a one-parameter automorphism group. KMS states are exactly the equilibrium states with respect to that dynamics, and via Tomita-Takesaki theory a faithful state is KMS at beta=1 with respect to its own modular automorphism group. This links equilibrium, time evolution, and the algebra-state structure that underlies the thermal-time hypothesis.
