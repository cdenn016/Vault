# Dirac Quantization

Dirac quantization is the canonical procedure for quantizing constrained Hamiltonian systems: first-class constraints generating gauge symmetries are imposed as operator conditions annihilating physical states (the 'quantize-then-constrain' route), yielding the physical Hilbert space as the kernel of the constraint operators. It underlies gauge-theory and gravity quantization and, in the quantum-reference-frame literature, the perspective-neutral structure from which frame-relative descriptions are recovered by gauge fixing.
