# Symplectic geometry

Symplectic geometry studies smooth manifolds equipped with a closed, non-degenerate 2-form (the symplectic form), the natural arena for Hamiltonian mechanics and phase space. The symplectic form converts a Hamiltonian function into a flow via Hamilton's equations and underlies the covariant phase-space / extended-phase-space formulation of gauge theory and gravity, where boundary symplectic structure gives rise to edge modes and surface charges. It also grounds the Hamiltonian view of belief dynamics in the program.
