# de Finetti representation theorem

The de Finetti representation theorem states that any exchangeable sequence of random variables can be written as a mixture of i.i.d. sequences, so a subjective Bayesian can treat 'unknown probabilities' as an integral over independent trials weighted by a prior. Its quantum generalization (the quantum de Finetti theorem) shows that permutation-symmetric multipartite states are approximated by mixtures of i.i.d. product states. In QBism / quantum-Bayesianism it is the key technical device that lets unknown quantum states be reconstrued as personalist degrees of belief rather than objective properties.
