# Quantum information

Quantum information is the theory of information encoded in quantum states (qubits), governed by superposition, entanglement, and the no-cloning and Holevo bounds. It generalizes classical (Shannon) information: the basic unit is the qubit, distinguishability is measured by quantum analogues of the Fisher metric, and physical processes are completely-positive trace-preserving maps. Within the participatory/it-from-bit program it grounds the view that physical law and spacetime structure are ultimately informational, and it sets the resource-theoretic and entropic bounds (e.g. Holevo, Bekenstein) on how much information a physical system can carry or process.
