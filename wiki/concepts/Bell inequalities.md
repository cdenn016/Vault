# Bell inequalities

Bell inequalities are constraints on correlations between spatially separated measurements that any local-hidden-variable (locally causal, deterministic-or-stochastic) theory must satisfy. Quantum mechanics predicts, and experiments confirm, violations of these bounds (notably the CHSH form), ruling out local realism and demonstrating genuine quantum entanglement. In the participatory/QBism context they underwrite no-go theorems for observer-independent facts and motivate observer-relative, agent-centered readings of measurement outcomes.
