# Structural realism

## Definition

**Structural realism** is the philosophy-of-science position that what science genuinely tells us about the world is its *relational structure* — the network of relations, symmetries, and functional dependencies among things — rather than the intrinsic nature of the things themselves. Objectivity is relocated from substance to structure: what is real and conserved is the invariant pattern of relations, not the identity of the relata. The position comes in graded strengths. **Epistemic** structural realism (Worrall) holds that we can *know* only structure while objects persist, unknowable, behind it. **Ontic** structural realism (French–Ladyman; Ladyman–Ross) goes further: structure is *metaphysically primary* and objects are derivative nodes or eliminated outright — "relations without prior relata." **Moderate** ontic structural realism (Esfeld–Lam) keeps objects as real but grants them no intrinsic identity, individuating them wholly by the relations in which they stand, and finds its cleanest expression in the *fibre-bundle* formalism of gauge theory. The lineage reaches back to Cassirer's neo-Kantian *function over substance* (*Funktionsbegriff* over *Substanzbegriff*): scientific concepts constitute their objects through serial/relational order rather than abstracting shared sensible marks, and objectivity just is invariance under the admissible transformations of the framework.

## Why it matters here

Structural realism is the philosophical backbone of the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] program. The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] identifies *objective content with gauge-invariant content*, and this is precisely Cassirer's "objectivity as invariance" and Worrall's "structure, not object, is preserved across theory change" made mathematical: under a [[Gauge transformation]] (a change of agent frame) the local belief readout shifts, but the relational, invariant content is conserved and is what counts as real. The ontic variants license the program's pan-agentic metaphysics directly. The model treats [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] with no intrinsic, frame-independent state; an agent is constituted by its relations to other sections and by the connection that transports beliefs between them via $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$. That is ontic structural realism almost literally — relata fixed by structure rather than the reverse. Esfeld–Lam's moderate, fibre-bundle reading fits the program best, since it carries concrete agents (sections) while denying them any view-from-nowhere identity.

## Details

Worrall's move answers the no-miracles argument and the pessimistic meta-induction at once: Fresnel's optical equations survived the death of the elastic-ether ontology, so the realist should bank structure, not posited objects. French and Ladyman press for what *grounds* that structure and, citing the metaphysical underdetermination of particle individuality in quantum theory, make structure primary. Ladyman and Ross naturalize this into a book-length OSR plus "real patterns" at every scale — resonating with the model's cross-scale coarse-graining, where [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence|meta-agents]] are higher-scale patterns over agent clusters. Cassirer's constitutive-but-revisable a priori prefigures the [[Relativized a priori]] thread and the reading of paradigm change as a kind of gauge transformation. The information-theoretic articulation in Ladyman–Ross bridges OSR to Wheeler's *it from bit*, grounding the participatory ontology in invariant relational content.
