# Stochastic Thermodynamics

Stochastic thermodynamics extends thermodynamic notions (heat, work, entropy production) to small systems where fluctuations dominate, defining these quantities along individual stochastic trajectories. Its central results — the Jarzynski equality, Crooks fluctuation theorem, and the thermodynamic uncertainty relation — quantify the statistics of irreversible processes far from equilibrium. Levitated optomechanical particles serve as well-controlled testbeds for these relations, and the framework connects to the free-energy / entropy-production accounting that underlies the VFE and active-inference treatment of self-organizing systems.
