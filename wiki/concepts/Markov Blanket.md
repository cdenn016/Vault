# Markov Blanket

A Markov blanket of a node is the minimal set (parents, children, co-parents) that renders it conditionally independent of the rest of a graphical model. In the free-energy principle the blanket — partitioned into sensory and active states — statistically separates internal from external states, providing the boundary that lets a system be read as an inference-performing agent. Whether such blankets are real structural boundaries or modeller-imposed is actively debated.
