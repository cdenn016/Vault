# Maximum entropy

The maximum-entropy (MaxEnt) principle, due to Jaynes, selects the least-biased probability distribution consistent with given constraints (typically expectation values of observables) by maximizing Shannon entropy subject to those constraints. The solution is always an exponential family whose natural parameters are the Lagrange multipliers enforcing the constraints, linking MaxEnt directly to statistical mechanics, Bayesian inference, and entropic-dynamics derivations of physics. In this program it underwrites the entropic / Bayesian-mechanics view in which belief dynamics and even physical law are read off from maximum-entropy inference over constraints.
