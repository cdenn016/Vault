# Decoherence-free subsystems

Subsystems of a quantum system that are immune to a collective noise process because they transform trivially under the relevant symmetry group, so information encoded in them is protected from decoherence. In the reference-frame analysis of Bartlett, Rudolph and Spekkens, the relational (frame-independent) information that survives twirling over a missing reference frame lives precisely in such decoherence-free / noiseless subsystems, the carrier of speakable information when no shared frame is available.
