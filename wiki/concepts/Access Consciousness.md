# Access Consciousness

Access consciousness (Ned Block's A-consciousness) is the functional notion that a mental state's content is poised for use in reasoning, verbal report, and the rational control of action. It contrasts with phenomenal consciousness (the subjective feel). Bayne and Chalmers show that access unity fails empirically (Sperling's partial-report bottleneck: subjects access each row of a letter matrix but not their conjunction), whereas phenomenal unity may still hold — a dissociation that maps onto bounded-capacity attention in the GL(K) attention mechanism versus the globally well-defined joint VFE.
