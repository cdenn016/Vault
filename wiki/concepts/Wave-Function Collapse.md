# Wave-Function Collapse

Wave-function collapse is the postulated discontinuous reduction of a quantum state to a single eigenstate upon measurement, central to the measurement problem. Interpretations range from literal physical collapse (objective-collapse / GRW, Penrose gravitational reduction) to epistemic or observer-relative accounts (QBism, relational QM, entropic dynamics) where 'collapse' is a Bayesian belief update rather than a physical event. The latter readings connect directly to the participatory it-from-bit picture in which measurement outcomes are observer-relative facts.
