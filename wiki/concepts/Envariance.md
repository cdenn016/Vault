# Envariance

Envariance (environment-assisted invariance), introduced by Zurek, is a symmetry of entangled states: a transformation acting on a system can be exactly undone by a counter-transformation acting solely on its environment, leaving the joint state invariant. The system's state is then 'envariant' under that transformation, implying the agent is necessarily ignorant of the affected property. Zurek uses envariance to derive the Born rule and to justify the reduced-density-matrix formalism from symmetry alone, without circularly assuming probabilities. Distinct from gauge equivariance.
