# Information is Physical

Landauer's thesis that information is not an abstraction but is always tied to a physical substrate, so that information processing is subject to physical law: erasing one bit of information necessarily dissipates at least k_B T ln 2 of heat. The corollary, pursued by Lloyd and others, bounds the computational capacity of physical systems (the universe as a computer) by their energy and entropy. For the program it motivates the participatory it-from-bit stance that physical and informational descriptions are two faces of one structure, and that thermodynamic/entropic constraints shape what can be inferred or represented.
