# Niche Construction

Niche construction is the process by which organisms modify their own and others' selective environments through their activity, creating an evolutionary feedback loop between agents and the niche they inhabit. In the active-inference reading (variational approach to niche construction), agents collectively offload expected-free-energy computation onto unambiguous epistemic resources embedded in a shared niche, so the niche itself acquires Markov-blanket structure and statistical robustness from the collective active inference of its denizens. This grounds the multi-agent VFE model's pairwise belief coupling and the social-physics extension, in which shared niches with affordance-structured interactions mediate opinion dynamics.
