# Matrix Calculus

Matrix calculus is the body of identities and rules for differentiating scalar-, vector-, and matrix-valued functions with respect to matrix arguments: gradients, Jacobians, and Hessians of traces, determinants, inverses, matrix exponentials, Kronecker products, and Gaussian integrals. It is the standard toolkit for deriving gradients of VFE / ELBO objectives over Gaussian belief states (mu, Sigma) and SPD covariance matrices. The Petersen-Pedersen Matrix Cookbook is the canonical desk reference for these formulas.
