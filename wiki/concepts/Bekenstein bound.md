# Bekenstein bound

The Bekenstein bound is an upper limit on the entropy (information content) that can be contained within a region of given radius R and energy E: S <= 2*pi*k_B*E*R/(hbar*c). It implies information is fundamentally finite and geometrically bounded, and is a cornerstone of the holographic principle (entropy scales with bounding area, not volume). In this program it supports the participatory / it-from-bit and emergent-spacetime themes, where physical degrees of freedom are treated as bounded informational capacity.
