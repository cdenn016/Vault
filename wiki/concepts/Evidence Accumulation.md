# Evidence Accumulation

Evidence accumulation is the core mechanism of sequential sampling models of decision making: a decision variable integrates noisy momentary evidence over time, drifting toward one of several response boundaries, with the first boundary reached determining the choice and the integration time determining the reaction time. Its rate (the drift) reflects evidence quality; the boundary reflects the response criterion (speed-accuracy tradeoff). In the VFE framework, accumulation maps onto precision-weighted prediction-error integration during belief updating, and in the multi-agent setting coupled accumulators model how agents integrate social evidence to form opinions.
