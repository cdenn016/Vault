# Quantum Darwinism

Quantum Darwinism, due to Zurek, explains the emergence of objective classical reality from quantum substrate: pointer states selected by einselection are those whose information is redundantly imprinted across many fragments of the environment. Because many independent observers can each read off the same pointer-state information from disjoint environmental fragments without disturbing the system, the state acquires effective objectivity ('selection' of states that proliferate redundant records). It complements decoherence by quantifying objectivity through environmental redundancy of mutual information.
