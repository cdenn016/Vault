# Quiddities

In the metaphysics of panpsychism and Russellian monism, quiddities are the intrinsic, categorical natures of fundamental physical properties — the 'what-it-is' that realizes the dispositional/relational structure physics describes. The 'quiddistic' move holds that physics characterizes only the causal-structural role of properties (mass, charge), leaving their intrinsic nature open; panpsychists propose these quiddities are (proto)phenomenal, offering a candidate solution to the hard problem by making consciousness the intrinsic ground of physical structure.
