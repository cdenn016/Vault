# Information Geometry

**Information geometry** is the differential-geometric study of *statistical manifolds*: it
treats a parametric family of probability distributions $\{p_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$ —
all diagonal Gaussians, say — not as a flat Euclidean parameter box but as a curved Riemannian
manifold whose points are distributions and whose coordinates are the parameters $\theta$. The
discipline was given its modern form by Amari ([[amari-2000-methods-information-geometry]],
[[amari-1998-natural-gradient]]); this page is the crisp definitional hub. For the full
synthesis of how it threads through the program — natural-gradient M-steps, K-FAC, the
Rényi/α-divergence loss — see the theme [[Information geometry and natural gradient]].

## The core structure

A statistical manifold carries three intertwined objects.

1. **The metric.** The [[Fisher information metric]] $g_{ij}(\theta) =
   \mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\!\big[\partial_i \log p_\theta\, \partial_j \log p_\theta\big]$
   measures how *distinguishable* nearby distributions are. It is the unique (up to scale)
   Riemannian metric invariant under sufficient statistics — Chentsov's theorem
   ([[ay2015-info-geometry-sufficient-statistics]]) — which is exactly why it, and not the
   Euclidean metric, is the right geometry on belief space.

2. **A dual pair of connections.** Unlike ordinary Riemannian geometry, a statistical manifold
   carries *two* flat affine connections — the **exponential (e-) connection** and the
   **mixture (m-) connection** — that are dual with respect to the Fisher metric, interpolated
   by the one-parameter family of $\alpha$-connections (the Levi–Civita connection sits at
   $\alpha=0$). Their asymmetry is captured by the [[Amari-Chentsov tensor]]. Exponential
   families (see [[Exponential family]]) are **dually flat** in these coordinates.

3. **A canonical divergence.** On a dually-flat manifold the KL divergence equals a
   [[Bregman divergence]] generated by the log-partition function, and a generalized Pythagorean
   theorem holds. This places the model's [[Renyi divergence]]/α-divergence objective inside one
   coherent geometry, with KL as the $\alpha\to 1$ special case.

## The natural gradient

The payoff for optimization is the [[Natural gradient]]: steepest descent in the Fisher geometry
preconditions the raw gradient by the inverse metric,
$$\tilde{\nabla} L = g(\theta)^{-1}\,\nabla L,$$
yielding an update that is *invariant to reparameterization* and asymptotically Fisher-efficient
([[amari-1998-natural-gradient]]).

## Why it underpins this research

Almost every quantity the [[VFE Transformer Program|vfe3 transformer]] and the multi-agent
[[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model|MAgent model]] optimize lives on a statistical manifold,
so information geometry is the load-bearing connective tissue, not decoration:

- **Belief space.** Per-token beliefs are Gaussians; the Fisher–Rao metric on their natural
  parameters is closed-form, and the [[Variational free energy]] E-step is a natural-gradient
  relaxation on it.
- **Covariance geometry.** The covariance block is an SPD matrix whose affine-invariant metric
  *is* the Fisher metric restricted to it — see [[SPD-manifold geometry and Riemannian optimization]].
- **Precision as curvature.** The Fisher metric *is* expected precision, so precision-weighting
  in attention is curvature on belief space, and the [[Evidence lower bound (ELBO)]] / divergence
  losses are geodesic-distortion functionals.
- **Quantum extension.** The uniqueness of the Fisher metric generalizes to states via the
  monotone-metric classification of [[Quantum information geometry]].

For applications and worked machinery (K-FAC, variational Rényi bounds, dual-flat exponential
families) see [[amari-2016-information-geometry-applications]] and the theme.
