# Information Geometry

**Information geometry** is the differential-geometric study of *statistical manifolds*: it
treats a parametric family of probability distributions $\{p_\theta : \theta \in \Theta\}$ —
all diagonal Gaussians, say — not as a flat Euclidean parameter box but as a curved Riemannian
manifold whose points are distributions and whose coordinates are the parameters $\theta$. The
discipline was given its modern form by Amari ([[amari-2000-methods-information-geometry]],
[[amari-1998-natural-gradient]]); this page is the definitional hub. In this program the
established application is joint Gaussian Fisher geometry, whose covariance block is one-half
conventional AIRM. The audited
frame table uses plain AdamW, its configured conditioner/heavy-ball path is inactive and is not identified as Fisher or K-FAC,
and the configured order-Rényi pairwise discrepancy is distinct from Amari's alpha-connection
construction. See [[Information geometry and natural gradient]] and
[[gl-k-attention-2026-07-09-review-revision]].

## The core structure

A statistical manifold carries three intertwined objects.

1. **The metric.** The [[Fisher information metric]] $g_{ij}(\theta) =
   \mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\big[\partial_i \log p_\theta \partial_j \log p_\theta\big]$
   measures how *distinguishable* nearby distributions are. It is the unique (up to scale)
   Riemannian metric invariant under sufficient statistics — Chentsov's theorem
   ([[ay2015-info-geometry-sufficient-statistics]]) — which is exactly why it, and not the
   Euclidean metric, is the right geometry on belief space.

2. **A dual pair of connections.** A statistical manifold carries exponential
   and mixture connections that are dual with respect to the Fisher metric, with
   Amari's alpha-connections interpolating between them. These connections are
   not flat on every statistical manifold. Exponential-family and mixture
   coordinates yield the **dually flat** setting in which the associated
   Pythagorean results apply.

3. **A canonical divergence.** On a dually-flat exponential-family manifold, KL is the
   [[Bregman divergence]] generated by the log-partition potential, and a generalized
   Pythagorean theorem holds. Order-[[Renyi divergence]] and Amari alpha-divergence can be
   related through a power integral, but the relation is nonlinear and monotone. It does not
   transfer gradients, Hessians, Bregman structure, affine connections, or global geometry.

## The natural gradient

The payoff for optimization is the [[Natural gradient]]: steepest descent in the Fisher geometry
preconditions the raw gradient by the inverse metric,
$$\tilde{\nabla} L = g(\theta)^{-1}\nabla L,$$
yielding a coordinate-invariant tangent vector field
([[amari-1998-natural-gradient]]). A finite coordinate update additionally
depends on step size and the integration or retraction map, so it is not
automatically invariant under reparameterization.

## Why it underpins this research

Information geometry directly governs the program's Gaussian belief family. Claims about
other parameter blocks require their own metrics and derivations:

- **Belief space.** Per-token beliefs are Gaussians; the Fisher–Rao metric on their natural
  parameters is closed-form, and the [[Variational free energy]] E-step is a natural-gradient
  relaxation on it.
- **Covariance geometry.** The covariance block is an SPD matrix whose affine-invariant metric
  is twice the zero-mean Gaussian covariance Fisher metric under the conventional normalization;
  see [[SPD-manifold geometry and Riemannian optimization]].
- **Precision and curvature.** Precision enters Gaussian Fisher geometry and also weights
  prediction errors, but those roles do not make every attention or divergence term a geodesic
  distortion functional.
- **Quantum extension.** Classical Fisher-metric uniqueness is replaced on
  quantum state space by Petz's non-unique family of monotone metrics; see
  [[Quantum information geometry]].

For applications and worked machinery (K-FAC, variational Rényi bounds, dual-flat exponential
families) see [[amari-2016-information-geometry-applications]] and the theme.
