# CSL model

Continuous Spontaneous Localization (CSL) is an objective-collapse model in which the Schrodinger equation is augmented by a nonlinear, stochastic term that spontaneously localizes the wavefunction at a rate scaling with mass/number of particles, so microscopic systems stay coherent while macroscopic superpositions collapse rapidly. A mass-proportional generalization of the GRW model, CSL is the paradigm class of theories predicting that quantum mechanics breaks down beyond some scale; macroscopicity experiments aim to push parameter bounds and potentially falsify it. It anchors the program's treatment of the measurement problem and the quantum-classical boundary.
