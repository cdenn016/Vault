# Covariant Entropy Bound

The covariant entropy bound (Bousso bound) states that the entropy passing through a light-sheet — a null hypersurface generated by non-expanding light rays emanating from a spacelike surface of area A — is bounded by A/4 in Planck units. Formulated by Bousso (1999), it is the covariant, generally applicable generalization of the Bekenstein and holographic entropy bounds (see [[bekenstein-1973-black-holes-entropy]]), and a central piece of evidence for the [[holographic principle]] — that the information content of a spacetime region is bounded by its boundary area rather than its volume. Reviewed in [[bousso-2002-holographic]].
