# Channel Capacity

Channel capacity is the maximum mutual information between a channel's input and output, maximized over input distributions — the supremum of rates (bits per channel use) at which information can be transmitted with arbitrarily small error, established by Shannon's noisy-channel coding theorem. It is the fundamental information-theoretic limit on reliable communication and an upper bound that information-bottleneck and rate-distortion analyses of representations operate beneath. In the program it frames bounds on how much belief/precision-weighted information can flow through attention and coarse-graining bottlenecks.
