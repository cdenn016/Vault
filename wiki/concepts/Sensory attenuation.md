# Sensory attenuation

Sensory attenuation is the active-inference phenomenon whereby an agent down-weights (reduces the precision of) the sensory consequences of its own actions, so that proprioceptive prediction errors can drive movement instead of being explained away. It accounts for why self-generated stimuli (e.g. self-tickling) are perceived as weaker, and is essential to active inference: attenuating sensory precision lets predictions, rather than incoming evidence, win and thereby command the motor periphery. In the VFE program it is a concrete instance of precision (attention) control, mapping onto the temperature/precision modulation of attention couplings.
