# Probabilistic opinion pooling

## What it is

Probabilistic opinion pooling is the theory of how to combine several agents' probability distributions over a common space into a single collective distribution. Given $n$ agents, each supplying a probability function $p_i$ on the same algebra of events, a *pooling operator* $F(p_1,\dots,p_n)$ returns one collective probability function. Unlike the dynamical opinion models catalogued under [[Opinion dynamics]] — which iterate a local update rule over time — pooling is a *static aggregation*: it asks not how beliefs evolve but how, at a single instant, a set of beliefs should be merged into one. The canonical survey by [[genest-zidek-1986-pooling]] organizes the entire field around two principal families, and [[dietrich-list-2016-opinion-pooling]] sharpens this into theorem-level adjudication. The first family is the **linear (arithmetic) pool**, a weighted average $p = \sum_i w_i p_i$ with $\sum_i w_i = 1$. The second is the **logarithmic (geometric / multiplicative) pool**, a weighted normalized product $p \propto \prod_i p_i^{w_i}$ — the externally-Bayesian, evidence-combining alternative whose original Bayesian derivation is due to [[bordley-1982-multiplicative-pooling]].

## The axioms that force each operator

The central message of the literature is that *no single pooling rule is universally correct*: each is uniquely forced by a different, mutually incompatible set of axioms, so the choice of aggregator is a substantive modeling commitment whose consequences can be derived rather than asserted. [[dietrich-list-2016-opinion-pooling]] proves the two representation theorems sharply. **Eventwise independence** — that the pooled probability of an event depend only on the agents' probabilities for *that* event — together with **consensus (unanimity) preservation** forces linear pooling: $p = \sum_i w_i p_i$. By contrast, **external Bayesianity** — that pooling commute with Bayesian updating on shared evidence — together with a multiplicative independence condition forces geometric / log-linear pooling: $p \propto \prod_i p_i^{w_i}$. [[genest-zidek-1986-pooling]] frames the same dichotomy through the **marginalization property** (pooling commutes with computing marginals), which characterizes the linear pool, versus external Bayesianity, which characterizes the logarithmic pool. The two operators differ on the properties practitioners care about. Linear pooling is simple and consensus-respecting and preserves unanimous unconditional assignments, but it is *not* externally Bayesian and generally destroys probabilistic independence judgments. Geometric pooling is externally Bayesian and **zero-preserving**: because it is a product, any agent who assigns an event probability zero forces the collective to assign it zero — a "veto" on impossibility. The incompatibility is genuine: in nondegenerate cases one cannot have both eventwise independence and external Bayesianity, so the pooling choice is a forced trade-off, not a matter of taste.

## Product of experts and the variational coupling fixed point

The logarithmic pool has a direct identification with the **product-of-experts** construction: a normalized product of component densities is exactly a product-of-experts model, and [[bordley-1982-multiplicative-pooling]] derives precisely this form by treating each expert's assessment as conditionally independent evidence and applying Bayesian revision, so the posterior is proportional to a product of expert-derived factors. For Gaussian beliefs $q_i = \mathcal{N}(\mu_i, \Sigma_i)$ the geometric pool is analytically transparent: the pooled precision is the weighted sum of component precisions, $\Sigma_\star^{-1} = \sum_i \lambda_i \Sigma_i^{-1}$, and the pooled mean is the corresponding precision-weighted average. This is the same fixed point that the variational coupling of beliefs reaches when an agent treats its neighbors' transported beliefs as independent likelihoods — connecting opinion pooling to the [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] functional. The linear pool, by contrast, is the *barycenter* under a different geometry: a coherence-weighted arithmetic mean of $(\mu, \Sigma)$ that respects consensus but is not the product-of-experts fixed point.

## Relevance to this research

Opinion pooling is the static, axiomatic counterpart to the *dynamical* [[Belief inertia]] program, and it supplies the principled vocabulary for the **barycenter / pooling choice** that recurs throughout the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]]. The meta-agent coarse-graining step (see [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]]) deliberately pools constituent beliefs by a *gauge-covariant linear (sandwich) average* — transport each constituent into the meta frame, then take a coherence-weighted arithmetic mean of $(\mu,\Sigma)$ — rather than by a product-of-experts. [[dietrich-list-2016-opinion-pooling]] gives the defensible reason: linear pooling is the operator uniquely forced by eventwise independence and consensus preservation, and it avoids the **veto pathology** of geometric pooling, in which a single overconfident constituent (an agent assigning near-zero probability) could annihilate the collective belief. The cost, which the theorems make explicit, is that the sandwich average is *not* externally Bayesian. This is exactly why [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] keeps the log-linear pool as a *separate* identification, used only for the multi-generation hyperprior — the discounted-KL ancestral stack — where external Bayesianity and the geometric-discount structure are wanted: there the strictly-normalized special case recovers the externally-Bayesian log-linear pool of [[genest-zidek-1986-pooling]], with Gaussian pooled precision $\Sigma_\star^{-1} = \sum_k \lambda_k \Sigma_k^{-1}$ — exactly Bordley's multiplicative aggregate ([[bordley-1982-multiplicative-pooling]]). The two pooling regimes thus live in different parts of the model, and the axiomatic taxonomy is the map that tells the program which operator is appropriate where: consensus-preserving linear pooling for coarse-graining, externally-Bayesian geometric pooling for the ancestral hyperprior.
