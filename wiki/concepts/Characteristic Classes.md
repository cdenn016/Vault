# Characteristic Classes

Characteristic classes are cohomology-class invariants assigned to fiber/vector bundles (Chern, Pontryagin, Stiefel-Whitney, Euler classes) that measure the global twisting or non-triviality of a bundle and obstruct its triviality. They are the topological invariants underlying gauge-field configurations: in gauge theory the Chern classes count instanton/topological charge, and they connect curvature (the connection's field strength) to integer topological data via Chern-Weil theory. They classify bundles up to isomorphism through pullbacks from the classifying space.
