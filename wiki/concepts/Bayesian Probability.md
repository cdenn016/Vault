# Bayesian Probability

Bayesian probability interprets probabilities as degrees of belief held by an agent, updated by Bayes' rule as evidence arrives, rather than as long-run frequencies. The personalist (subjective/de Finetti) variant - probabilities are an individual agent's coherent betting commitments - is the reading QBism adopts when it casts quantum states as Bayesian degrees of belief and the Born rule as an added coherence constraint. This agent-centric notion of probability is the connective tissue between QBism, the free-energy principle, and the program's view of inference as belief updating.
