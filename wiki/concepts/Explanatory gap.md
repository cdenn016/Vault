# Explanatory gap

## Definition

The **explanatory gap**, named by Levine (1983), is a specifically *epistemic* deficiency in psychophysical identity claims. Even granting that a mental state is metaphysically identical to a physical state, the identity leaves something unexplained that paradigm scientific reductions do not: it fails to make intelligible *why* that physical configuration should feel the way it does, or feel like anything at all. Levine contrasts the inferential closure of "heat is molecular kinetic energy" with the inferential openness of "pain is C-fiber firing" — the physical story exhausts what heat does, but not why pain feels as it does. The gap is the residue. It is the descendant of Nagel's (1974) **subjective character of experience** — the "what it is like" that an objective, third-person physical description structurally omits — and it is folded into Chalmers's (1995, 1996) **hard problem of consciousness**, the question of why physical processing is accompanied by experience at all, distinguished from the tractable "easy problems" of cognitive function.

## Why it matters here

The gap is the precise object the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] claims to *relocate* rather than dissolve. PIFB self-classifies as **panprotopsychist** in Chalmers's (2013) exact sense: it does not assert that its gauge frames or beliefs are themselves conscious, only that frame-relative informational structure supplies the *protophenomenal* basis from which experiential character could be constituted. This is the [[Russellian monism|Russellian-monist]] move — physics specifies dispositional and structural roles while the intrinsic, categorical natures filling those roles are (proto)phenomenal. The program's "physics" (gauge structure, transport, [[Variational free energy]], the [[Fisher information metric]] pullback of [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]]) gives the structural role; the protophenomenal occupies the categorical slot. The gap therefore does not close — it moves from a brute physical-state-to-quale step onto a question about frame-relative registered content within the participatory ontology. Holding the claim to Levine's careful epistemic-versus-metaphysical distinction is the editorial discipline here: a relocation of where the gap sits is not a closure of it, and the manuscript must never conflate the two.

## Details

The Russellian lineage runs Russell (1927) → Strawson (2006) → Goff (2017): physics describes only structure and dispositions, leaving intrinsic natures open, and (proto)experience is the candidate filler. Strawson supplies the **anti-(brute-)emergence** pressure — experiential being cannot appear from the wholly non-experiential by brute emergence — which is exactly why PIFB commits to *constitutive* panprotopsychism rather than emergentism. The price of that synthesis is the [[Combination problem]] (Chalmers 2016): how protophenomenal structure at one scale composes into unified experience at a higher one. PIFB's candidate answer is its gauge-covariant coarse-graining via [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]] — addressing the *structure* combination problem while conceding the *subject* combination problem remains open. Empirically minded structural and process theories — Seth's (2021) predictive-processing program and the [[Free-energy principle active inference|free-energy/active-inference]] route — sit alongside as attempts to span the gap from the neuroscience side, marking the threshold for the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] thread and for [[Structural realism]] as the background metaphysics. The full landscape of competing theories is synthesized in [[Consciousness and the hard problem]].
