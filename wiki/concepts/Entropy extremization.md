# Entropy extremization

Entropy extremization is the variational principle in which physical equations of motion are obtained by extremizing an entropy functional (rather than an action). In Padmanabhan's emergent-gravity program, extremizing an entropy density associated with null surfaces, over all such surfaces, yields Einstein's equations as the condition for an extremum — recasting gravitational dynamics as a maximum-entropy/thermodynamic statement. It links horizon thermodynamics to the broader maximum-entropy and entropic-dynamics tradition (Jaynes, Caticha) that derives dynamics from information-theoretic extremal principles.
