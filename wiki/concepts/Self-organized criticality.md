# Self-organized criticality

Self-organized criticality (SOC) is the tendency of certain driven, dissipative many-body systems to evolve without fine-tuning toward a critical state characterized by scale-free, power-law-distributed avalanches (the Bak-Tang-Wiesenfeld sandpile paradigm). At criticality the system maximizes dynamic range, susceptibility, and information transmission. In the entropic-brain hypothesis, conscious states are argued to sit near a critical point, and psychedelics push the brain toward (super)critical, high-entropy dynamics; the concept also recurs in collective social systems poised between order and disorder.
