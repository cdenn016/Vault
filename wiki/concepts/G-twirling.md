# G-twirling

The operation of averaging a quantum state or channel over the action of a group G via the Haar measure, projecting onto the G-invariant (relationally meaningful) component. In the reference-frame program, twirling models the loss of a shared reference frame: integrating over the unknown frame orientation maps a state to its frame-independent description, decomposing the Hilbert space into G-irreps and generating effective superselection rules and decoherence-free subsystems. It is the operational link between symmetry, group averaging, and what information is communicable without a common frame.
