# Reaction time distributions

The distribution of choice reaction times is characteristically right-skewed (often fit by ex-Gaussian, shifted-Wald, or Weibull forms) and is the central empirical signature that sequential-sampling models of decision making aim to reproduce. In the drift-diffusion / evidence-accumulation account, the shape of the RT distribution (and its split by accuracy) is generated by noisy accumulation of evidence to a threshold, linking mean RT, skew, and error rates to a small set of latent parameters (drift rate, boundary separation, non-decision time). In the gauge-theoretic VFE setting these are of interest as a behavioural readout of belief-updating dynamics and the speed-accuracy trade-off.
