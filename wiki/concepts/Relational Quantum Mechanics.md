# Relational Quantum Mechanics

Relational quantum mechanics (Rovelli 1996) holds that quantum states and measurement outcomes are not absolute but relative to the observing physical system: there are no observer-independent facts, only facts relative to interactions. It dovetails with QBism and cross-perspective no-go results (observer-dependent facts) and provides a key conceptual pillar for the participatory it-from-bit program, where beliefs and gauge frames are inherently agent-relative.
