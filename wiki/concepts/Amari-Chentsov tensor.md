# Amari-Chentsov tensor

The Amari-Chentsov tensor is the unique (up to scale) totally symmetric 3-tensor on a statistical manifold that is invariant under sufficient statistics, given by the expectation of the product of three score functions T_ijk = E[d_i log p d_j log p d_k log p]. Together with the Fisher metric it forms the second pillar of Chentsov's characterization: the metric and this cubic tensor are the only statistically invariant geometric objects, and the tensor generates the dual alpha-connections via g and (1/2) alpha T. It is what makes information geometry's dually flat (exponential/mixture) structure canonical rather than arbitrary.
