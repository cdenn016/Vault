# Belief Propagation

Belief propagation is a message-passing algorithm for marginal inference on graphical models: nodes exchange messages summarizing local evidence (the sum-product algorithm), exact on trees and approximate (loopy BP) on general graphs. It is the inference scheme underlying Friston's graphical/process-theory formulations of active inference, and motivates reading attention as learned message passing among token-beliefs.
