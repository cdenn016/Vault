# Classifying Space

For a topological group G, the classifying space BG carries a universal principal G-bundle EG -> BG such that every principal G-bundle over a (paracompact) base X arises, up to isomorphism, as the pullback of EG along a map X -> BG, with the homotopy class of that map a complete invariant. Classifying spaces thereby reduce bundle classification to homotopy theory and are the natural home of characteristic classes (the cohomology of BG). They underpin the topological organization of gauge fields and frame the global structure behind the program's fiber-bundle picture of agents and gauge frames.
