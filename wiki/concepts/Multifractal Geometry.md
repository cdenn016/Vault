# Multifractal Geometry

Multifractal geometry describes objects and measures whose scaling cannot be captured by a single fractal dimension but require a continuous spectrum of local scaling exponents, summarized by the singularity spectrum f(alpha) or the generalized (Renyi) dimensions D_q. It arises in turbulence, strange attractors, and the order parameter of partially synchronized systems, where different regions scale with different Holder exponents. Multifractal analysis quantifies heterogeneous, scale-dependent structure in dynamical and network data.
