# Nonequilibrium steady state

A nonequilibrium steady state (NESS) is a stationary probability density of a stochastic dynamical system that is maintained by persistent probability currents rather than detailed balance — the system's density stops changing in time even though net fluxes keep circulating. In the free-energy-principle literature it is the formal setting for self-organization: a system with a Markov blanket that possesses a NESS will, on average, look as if its internal states infer external states by minimizing variational free energy. The NESS density defines the system's generative model (its 'beliefs') via the negative log of the stationary density, linking thermodynamic stationarity to Bayesian inference.
