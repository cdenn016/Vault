# Macroscopicity

Macroscopicity is a quantitative measure introduced by Nimmrichter and Hornberger (used in Arndt-Hornberger 2014) to compare how strongly disparate superposition experiments falsify minimal classicalizing modifications of quantum mechanics. Defined as mu = log10(tau_e / 1 s), where tau_e is the coherence-time parameter of the most adversarial Galilean- and scale-invariant collapse model still consistent with the data, it places neutron interferometry, SQUIDs, and large-molecule interference on a common scale (mu ~ 5-12). It operationalizes 'macroscopicness' as an empirical degree rather than a metaphysical kind.
