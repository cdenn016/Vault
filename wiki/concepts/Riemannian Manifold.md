# Riemannian Manifold

A Riemannian manifold is a smooth manifold equipped with a smoothly varying inner product (the metric) on each tangent space, which lets one measure lengths, angles, geodesic distances, curvature, and parallel transport. It is the general geometric setting underlying the program's specific manifolds: the SPD covariance cone with its affine-invariant metric ([[SPD-manifold geometry and Riemannian optimization]]) and the statistical manifolds of information geometry with the Fisher metric ([[Information geometry and natural gradient]]). Treating beliefs as points on a curved manifold rather than in flat space is what motivates natural-gradient updates and geometry-respecting retractions.
