# Generalised synchrony

Generalised synchrony is the regime in which two coupled dynamical systems become locked through a (generally nonlinear) functional relationship between their states, rather than identical-state synchronization. In Friston's active-inference account of communication and dyadic interaction, two agents that exchange signals and minimize free energy come to mirror each other's generative models, achieving generalised synchrony of their internal (belief) dynamics. This is the mechanism by which separate agents form a shared, coupled inferential system and is the dynamical basis for emergent meta-agents in the multi-agent VFE model.
