# Optomechanics

Optomechanics studies the coherent coupling between electromagnetic (optical or microwave) fields and mechanical motion, typically via radiation pressure in a high-finesse cavity. The canonical interaction Hamiltonian is H = hbar*omega_c a^dag a + hbar*omega_m b^dag b - hbar*g a^dag a (b + b^dag), where the photon number modulates the mechanical mode. In the quantum-foundations thread it provides a platform for putting macroscopic objects (e.g. a ~10^14-atom mirror) into spatial superpositions and thereby testing gravitationally induced (Penrose-Diosi) wavefunction collapse. Levitated optomechanics extends this to trapped nanoparticles freed from clamping losses.
