# Expected Free Energy

The free-energy functional evaluated over future (counterfactual) outcomes that an active-inference agent minimizes when selecting policies. Expected free energy (G) decomposes into an epistemic (information-gain / exploratory) term and a pragmatic (goal-directed / extrinsic-value) term, so that policy selection naturally balances exploration and exploitation. It generalizes the (present-tense) variational free energy to action selection and underpins Friston's active-inference process theory.
