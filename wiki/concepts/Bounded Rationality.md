# Bounded Rationality

Bounded rationality (Simon) is the idea that agents make decisions under limited information, computational capacity, and time, satisficing rather than optimizing. In the free-energy / active-inference framework it is formalized via information-processing costs: an agent minimizes expected utility plus a KL-divergence (information) cost between its policy and a prior, equivalent to variational free-energy minimization at finite inverse temperature. This links bounded rationality to precision/temperature-weighted belief updating and underwrites realistic, resource-limited inference in the multi-agent and social-physics models.
