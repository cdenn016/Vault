# Quantum Measurement

Quantum measurement is the process by which a quantum system's superposed state yields a definite outcome upon interaction with an apparatus or observer, governed by the Born rule for outcome probabilities. The measurement problem — the apparent tension between unitary Schrodinger evolution and definite, observer-relative outcomes (collapse) — is the crux of quantum foundations and motivates observer-dependent interpretations (QBism, relational QM, Wigner's friend). For the program it grounds the participatory thesis that 'facts' are registered relative to an observer, paralleling the agent-relative belief updates of the multi-agent VFE model.
