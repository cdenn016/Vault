# Causal Explanation

The cognitive mechanism whereby people generate a causal story to account for a proposed fact, and that self-constructed explanation then sustains belief in the fact even after the original evidence is fully discredited. Anderson, Lepper and Ross showed this is the engine of belief perseverance: once a causal account is in place, debriefing fails to undo the belief. It is a psychological microfoundation for belief inertia / momentum in opinion dynamics — explanations act as a hysteresis term resisting evidential updating.
