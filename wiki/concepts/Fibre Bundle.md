# Fibre Bundle

A fibre bundle is a space E that locally looks like a product B x F of a base space B and a fibre F, glued together by transition functions valued in a structure group G; principal G-bundles (fibre = G) and their associated vector bundles are the central cases. A connection on the bundle specifies horizontal parallel transport whose curvature is the gauge field strength and whose holonomy measures path-dependent twisting. Fibre bundles are the geometric backbone of gauge theory and of the program's view of agents and gauge frames as sections of an associated bundle over a belief/space base.
