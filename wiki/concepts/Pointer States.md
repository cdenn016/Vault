# Pointer States

Pointer states are the preferred, environment-selected quantum states that survive decoherence and behave classically. They are picked out by einselection because the system-environment interaction Hamiltonian (nearly) commutes with the corresponding pointer observable, leaving these states least disturbed by environmental monitoring; operationally they are identified by the predictability sieve as the states minimizing entropy production. Pointer states form the basis in which the reduced density matrix diagonalizes and are the information carriers of Quantum Darwinism.
