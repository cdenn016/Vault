# Confirmation holism

Holism (the Quine-Duhem thesis) holds that scientific hypotheses are not tested in isolation but only as part of a whole web of belief, so any experiment confronts the totality of one's theory and no single statement is conclusively refuted or confirmed on its own. Quine's 'Two Dogmas of Empiricism' radicalizes this into semantic holism and a rejection of the analytic/synthetic distinction. It is a philosophical touchstone for the program's participatory and inferential views, where belief-revision is a global, network-level process rather than local fact-by-fact updating.
