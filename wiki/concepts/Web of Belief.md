# Web of Belief

Quine's 'web of belief' is the holistic picture of knowledge on which beliefs form an interconnected network that confronts experience as a whole rather than statement-by-statement (**confirmation holism**, the Quine-Duhem thesis). Because any experiment confronts the totality of one's theory, no single statement is conclusively refuted or confirmed on its own; Quine's 'Two Dogmas of Empiricism' radicalizes this into *semantic holism* and a rejection of the analytic/synthetic distinction. Beliefs near the periphery are revised readily under recalcitrant evidence; those near the center (logic, mathematics) are held more firmly because revising them propagates widely, but none is in principle immune to revision. This graded, momentum-like resistance to revision — peripheral beliefs flexible, core beliefs inertial — resonates with the program's notion of belief inertia and renormalization-group flow of beliefs, where deeply held convictions act like high-mass (high-Fisher-information) nodes that resist update, and where belief-revision is a global, network-level process rather than local fact-by-fact updating.
