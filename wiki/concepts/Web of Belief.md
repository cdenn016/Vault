# Web of Belief

Quine's 'web of belief' is the holistic picture of knowledge on which beliefs form an interconnected network that confronts experience as a whole rather than statement-by-statement (confirmation holism). Beliefs near the periphery are revised readily under recalcitrant evidence; those near the center (logic, mathematics) are held more firmly because revising them propagates widely, but none is in principle immune to revision. This graded, momentum-like resistance to revision — peripheral beliefs flexible, core beliefs inertial — resonates with the program's notion of belief inertia and renormalization-group flow of beliefs, where deeply held convictions act like high-mass (high-Fisher-information) nodes that resist update.
