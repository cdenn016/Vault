# Non-flat connection and the photon analogy

## Definition

In both gauge-VFE codebases the inter-agent (inter-token) transport is the cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = \Omega_i\,\Omega_j^{-1}$ (equivalently $\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$), a **flat** connection: every closed-loop [[Holonomy]] is the identity and the connection is pure gauge. This is **Regime I**, the vacuum — the connection does no frame-independent work and can be rotated away. **Regime II** promotes the connection to a genuinely **non-flat** one by inserting an edge factor $\Omega_{ij} = \Omega_i\,V_{ij}\,\Omega_j^{-1}$ (or $\exp(\phi_i)\exp(\delta_{ij})\exp(-\phi_j)$) whose closed-loop holonomy is nontrivial: transport around a loop returns a belief transformed by a real group element, the integrated [[Holonomy|curvature]].

The **photon analogy** reads this connection as the gauge field that mediates interaction between belief "matter" fields, exactly as the electromagnetic potential $A_\mu$ mediates interaction between electrons in QED. The dictionary is direct: the belief $q_i(c) = \mathcal N(\mu,\Sigma)$ plays the electron field $\psi$, the induced connection plays the photon $A_\mu$, the gauge-covariant transport plays minimal coupling $D_\mu\psi = (\partial_\mu + A_\mu)\psi$, and the Yang–Mills action plays the Maxwell action. The analogy has **two halves**:

1. **The Maxwell / Yang–Mills kinetic term** — a kinetic action $\int \operatorname{tr}(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})$ for the connection itself, making it a propagating degree of freedom whose curvature $F$ measures the failure to be pure gauge ([[yang-mills-1954]]).
2. **Minimal coupling** — the connection must do real work *on the matter*: a covariant-derivative vertex by which the connection mediates belief interactions across the base, with the Gaussian covariance carried by the sandwich product $\Sigma\mapsto M\Sigma M^\top$.

The decisive subtlety — the place implementations go wrong — is that **a non-identity edge factor is not curvature**. A single twist $V\neq I$ can be gauge-removable on a tree, can belong to a globally flat connection, or can sit inside trivial plaquette holonomy; and the induced form $A_\mu = U^{-1}\partial_\mu U$ is *pure gauge* (the Maurer–Cartan identity gives $F\equiv 0$), curvature-free no matter how nonzero $A$ looks ([[nakahara-2003-geometry-topology-physics]], [[baez-muniain-1994-gauge-fields]]). Genuine Regime II requires nontrivial **closed-loop holonomy** — a plaquette/Wilson-loop or triangle product that deviates from the identity *and survives a gauge transformation* — the gauge-invariant loop observable that the lattice gauge program builds its dynamics on ([[wilson-1974-confinement-quarks]], [[kogut-susskind-1975-hamiltonian-lattice-gauge]], [[creutz-1983-quarks-gluons-lattices]]). Equivalently, the curvature certificate must be a conjugation-invariant of the loop (its eigenvalue spectrum, or $\operatorname{Re}\operatorname{tr}$), not the frame-dependent $\lVert W - I\rVert_F$.

## Why it matters here

A flat connection can only express consensus: the inter-agent coupling $\beta_{ij}\,\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ pulls beliefs toward a single gauge-fixed agreement, with no room for path-dependent, curvature-selected structure. A non-flat connection is what lets the population carry frame-independent relational content that cannot be averaged away — the gauge-theoretic substrate for emergent organization. The gauge principle also *forces the form* of any term that differentiates beliefs along the base: the covariant derivative $D_\mu$ is the unique lowest-order operator that keeps $q_i$ in its own representation, so a base-kinetic belief term, if added, must use $D_\mu$ on pain of breaking gauge covariance. This form-forcing is exact (the same logic that fixes minimal coupling in QED); the *existence* of such a term is a modeling choice, not a consequence forced by the existing free energy.

This concept is shared core theory across the two sibling projects — the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]] (MAgent) and the [[VFE Transformer Program]] (`vfe3`) — and the two implement Regime II differently, with instructively opposite shortfalls.

## Details

**The two implementations, and why they are mirror images.** A 2026-06-18 multi-agent audit compared the two against the full photon standard.

*`vfe3` (the transformer).* Regime II is the edge-relaxed cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i\!\cdot\!G)\,\exp(\delta_{ij}\!\cdot\!G)\,\exp(-\phi_j\!\cdot\!G)$ with a learned **bilinear** edge factor $\delta_{ij}^a = \mu_i^\top W^a \mu_j$ (`vfe3/geometry/transport.py:_build_regime_ii`; the connection $W$ is a sanctioned, default-off neural-network exception, selected by `transport_mode='regime_ii'`). A nonzero $W$ does produce genuine triangle holonomy. But the raw-mean bilinear is **gauge-invariant only at $W=0$**: under a gauge action $\mu_i\mapsto g\mu_i$ the edge factor is not equivariant, so $\Omega_{ij}$ ceases to transform as $g_i\Omega_{ij}g_j^{-1}$ and the deviation grows with $\lVert W\rVert$ (the codebase documents this in `test_regime_ii_edge_factor_breaks_gauge_invariance_for_nonzero_W`). The gauge-*invariant* alternative — building $\delta$ from the Mahalanobis/trace/log-det features of the belief KL, which are congruence-invariant (`gauge_invariant_edge_features`, "Route B") — is now **wired** as the registered builder `transport_mode='regime_ii_covariant'` (`_build_regime_ii_covariant`, a sanctioned default-off NN exception with a learned `connection_M` of shape `(n_gen, 3)`; added 2026-06-18, threaded end-to-end through the E-step and trainable, with gauge-equivariance pinned by `tests/test_regime_ii_covariant.py`). The covariance $\Omega_{ij}\mapsto g_i\Omega_{ij}g_j^{-1}$ holds under the *coherent* gauge action — the frame co-transforms, $\exp(\phi_i)\mapsto g_i\exp(\phi_i)$, so the transported key $\Omega^0_{ij}\mu_j$ and the query $\mu_i$ both land in vertex $i$'s frame and the KL edge features see a *common* push-forward — not from transforming the raw beliefs alone with $\phi$ held fixed (a 2026-06-18 audit clarified this mechanism; the end-to-end $\Omega$ transformation law, beyond the edge-feature invariance, is now pinned by `test_regime_ii_covariant_omega_transforms_covariantly`). The invariance is fp32-fragile: the transported-key congruence $\Omega^0\Sigma\Omega^{0\top}$ squares $\operatorname{cond}(\Omega^0)\sim\exp(2\lVert\phi\rVert)$ on the non-compact `block_glk` frame (>100% relative error at $K{=}70$), so the feature congruence and its Cholesky now run in a **float64 island** with a `safe_cholesky` non-PD fallback (audit fix 2026-06-18), mirroring `transport_covariance`'s sandwich upcast. And `vfe3` carries **no Maxwell/Yang–Mills kinetic term** in its free energy. So the bilinear `regime_ii` buys curvature by sacrificing the gauge principle, while the covariant `regime_ii_covariant` (Route B) keeps **both** the gauge principle and the belief coupling — leaving the missing Yang–Mills kinetic term as `vfe3`'s sole remaining gap versus the full photon standard.

*MAgent.* Regime II is $\Omega_{ij} = \Omega_i\,V_{ij}\,\Omega_j^{-1}$ with $V_{ij}$ a **state-independent** learnable edge field declared to transform in the *trivial representation* (`gauge_agent/non_flat_connection.py:StateIndependentLatticeConnection`). The module's no-go argument is precisely that under the per-agent convention a state-*dependent* edge factor (such as `vfe3`'s $\mu^\top W\mu$) cannot stay covariant, so the only covariant choice is a state-independent $V$. The composite triangle $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}\Omega_{ki}$ then telescopes to a pure conjugation $\Omega_i(V_{ij}V_{jk}V_{ki})\Omega_i^{-1}$, whose **spectrum is gauge-invariant** — genuine, non-removable curvature. MAgent also has a real **Maxwell/Yang–Mills kinetic term wired into the free energy** (the lattice Wilson plaquette action on an independent edge-link parameter, `lattice_gauge.py` summed into $\mathcal F$ at `full_vfe.py`, gauge-invariant to round-off). As of 2026-06-18 MAgent also has the **second half**: a base-neighbor minimal-coupling vertex `lambda_belief_neighbor` (`gauge_agent/belief_neighbor.py`, default $0.0$, opt-in) that transports each belief across a base-lattice edge by the connection and scores the forward KL to its neighbour, with the Gaussian covariance carried by the sandwich $M\Sigma M^\top$. A second commit the same day closes the analogy by **threading the live connection**: the toggle `belief_neighbor_from_lattice` (default on) reindexes the *same* `LatticeGaugeField` edge twists $V$ that the Yang–Mills term penalizes into the vertex's base links $\Lambda_{i,\mu}(c)$ (`lattice_links_to_base_links`, a transpose of the agent/axis axes), so one connection $A_\mu$ enters **both** $\int\operatorname{tr}(F^2)$ and the minimal-coupling vertex $D_\mu q$. The vertex sources the connection ($\partial\mathcal F/\partial V\neq 0$, the matter current), confirmed at runtime under the active ouroboros config.

The contrast in one line, as the two codebases stood at the *start* of the 2026-06-18 audit: **`vfe3` coupled the connection to beliefs but broke gauge covariance and had no kinetic term; MAgent was covariant and had a kinetic term but did not couple the connection to the beliefs.** Both shortfalls were then addressed the same day. `vfe3` gained the covariant `regime_ii_covariant` (Route B), removing its gauge-covariance break and leaving the Maxwell action as its sole remaining gap. MAgent gained the minimal-coupling belief vertex with the live connection threaded, so it now realizes **both** halves of the photon — a gauge-covariant non-flat connection, a Yang–Mills kinetic term, **and** a minimal-coupling vertex by which that same connection does covariant-derivative work on the beliefs — the first of the two codebases to do so. The residual asymmetry is thus reversed from the audit's start: `vfe3` couples covariantly but lacks the kinetic term; MAgent has both halves, though both remain default-off opt-in research paths.

**The honest status of the minimal-coupling term.** Of the implemented forms, only a narrow one is a genuine closed-ELBO object: the **frozen-connection, attention-free, $i=j$** base-kinetic term (`base_neighbor_self_energy`) is the gauge-covariant intrinsic Gaussian-Markov-random-field complexity term (a covariant Bochner/graph-Laplacian on the belief means). The live-$\phi$ variant fails the EM-blindness criterion (the E-step rewrites the very connection that defines the penalty), and the attention-weighted $i\neq j$ variant (`base_neighbor_attn_energy`) inherits consensus-energy status (no global potential, no interior fixed point) — both are regularizers, not closed-ELBO terms. The presence of the attention-entropy term $\tau\beta\log(\beta/\pi)$ is the tell of the source-selection latent that separates the two readings. The forward direction of the KL is the natural directed choice for compatibility with the existing E-step/M-step machinery; a symmetric kinetic (Dirichlet-energy) reading instead wants Jeffreys or the Fisher–Rao geodesic, which agree with forward KL only to leading order.

The photon-mediation *form* is proven; the claim that the minimal-coupling term *must exist* is conjectural and so marked in the memos.

**Open gaps (MAgent, 2026-06-18 audit — two of four now closed).** (1) *Closed.* The base-lattice Yang–Mills density entering $\mathcal F$ was the signed $K - \operatorname{Re}\operatorname{tr}(W)$, unbounded below for non-unitary $GL(K)$ twists; it is now the gauge-invariant non-negative eigenvalue density $\sum_k|\lambda_k(W)-1|$ by default (`yang_mills_action(bounded=True)`, `FullVFE.ym_bounded`, `CONFIG['vfe']['ym_bounded']`) — the same magnitude `curvature_norm` reports, $\geq 0$ and $0$ iff $W=I$ — with the signed Wilson form kept as an opt-in for the compact/unitary case. (2) The inter-agent triangle curvature *certificate* uses the frame-dependent $\lVert V_{ij}V_{jk}V_{ki}-I\rVert_F$ — the same class of bug as `vfe3`, but diagnostic-only here (it drives no loss), whereas in `vfe3` it is the advertised proof of curvature. (3) `grid_plaquette_norm`, the gauge-invariant Wilson action for the inter-agent edge field, is a `NotImplementedError` stub. (4) *Closed.* The minimal-coupling belief vertex `lambda_belief_neighbor` is implemented (`belief_neighbor.py`) with the live lattice connection threaded in (`belief_neighbor_from_lattice`), and the connection is now genuinely optimized: the `LatticeGaugeField` twists are stepped in the M-step (`NaturalGradientDynamics.lr_twists`, `CONFIG` default $0.01$) with the bounded Lie-group retraction $U\!\cdot\!\exp(-\eta\,U^{\top}\!\Delta)$, so the $GL(K)$ links stay invertible (no runaway-to-singular), driven by both the bounded Yang–Mills term and the minimal-coupling current $\partial\mathcal F/\partial V$. Verified at runtime under the active ouroboros config: with the twists stepped, the bounded YM action *descends* toward the flat connection $W=I$ instead of running away. Under the default config the non-flat connection and the belief vertex remain present-but-off opt-ins, with the theoretically-pure paths wired and reachable when toggled on. The two remaining gaps (2,3) are both diagnostic-only (they drive no loss).

## In this work

- **`vfe3`:** `transport_mode='regime_ii'` selects the bilinear edge-relaxed cocycle (`connection_W`, `cocycle_relaxation` homotopy $\alpha\in[0,1]$; flat at $\alpha=0$ or $W=0$), gauge-covariant only at $W=0$. `transport_mode='regime_ii_covariant'` selects the covariant Route B (`_build_regime_ii_covariant`, learned `connection_M` over the congruence-invariant `gauge_invariant_edge_features`; flat at $M=0$): non-flat **and** gauge-covariant for any $M$, default-off, end-to-end trainable (the config auto-enables `oracle_unroll_grad` for both non-flat regimes). Still no Yang–Mills kinetic term; `TODO(Route A)` in the builder targets a commutant-restricted connection on a compact subgroup for exact equivariance plus a bounded Wilson action.
- **MAgent:** `StateIndependentLatticeConnection` ($V_{ij}$, `init_twist_scale` — $0$ collapses to flat Regime I), with curvature regularizers `triangle_holonomy_norm` and `identity_anchor_penalty` (`non_flat_regularizers.py`); the base-lattice photon sector is `LatticeGaugeField` (plaquette / `yang_mills_action` / `wilson_loop_trace` / eigenvalue `curvature_norm`), entering $\mathcal F$ via `lambda_ym`. The Yang–Mills density defaults to the bounded gauge-invariant eigenvalue form $\sum_k|\lambda_k(W)-1|$ (`yang_mills_action(bounded=True)`, `FullVFE.ym_bounded`); the legacy signed Wilson density is the `bounded=False` opt-in. The connection itself is optimized in the M-step (`NaturalGradientDynamics.lr_twists`, bounded Lie-group retraction) so the twists genuinely descend toward flat under the bounded action rather than sitting frozen. The minimal-coupling vertex is implemented in `belief_neighbor.py`: `base_neighbor_self_energy` (`lambda_belief_neighbor`, the frozen-connection attention-free $i=j$ covariant-GMRF / Dirichlet energy, the closed-ELBO form) and `base_neighbor_attn_energy` (`lambda_belief_neighbor_attn`, the attention-weighted $i\neq j$ consensus-energy path), both default $0.0$. The `belief_neighbor_from_lattice` toggle (default on) feeds the live `LatticeGaugeField` twists — the same connection $\lambda_{ym}$ penalizes — into the vertex's base links via `lattice_links_to_base_links`, so enabling the term with a gauge attached gives genuine Regime-II curvature; `base_links=None` (no gauge / toggle off) recovers the flat-base Regime-I GMRF.
