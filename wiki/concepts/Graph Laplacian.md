# Graph Laplacian

The graph Laplacian L = D - A (degree matrix minus adjacency) is the discrete analogue of the Laplace operator on a graph. It is symmetric positive-semidefinite; its smallest eigenvalue is zero (with multiplicity equal to the number of connected components), and its second-smallest eigenvalue — the algebraic connectivity or Fiedler value — measures how well-connected the graph is. Its eigenvectors (notably the Fiedler vector) drive spectral clustering, consensus/diffusion dynamics, and opinion-dynamics models on networks.
