# Riemannian geometry

Riemannian geometry studies smooth manifolds equipped with a smoothly varying inner product (the metric tensor) on each tangent space, yielding notions of length, angle, geodesic distance, parallel transport, curvature, and the exponential/logarithm maps. It is the general geometric setting underlying the program's specific manifolds: the SPD covariance cone with its affine-invariant metric ([[SPD-manifold geometry and Riemannian optimization]]), the Fisher-information / statistical manifolds of information geometry ([[Information geometry and natural gradient]]), natural-gradient descent, and Riemannian optimization all instantiate Riemannian-geometric structure on spaces of belief states. Treating beliefs as points on a curved manifold rather than in flat space is what motivates natural-gradient updates and geometry-respecting retractions.
