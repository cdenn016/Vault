# Entanglement entropy

Entanglement entropy is the von Neumann entropy S = -tr(rho_A log rho_A) of the reduced density matrix of a subsystem A, quantifying the quantum correlations between A and its complement. In holography it obeys the Ryu-Takanagi area law and is the central quantity linking quantum information to emergent geometry: Van Raamsdonk argues that spatial connectivity in the bulk is built from entanglement between boundary regions, so 'spacetime is glued together by entanglement.' For the participatory/it-from-bit thread it operationalizes the idea that geometric structure is informational, and ties to the gauge-theoretic treatment of subsystem boundaries (edge modes).
