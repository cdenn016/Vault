# Quantum Decoherence

The process by which a quantum system loses coherence (well-defined phase relations / superposition) through entanglement with environmental degrees of freedom, yielding effectively classical statistical mixtures. Decoherence explains the apparent emergence of classicality and the suppression of macroscopic superpositions, and sets the scale at which proposals (gravitational or otherwise) for objective collapse or macroscopic-superposition tests (levitated optomechanics, mirror superpositions) must compete. Relevant to the participatory-realism thread as the mechanism mediating between quantum description and observed classical facts.
