# Quantum State Reduction

Quantum state reduction (the 'R' process) is the discontinuous, probabilistic collapse of a superposed quantum state to a single eigenstate upon measurement, in contrast to continuous unitary evolution (the 'U' process). The tension between R and U is the measurement paradox. Objective-collapse proposals such as the Diosi-Penrose model treat R as a physically real, gravitationally induced process: a mass superposition decays spontaneously with lifetime tau ~ hbar/E_G, where E_G is the gravitational self-energy of the mass difference. In the participatory-realism / it-from-bit thread, R is compared to Bayesian belief updating as observer-driven information acquisition.
