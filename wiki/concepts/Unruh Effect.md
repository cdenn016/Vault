# Unruh Effect

The Unruh effect predicts that a uniformly accelerated observer perceives the Minkowski vacuum as a thermal bath at the Unruh temperature T = a/(2 pi) (in natural units), proportional to proper acceleration a. It establishes that the particle content of a quantum field is observer-dependent and ties acceleration to temperature. In Jacobson's derivation of the Einstein equation as an equation of state, the Unruh temperature attached to local Rindler horizons supplies the temperature in the Clausius relation delta Q = T dS.
