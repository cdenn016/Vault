# Observer Dependence

Observer dependence is the thesis that certain facts — measurement outcomes, simultaneity, or even physical properties — are defined only relative to an observer or reference frame rather than absolutely. It spans special-relativistic frame-dependence of time and space, the observer-dependent facts of Wigner's-friend and Frauchiger-Renner type no-go theorems, and the agent-relative quantum states of QBism and relational quantum mechanics. In the program it underpins participatory realism: the claim that observers are constitutive of, not external to, the facts they register.
