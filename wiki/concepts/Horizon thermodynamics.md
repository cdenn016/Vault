# Horizon thermodynamics

Horizon thermodynamics is the program, originating with black-hole thermodynamics and extended to general causal horizons (e.g. Rindler horizons seen by accelerated observers), that assigns a temperature and entropy to any horizon and treats gravitational field equations as thermodynamic equations of state. In Padmanabhan's formulation, Einstein's equations near a horizon take the form of the first law (TdS = dE + PdV), so gravity is emergent/thermodynamic rather than fundamental. This observer- and horizon-relative reading of spacetime thermodynamics connects to the participatory, information-theoretic view of physics central to the it-from-bit program.
