# Philosophy of Mind

The branch of philosophy concerned with the nature of mind, consciousness, mental states, and their relation to the physical — including the mind-body problem, the hard problem of consciousness, physicalism, dualism, panpsychism/panprotopsychism, and functionalism. It frames the interpretive stakes of the program's participatory and consciousness-adjacent claims: whether informational/relational structure can ground experience, and what it would mean for a free-energy agent to have a mind.
