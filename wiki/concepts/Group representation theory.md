# Group representation theory

Group representation theory studies how abstract groups act linearly on vector spaces, decomposing representations into irreducibles. It is the mathematical backbone of gauge-equivariant deep learning: equivariant feature maps transform as representations of the symmetry group (e.g. SO(3), SE(3)), and the building blocks of steerable/equivariant networks (irreps, Clebsch-Gordan tensor products, spherical harmonics) come directly from this theory. In the gauge-theoretic VFE program it underpins how Lie-group symmetries constrain attention and message passing.
