# Syntactic structure in neural networks

Refers to the body of work asking whether and how neural language models encode hierarchical syntactic structure (constituency, dependency relations, agreement) in their representations and attention patterns. Findings include attention heads that specialize for syntactic dependencies and structural probes that recover dependency trees from hidden states, suggesting emergent grammatical structure without explicit supervision. This bears on the program's claim that geometric/relational structure emerges in attention.
