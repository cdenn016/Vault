# Critical Phenomena

Critical phenomena are the singular, scale-invariant behaviors a system exhibits near a continuous phase-transition point: the correlation length diverges, observables follow power laws set by critical exponents, and microscopically distinct systems collapse onto shared universality classes determined by dimensionality and symmetry rather than microscopic detail. The renormalization group explains this universality as flow toward fixed points. In the program these ideas import to networks and collective inference — Boettcher's exact RG on Hanoi networks ([[boettcher2012renormalization]]) and complex-network criticality — as a model of multi-scale belief coarse-graining and the universality class of the VFE flow.
