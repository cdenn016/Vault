# Critical Phenomena

A **phase transition** is a qualitative change in the macroscopic state of a many-body system as a control parameter (temperature, coupling, noise) crosses a critical value — e.g. the paramagnet-to-ferromagnet transition in the Ising model. **Critical phenomena** are the singular, scale-invariant behaviors a system exhibits near a *continuous* (second-order) transition point: the correlation length diverges, observables follow power laws set by critical exponents, and microscopically distinct systems collapse onto shared universality classes determined by dimensionality and symmetry rather than microscopic detail. The renormalization group explains this universality as flow toward fixed points. In the program these ideas import to networks and collective inference — Boettcher's exact RG on Hanoi networks ([[boettcher2012renormalization]]) and complex-network criticality — as a model of multi-scale belief coarse-graining and the universality class of the VFE flow; phase transitions specifically model the *onset of collective order* (consensus or belief synchronization in multi-agent systems and opinion dynamics) and the regime boundaries of the VFE-minimization flow under varying coupling.
