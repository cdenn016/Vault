# Transformer interpretability and scaling

This theme weaves together the attention-cluster literature that tries to say, with some rigor, *what a transformer actually computes* — and how that computation is shaped by depth, scale, position encoding, and context length. Two complementary strands run through it. The first is bottom-up and empirical: open up a trained model and read off what individual heads do. The second is top-down and mathematical: model the whole stack as a dynamical system and prove how the token representations evolve through depth. The interest of the cluster, and its bearing on the gauge-theoretic [[Variational free energy]] program, is that the two strands converge on the same handful of phenomena — directed copy circuits, head specialization, syntactic structure in representations, consensus and collapse with depth, and depth-as-iterated-optimization — and that each of these phenomena is exactly what the [[VFE Transformer Program]] needs either as supporting evidence or as a failure mode to defend against.

The empirical strand begins where attention itself began. Soft attention was introduced as a learned *alignment*: at each decoding step the model computes a normalized, content-based distribution over all source positions and reads out a weighted average, with the weights recovering linguistically sensible word correspondences ([[bahdanau-2014-neural-machine-translation]]). This established the durable template — a normalized mixture over a variable-length set of sources — that scaled dot-product self-attention later generalized ([[vaswani-2017-attention]]). From the start, then, an attention weight was something one could *interpret*: it was a soft answer to "which source does this output draw on?". Mechanistic interpretability picks up exactly that question and pushes it to the level of single heads. Analysis of BERT shows that particular heads attend along identifiable grammatical relations — objects to verbs, determiners to nouns, coreferent mentions — recovering dependency structure above chance straight from the attention weights, while heavy attention to delimiter tokens functions as a no-op ([[clark-2019-bert-attention]]). In machine-translation transformers the heads turn out to be far from interchangeable: a specialized minority do positional (fixed-offset), syntactic (relation-specific), and rare-token jobs, while most heads are redundant and can be pruned away with a differentiable gate at little cost ([[voita-2019-attention-heads|voita-2019-multihead]]). And the syntactic content is not confined to the weights: a *structural probe* finds a single linear transform of the contextual embedding space under which squared distance approximates dependency-tree distance and squared norm approximates parse depth, so syntax is embedded as a low-rank metric on the representations the attention layers build, recoverable for contextual encoders but not for static baselines ([[hewitt-2019-structural-probe|hewitt-manning-2019-structural-probe]]). The sharpest single result in this strand is the *induction head*: a two-head circuit (a previous-token head feeding a match-and-copy head) that implements in-context copy-completion, $[A][B]\dots[A]\!\to\![B]$, whose emergence during training coincides with a phase-change bump in in-context-learning ability and whose ablation removes much of that ability ([[olsson-2022-induction-heads]]). That circuit is read off within a now-canonical decomposition — the residual stream as a shared communication channel that each head reads from and writes back into, with every head factored into a QK circuit that decides *where* to attend and an OV circuit that decides *what* to move ([[elhage-2021-transformer-circuits-framework]]), the parent framework of the induction-heads result and the very architectural object the gauge-theoretic program reinterprets when it writes attention transport as $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ and treats heads as block-diagonal $\mathrm{GL}(K_h)$ channels writing into one shared stream. Together these papers are catalogued under the concept [[Mechanistic interpretability of attention]], and they bracket a single claim: attention weights and the representations they produce carry real, if partial and head-specific, structure.

The mathematical strand asks the same "what does attention compute" question but about the *collective* dynamics of the token cloud through depth, and it arrives at results that are simultaneously elegant and cautionary. Stripped of skip connections and feed-forward layers, pure stacked self-attention is a contraction: it loses rank *doubly exponentially* in depth, driving every token row to the same vector — token uniformity, or over-smoothing — with skip connections the dominant counterforce and the per-token MLP a secondary brake ([[dong-2021-rank-collapse]]). The mean-field view makes the same point geometrically: treating each token as a particle on the sphere and the layers as a time-discretized interacting-particle flow with a softmax interaction kernel, the dynamics drive *clustering*, collapsing to a single consensus cluster in the long-depth limit, with long-lived metastable multi-cluster states at intermediate temperature and depth ([[geshkovski-2023-mathematical-transformers]]). The inverse temperature of that kernel is set by the query-key scaling, tying *how sharply and fast consensus forms* directly to the attention temperature. A third mathematical result reinterprets depth as computation rather than collapse: a linear self-attention layer can be constructed to perform exactly one step of gradient descent on the in-context least-squares loss, so a stack of layers is an unrolled optimizer and trained transformers recover this solution, including a preconditioned (curvature-corrected) variant ([[von-oswald-2022-transformers-gradient-descent]]). In-context learning — the very ability the induction heads underwrite empirically — is thus, mechanistically, an inner optimization run across depth.

Position encoding is where these abstract pictures meet engineering, and the cluster supplies the menu of schemes the VFE construction reinterprets. Rotary embeddings (RoPE) inject position by rotating queries and keys by a position-proportional angle, so the inner product depends only on *relative* position $m-n$ — a multiplicative, abelian construction the program reads as a one-parameter gauge frame $\Omega_m = \exp(m\,\theta J)$ ([[su2024roformer|su-2021-roformer-rope]]). ALiBi instead adds a bias *linear in distance* to each attention logit, a static recency prior that enables length extrapolation and which the program derives as the additive log-prior $\log\pi_{ij}$ falling out of its entropy-regularized consensus objective ([[press2022-alibi|press-2021-alibi]]); T5's learned relative-position bucket biases are the trainable cousin of the same additive scheme ([[raffel2020exploring|raffel-2020-t5]]). These multiplicative and additive readings are synthesized in [[Attention mechanisms — theory and positional structure]]. The cluster's model-family and long-context references — the decoder-only autoregressive baseline GPT-2 ([[radford2019-gpt2|radford-2019-gpt2]]), the efficient open baseline Mistral 7B with its sliding-window and grouped-query attention ([[jiang2023-mistral7b|jiang-2023-mistral]]), and Longformer's local-window-plus-global sparse attention ([[beltagy2020longformer|beltagy-2020-longformer]]) — fill in the architectural furniture: each sparse or windowed scheme is a hard, hand-designed special case of the distance-decaying coupling prior the program derives softly, and each fixes a baseline against which the no-neural-network VFE alternative is positioned. The normalization of the modern decoder-only (LLaMA) convention the program baselines against is RMSNorm, which drops the mean-recentering and learned bias of LayerNorm and keeps only an RMS rescaling ([[zhang-sennrich-2019-rmsnorm]]) — a no-bias, rescaling-only form that maps directly onto the program's gauge norm-cancellation story (already invoked in GL(K)_supplementary). Scaling, the cluster's other axis, connects here through [[Neural scaling laws]]: the induction-head phase change is a *scaling phenomenon* (it appears at a particular point in training and is more pronounced at larger scale), and the metastable-then-collapse picture predicts how representational diversity should behave as depth grows.

For the gauge-theoretic VFE program the synthesis is pointed. The empirical interpretability results are the *foils* for the source-attribution and parse-completeness conjectures examined in [[Mechanistic interpretability of attention]]: if specialized syntactic and induction heads are the established baseline for "attention recovers structure," then the VFE coupling $\beta_{ij}=\mathrm{softmax}_j(-\mathrm{KL}(q_i\|\Omega_{ij}q_j)/\tau)$ must reproduce them to earn its source-attribution reading, and the structural probe sets a geometric bar that the intrinsic belief-covariance metric on the [[Symmetric spaces and the SPD cone|SPD cone]] could in principle meet. The mathematical results are the *failure modes and warrants*: rank collapse and mean-field consensus are precisely the "heat death" that the attention-entropy term $\tau\,\beta_{ij}\log(\beta_{ij}/\pi_{ij})$ in the [[Variational free energy]] functional exists to forestall — and the metastable multi-cluster regime is the structured intermediate the program actually wants, the substrate for [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]]. The depth-as-gradient-descent result is the cleanest external warrant for the program's signature claim that a forward pass *is* iterative free-energy minimization — depth indexing E-steps of [[Iterative amortized inference]] — with the preconditioned variant matching the program's [[Natural gradient]] descent on the [[Fisher information metric]]. Interpretability tells the program what its attention must look like; scaling and the dynamical-systems theory tell it what its attention will do as it deepens.

## Open questions / gaps

- **Does the VFE $\beta_{ij}$ actually pass the interpretability tests?** No source applies induction-head metrics, head-pruning, BERT-style relation probes, or a structural probe to the gauge-theoretic transformer's coupling weights or beliefs. The parse-completeness and source-attribution claims remain conjectures until measured against these established baselines.
- **Can a KL-geometric, gauge-transported coupling express a directed copy circuit?** Induction heads route information from a specific earlier source; it is unproven whether the consensus-flavored $\sum_{ij}\beta_{ij}\,\mathrm{KL}$ coupling can implement directed copy-completion or only diffusive belief-averaging.
- **Is the entropy regularizer sufficient where MLPs are absent?** Rank-collapse analysis names skip connections and the per-token MLP as the brakes on consensus; under the no-neural-network constraint the program must lean entirely on the entropy term and the token-dependent transport $\Omega_{ij}$. Whether these alone hold the system in the metastable multi-cluster regime, rather than collapsing, is open.
- **Scaling of the phase change.** Whether a VFE transformer exhibits an induction-head-like phase change in in-context learning, and how it scales, is untested; this is the natural bridge from this cluster to [[Neural scaling laws]].
