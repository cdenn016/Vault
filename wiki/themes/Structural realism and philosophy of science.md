# Structural realism and philosophy of science

This theme assembles the philosophy-of-science literature that the gauge-theoretic
variational-free-energy program leans on to justify its central metaphysical wager: that
physics — and, by the program's lights, *cognition* — is about **structure, relations, and
information** rather than intrinsic substances. The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]]
builds a multi-agent model in which [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] carry no
frame-independent state and objectivity is identified with **gauge-invariant content**. That
move is not an idiosyncrasy of the formalism; it is the inheritor of a long and well-developed
tradition in the philosophy of science, running from Kant through neo-Kantian constitutive
epistemology, the structural-realism debate, the underdetermination and theory-change
literature, anti-realist and constructive-empiricist alternatives, and the social epistemology
of inquiry. The purpose of this page is to weave that literature into one picture and to mark
exactly where it supports, and where it only loosely analogizes with, the program's
commitments.

## From substance to structure

The deepest root is Kant's "Copernican revolution" in the
[[kant-1781-critique-pure-reason|Critique of Pure Reason]]: objects of experience conform to
the constitutive forms of the knowing subject, not the reverse. Space and time are *a priori
forms of intuition* and the categories organize sensibility into objective experience, so we
know only phenomena — appearances structured by our own forms — never the noumenal
thing-in-itself. Objectivity, for Kant, is already a *constitutive achievement* rather than a
given, and there is no cognition of a mind-independent world "as it is in itself." This is the
philosophical ancestor of the program's denial of any privileged, frame-independent description
of an agent's beliefs, and of the move from Wheeler's *it from bit* to a constitutive,
observer-dependent ontology catalogued in [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]].

Cassirer's *Substance and Function* ([[cassirer-1910-substance-function]]) turns the Kantian
constitutive turn into a thesis about *concepts*: the working concepts of mathematics and
physics — number, point, energy, function — are not abstractions of shared sensible marks
(*Substanzbegriff*) but laws of serial order and relation (*Funktionsbegriff*) that constitute
their objects. A point is fixed by the relations it enters; a physical magnitude by the
invariants of the relevant transformation group. From this Cassirer draws the slogan that has
become the program's hidden axiom: **objectivity is invariance under the admissible
transformations of the descriptive framework.** That is, almost verbatim, "the objective
content of a theory is its gauge-invariant content," and it is the conceptual seed of the whole
[[Structural realism]] lineage — relations prior to relata.

The modern structural-realism debate makes this explicit and graded. Worrall's "best of both
worlds" ([[worrall-1989-structural-realism]]) launched *epistemic* structural realism: across
theory change the *mathematical structure* is retained and accumulates (Fresnel's equations
survived the demise of the elastic ether) even as posited objects are discarded, so the realist
should commit to structure, not to the nature of entities. This simultaneously honours the
no-miracles intuition and dodges the pessimistic meta-induction. French and Ladyman
([[french-ladyman-2003-remodelling]]) push to *ontic* structural realism: pressing the question
of what *grounds* the retained structure, and pointing to the metaphysical underdetermination of
particle individuality in quantum theory, they make structure metaphysically primary and treat
objects as derivative nodes — "relations without prior relata." Ladyman and Ross
([[ladyman-ross-2007-every-thing-must-go]]) naturalize this into a book-length OSR ("there are
no things; structure is all there is"), add *rainforest realism* about real patterns at every
scale, and articulate physical structure in **information-theoretic** terms — the cleanest
bridge from OSR to the *it-from-bit* tradition the program inhabits. Esfeld and Lam
([[esfeld-lam-2008-moderate-osr]]) supply the version that fits the model best: a *moderate* OSR
on which objects are real but have no intrinsic identity, being individuated wholly by their
relations, and which finds its natural expression in the **fibre-bundle** formalism of gauge
theory — spacetime points (or, by analogy, agents) individuated only by the metric/connection
structure laid over them. This is the closest literal precedent for treating
[[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] that are constituted by their relations and by the
connection $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ that transports beliefs between them.

## The constitutive a priori and theory change

Running parallel to structural realism is the *relativized a priori* tradition, given its own
page in [[Relativized a priori]]. Reichenbach ([[reichenbach-1920-relativity-apriori]]) split
Kant's a priori into "necessarily true forever" and "constitutive of the object," argued that
general relativity refutes the first while vindicating the second, and so relativized the
constitutive a priori to a framework. Friedman's *Dynamics of Reason*
([[friedman-2001-dynamics-of-reason]]) develops this into a layered account — empirical laws,
constitutive a priori principles, and philosophical meta-frameworks — in which paradigm change
is real but *rationally bridgeable*. This is the program's license for reading an agent's slow
generative-model geometry as a learned, fungible, gauge-dependent "form of intuition," and for
treating theoretical frameworks as conventions open to collective revision.

The theory-change literature supplies the dynamics. Kuhn ([[kuhn-1962-structure]]) gives the
cycle of normal science, anomaly, crisis, and revolution, with *incommensurable* paradigms and
theory-laden observation; Hoyningen-Huene ([[hoyningen-huene-1993-reconstructing]]) reconstructs
Kuhn's view in its neo-Kantian, world-constituting form, defusing the relativist caricature. The
manuscript reads paradigms as shared frames/gauges, normal science as belief-updating within a
fixed connection, and revolutions as a collective, discontinuous **re-gauging** across the agent
population — a global [[Gauge transformation]] that resets which quantities are coordinated to
experience, with incommensurability becoming the absence of a single privileged frame relating
pre- and post-revolution descriptions. Quine's "Two Dogmas" ([[quine-1951-two-dogmas]])
contributes confirmational holism and underdetermination: beliefs face experience only as a
corporate "web," any statement is revisable, and multiple incompatible theories can fit the same
data. The program reads this as a quantitative *field of force* — an agent's commitments are a
coupled belief structure updated globally under [[Variational free energy]] minimization, so
observations adjust the whole web rather than isolated propositions, and underdetermination
becomes the existence of many (often gauge-related) belief configurations compatible with the
same data. Laudan ([[laudan-1984-science-and-values]]) replaces hierarchical justification with
the *reticulated model* — aims, methods, and facts mutually adjust — which is a closer analogue
to the model's gradual, network-distributed, multi-level belief co-adjustment than Kuhn's
discontinuous gestalt-switch, and whose historical catalogue of successful-but-false theories is
a principal driver of the pessimistic meta-induction that structural realism answers. Worrall's
own later treatment of use-novelty ([[worrall-2014-prediction-accommodation-revisited]]) — that
evidence supports a theory only insofar as the theory was not built to fit it — supplies the
operational criterion for the program's Lakatosian progressive-versus-degenerating audit: whether
the gauge-theoretic results are genuine novel predictions or post-hoc accommodations of structure
the canon already contains under other notation.

## No view from nowhere: realism, anti-realism, and the social

The program's denial of a privileged frame is the geometric face of a cluster of
anti-correspondence positions. Putnam's internal realism ([[putnam-1981-reason-truth-history]])
relocates truth to idealized rational acceptability *within* a conceptual scheme and denies any
"God's-eye view"; objecthood and reference are scheme-relative, so what travels across schemes
is structure, not object-identity. Nagel ([[nagel-1986-view-from-nowhere]]) makes the
complementary epistemic-limits point: objectivity is achieved by degrees of detachment from
one's standpoint but a complete *view from nowhere* is unattainable, and the situated
perspective is ineliminable. These are the analytic-philosophy statements of the program's
consensus-objectivity thesis: objectivity is what survives across agent-local frames under a
gauge transformation, an internal, intersubjective achievement rather than correspondence to a
ready-made world. Sellars ([[sellars-1962-philosophy-scientific-image]]) frames the residual
problem — the *manifest image* of persons and intentions versus the *scientific image* of
imperceptible structure — and demands their "stereoscopic" fusion; the manuscript's wager is
that the fibre-bundle picture supplies exactly this fusion, with agents simultaneously manifest
participants and scientific-image structure.

On the anti-realist side, van Fraassen's constructive empiricism
([[vanfraassen-1980-scientific-image]]) supplies the methodological backbone: science aims at
*empirical adequacy*, and to accept a theory is to adopt it as a *stance* (values plus
commitments) rather than to believe its theoretical posits literally true. The manuscript
explicitly positions its own attitude as a stance "in roughly van Fraassen's sense," advancing
participatory realism not as dogma but as a useful, operationally adequate lens — which
sidesteps the demand to certify a view-from-nowhere truth. Cartwright
([[cartwright-1983-laws-physics-lie]]) sharpens the instrumentalist reading of *laws*:
fundamental laws are true of idealized models, not of messy real systems, so they function as
organizing, framework-constituting tools — a "laws-as-grammar" reading congenial to Cassirer's
function-over-substance and to the program's treatment of an agent's "laws" as the gauge-relative
grammar of its belief-updating. Longino ([[longino-2002-fate-of-knowledge]]) adds the social
dimension: objectivity is a property of *communities* that subject claims to transformative
critical discourse under shared norms, not of isolated minds. This is the social-epistemology
restatement of consensus objectivity — the program's many coupled agents reach agreement through
interaction, and the objective content is what survives that collective process — and it links
the theme to the [[Collective active inference]] modeling of belief dynamics.

Finally, Wigner's puzzle of the "unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics"
([[wigner-1960-unreasonable-effectiveness]]) functions here as a *foil the program claims to
dissolve*: if what is real is relational structure (structural realism) and the agent
constitutes its objective field through mathematically expressible invariant forms (the
constitutive/participatory side), then the fit of mathematics to physics is unsurprising, since
mathematics is the science of structure and invariance/symmetry is precisely where it earns its
physical keep — the same place the gauge-theoretic core of the program operates.

## Open questions / gaps

- **Ontic vs. moderate OSR for agents.** The program oscillates between eliminating
  frame-independent agent states (radical OSR) and retaining agents as relationally individuated
  sections (Esfeld–Lam moderate OSR). The formalism does carry concrete sections, favouring the
  moderate reading, but the manuscript's rhetoric sometimes reaches for the radical claim. Which
  is the considered position?

> [!note] Editorial: The "Kuhnian revolution as collective gauge transformation" reading and the
> "objective content = gauge-invariant content" identification are *structural analogies*
> licensed by these sources, not derivations from them. A genuinely frame-independent
> (revolution-invariant) structure, if it exists, must be recovered by averaging over realised
> frames and is regulator-dependent over a non-compact gauge group — so the philosophical
> sources motivate, but do not establish, the formal claim. This caveat is internal to the
> program (see [[Relativized a priori]]); none of the cited philosophers commit to it.

- **Stance vs. realism.** Adopting van Fraassen's "stance" framing sits uneasily beside the
  robustly *realist* commitments of OSR (structure is *real*). Is the program a realism about
  structure held with an empiricist stance about everything else, or a thoroughgoing stance with
  no metaphysical residue? The sources pull in both directions and the manuscript has not fully
  reconciled them.
- **Underdetermination, formalized.** Quine's web-of-belief and underdetermination are claimed
  as things the multi-agent VFE setting *formalizes*, but the precise mapping from "many total
  theories fitting the evidence" to "many gauge-related belief configurations" is asserted rather
  than worked out. A theorem relating empirical-equivalence classes to gauge orbits would turn
  the analogy into a result.
- **Social vs. geometric objectivity.** Longino's and Laudan's network/community accounts of
  objectivity are invoked alongside the geometric invariance account, but whether collective
  critical interaction *yields* the gauge-invariant content, or merely resembles it, is open.
