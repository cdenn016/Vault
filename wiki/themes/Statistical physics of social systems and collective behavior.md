# Statistical physics of social systems and collective behavior

## The landscape

Statistical social physics contains many mechanism-specific models rather than one universal update law. The [[Voter model]] studies stochastic imitation; [[Discrete spin and majority-rule models of opinion]] study conformist alignment and frustration; [[Bounded confidence]] models selective interaction; [[Threshold models and complex contagion]] model reinforcement-dependent adoption; [[Synchronization and the Kuramoto model]] studies phase locking; and kinetic or mean-field approaches pass from agents to population densities. The surveys [[castellano-2009-statistical-physics-social-dynamics]], [[galam-2008-sociophysics]], [[flache-2017-social-influence-models]], and [[jusup-2022-social-physics]] organize this plural landscape.

The theorem-first revision [[belief-inertia-2026-07-12-theorem-first-revision]] places [[SocialPhysics]] within it more narrowly than the earlier synthesis did. The established result is an engineered gauge-covariant Gaussian KL consensus energy, entropy-retaining optimized attention, and a local four-part loss stiffness. Fisher--Rao natural-gradient flow is primary. A positive kinetic metric is a conditional postulate and must be distinguished from both intrinsic Fisher geometry and loss curvature.

## Derived, analogous, interpretive, and outside scope

The social correspondences now fall into four classes.

### Derived under restrictions

Under the primary unweighted product Fisher metric, the model derives continuous-time DeGroot only for fixed symmetric influence under flat transport and common fixed covariance. Nonuniform reversible influence requires the additional metric $G_\rho=\sigma^{-2}(D_\rho\otimes I)$, equivalently a fixed-label joint family or agent-specific rates. Persistent anchors give the restricted Friedkin--Johnsen equilibrium under the same metric scope. These results do not extend to arbitrary directed row-stochastic influence, and heterogeneous susceptibility requires agent-indexed anchor precision or coupling.

### Soft analogs

Gibbs similarity weighting is a finite-temperature soft analog of bounded confidence. It does not become the Hegselmann--Krause ball average merely by taking low temperature, and it does not reproduce the Deffuant pair-selection rule. Axelrod homophily, Schelling segregation, threshold contagion, and information cascades share parts of the selective-interaction logic but are not term-for-term recoveries.

### Interpretive mappings

The precision-weighted relational force can be discussed in the vocabulary of Social Impact Theory, but row normalization does not reproduce Latan\'e's increasing-number or multiplicative strength--immediacy--number law. Likewise, voter, majority-rule, flocking, Kuramoto, replicator, cultural-evolution, and mean-field-game connections are structural neighbors unless an explicit reduction is supplied.

### Outside the present state model

Diffusion of innovations requires an adoption state, hazard, irreversibility rule, and population equation. [[bass-1969-product-growth]] is the formal comparator; visual or qualitative S-curve language cannot substitute for that model. The gauge-VFE belief flow derives no adopter categories.

## Polarization is metastable under passive attraction

For the stated unanchored, symmetric reciprocal two-cluster reduction, positive finite-temperature attractive attention retains a nonzero cross-cluster tail. Separated clusters can merge on an exponentially long timescale, so the result is **metastability**, not exact stable polarization. Persistent separation requires hard support or severed edges, persistent anchors with a proved separated equilibrium, signed/repulsive influence, or active evidence selection.

This distinction separates passive attractive attention from the active confirmation-biased sampling in [[albarracin-2022-epistemic-communities]]. The former reweights a fixed candidate set and continues to attract; the latter changes which evidence is sampled and can maintain segregated epistemic communities. See [[Echo chambers and polarization]] and [[Collective active inference]].

The current vertex transport is pure gauge and has trivial loop holonomy. Spin-glass-like frustration and holonomy-stabilized disagreement require an edge-local/nonflat model; they are future extensions, not properties of the present flat transport.

## Selective exposure, latency, and perseverance

Three mechanisms must remain separate:

- similarity weighting gives selective exposure conditional on an imposed kernel;
- at fixed Fisher geometry and learning rate, local stiffness reduces matched-force displacement but speeds first-order relaxation; revision latency needs a separate mobility, damping, kinetic, or slow-state mechanism;
- explanation-based belief perseverance requires a slow explanatory or generative-model state that survives evidence withdrawal.

The outgoing relational/recoil Hessian block is contemporaneous curvature, not accumulated leadership history. None of these constructions alone derives universal confirmation bias, moral rigidity, or explanatory perseverance. [[nevin-mandell-atak-1983-behavioral-momentum]] supplies a behavioral resistance-to-disruption comparator, not validation of Hamiltonian opinion dynamics.

## Direct mechanics and dynamics comparators

The immediate comparison set sharply limits novelty claims:

- [[martins-2015-opinion-particles]] already develops Bayesian-inspired opinion particles with inertia and a harmonic example.
- [[xue-hirche-cao-2020-opinion-port-hamiltonian]] already uses port-Hamiltonian systems and passivity for opinion networks.
- [[baumann-sokolov-tyloo-2020-second-order-consensus]] already obtains Laplacian-mode resonance in second-order consensus under periodic coupling.
- [[sampson-porter-restrepo-2025-oscillatory-opinion]] obtains oscillatory and excitable opinion dynamics through individual--group feedback without the proposed kinetic metric.
- [[nevin-mandell-atak-1983-behavioral-momentum]] operationalizes behavioral persistence under disruption.
- [[bass-1969-product-growth]] supplies the population law required for diffusion claims.

The information-geometric mechanics neighborhood is equally important: [[chirco-2022-statistical-bundle-dynamics]], [[girolami-calderhead-2011-riemann-hmc]], [[leok-zhang-2017-information-geometric-mechanics]], and [[pistone-2018-statistical-bundle-lagrangian]] show that mechanics on statistical manifolds and Fisher-dependent kinetic geometry have substantial precedent. The residual contribution is therefore the gauge-transported Gaussian KL potential, optimized attention, four-part local relational stiffness, and an explicitly conditional kinetic interpretation.

## Continuum and multi-scale directions

[[Kinetic theory of opinion dynamics]] and [[Mean-field games and continuum limits]] are natural external tools for passing from finite populations to field descriptions, but no Boltzmann or Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman equation is derived here. [[Meta-entropy]] and [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]] supply internal coarse-graining directions; they should be treated as adjacent mathematical programs until explicit continuum equivalences are proved.
