# Statistical physics of social systems and collective behavior

## The big picture

For half a century, statistical physicists have treated populations of interacting people the way they treat populations of interacting spins: write down a local rule for how one unit responds to its neighbors, iterate it over a network, and ask what macroscopic order emerges — consensus or fragmentation, a single culture or many, a quiescent crowd or a marching one. The field that resulted is broad and somewhat fragmented, a collection of canonical models each capturing one mechanism: the [[Voter model|voter rule]] of imitation, the [[Discrete spin and majority-rule models of opinion|majority dynamics]] of conformist pressure, the [[Axelrod model of cultural dissemination|Axelrod homophily rule]] of cultural alignment, the [[Bounded confidence|bounded-confidence kernel]] of selective listening, the [[Threshold models and complex contagion|threshold rule]] of contagion, the [[Synchronization and the Kuramoto model|Kuramoto coupling]] of phase locking, and so on. The standard surveys — [[castellano-2009-statistical-physics-social-dynamics|castellano-fortunato-loreto-2009-social-dynamics]] for the statistical-physics-of-social-dynamics canon, [[galam-2008-sociophysics]] for the [[Sociophysics|sociophysics]] lineage specifically, and [[flache-2017-social-influence-models]] for the social-influence-model taxonomy — read as catalogs of distinct phenomenological rules rather than as instances of a single law.

The thesis of the [[SocialPhysics]] program (founded on the manuscript [[belief-inertia]], "The Inertia of Belief") is that this fragmentation is an artifact of stopping at first order. The claim has two parts, and they carry very different epistemic weight. The first, and the settled one, is that a large fraction of these models are the **overdamped limit** of one variational law: gradient flow on a [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] functional in which inter-agent disagreement is the gauge-transported divergence $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ between Gaussian beliefs $q_i = \mathcal{N}(\mu_i, \Sigma_i)$, weighted by softmax attention $\beta_{ij}$, with the transport $\Omega_{ij} = e^{\phi_i} e^{-\phi_j}$ carrying one agent's belief into another's gauge frame. Run that functional as first-order descent and the canonical averaging-toward-consensus, anchoring-to-prior, and selective-listening models reappear as special cases. The second part, the novel and not-yet-validated frontier, is the **underdamped extension**: reading the Fisher/precision tensor not only as the stiffness of the free-energy landscape but as an inertial **mass** gives belief a momentum, and the equation of motion $M_i \ddot{\mu}_i + \gamma_i \dot{\mu}_i + \nabla_{\mu_i}\mathcal{F} = 0$ then predicts oscillation, overshoot, resonance, and momentum transfer — second-order phenomena that every first-order model on this map structurally cannot produce. The mass reading is an explicit *ansatz* flagged in [[belief-inertia]], not a theorem: the curvature of a potential does not by itself fix a kinetic term, and the program says so plainly.

This theme assembles the surrounding neighborhood so the program's position can be stated precisely rather than rhetorically. For each strand below the question is the same and the answer is one of three: does the belief-inertia functional **recover** this model in its overdamped limit (a mathematical claim), does it **extend** this model with the momentum that the strand itself lacks, or is the strand **adjacent context** — sharing the intellectual neighborhood, supplying vocabulary or phenomenology, but not machinery the VFE functional actually instantiates? Keeping those three categories distinct is the entire point; the honest map matters more than a flattering one.

## Imitation and the voter model

The [[Voter model|voter model]] is the minimal imitation rule: pick an agent, copy a random neighbor's binary state, repeat. It is exactly solvable on many topologies, and its central result — consensus time scaling with system size and dimension, with no surviving order below the critical dimension — is a benchmark for any opinion model. Its relation to the belief-inertia functional is genuine but partial. The voter rule is a stochastic, discrete-state caricature of precision-weighted averaging: in the overdamped limit the VFE flow drives $\mu_i$ toward a $\beta_{ij}$-weighted mean of its neighbors' (transported) means, which is the continuous, deterministic, soft-attention analog of "copy a neighbor." What the voter model does *not* contain, and what the functional adds even in the overdamped regime, is the covariance channel: the rate of copying is modulated by relative precision, so confident agents are copied more and uncertain agents update faster. The voter model is therefore best read as the consensus-time skeleton that the overdamped VFE fleshes out with geometry, and the momentum extension is orthogonal to it — there is no inertia in a memoryless copy rule to begin with.

## Discrete spin and majority-rule models

The [[Discrete spin and majority-rule models of opinion|majority-rule and spin-glass models of opinion]] — Galam's majority dynamics, the Sznajd model, Ising-like opinion lattices — are the most literal transcription of statistical mechanics into social science: opinions are spins, conformity is ferromagnetic coupling, and the order parameter is net magnetization. This is the strand the belief-inertia program is closest to in spirit and most careful to distinguish from in mechanism. The overdamped VFE recovers the conformist tendency (the coupling term lowers free energy by aligning beliefs) and reproduces the consensus/polarization dichotomy as two boundary conditions of one functional rather than two models. The deeper connection the program flags, but does **not** yet realize, is frustration: spin-glass behavior (persistent disagreement, many metastable configurations) requires geometric frustration, and in the gauge language that means nontrivial [[Holonomy|holonomy]]. The current transport $\Omega_{ij} = e^{\phi_i} e^{-\phi_j}$ is pure gauge with trivial holonomy — a cocycle that integrates to zero around any loop — so the present functional **cannot** produce genuine spin-glass frustration. Reaching it needs the edge-local link variable $\Omega_{ij} = U_i e^{\delta_{ij}} U_j^{-1}$ flagged as future work in [[belief-inertia]]; until that lands, the spin-glass connection is a target, an analogy with a precise unmet condition, not an achieved identity. The honest statement is that majority-rule alignment is recovered, but the frustrated-disorder physics that makes spin glasses interesting remains adjacent.

## Cultural dissemination — the Axelrod model

The [[Axelrod model of cultural dissemination|Axelrod model]] couples imitation to homophily: agents interact in proportion to their existing cultural overlap and, on interacting, become more similar, so the dynamics can freeze into a patchwork of distinct stable cultures. Its signature result — a phase transition between global monoculture and frozen multicultural states as a function of the number of cultural traits — is a homophily-driven fragmentation that the bounded-confidence and echo-chamber strands echo in continuous-opinion space. The relation to the VFE functional is by analogy at the mechanism level and partial at the recovery level. The softmax attention $\beta_{ij} = \mathrm{softmax}(-\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])/\tau)$ *is* a homophily kernel — agents attend more to those whose transported beliefs are KL-close — so the overdamped flow reproduces the qualitative Axelrod logic of "interact where already similar, and grow more similar by interacting," and hence its frozen-fragment outcomes ([[Echo chambers and polarization]]). But Axelrod's specific discrete trait-vector machinery, its feature-by-feature matching, and its exact monoculture/multiculture transition are not derived from the functional; they are a discrete realization of the same homophily principle. This is adjacent context that shares a mechanism, not a model the functional instantiates term-for-term.

## Network structure — small-world and scale-free

The [[Network structure — small-world and scale-free|network-topology]] strand is not a dynamics at all but the substrate on which every dynamics above runs, and the discovery that real social networks are small-world and scale-free reshaped the whole field: hubs dominate influence, short path lengths accelerate consensus, and degree heterogeneity changes critical thresholds. Its relation to the belief-inertia program is structural and complementary rather than competitive. The functional's coupling graph — encoded in $\beta_{ij}$ and the underlying adjacency — *is* a network, and everything the topology literature establishes about how hubs and path length govern consensus carries over directly to the overdamped VFE flow. The program's own contribution on the network side is orthogonal and lives at a different level: the [[Community detection and modularity|community/modularity]] structure of the coupling graph becomes the seed for coarse-graining into meta-agents (the [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics|Ouroboros tower]] and its [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs|RG flow of beliefs]]), so network structure enters the program as the thing that gets renormalized, not as a rule that gets recovered. This is adjacent, enabling context: the program inherits the topology results wholesale and adds a multi-scale reading on top.

## Threshold models and complex contagion

[[Threshold models and complex contagion|Threshold models]] (Granovetter) and complex contagion theory hold that some behaviors spread only once a critical fraction of an agent's neighbors have adopted — adoption is nonlinear in social exposure, unlike simple disease-like contagion. This nonlinearity, the requirement of *reinforcement* from multiple sources, is the strand's defining feature. The belief-inertia functional contains a soft, geometrically-grounded version of a threshold rather than a hard one. The state-dependent self-coupling $\alpha_i^*(c) = c_0/(b_0 + \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| p_i))$ and the softmax attention together produce a nonlinear response in which an agent resists movement until aggregate social forcing is large enough to overcome its prior anchoring — a finite-temperature analog of a threshold, where the sharpness is set by $\tau$. Whether the functional reproduces *complex* contagion specifically — the genuine super-additivity of multiple reinforcing sources, as opposed to a smooth saturating response — is not established and is the honest open question here; the overdamped flow gives a threshold-*like* nonlinearity, and calling it complex contagion would overstate the match. This is a partial, soft recovery with an unverified boundary.

## Synchronization and the Kuramoto model

The [[Synchronization and the Kuramoto model|Kuramoto model]] of coupled phase oscillators is the canonical theory of emergent synchrony: each oscillator has an intrinsic frequency, coupling pulls phases together, and above a critical coupling strength a macroscopic fraction locks into a common rhythm. This strand is where the *underdamped* side of the belief-inertia program finds its closest external relative, and the relationship is illuminating precisely because it is an analogy of structure rather than a derivation. Kuramoto oscillators are intrinsically second-order in spirit (a phase that advances, with a natural frequency), and synchronization is the collective phenomenon of many such units; the belief-momentum extension likewise endows each agent with a dynamical degree of freedom that can oscillate at a natural frequency $\omega = \sqrt{K/M - \gamma^2/4M^2}$ and, under coupling, exhibit phase relationships, resonance, and momentum transfer. The program's claim of displacement resonance under periodic forcing and of a conserved momentum current under reciprocal symmetric coupling is conceptually a Kuramoto-flavored statement. But the belief equation of motion is a damped harmonic oscillator in belief space, not a phase oscillator on a circle, and no exact Kuramoto reduction has been derived; the order-parameter machinery and the precise synchronization transition are not imported. Kuramoto is therefore the right intuition pump for what underdamped belief dynamics would *feel* like collectively, offered honestly as a structural analogy and the nearest neighbor of the program's novel and still-untested second-order claims.

## Collective motion and flocking

[[Collective motion and flocking|Flocking models]] — Vicsek's self-propelled particles, Couzin's zonal rules ([[couzin-2009-collective-cognition]]) — explain how local alignment of velocity produces global coherent motion, a swarm that turns as one. This is the most physically embodied strand and the furthest from the belief-inertia functional in literal content: its state variable is a velocity in physical space, and its order is spatial coordination, neither of which the opinion functional has. The connection is at the level of organizing principle, and it is genuinely interesting on the underdamped side. Flocking is a momentum-alignment phenomenon — particles align *velocities*, not positions — and the belief-momentum extension is, structurally, the proposal that beliefs too carry a momentum that can be transferred and aligned between coupled agents. There is also a substantive bridge through [[Collective active inference]]: the active-inference reading of collective behavior ([[heins-2024-surprise-minimization]], [[couzin-2009-collective-cognition]]) casts flocking itself as surprise minimization over a shared generative model, which is the same variational principle the belief-inertia functional runs, now over spatial rather than doxastic states. So flocking is adjacent context for the overdamped opinion claims, but a meaningful conceptual cousin of the momentum extension, and a genuine point of contact through collective active inference.

## Social influence and conformity

[[Social influence and conformity|Social-influence theory]] — Latané's Social Impact Theory ([[latane1981psychology|latane-1981-social-impact]]), the Asch and Sherif conformity tradition, French's formal theory of social power — supplies the psychological grounding for why agents move toward one another at all, quantifying influence as a multiplicative function of source strength, immediacy, and number. This strand the overdamped functional recovers cleanly and is one of the settled results of [[belief-inertia]]: Social Impact Theory appears as the precision-weighted aggregation of social forcing, with the "strength" of a source naturally identified with its belief precision (confident sources exert more pull), "number" with the sum over neighbors, and the saturating, sublinear impact law emerging from the softmax normalization of $\beta_{ij}$. The Friedkin–Johnsen model of social influence networks ([[friedkin1990-social-influence-opinions|friedkin-johnsen-1990]], [[friedkin-johnsen-2011-social-influence-network]]) is recovered in the same limit as the flow with a persistent anchor to initial conviction supplied by the prior term, and DeGroot averaging ([[degroot-1974-consensus]]) as its zero-anchor special case. This is the program's home turf: social influence is recovered as precision-weighted Bayesian updating, with the psychology of conformity ([[nickerson-1998-confirmation-bias]], [[anderson1980-belief-perseverance|anderson-1980-belief-perseverance]]) reread as the geometric inevitability of epistemic inertia rather than as irrationality.

## Information cascades and herding

[[Information cascades and herding|Information-cascade theory]] (Bikhchandani–Hirshleifer–Welch, Banerjee herding) is a Bayesian-rational account of how agents who observe predecessors' *actions* rather than private signals can rationally abandon their own information and copy the herd, producing fragile, history-dependent cascades. Of all the strands this is the one whose native language is closest to the program's, since it is already cast as sequential Bayesian inference. The relation is conceptual and promising rather than a completed recovery. The belief-inertia functional is exactly a theory of agents updating beliefs from socially transported evidence, and the self-coupling/prior anchoring is the machinery by which an agent can be swept into agreement with its neighbors against its initial conviction; herding is therefore the kind of behavior the overdamped flow can exhibit. What the program adds, and what makes the contact more than verbal, is the inertial reading: belief momentum predicts *overshoot* past the equilibrium opinion (the square-root overshoot law $d_{\text{overshoot}} = |\dot{\mu}|\sqrt{M/K}$), which is a second-order signature of cascade-like collective swings — a herd that does not merely settle but overshoots and rebounds. No formal cascade-fragility result has been derived from the functional, so this is honestly an intellectual-neighborhood match with a suggestive underdamped twist, not a theorem.

## Schelling segregation and tipping points

The [[Schelling segregation and tipping points|Schelling segregation model]] showed that mild individual preferences for like neighbors can tip a population into near-total spatial segregation — a stark demonstration that macro-segregation need not reflect strong micro-prejudice. It is a model of spatial sorting driven by a homophily threshold, and its enduring lesson is the gap between micromotives and macrobehavior. Its relation to the belief-inertia functional is by analogy of mechanism, not by recovery. The homophily kernel that drives Schelling sorting is, in opinion space, the same selective-attention mechanism ($\beta_{ij}$ falling off with belief distance) that drives [[Echo chambers and polarization|echo-chamber]] formation in the overdamped VFE flow; both are tipping phenomena where a smooth parameter change flips the macrostate. But Schelling's state is a physical location on a lattice with a discrete satisfaction threshold and relocation dynamics, none of which the opinion functional contains, so the connection is the shared logic of homophily-driven tipping rather than a derivation. Adjacent context with a clean conceptual parallel: segregation in space and polarization in belief are two faces of the same micromotive-to-macrobehavior tipping, and the functional realizes the belief face.

## Kinetic theory of opinion dynamics

The [[Kinetic theory of opinion dynamics|kinetic-theory]] approach (Toscani, Pareschi, and collaborators) treats opinion as a continuous variable and writes Boltzmann- and Fokker-Planck-type equations for the evolving *distribution* of opinions over a population, deriving steady-state opinion profiles and the conditions for consensus or clustering from binary-interaction kernels. This strand is methodologically the most directly compatible with the belief-inertia program, because it operates at the same level the program reaches for its thermodynamic limit. Where kinetic theory coarse-grains a population of opinions into a density obeying a transport equation, the program's [[Meta-entropy|meta-entropy]] construction ([[meta-entropy-manuscript]]) coarse-grains a population of *beliefs* into a configurational entropy and a continuum thermodynamic limit via Kac-normalized extensivity. The two are doing the same kind of move — from agent-level microdynamics to a population-level field theory — and the kinetic binary-interaction kernel plays the role the KL-coupling-plus-attention term plays in the VFE functional. The honest statement is that this is a methodological cousin and a natural formal partner rather than something the functional recovers: the program has not derived a Boltzmann equation for beliefs, but the kinetic framework is the obvious bridge for turning the discrete multi-agent functional into a continuum opinion-density theory, and the meta-entropy work is its closest internal analog.

## Sociodynamics and synergetics

[[Sociodynamics and synergetics|Sociodynamics]] (Weidlich, Haken's synergetics) is the older German-school program that first wrote master equations for social configurations and applied Haken's synergetic order-parameter and slaving-principle machinery to collective social transitions. It is in many ways the historical ancestor of the whole statistical-physics-of-society enterprise, and its relevance to the belief-inertia program is one of lineage and shared ambition rather than imported machinery. Synergetics' central idea — that near a transition a few slow order parameters "enslave" the many fast microscopic modes, so the collective state is governed by a low-dimensional dynamics — is conceptually the same separation of timescales the program uses (fast belief inference, slow model learning) and the same coarse-graining-to-order-parameters logic that the [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs|RG flow]] and [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence|meta-agent]] formation formalize. But the program does not use Haken's master-equation or slaving formalism directly; it reaches the analogous structure through variational free energy and geometric singular perturbation. Sociodynamics is therefore honest historical and conceptual context — the program's grandparent in spirit — not a model it recovers or a technique it deploys.

## Replicator dynamics and evolutionary games

[[Replicator dynamics|Replicator dynamics]] and [[Evolutionary game theory and cooperation|evolutionary game theory]] describe how the frequencies of competing strategies in a population evolve under differential payoff, with the replicator equation $\dot{x}_i = x_i(f_i - \bar{f})$ as the workhorse and the evolution of cooperation (reciprocity, network reciprocity, kin and group selection) as the headline application. The relation to the belief-inertia program is structural and partly formal but should not be oversold. There is a real and well-known mathematical bridge: the replicator equation is a natural-gradient / mirror-descent flow on the probability simplex under the Fisher-Shannon information metric, and the belief-inertia program runs precisely on [[Natural gradient|natural-gradient]] flow under the [[Fisher information metric|Fisher metric]] — so the *style* of dynamics is shared, both being information-geometric gradient flows. The honest qualifier is that the state spaces differ: replicator dynamics evolves a distribution *over strategies* driven by payoff, while the VFE functional evolves Gaussian *beliefs* driven by free energy, and payoff is not free energy. The functional does not recover the replicator equation as a special case in the way it recovers DeGroot or Friedkin–Johnsen; rather, the two share the information-geometric gradient-flow skeleton. The cooperation literature is then adjacent application context: the program could in principle express cooperation as low-free-energy coupled-belief configurations, but [[belief-inertia]] does not do so, and claiming an evolutionary-game-theory recovery would be a loose analogy dressed as identity.

## Cultural evolution and social learning

[[Cultural evolution and social learning|Cultural-evolution theory]] (Boyd–Richerson dual-inheritance, Cavalli-Sforza–Feldman transmission models, the social-learning-strategies literature) models how beliefs, norms, and technologies are transmitted, varied, and selected across a population over generations, with conformist and prestige-biased transmission as central mechanisms. Its relation to the belief-inertia functional is at the level of phenomenon and partial mechanism. Conformist transmission — the disproportionate copying of common variants — is the cultural-evolution name for the same precision-weighted, attention-biased averaging the overdamped VFE recovers as social influence, so the per-generation conformist update is the kind of dynamics the functional produces. The diffusion-of-innovations tradition ([[rogers-2003-diffusion-of-innovations]]) sits here too and is recovered as belief spreading through the coupling network. What the functional does not contain natively is the *selection-and-inheritance* layer that makes cultural evolution genuinely evolutionary — variation, differential fitness of cultural variants, transmission across generations — which is closer to the replicator strand above. So cultural evolution is a phenomenon the overdamped flow speaks to (transmission and conformist copying) layered on top of an evolutionary mechanism (selection, inheritance) the functional does not instantiate: a partial recovery of the transmission dynamics, adjacent on the selection dynamics.

## Mean-field games and continuum limits

[[Mean-field games and continuum limits|Mean-field game theory]] (Lasry–Lions, Huang–Caines–Malhamé) studies the limit of infinitely many strategically interacting agents, where each best-responds to the population's aggregate state and the result is a coupled forward-backward PDE system (a Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation for the representative agent's value, a Fokker-Planck equation for the population density). This strand connects to the belief-inertia program along the same axis as kinetic theory but with the strategic and *control-theoretic* content the kinetic approach lacks. The relevance is the continuum limit: mean-field games are the rigorous machinery for passing from finitely many interacting optimizers to a population field, exactly the passage the program needs to take its discrete multi-agent functional to a continuum, and the appearance of a Hamilton-Jacobi structure is suggestive given the program's Hamiltonian belief-momentum reading. The honest qualifier is that the program's agents are not strategic optimizers solving an HJB control problem; they descend a shared free energy. The closer formal partner for the program's continuum limit is the kinetic/meta-entropy route, which does not presuppose strategic optimization. Mean-field games are therefore relevant as the canonical large-population continuum formalism and as a possible bridge for a control-theoretic reading of belief momentum, but they are adjacent context rather than machinery the functional currently uses — and the program should resist the temptation to claim the HJB structure it has not derived.

## How it lands in this work

Read across the strands, the map sorts cleanly into the three categories the thesis demands. The **recovered** core — the settled, geometrically necessary results of [[belief-inertia]] — is the social-influence cluster: DeGroot averaging ([[degroot-1974-consensus]]), Friedkin–Johnsen anchoring ([[friedkin1990-social-influence-opinions|friedkin-johnsen-1990]]), Social Impact Theory ([[latane1981psychology|latane-1981-social-impact]]), bounded-confidence selective listening ([[Bounded confidence]], [[hegselmann-2002-opinion|hegselmann-krause-2002]], [[deffuant2000-bounded-confidence|deffuant-2000-bounded-confidence]]), and echo-chamber/polarization formation ([[Echo chambers and polarization]]) all emerge as overdamped gradient flow on the one functional, with the voter and majority-rule imitation models as discrete-state caricatures of the same averaging. The **extended** frontier — the novel and not-yet-empirically-validated contribution resting on the mass ansatz — is the second-order physics that has no first-order analog anywhere on the map: oscillation, overshoot, displacement resonance, and momentum transfer, whose nearest external relatives are Kuramoto synchronization and velocity-alignment flocking, offered as structural analogies rather than reductions. The **adjacent** context — genuine intellectual neighborhood, shared principles, but not machinery the functional instantiates term-for-term — is the Axelrod homophily rule, network topology, Schelling tipping, replicator and evolutionary-game dynamics (linked by the shared natural-gradient skeleton but not recovered), sociodynamics/synergetics (the program's conceptual grandparent), and the cultural-evolution selection layer. The kinetic theory of opinion dynamics and mean-field games occupy a special slot: they are not models to be recovered but the formal partners for the program's own continuum limit, the external counterparts of the internal [[Meta-entropy|meta-entropy]] thermodynamic-limit construction. Stating the categories this way is the discipline the program imposes on itself: the recovered results are claimed as mathematical limits, the extension as a falsifiable and still-open ansatz, and the rest as honest context — and the value of laying the neighborhood out in full is that it specifies exactly what a second-order, momentum-carrying theory of collective belief would have to deliver before it could claim to subsume, rather than merely sit beside, the canon of statistical social physics.

## Open questions / gaps

The sharpest gap is empirical: the underdamped predictions that distinguish the program from every recovered first-order model — oscillation, the square-root overshoot law, resonance — have not been observed, and the one existing fit (the McGuire helicopter task) lands near critical damping where the framework collapses back to first-order updating, leaving the momentum regime untested. Finding a low-noise, stable-environment paradigm where second-order belief dynamics would be visible is the program's central open problem. Several theoretical bridges named above are likewise unbuilt: the spin-glass/frustration connection to the [[Discrete spin and majority-rule models of opinion|majority-rule]] strand is blocked until the holonomy-carrying edge-local link variable $\Omega_{ij} = U_i e^{\delta_{ij}} U_j^{-1}$ replaces the current pure-gauge transport ([[Holonomy]]); a genuine *complex* contagion result (super-additive reinforcement, not just a soft threshold) has not been shown; no formal information-cascade-fragility theorem has been derived; and the continuum limit, for which kinetic theory and mean-field games are the obvious partners, has been pursued through [[Meta-entropy|meta-entropy]] but not yet connected to a Boltzmann or mean-field-game equation for beliefs. The replicator/natural-gradient correspondence is real at the level of flow geometry but has not been turned into a recovery of evolutionary-game dynamics, and the program should not overstate it. These gaps are the agenda: the recovered core is secure, and the neighborhood map exists precisely to mark where the program must still do work before its more ambitious correspondences earn the word "recovers."
