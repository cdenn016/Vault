# Information geometry and natural gradient

## The big picture

Information geometry is the differential geometry of probability distributions. Rather than treating a parametric family — say all diagonal Gaussians indexed by their means and variances — as a flat Euclidean parameter space, it equips that space with a Riemannian metric derived from the distributions themselves. That metric is the [[Fisher information metric]]: the local quadratic form measuring how distinguishable two nearby distributions are, as quantified by the curvature of the [[Renyi divergence|Kullback-Leibler divergence]] near the diagonal. Once the parameter space carries this metric, "downhill" stops meaning "opposite the raw gradient" and starts meaning "opposite the gradient corrected by the inverse metric." That corrected direction is the [[Natural gradient]], and it is the organizing object of this theme.

The reason this matters for the VFE transformer is that almost every quantity the model optimizes lives on a statistical manifold rather than a Euclidean space. Per-token beliefs are Gaussians over latent states; the training loss is a free-energy / divergence functional; the covariance is a symmetric-positive-definite matrix with its own curved geometry; and the gauge parameters act by group transformations whose natural step size is set by representation-theoretic structure, not by raw coordinates. In every one of these cases, the Euclidean gradient is the wrong direction, sometimes badly so, because the loss landscape's curvature is dominated by how the parameterization stretches and compresses the underlying distributions. The natural gradient is precisely the reparameterization-invariant fix: it gives the same update regardless of how you coordinatize the family ([[amari-1998-natural-gradient]], [[ollivier-2015-riemannian-metrics-nn]]).

The canonical reference frame here is Amari's program. [[amari-1998-natural-gradient]] proved that the steepest-descent direction on a statistical manifold is the ordinary gradient preconditioned by the inverse Fisher information matrix, and that this direction is Fisher-efficient — asymptotically optimal in the statistical sense — for online learning. [[amari-2000-methods-information-geometry]] then laid the full structural foundation: the Fisher metric, the family of dual affine connections (the e-connection and m-connection), the alpha-connections that interpolate between them, and the dually-flat geometry of exponential families in which the KL divergence becomes a canonical Bregman divergence and the Pythagorean theorem holds. This dual structure is not decorative; it is what links natural-gradient optimization to the [[Alpha-divergence|alpha-]] and [[Renyi divergence|Renyi-divergence]] objectives the model trains against.

A second, equally important thread reads the natural gradient not as exotic geometry but as practical second-order optimization. [[martens-2020-natural-gradient-insights]] shows that under standard conditions the Fisher information matrix coincides with the Generalized Gauss-Newton matrix, so the natural gradient is a curvature-aware update that substitutes the Fisher for the Hessian — with the bonus that the Fisher is positive-semidefinite by construction and pairs naturally with damping. This perspective makes the whole apparatus implementable: it tells you exactly which matrix to approximate, why approximating it is principled, and how to stabilize the result.

## Key threads

**Natural gradient as Fisher-preconditioned, invariant descent.** The foundational claim is Amari's: precondition the gradient by the inverse [[Fisher information metric]] and you get the steepest direction in the geometry that actually governs the model's distributions ([[amari-1998-natural-gradient]]). The decisive property is invariance — the update is independent of the chosen coordinates, so two parameterizations of the same belief family produce the same learning trajectory. [[ollivier-2015-riemannian-metrics-nn]] develops this for neural networks specifically, deriving a hierarchy of Fisher-based, reparameterization-invariant training metrics — full, backpropagated, unitwise, and quasi-diagonal natural gradients — that trade exactness for tractability while preserving invariance. That hierarchy is the direct ancestor of the model's block-preconditioned updates: the unitwise and quasi-diagonal variants are the principled justification for not inverting a giant dense Fisher matrix. [[khan-rue-2023-bayesian-learning-rule]] turns this geometry into the program's actual learning rule, showing that natural-gradient descent on the natural parameters of an exponential-family posterior unifies Adam, Newton, variational inference, and online learning under one update that reads off the expectation-parameter gradient — precisely the closed-form Fisher-preconditioned Gaussian belief update the VFE transformer claims to perform.

**Making it tractable: Gauss-Newton equivalence and Kronecker factorization.** The Fisher matrix for a real network is enormous, so natural gradient is only useful with structured approximations. [[martens-2020-natural-gradient-insights]] supplies the conceptual bridge by identifying the Fisher with the Generalized Gauss-Newton matrix and clarifying the role of damping, while [[martens-2015-kfac]] supplies the workhorse algorithm: K-FAC approximates each layer's Fisher block as a Kronecker product of two small factors, turning an intractable inverse into a pair of small inverses. This is the practical template for the VFE transformer's per-block GL(k) Fisher preconditioning — the #cluster/gauge-theory is block general-linear, so the Fisher naturally decomposes block-wise, and Kronecker structure is exactly what makes per-block natural-gradient M-steps affordable.

**Dual connections and the divergence family.** Beyond optimization, information geometry supplies the geometry of the loss itself. [[amari-2000-methods-information-geometry]] introduces the alpha-connections, a one-parameter family of affine connections dual under the Fisher metric, with the exponential (alpha = 1) and mixture (alpha = -1) connections at the extremes and the Levi-Civita connection at the midpoint. This is the geometric home of the model's [[Renyi divergence|Renyi]]/[[Alpha-divergence|alpha-divergence]] objective. [[zhang-2004-divergence-duality-convex]] supplies the convex-analytic underpinning: from a single convex potential it constructs the parametric alpha-divergence family, each member inducing one common Fisher-type metric together with a pair of dual alpha-connections and recovering the Bregman/canonical (KL) divergence of a dually-flat space at alpha = ±1 — the reason the program's Renyi/alpha-divergence loss sits inside one dually-flat geometry with KL as the special case. [[vanerven-2014-renyi-kl]] is the definitive survey of the order-alpha Renyi divergence family — establishing its convexity, monotonicity in alpha, data-processing inequality, and Pythagorean-style geometry — and shows that ordinary KL is exactly the alpha to 1 limit, which is why the model can declare a `renyi` divergence family and recover KL as a special case for free. [[li-turner-2016-renyi-vi]] closes the loop on the variational side: the variational Renyi bound is a one-parameter family of objectives that smoothly interpolates from the standard [[Evidence lower bound (ELBO)|ELBO]] (alpha to 1) toward the log marginal likelihood, giving a tunable knob between mass-covering and mode-seeking inference. Together these three sources mean the model's training loss is not an ad hoc choice but a point on a well-understood geometric continuum.

**Connection to the variational machinery.** The natural gradient is the metric that the [[Variational free energy|free-energy]] objective wants. Because the per-token beliefs are exponential-family Gaussians, the Fisher metric on belief parameters is available in closed form, and natural-gradient belief updates can be read as the precision-weighted relaxation derived in the free-energy literature ([[bogacz-2017-free-energy-tutorial]] derives that relaxation with *ordinary* gradient descent; identifying it with the **natural** gradient is this program's synthesis, not a result stated in the cited paper). This is the quiet bridge to the [[Variational free energy and predictive coding]] theme: what predictive coding writes as precision-weighted [[Prediction error|prediction-error]] dynamics, information geometry can write as natural-gradient descent on the [[Fisher information metric]]. For Gaussian beliefs the two descriptions can be identified — an editorial reading the program adopts (see the callout below). The same Fisher-information thread runs deeper into physics: the maximum-entropy foundation of statistical mechanics ([[jaynes-1957-information-statistical-mechanics]]) is the information-theoretic root of the exponential-family geometry, Fisher-information variational principles recover physical dynamics including the Schrodinger equation ([[reginatto-1998-fisher-quantum]], see [[Physics from Fisher information]]), and the uniqueness of the Fisher metric extends to the noncommutative setting through Petz's classification of monotone metrics on quantum states ([[petz-1996-monotone-metrics]], see [[Quantum information geometry]]).

## How it lands in this work

In the VFE transformer the information-geometric ingredients are load-bearing rather than ornamental. The M-step that updates parameters is a natural-gradient step: the raw gradient of the free energy is preconditioned by a Fisher metric, following [[amari-1998-natural-gradient]], and made invariant to the model's many redundant parameterizations following [[ollivier-2015-riemannian-metrics-nn]]. Because the parameters carry the block-general-linear GL(k) #cluster/gauge-theory, the Fisher is preconditioned per block — a [[Killing form|Killing-form]]-weighted, block-diagonal metric — and the practical realization of "invert a block-structured Fisher cheaply" is exactly the Kronecker-factored approximation of [[martens-2015-kfac]], with the Gauss-Newton reading of [[martens-2020-natural-gradient-insights]] justifying the damping that keeps the inverse well-conditioned.

The divergence side lands just as directly. The configured `renyi` divergence family is the variational Renyi bound of [[li-turner-2016-renyi-vi]], a tunable alpha-objective whose alpha to 1 limit is the ordinary [[Evidence lower bound (ELBO)|ELBO]]/KL training loss ([[vanerven-2014-renyi-kl]]), all sitting inside the dual-affine, alpha-connection geometry catalogued by [[amari-2000-methods-information-geometry]]. The same monograph's dually-flat exponential-family picture is what makes the closed-form Gaussian Fisher metric and the natural-gradient belief updates mutually consistent.

> [!note] Editorial: The cleanest way to read the architecture is that information geometry is the connective tissue between its clusters. The Fisher metric on Gaussian beliefs is the same object as the SPD affine-invariant metric restricted to the covariance block (see [[SPD-manifold geometry and Riemannian optimization]]); the Killing-form per-block preconditioner is the gauge cluster's representation-theoretic refinement of the same Fisher metric (see [[Gauge equivariance and geometric deep learning]]); and the precision-weighted E-step is the natural gradient of the free energy on belief space (see [[Variational free energy and predictive coding]]). The sources below establish each piece; the model's contribution is to insist they are one metric viewed through different coordinates.

## Open questions / gaps

Several gaps separate the clean theory from the model's actual implementation. First, the foundational results assume the Fisher metric of a fixed family, but the gauge structure changes the family across blocks and the covariance block lives on a curved SPD manifold; whether a single coherent natural-gradient step over the *joint* (belief, gauge, covariance) manifold inherits the invariance and efficiency guarantees of [[amari-1998-natural-gradient]] is not addressed by any source here. Second, the [[martens-2015-kfac]] Kronecker factorization was derived for ordinary feedforward layers; its validity for GL(k)-structured, Killing-form-weighted blocks is plausible but unproven in the digest. Third, the alpha in the [[li-turner-2016-renyi-vi]] Renyi bound trades bias for variance in a way that interacts with the natural-gradient preconditioner, and none of the sources characterizes that interaction — i.e., how the effective metric shifts as alpha moves away from 1. Fourth, [[martens-2020-natural-gradient-insights]]'s Fisher-equals-Gauss-Newton identity holds under specific loss/output-distribution matching conditions; whether those conditions survive the precision-weighted, Renyi-divergence loss is an open verification task. These are exactly the points where the model's empirical runs would have to substitute for theory.
