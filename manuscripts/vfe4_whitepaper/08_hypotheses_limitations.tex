\chapter{Falsifiable Hypotheses and Experimental Program}

This chapter states tests that follow from the completed construction, separating identity checks, posterior-family comparisons, optimization checks, predictive evaluation, and gauge-covariance tests. These are empirical hypotheses and promotion criteria, not conclusions derived by the preceding sections.

\chapter{Limitations and Research Outlook}

This chapter identifies the limits of the initial exact regime and the questions that require later derivations or experiments. It keeps approximation layers, noncompact gauge-volume issues, and scaling claims outside the established ELBO identity until their assumptions are made explicit.
