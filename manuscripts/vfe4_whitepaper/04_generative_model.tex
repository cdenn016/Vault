\chapter{A Fixed Normalized Generative Model}

This chapter specifies the normalized causal joint after the observation semantics and geometric objects have been fixed. Its transition, source-prior, and categorical emission factors establish the model against which the later structured recognition law and ELBO are defined.

\section{Causal sources and reference measures}

Let the vocabulary be the finite measurable space
\begin{equation}
\mathcal X:=\{1,\ldots,V\},
\label{eq:vocabulary-space}
\end{equation}
equipped with counting measure.  For every $t\in\{1,\ldots,T\}$, choose nonempty causal parent sets
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\varnothing\ne\operatorname{Pa}_z(t)
&\subseteq\{j\in V_T:(j,t)\in E_T\}
\subseteq\{0,\ldots,t-1\},
\\
\varnothing\ne\operatorname{Pa}_m(t)
&\subseteq\{j\in V_T:(j,t)\in E_T\}
\subseteq\{0,\ldots,t-1\}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:nonempty-causal-parent-sets}
\end{equation}
The last inclusions follow from \cref{eq:causal-token-graph}.  Hence every selected source is a permitted edge predecessor and has the typed state and model transports declared in \cref{eq:typed-associated-transports}.  The categorical variables $a_t\in\operatorname{Pa}_z(t)$ and $b_t\in\operatorname{Pa}_m(t)$ select the state and model source, respectively.  Their fixed priors are probability mass functions satisfying
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\pi_t^z(j)&\geq0,
&\sum_{j\in\operatorname{Pa}_z(t)}\pi_t^z(j)&=1,
&\pi_t^z(j)&=0\quad\text{for }j\notin\operatorname{Pa}_z(t),\\
\pi_t^m(j)&\geq0,
&\sum_{j\in\operatorname{Pa}_m(t)}\pi_t^m(j)&=1,
&\pi_t^m(j)&=0\quad\text{for }j\notin\operatorname{Pa}_m(t).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:normalized-causal-source-priors}
\end{equation}
These priors are model quantities and do not depend on a later recognition law. In the exact core they are also fixed with respect to the realized latent content, so the generative source variables define normalized mixture ancestry rather than content-dependent transformer attention. A future causal extension could replace them by target-blind normalized laws conditioned only on $x_{<t}$, earlier generated latents, and $\Gamma$. Such a replacement would change the generative joint and would require a new normalization and ELBO audit; it is not an identity of the present model.

Each finite-dimensional fiber carries its Borel $\sigma$-algebra.  A choice of local frame identifies $E_{z,t}$ and $E_{m,t}$ with Euclidean coordinate spaces and supplies Lebesgue reference measures $\lambda_{z,t}$ and $\lambda_{m,t}$.  The initial density is jointly normalized with respect to the receiver-fiber product measure,
\begin{equation}
\int_{E_{z,0}\times E_{m,0}}
p_{\theta,0}(z_0,m_0\given\Gamma)
\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{z,0}(z_0)}
\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{m,0}(m_0)}
=1.
\label{eq:normalized-initial-law}
\end{equation}
For fixed deterministic $\Gamma$, the transition densities are Borel Markov kernels in all displayed conditioning arguments and obey
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\int_{E_{m,t}}K^m_{\theta,tj}
(m_t\given m_j,x_{<t},\Gamma)
\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{m,t}(m_t)}&=1,\\
\int_{E_{z,t}}K^z_{\theta,tj}
(z_t\given z_j,m_t,x_{<t},\Gamma)
\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{z,t}(z_t)}&=1,
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:normalized-transition-kernels}
\end{equation}
for every permitted parent and every conditioning value.  The emission is a measurable categorical kernel satisfying
\begin{equation}
L_{\theta,t}(v\given z_t,m_t,\Gamma)\geq0,
\qquad
\sum_{v\in\mathcal X}L_{\theta,t}(v\given z_t,m_t,\Gamma)=1.
\label{eq:normalized-emission-kernel}
\end{equation}
All history-dependent coefficient maps used below are Borel measurable in $x_{<t}$ and $\Gamma$.  No transition coefficient depends on the current target $x_t$.

\section{The authoritative conditional joint}

Retain the complete-path notation $\boldsymbol z=(z_t)_{t=0}^{T}$ and $\boldsymbol m=(m_t)_{t=0}^{T}$ from \cref{eq:random-vertex-sections}, and write $x=x_{1:T}$, $a=a_{1:T}$, and $b=b_{1:T}$.  With $\Gamma$ held fixed as deterministic geometric data, the generative density is defined once by
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
p_\theta(x,\boldsymbol z,\boldsymbol m,a,b\given\Gamma)
={}&p_{\theta,0}(z_0,m_0\given\Gamma)\\
&\times\prod_{t=1}^{T}\pi_t^m(b_t)
K^m_{\theta,tb_t}(m_t\given m_{b_t},x_{<t},\Gamma)\\
&\times\pi_t^z(a_t)
K^z_{\theta,ta_t}(z_t\given z_{a_t},m_t,x_{<t},\Gamma)\\
&\times L_{\theta,t}(x_t\given z_t,m_t,\Gamma).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:vfe4-generative-joint}
\end{equation}
The order inside each time slice is generative: the model state is drawn before the state kernel consumes $m_t$, and the observation is drawn after both continuous latents.  Dependence on $x_{<t}$ is causal history dependence, not conditioning on the target.  Equation~\eqref{eq:vfe4-generative-joint} contains no variationally moving factor.

\section{Receiver-fiber Gaussian transitions}

A minimal non-neural realization uses offsets $c_{\theta,t}^m\in E_{m,t}$ and $c_{\theta,t}^z\in E_{z,t}$ and receiver covariances
\begin{equation}
R_{\theta,t}^m\in\operatorname{Sym}_+^2(E_{m,t}),
\qquad
R_{\theta,t}^z\in\operatorname{Sym}_+^2(E_{z,t}),
\label{eq:receiver-spd-transition-covariances}
\end{equation}
which may be measurable functions of $(x_{<t},\Gamma)$ but are symmetric positive definite for every history.  Their conditional locations are
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mu^m_{\theta,tj}
&:=\Omega^m_{tj}m_j+c_{\theta,t}^m
\in E_{m,t},\\
\mu^z_{\theta,tj}
&:=\Omega^z_{tj}z_j+B_tm_t+c_{\theta,t}^z
\in E_{z,t}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:typed-gaussian-transition-locations}
\end{equation}
The state location is well typed because $\Omega^z_{tj}z_j$, $B_tm_t$, and $c_{\theta,t}^z$ all lie in the receiver fiber $E_{z,t}$.  In local coordinates, the normalized densities are
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
K^m_{\theta,tj}
&=\frac{\exp\left[-\tfrac12
(m_t-\mu^m_{\theta,tj})^\top
(R_{\theta,t}^m)^{-1}
(m_t-\mu^m_{\theta,tj})\right]}
{(2\pi)^{d_m/2}\det(R_{\theta,t}^m)^{1/2}},\\
K^z_{\theta,tj}
&=\frac{\exp\left[-\tfrac12
(z_t-\mu^z_{\theta,tj})^\top
(R_{\theta,t}^z)^{-1}
(z_t-\mu^z_{\theta,tj})\right]}
{(2\pi)^{d_z/2}\det(R_{\theta,t}^z)^{1/2}}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:normalized-gaussian-transition-densities}
\end{equation}
Here the conditioning arguments are those in \cref{eq:normalized-transition-kernels} and are suppressed only to keep the density formula legible.  Positive definiteness gives finite positive determinants and makes both kernels normalized with respect to their receiver measures.

\section{Gauge covariance of transition measures}

For a local frame change, abbreviate
\begin{equation}
\mathsf G_{z,t}:=\rho_z(g_t),
\qquad
\mathsf G_{m,t}:=\rho_m(g_t).
\label{eq:generative-coordinate-change-matrices}
\end{equation}
Together with the link and morphism laws in \cref{eq:link-morphism-gauge-laws}, the offsets and receiver covariances transform as
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
c_{\theta,t}^{m\prime}&=\mathsf G_{m,t}c_{\theta,t}^m,
&R_{\theta,t}^{m\prime}
&=\mathsf G_{m,t}R_{\theta,t}^m\mathsf G_{m,t}^{\top},\\
c_{\theta,t}^{z\prime}&=\mathsf G_{z,t}c_{\theta,t}^z,
&R_{\theta,t}^{z\prime}
&=\mathsf G_{z,t}R_{\theta,t}^z\mathsf G_{z,t}^{\top}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:transition-offset-covariance-gauge-laws}
\end{equation}
Thus $\mu_{\theta,tj}^{m\prime}=\mathsf G_{m,t}\mu_{\theta,tj}^m$ and $\mu_{\theta,tj}^{z\prime}=\mathsf G_{z,t}\mu_{\theta,tj}^z$.  Covariance congruence alone does not make a coordinate density invariant.  The determinant and receiver reference measure change according to
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\log\det R_{\theta,t}^{m\prime}
&=\log\det R_{\theta,t}^m+2\log|\det\mathsf G_{m,t}|,
&\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{m,t}'(m_t')}
&=|\det\mathsf G_{m,t}|\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{m,t}(m_t)},\\
\log\det R_{\theta,t}^{z\prime}
&=\log\det R_{\theta,t}^z+2\log|\det\mathsf G_{z,t}|,
&\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{z,t}'(z_t')}
&=|\det\mathsf G_{z,t}|\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{z,t}(z_t)}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:receiver-logdet-measure-jacobians}
\end{equation}
The quadratic forms in \cref{eq:normalized-gaussian-transition-densities} are unchanged.  The normalized receiver densities consequently obey
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\log K_{\theta,tj}^{m\prime}
&=\log K_{\theta,tj}^m-\log|\det\mathsf G_{m,t}|,
&K_{\theta,tj}^{m\prime}\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{m,t}' }
&=K_{\theta,tj}^m\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{m,t}},\\
\log K_{\theta,tj}^{z\prime}
&=\log K_{\theta,tj}^z-\log|\det\mathsf G_{z,t}|,
&K_{\theta,tj}^{z\prime}\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{z,t}' }
&=K_{\theta,tj}^z\mathop{\mathrm d\lambda_{z,t}}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:receiver-density-kernel-measure-invariance}
\end{equation}
The arguments on the right are the unprimed coordinates corresponding to the primed arguments on the left.  The initial density must be pushed forward in the same way,
\begin{equation}
p_{\theta,0}'(z_0',m_0'\given\Gamma')
=\frac{p_{\theta,0}(z_0,m_0\given\Gamma)}
{|\det\mathsf G_{z,0}||\det\mathsf G_{m,0}|}.
\label{eq:initial-density-gauge-jacobian}
\end{equation}
Equations~\eqref{eq:receiver-density-kernel-measure-invariance} and \eqref{eq:initial-density-gauge-jacobian} state invariance of probability measures.  Raw coordinate densities acquire Jacobian terms and are not invariant scalars.

\section{Normalized categorical observation law}

Let $W_t^z:E_{z,t}\to\mathbb R^V$ and $W_t^m:E_{m,t}\to\mathbb R^V$ be dual readout maps, and let $d_t\in\mathbb R^V$ be a deterministic vocabulary offset.  Define the vocabulary logits and emission by
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\ell_t(z_t,m_t,\Gamma)
&:=W_t^zz_t+W_t^mm_t+d_t,\\
L_{\theta,t}(x_t=v\given z_t,m_t,\Gamma)
&:=\frac{\exp(\ell_{t,v})}
{\sum_{u\in\mathcal X}\exp(\ell_{t,u})}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:gauge-compatible-categorical-emission}
\end{equation}
The finite denominator is positive, so this is a normalized probability mass function.  The dual laws
\begin{equation}
W_t^{z\prime}=W_t^z\rho_z(g_t)^{-1},
\qquad
W_t^{m\prime}=W_t^m\rho_m(g_t)^{-1},
\qquad
d_t'=d_t,
\label{eq:dual-decoder-gauge-laws}
\end{equation}
make every logit, and hence every categorical probability, invariant under simultaneous frame and parameter transformation.

If the decoder is instead held fixed while the latent coordinates transform, the surviving local gauge group is exactly its stabilizer.  For the full local gauge group $\mathcal G_T:=G^{T+1}$, this subgroup is
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Stab}_{\mathcal G_T}(W^z,W^m)
:=\bigl\{(g_0,\ldots,g_T)\in\mathcal G_T:
&W_t^z\rho_z(g_t)^{-1}=W_t^z,\\
&W_t^m\rho_m(g_t)^{-1}=W_t^m
\text{ for every }t=1,\ldots,T\bigr\}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:fixed-decoder-stabilizer}
\end{equation}
No larger subgroup leaves the two fixed linear readouts unchanged.  The offset $d_t$ adds no further condition because it does not act on either latent fiber.

For any later normalized law $Q$ under which the displayed expectation is finite, the negative observation contribution is
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal C_{\mathrm{obs}}(Q;x,\Gamma)
&:=-\mathbb E_Q\left[
\sum_{t=1}^T\log L_{\theta,t}(x_t\given z_t,m_t,\Gamma)
\right]\\
&=\mathbb E_Q\left[
\sum_{t=1}^T
\left\{-\ell_{t,x_t}+\log\sum_{u\in\mathcal X}\exp(\ell_{t,u})\right\}
\right].
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:expected-categorical-cross-entropy}
\end{equation}
This is expected categorical cross-entropy and only the observation contribution.  It neither defines $Q$ nor includes transition, source, initial-law, or entropy contributions.

\section{Normalization and the latent-geometry boundary}

\paragraph{Proposition (normalization of the fixed causal joint).}
Suppose \cref{eq:nonempty-causal-parent-sets,eq:normalized-causal-source-priors,eq:normalized-initial-law,eq:normalized-transition-kernels,eq:normalized-emission-kernel} hold, and suppose all factors are nonnegative and jointly measurable with respect to the stated Borel, Lebesgue, and counting measures.  Then, for every fixed deterministic $\Gamma$, the density in \cref{eq:vfe4-generative-joint} integrates and sums to one.

\emph{Proof sketch.}
Nonnegativity permits Tonelli's theorem.  Starting at $t=T$, first sum over $x_T$, then integrate over $z_T$, sum over $a_T$, integrate over $m_T$, and sum over $b_T$.  Each operation replaces its normalized conditional factor or probability mass function by one while holding its causal history fixed.  Repeat this terminal-block elimination for $t=T-1,\ldots,1$.  The remaining integral is the normalized initial law in \cref{eq:normalized-initial-law}.  History dependence causes no extra term because every coefficient at time $t$ is measurable in already-fixed $x_{<t}$ and no earlier factor depends on a later variable.  The complete measure-theoretic argument appears in the appendix.  \hfill$\square$

The exact core conditions on $\Gamma$ and assigns it no random law.  Promoting the geometric data to latent variables would require a declared measurable geometry space with reference measure $\nu_\Gamma$ and a proper density satisfying
\begin{equation}
\int p_\theta(\Gamma)\mathop{\mathrm d\nu_\Gamma(\Gamma)}=1.
\label{eq:proper-latent-geometry-prior}
\end{equation}
A later variational law for that latent geometry must also contribute its own finite entropy, computed against the same declared reference measure, rather than being absorbed into a state or source entropy.  Because noncompact $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ has no uniform Haar probability measure, a proper prior written directly on noncompact frame coordinates can make the integral finite but normally selects frames and thereby gauge-fixes or breaks the redundancy. Preserving the redundancy requires a proper law on a quotient or a declared gauge-fixing map with its induced Jacobian. None of these extension choices is part of \cref{eq:vfe4-generative-joint}.
