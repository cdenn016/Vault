\markboth{Executive Summary}{}

This paper develops a reproducible theoretical shell for gauge-covariant multi-agent variational inference. Its organizing distinction is between agents, which are local section-bearing objects over a contextual base, and finite-population latent samples, which are random variables evaluated at a declared context or finite design. The chapters introduce a normalized state model, a correlated recognition law, restricted mean-field families, and an optional configuration tier as separately typed constructions. The shell records these proof roles before presenting derivations or claims about executable behavior.

\chapter{Scope, Contribution, and Claim Status}

The scope is a standalone MAgent theory document rather than an extension of the VFE 4.0 manuscript. It uses a construction-before-inference order: geometric types determine the finite probability space, a fixed generative law precedes recognition, and each later variational statement is tied to its stated assumptions. The manuscript will classify definitions, identities, restricted approximations, executable observations, and research proposals separately so that the status of each claim remains visible.
