\chapter{Correlated Population Recognition and the Exact State ELBO}

This chapter treats a normalized correlated kernel \(Q_X(dY\mid o)\) on the complete finite population fiber as the primary recognition object. Local state and model beliefs are recovered as marginals and therefore do not by themselves determine cross-agent dependence. Given the stated support, measurability, absolute-continuity, and integrability conditions, the chapter will relate this recognition law to the fixed joint through the state evidence identity. The role of the construction is to preserve the distinction between a population law and its marginal summaries.
