\chapter{Configuration Thermodynamics and the Nested ELBO}

This chapter introduces a separate probabilistic tier in which structural data \(X\) may be random under a proper configuration prior and configuration posterior. The state recognition law is then a measurable kernel indexed by \(X\), while configuration entropy and state entropy remain defined on their different sample spaces. A positive \(T_{\mathrm{cfg}}\) will scale the complete nested identity rather than selectively tempering one tier. The chapter therefore states the assumptions needed before a configuration-level construction is compared with the conditional state model.
