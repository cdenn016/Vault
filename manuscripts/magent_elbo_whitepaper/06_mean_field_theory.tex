\chapter{Explicit Mean-Field Theory}

This chapter defines mean field as a restriction of the correlated recognition family, not as a replacement ontology or a different generative model. It introduces agent-block and fully factorized product families, with the declared partition determining which dependencies are removed. Functional coordinate equations and Gaussian information-form controls will be derived only from the already fixed joint. The chapter consequently frames any factorwise update as a statement about a specified restricted family and complete Markov blanket.
