\chapter{The PIFB2 Functional: Exact Sectors, Obstruction, and Exact Lifts}
\label{ch:pifb2-crosswalk}

This chapter locates the legacy PIFB2 population functional relative to the fixed-joint theory developed in Chapters~\ref{ch:generative-model}--\ref{ch:configuration-elbo}. The comparison is classificatory. A term can be an exact KL divergence, a normalized likelihood contribution, or the value of an exact simplex minimization without making the complete population scalar the negative ELBO of the fixed state model in Chapter~\ref{ch:generative-model}. Conversely, failure of that state-level identification does not invalidate the scalar as an engineered consensus objective or prevent an exact model on an enlarged sample space. Standard relative-entropy and mean-field claims below use the fixed-model convention of \citet{Kullback1951}, \citet{CoverThomas2006}, and \citet{Wainwright2008}; the PIFB2 source is used only to identify the project's formulas and declared executable profile.

\section{The canonical entropy-retaining population scalar}

Let \((\mathcal C,\mathscr C,\mu_{\mathcal C})\) be the contextual base equipped with a fixed sigma-finite Borel measure. All fields in this section are assumed measurable, and every displayed integral is taken with respect to \(d\mu_{\mathcal C}(c)\). The state sections \(q_i(c)\) and \(p_i(c)\) are probability laws on the state fiber, while \(s_i(c)\) and \(r_i(c)\) are probability laws on the model fiber. The represented state transport is \((\Omega_{ij})_{\#}\), and the model transport \((\widetilde\Omega_{ij})_{\#}\) is separately typed; it may be induced by a different representation or by an independent model-frame bundle. Let \(\zeta_i(dk_i,dm_i\mid c)\) be the declared joint state--model recognition marginal, with coordinate marginals \(q_i(c)\) and \(s_i(c)\). The fully factorized specialization is \(\zeta_i(c)=q_i(c)\otimes s_i(c)\). If the legacy observation model instead treats \(m_i\) as a fixed value \(\bar m_i(c)\), the corresponding specialization is \(\zeta_i(c)=q_i(c)\otimes\delta_{\bar m_i(c)}\). The observation factor \(p(o(c)\mid k_i,m_i)\) is a fixed normalized kernel whenever it is assigned state-model-factor status below; no model-fiber variable is left outside the expectation.

Write \(\chi_i:\mathcal C\to[0,1]\) for the agent-presence field and \(\chi_{ij}=\chi_i\chi_j\) for pair presence. Let \(\pi_{ij}\) and \(\pi_{ij}^{(s)}\) be nonnegative source-prior rows. First define the row normalizers
\begin{align}
Z_i^{\chi}(c)
&=\sum_k\chi_{ik}(c)\pi_{ik}(c),
&
Z_i^{\chi,s}(c)
&=\sum_k\chi_{ik}(c)\pi_{ik}^{(s)}(c).
\label{eq:pifb2-row-normalizers}
\end{align}
Define the active receiver domains
\begin{equation}
\mathcal C_i^{\beta}
:=\{c\in\mathcal C:Z_i^{\chi}(c)>0\},
\qquad
\mathcal C_i^{\gamma}
:=\{c\in\mathcal C:Z_i^{\chi,s}(c)>0\}.
\label{eq:pifb2-active-receiver-domains}
\end{equation}
The absorbed priors are then defined on their respective domains by
\begin{align}
\widetilde\pi_{ij}(c)
&=\frac{\chi_{ij}(c)\pi_{ij}(c)}{Z_i^{\chi}(c)},
\quad c\in\mathcal C_i^{\beta},
\label{eq:pifb2-absorbed-state-prior}\\
\widetilde\pi_{ij}^{(s)}(c)
&=\frac{\chi_{ij}(c)\pi_{ij}^{(s)}(c)}{Z_i^{\chi,s}(c)},
\quad c\in\mathcal C_i^{\gamma}.
\label{eq:pifb2-absorbed-model-prior}
\end{align}
On these domains, the corresponding absorbed prior is normalized. A row for which every pair-presence weight vanishes lies outside its active receiver domain and is omitted rather than assigned an undefined \(0/0\) prior. Equivalently, each retained state row has a nonempty active set \(\mathcal J_i(c)=\{j:\chi_{ij}(c)\pi_{ij}(c)>0\}\), with the analogous model set. The variational rows obey
\begin{equation}
\beta_{ij}(c)\geq0,
\qquad
\sum_j\beta_{ij}(c)=1
\quad(c\in\mathcal C_i^{\beta}),
\qquad
\gamma_{ij}(c)\geq0,
\qquad
\sum_j\gamma_{ij}(c)=1
\quad(c\in\mathcal C_i^{\gamma}),
\label{eq:pifb2-row-simplexes}
\end{equation}
and are absolutely continuous with respect to their absorbed priors: a row weight must vanish wherever the corresponding \(\widetilde\pi\) vanishes. As usual, \(0\log(0/a)=0\) for \(a\geq0\), while a positive row weight over a zero prior has infinite cost.

With these types and conventions, the canonical entropy-retaining scalar recorded by PIFB2 is
\begin{align}
\mathcal F_{\mathrm{PIFB2}}
={}&\sum_i\int_{\mathcal C}
\chi_i(c)\KL\bigl(q_i(c)\Vert p_i(c)\bigr)d\mu_{\mathcal C}(c)
\label{eq:pifb2-canonical-functional}\\
&+\lambda_h\sum_i\int_{\mathcal C}
\chi_i(c)\KL\bigl(s_i(c)\Vert r_i(c)\bigr)d\mu_{\mathcal C}(c)\notag\\
&+\sum_{ij}\int_{\mathcal C_i^{\beta}}\left[
\beta_{ij}(c)\KL\bigl(q_i(c)\Vert(\Omega_{ij}(c))_{\#}q_j(c)\bigr)
+\tau\beta_{ij}(c)\log\frac{\beta_{ij}(c)}{\widetilde\pi_{ij}(c)}
\right]d\mu_{\mathcal C}(c)\notag\\
&+\sum_{ij}\int_{\mathcal C_i^{\gamma}}\left[
\gamma_{ij}(c)\KL\bigl(s_i(c)\Vert(\widetilde\Omega_{ij}(c))_{\#}s_j(c)\bigr)
+\tau\gamma_{ij}(c)\log
\frac{\gamma_{ij}(c)}{\widetilde\pi_{ij}^{(s)}(c)}
\right]d\mu_{\mathcal C}(c)\notag\\
&-\sum_i\int_{\mathcal C}\chi_i(c)
\E_{\zeta_i(c)}\log p\bigl(o(c)\mid k_i,m_i\bigr)d\mu_{\mathcal C}(c).
\notag
\end{align}
The displayed state self-coupling has unit weight. The model self or hyperprior term has the explicit coefficient \(\lambda_h\), with the unit profile obtained at \(\lambda_h=1\). PIFB2 later replaces the state coefficient by an adaptive scalar \(\alpha_i\) and adds the regularizer \(b_0\alpha_i-c_0\log\alpha_i\). That later precision envelope is a separate extension and is not silently included in Equation~\eqref{eq:pifb2-canonical-functional}. Optional frame-smoothness, Fisher-mass, and plaquette penalties are also additional regularizers rather than omitted terms of the displayed scalar.

Equation~\eqref{eq:pifb2-canonical-functional} is the legacy population consensus scalar. It is not the state ELBO \(\Lstate\) of Equation~\eqref{eq:state-elbo-definition}, nor is its negative identified with that ELBO. The state ELBO is defined from one fixed normalized posterior-independent joint on the finite design. Equation~\eqref{eq:pifb2-canonical-functional} instead contains transported live recognition marginals in the denominators of its within-scale peer KL terms and is written as a continuum integral without asserting a probability measure on the full section space.

\section{The scoped moving-peer obstruction}

The obstruction below concerns equality of functionals on an open product family. It does not assert that a live peer KL is ill-defined, noncovariant, or unusable as an optimization term.

\paragraph{Theorem (open-family moving-peer obstruction).}
\label{thm:magent-moving-peer-obstruction}
For each agent \(a\), let \((\mathsf Y_a,\mathscr Y_a,\nu_a)\) be a sigma-finite measure space, and let \(\mathcal Q_a\) be a mixture-open family of normalized densities. At the density \(q_a>0\), mixture openness means that every tangent \(h_a\) used below satisfies
\begin{equation}
\int h_a d\nu_a=0,
\qquad
\frac{h_a}{q_a}\in L^\infty(q_a),
\qquad
q_a+t h_a\in\mathcal Q_a
\quad\text{for all sufficiently small \(|t|\)}.
\label{eq:moving-peer-mixture-openness}
\end{equation}
Let the factors vary independently in the open product family \(Q_q=\bigotimes_aq_a\). Assume a fixed posterior-independent joint density \(p_\theta(o,y)\) has support containing every such \(Q_q\), and assume its logarithm and all differentiated expressions below are integrable so that differentiation under the integral is valid. Then, for distinct \(i\) and \(j\),
\begin{equation}
D^3_{q_iq_jq_j}\mathcal F_{\text{fixed joint}}
[a_i,h_j,h_j]=0.
\label{eq:fixed-joint-mixed-third-zero}
\end{equation}

Let \(T_{ij}:L^1(\nu_j)\to L^1(\nu_i)\) be fixed, positive, linear, and mass preserving. Fix \(\beta_{ij}>0\). Assume \(q_i\ll T_{ij}q_j\), choose a tangent \(h_j\) satisfying Equation~\eqref{eq:moving-peer-mixture-openness}, and require \(T_{ij}q_j>0\) on the relevant \(q_i\)-support. Positivity and \(|h_j|\leq\lVert h_j/q_j\rVert_\infty q_j\) imply
\begin{equation}
|T_{ij}h_j|
\leq\left\lVert\frac{h_j}{q_j}\right\rVert_\infty T_{ij}q_j,
\label{eq:moving-peer-transported-tangent-bound}
\end{equation}
so \(T_{ij}(q_j+t h_j)>0\) there for all sufficiently small \(|t|\). Suppose
\begin{equation}
g_{ij}
:=\left(\frac{T_{ij}h_j}{T_{ij}q_j}\right)^2
\label{eq:moving-peer-g-definition}
\end{equation}
has a bounded measurable version and is nonconstant \(q_i\)-almost everywhere. Assume the nearby KL divergences are finite and that the boundedness and support conditions dominate the first three directional derivatives. Then
\begin{equation}
D^3_{q_iq_jq_j}
\left[\beta_{ij}\KL(q_i\Vert T_{ij}q_j)\right]
[a_i,h_j,h_j]
=\beta_{ij}\int a_i g_{ij}d\nu_i.
\label{eq:moving-peer-mixed-third}
\end{equation}
For
\begin{equation}
a_i=q_i\left(g_{ij}-\E_{q_i}g_{ij}\right),
\label{eq:moving-peer-receiver-tangent}
\end{equation}
this variation equals
\begin{equation}
\beta_{ij}\operatorname{Var}_{q_i}(g_{ij})>0.
\label{eq:moving-peer-positive-variance}
\end{equation}
Consequently, a scalar containing this active edge and otherwise having zero \((q_i,q_j,q_j)\) third variation cannot equal the negative ELBO of one fixed posterior-independent joint plus a constant throughout that open product neighborhood.

\paragraph{Proof.}
For a fixed joint, the product-family negative ELBO has the form
\begin{equation}
\mathcal F_{\text{fixed joint}}(q)
=\sum_a\int q_a\log q_a d\nu_a
-\int\left(\prod_aq_a\right)\log p_\theta(o,y)d\nu_{1:N}.
\label{eq:fixed-joint-product-structure}
\end{equation}
The entropy summands are factor-separable. The energy expectation is affine in each factor with the remaining factors held fixed. A second \(q_j\) derivative of the energy therefore vanishes, while the second derivative of the \(q_j\) entropy is independent of \(q_i\). Taking the \(q_i\) derivative proves Equation~\eqref{eq:fixed-joint-mixed-third-zero} \citep{Wainwright2008}.

Set \(r=T_{ij}q_j\) and \(u=T_{ij}h_j\). Along \(q_i(s)=q_i+s a_i\) and \(q_j(t)=q_j+t h_j\), only the cross-entropy part of the KL contributes to the mixed derivative:
\begin{align}
\left.
\frac{\partial^3}{\partial s\partial t^2}
\beta_{ij}\KL(q_i(s)\Vert T_{ij}q_j(t))
\right|_{s=t=0}
&=\left.
\frac{\partial}{\partial s}
\beta_{ij}\int(q_i+s a_i)
\frac{u^2}{(r+t u)^2}d\nu_i
\right|_{s=t=0}\notag\\
&=\beta_{ij}\int a_i\frac{u^2}{r^2}d\nu_i
=\beta_{ij}\int a_i g_{ij}d\nu_i.
\label{eq:moving-peer-derivative-calculation}
\end{align}
The sign is positive because the second sender derivative of \(-\log(r+t u)\) is \(u^2/(r+t u)^2\).

Equation~\eqref{eq:moving-peer-receiver-tangent} has zero mass:
\begin{equation}
\int a_i d\nu_i
=\E_{q_i}g_{ij}-\E_{q_i}g_{ij}=0.
\label{eq:moving-peer-tangent-zero-mass}
\end{equation}
Since \(g_{ij}\) is bounded, \(a_i/q_i=g_{ij}-\E_{q_i}g_{ij}\) is bounded. If this centered function is not identically zero, then
\begin{equation}
q_i+s a_i
=q_i\left[1+s\left(g_{ij}-\E_{q_i}g_{ij}\right)\right]
\label{eq:moving-peer-admissible-path}
\end{equation}
is nonnegative and normalized for every sufficiently small \(|s|\), and mixture openness places it in \(\mathcal Q_i\). Substitution into Equation~\eqref{eq:moving-peer-mixed-third} gives Equation~\eqref{eq:moving-peer-positive-variance}; nonconstancy makes the variance strict. Appendix~\ref{sec:moving-peer-calculus-oracle} records the typed derivative and a finite discrete directional-difference oracle. \(\square\)

For the unreduced entropy-retaining functional, the theorem is applied on a slice where the row variables \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) are held fixed. Their categorical relative entropies, the self terms, and fixed likelihood terms have zero mixed third variation of the required type. A reverse edge \(\KL(q_j\Vert T_{ji}q_i)\) is linear in \(q_j\) outside its separate entropy, so its second \(q_j\) derivative is independent of \(q_i\); unrelated edges omit at least one varied factor. The theorem does not by itself classify the already minimized envelope in which \(\beta^*(q)\) has been substituted, because that substitution changes the derivative structure.

\section{Why the scoped exceptions are genuine exceptions}

Frozen source templates remove the sender variation. Replacing \(T_{ij}q_j\) by a fixed normalized \(f_{ij}\) makes the row an ordinary auxiliary source model. At unit temperature, Equations~\eqref{eq:fixed-source-row-joints}--\eqref{eq:fixed-source-row-optimum} give
\begin{equation}
\KL(Q_i\Vert P_i)
=\sum_j\beta_{ij}\KL(q_i\Vert f_{ij})
+\KL(\beta_i\Vert\pi_i),
\label{eq:pifb2-unit-temperature-frozen-row}
\end{equation}
with no live \(q_j\) direction. This is the exact Task~6 construction. A general \(\tau\) still defines a valid convex row scalar and the exact envelope derived below, but it is not the same unscaled joint KL. A probabilistic nonunit-temperature reading needs a separately normalized tempered model and its temperature-dependent normalizers; refreshing \(f_{ij}=T_{ij}q_j\) changes that model between updates \citep{BissiriHolmesWalker2016}.

Restricted parametric families can fail mixture openness. A finite-dimensional Gaussian, tied-covariance, or symmetry-reduced family may not contain the tangent in Equation~\eqref{eq:moving-peer-receiver-tangent}; the required \(g_{ij}\) may also be constant on its available tangent space. The theorem makes no claim outside the tangent cone it states.

Compatible auxiliary variables change the latent inventory and the factorization. A fixed normalized model with a source label, replicated row variables, or another declared auxiliary coordinate can have an exact ELBO if all conditionals are mutually compatible and the global normalization is proved. That construction is not equality with a fixed joint on only the original shared agent-state variables.

A model fixed after equilibrium selection reverses the quantifiers. One may first choose \(q^\star\), then freeze \(T_{ij}q_j^\star\), and obtain the exact auxiliary identity in Equation~\eqref{eq:pifb2-unit-temperature-frozen-row}. The resulting model is fixed at that selected point, not uniformly fixed while \(q\) varies over the open neighborhood used by Theorem~\ref{thm:magent-moving-peer-obstruction}.

The zero-within-scale hierarchical Gaussian joint removes the active edge assumption. When the raw within-scale belief and model couplings vanish, the cross-scale tree has fixed Gaussian transitions. A proper top prior and finite positive link variances make its stacked precision SPD, so ordinary mean-field inference and the state ELBO apply to that fixed hierarchy. This is an exact state-model construction rather than a representation of the active live-peer term.

Correlated structured recognition removes the product-family assumption. The exact recognition law \(Q_X(dY\mid o)\) in Chapter~\ref{ch:structured-recognition-elbo} can retain cross-agent and cross-channel dependence. Its entropy is joint, and the separate-affinity calculation in Equation~\eqref{eq:fixed-joint-product-structure} is not the functional form being varied. The exact state evidence identity remains available for a fixed normalized joint.

Configuration-level Gibbs laws change both the random variable and the entropy tier. If a complete belief configuration is denoted by \(X\), a proper reference law and finite partition function produce the exact law in Equation~\eqref{eq:configuration-gibbs-prior}. Its variational identity contains configuration relative entropy. It does not turn the live peer scalar into the state ELBO over the original samples \(Y\).

\section{Four row objects that must not be conflated}

For one row, let \(E_{ij}(x)\) be differentiable edge energies, let \(\widetilde\pi_i\) be a fixed normalized prior on the row support, and let \(\tau>0\). The full entropy-retaining objective is the scalar
\begin{equation}
\mathcal R_i(\beta_i,x)
=\sum_j\beta_{ij}E_{ij}(x)
+\tau\sum_j\beta_{ij}\log\frac{\beta_{ij}}{\widetilde\pi_{ij}}.
\label{eq:pifb2-full-row-scalar}
\end{equation}
Its unique simplex minimizer and minimized envelope are
\begin{align}
\beta_{ij}^*(x)
&=\frac{\widetilde\pi_{ij}\exp[-E_{ij}(x)/\tau]}
{Z_i(x)},
&
Z_i(x)
&=\sum_k\widetilde\pi_{ik}\exp[-E_{ik}(x)/\tau],
\label{eq:pifb2-row-optimum}\\
\mathcal R_i^{\mathrm{red}}(x)
&=-\tau\log Z_i(x),
&
d\mathcal R_i^{\mathrm{red}}
&=\sum_j\beta_{ij}^*dE_{ij}.
\label{eq:pifb2-row-envelope}
\end{align}
Both \(\mathcal R_i\) and \(\mathcal R_i^{\mathrm{red}}\) are scalar potentials. Equation~\eqref{eq:pifb2-row-envelope} is an exact simplex envelope identity; it does not depend on a state-ELBO interpretation.

The entropy-suppressed expression
\begin{equation}
S_i(x)=\sum_j\beta_{ij}^*(x)E_{ij}(x)
\label{eq:pifb2-entropy-suppressed-scalar}
\end{equation}
is also a scalar potential, but it is a different scalar. Its differential is
\begin{equation}
dS_i
=\sum_j\beta_{ij}^*dE_{ij}
-\frac{1}{\tau}
\operatorname{Cov}_{\beta_i^*}(E_{ij},dE_{ij}).
\label{eq:pifb2-entropy-suppressed-differential}
\end{equation}
The response term does not make \(S_i\) nonintegrable; it proves only that \(S_i\neq\mathcal R_i^{\mathrm{red}}\).

Finally, let \(x_i\) denote receiver coordinates in a population state and define the receiver-only field by retaining the derivative of receiver row \(i\) while discarding every contribution in which \(x_i\) appears as a sender in another row:
\begin{equation}
V_i^{\mathrm{rec}}(x)
=\partial_{x_i}S_i(x),
\qquad
\partial_{x_i}S_\ell(x)\ \text{discarded for every \(\ell\neq i\)}.
\label{eq:pifb2-receiver-only-field}
\end{equation}
The assembled field \(V^{\mathrm{rec}}\) is generally not the gradient of \(\sum_iS_i\), \(\sum_i\mathcal R_i^{\mathrm{red}}\), or any other scalar without additional mixed-derivative compatibility. It is an update approximation outside the scalar. The PIFB2 source reports the checked-in \texttt{FullVFE} path as differentiating the complete entropy-retaining scalar, not Equation~\eqref{eq:pifb2-receiver-only-field}.
