\chapter{Verification Obligations and Experimental Program}

This chapter defines the verification contract for the manuscript. It will separate algebraic checks, finite-dimensional numerical oracles, source-traceable executable crosswalks, build checks, and empirical research questions. Each check is attached to a stated equation, probability law, transformation convention, or implementation path. This structure prevents a successful build or an optimizer trace from being treated as evidence for a mathematical identity that has not been independently checked.

\chapter{Limitations and Research Outlook}

This chapter records the boundaries of the construction and the requirements for extensions. A general section-valued geometry does not itself provide a normalized continuum field theory, and a noncompact gauge configuration ensemble requires a proper reference measure and a finite partition-function argument. The chapter will distinguish these open constructions from the finite-context theory defined here. It also reserves empirical adequacy, optimization behavior, and computational cost for separately designed investigations.
