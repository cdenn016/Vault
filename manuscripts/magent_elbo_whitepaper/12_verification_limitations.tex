\chapter{Verification Obligations and Experimental Program}
\label{ch:verification-program}

The identities proved in this manuscript create finite verification obligations; they do not report that a future implementation has passed them. Table~\ref{tab:magent-verification-oracles} separates the exact analytic statement already established in the body from the executable oracle that remains to be implemented. Each numerical tolerance below applies only to the stated well-conditioned reference envelope. Task 12 of the implementation program must freeze the operands, precision, condition-number ceiling, estimator, and error budget before execution. A stress trial outside that envelope may diagnose numerical fragility but cannot refute an exact-arithmetic identity.

For deterministic float64 reference problems, define the scale function
\begin{equation}
\operatorname{sc}(u_1,\ldots,u_r)
=\max\left\{1,\lVert u_1\rVert,\ldots,\lVert u_r\rVert\right\}.
\label{eq:verification-scale-function}
\end{equation}
Unless a row states a stricter value, scalar and matrix residuals use an absolute-plus-relative budget of \(10^{-10}\operatorname{sc}(u_1,\ldots,u_r)\) on inputs whose relevant SPD condition numbers do not exceed \(10^6\). Exact rational or symbolic checks have tolerance zero. These are preregistered reference tolerances, not universal accuracy claims for arbitrary dimensions or conditioning.

\chapter{Limitations and Research Outlook}
\label{ch:limitations}

The construction answers a finite probability-model question. It does not establish a continuum field theory, a practical inference algorithm, or an empirical advantage. The limits below constrain every use of the exact results.

\section{Finite-design and continuum boundaries}

Every state-level normalization and ELBO identity is proved for one context or a finite design \(D\). The general geometry remains section-valued over \(\mathcal C\), but the paper supplies no measurable space of full sections, no continuum reference measure, no consistent extension theorem for the finite laws, and no normalizability proof for a continuum interaction energy. Pointwise densities and finite-dimensional marginals do not supply those missing objects. A continuum section-space ELBO remains open until all of them are constructed.

The directed reference model is also a simplification. The abstract state tier permits a finite DAG, while the analytic Gaussian model uses a forest and the reference finite-design law assumes conditional independence across design points given \(X\). Reciprocal or loopy population interactions do not inherit directed normalization. An undirected alternative must declare a global potential and prove \(0<Z_X<\infty\); local factor normalization does not determine the loopy partition function.

\section{Configuration and gauge normalization}

The exact hierarchical identity assumes a proper configuration prior. The optional Gibbs prior exists only under a proper reference probability and a finite nonzero partition function. Since \(\mathrm{GL}^{+}(K)\) is noncompact, an invariant uniform frame prior is unavailable. Treating frames as physical variables requires a proper prior or regulator. Treating them as gauge redundancy requires a quotient or gauge-fixing construction with its induced Jacobian and a treatment of stabilizers and singular strata. Either route must still prove integrability in the remaining configuration directions.

The finite reference uses local trivializations and Regime-I coboundaries. Their same-context loop products telescope, but this establishes neither global triviality nor nontrivial topology of the principal bundle. Cross-context comparisons require base parallel transport, and a nontrivial bundle claim would require an atlas, overlap cocycle, and global obstruction analysis. Independent Regime-II links can support discrete holonomy only after the interaction complex and link law are separately declared.

\section{Approximation and optimization limits}

Mean field can understate posterior uncertainty, but the precise result proved here is narrower than that general warning. For the Gaussian reverse-KL block optimum of Chapter~\ref{ch:mean-field-theory}, block inversion and the Schur complement give
\begin{equation}
J_{bb}^{-1}\preceq(J^{-1})_{bb},
\label{eq:gaussian-mean-field-block-underdispersion}
\end{equation}
with equality when block \(b\) is precision-decoupled from its complement. Thus the optimal product block covariance is no larger in Loewner order than the exact marginal covariance in this Gaussian regime. The manuscript does not claim a universal covariance order for arbitrary non-Gaussian mean-field families.

The abstract observation kernel need not be Gaussian or conjugate. For categorical, robust, nonlinear, or other nonconjugate likelihoods, the ELBO identity remains exact when its measure-theoretic assumptions hold, but the Gaussian coordinate equations generally cease to close. Quadrature, Monte Carlo estimates, auxiliary bounds, or accepted gradient proposals then add numerical error, stochastic error, or an approximation gap that must be reported separately.

One scalar objective does not prove convergence of a practical optimizer. Exact coordinate maxima and accepted generalized-EM proposals have the conditional monotonicity statements given earlier. Finite natural-gradient, Euclidean, retracted, simultaneous, or stochastic steps need not be monotone, may converge locally, and may fail to converge. No global convergence or finite-step rate is established for the proposed correlated family or configuration hierarchy.

\section{Executable and empirical boundaries}

The active MAgent runtime is not an implementation of the new correlated-state theory. It stores node-local Gaussian fields, evaluates live moving-peer consensus terms, takes simultaneous finite updates, and applies an external apex closure. Chapter~\ref{ch:executable-crosswalk} documents these facts from a pinned source snapshot. A future implementation must construct the missing fixed joint, correlated recognition law, once-counted entropy sectors, and accepted update semantics before it can inherit the new ELBO interpretation.

This paper reports no new empirical evidence. It contains analytic identities, source traces, and future finite oracles, but no completed comparison among recognition families, no gauge-change implementation result, no CAVI convergence trace, no runtime implementation of the new theory, and no predictive or computational benchmark. The five studies in Table~\ref{tab:magent-future-studies} therefore remain future work. Their outcomes may reject an implementation or an empirical hypothesis without invalidating an exact identity whose assumptions remain satisfied.

\section{Outlook}

The next defensible step is the smallest finite implementation that exposes every normalized factor and every entropy once. A conjugate Gaussian DAG can first test normalization, CAVI, determinant gaps, and gauge changes without inference-estimator noise. Nonconjugate observations, loopy interactions, random noncompact geometry, and continuum section laws should enter only after their additional normalization and verification obligations have been met. This order preserves the paper's core ontology: agents remain sections of associated bundles over one principal \(G\)-bundle, while every finite latent vector, graph coordinate, and variational factor retains its separate probabilistic type.
