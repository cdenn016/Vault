\chapter{Principal Bundles, Associated Statistical Bundles, and Agents as Sections}

This chapter fixes the geometric types used throughout the paper. A principal bundle over the contextual base \(\mathcal C\) supplies the frame data, while associated statistical bundles carry distribution-valued state and model sections. An agent is defined through its local support and these sections, rather than by a latent sample vector or by an interaction-graph vertex. The chapter also separates base-direction transport from frame comparisons and from any independently declared interaction links.
