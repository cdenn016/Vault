\chapter{Information Geometry, Natural Gradients, and Gauge Covariance}

This chapter supplies the coordinate and transformation conventions used in the analytic reference regime. It distinguishes statistical coordinates from gauge frames, identifies covariance and precision as dual geometric objects, and specifies the congruence actions appropriate to each. Natural-gradient language will be attached to a declared statistical metric and objective rather than used as a synonym for an arbitrary parameter update. The chapter's proof role is to make later covariance and invariance calculations type-consistent.
