# Integrated Information Theory: From Consciousness to Its Physical Substrate

> [!info] Citation
> Tononi, G., Boly, M., Massimini, M., & Koch, C. (2016). "Integrated Information Theory: From Consciousness to Its Physical Substrate." *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 17(7), 450–461.

## TL;DR

This Opinion article lays out Integrated Information Theory (IIT), which begins from the essential phenomenal properties of experience (its axioms) and derives the requirements that any physical substrate of consciousness must satisfy (its postulates). The central claim is that the substrate of consciousness is a system that constitutes a *maximum of intrinsic cause–effect power*, quantified by integrated information $\Phi$, with the structure of that cause–effect repertoire specifying the *quality* of an experience and its irreducibility specifying the *quantity*.

## What it establishes

IIT proceeds axiomatically. From phenomenological axioms — that experience exists intrinsically, is structured (composition), is specific (information), is unified (integration), and is definite (exclusion) — the authors infer corresponding *postulates* about the physical substrate. A conscious system must:

- exist intrinsically, exerting **cause–effect power upon itself**;
- specify a structured **cause–effect repertoire** over its own past and future states;
- be **integrated**, i.e. irreducible to the product of its parts — measured by integrated information $\Phi$ across the minimum information partition;
- attain a **maximum** of $\Phi$ over candidate sets of elements and spatiotemporal grains (the exclusion postulate), defining a single definite "complex".

Consciousness is thereby identified with the *conceptual structure* (the constellation of irreducible cause–effect "concepts") specified by a maximally irreducible substrate. The paper emphasizes that this is an *intrinsic*, observer-independent quantity, derives counterintuitive predictions (e.g. that feedforward systems and certain functionally equivalent networks differ in $\Phi$), and motivates empirical proxies for assessing consciousness in non-communicative patients.

## Why the project cites it

This reference anchors the project's engagement with [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] and the broader participatory cluster, where the question is how information-bearing structure becomes *intrinsic* to a system rather than imposed by an external observer. IIT is the strongest existing program that makes information an intrinsic, irreducible, observer-independent property of a physical substrate — a stance that contrasts instructively with the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] and with Wheeler's program (see [[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]]).

Several conceptual bridges are relevant to the gauge-theoretic [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] model:

- **Irreducibility and emergence.** IIT's exclusion/integration postulate — selecting the substrate that *maximizes* irreducible cause–effect power — is structurally analogous to the project's account of [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and the [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]], where a coarse-grained collective of agents becomes a genuine higher-level unit only when it is not decomposable into independent parts. IIT supplies a precedent for grounding emergence in a quantitative irreducibility measure rather than mere description.

- **Information as a system-intrinsic geometric object.** IIT measures distinctions in cause–effect space using divergences between probability distributions, which connects to the project's use of the [[Fisher information metric]] and [[Meta-entropy]] to characterize belief structure intrinsically. The contrast between $\Phi$-based intrinsic information and the extrinsic, prediction-error-driven information of the [[Free-energy principle active inference]] framework is a recurring point of comparison (cf. [[seth-2021-being-you]]).

- **Self-referential cause–effect power.** IIT's requirement that a substrate exert cause–effect power *upon itself* parallels the self-modeling, self-transporting structure of [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] and [[Hamiltonian belief dynamics]], where belief states evolve under dynamics they themselves parameterize.

> [!note] Editorial: IIT is cited as a contrasting and complementary theory of intrinsic information, not as a component the project adopts wholesale. The project's commitments to [[Variational free energy]] and active inference treat consciousness/agency operationally, whereas IIT makes a stronger metaphysical identification; the manuscripts use this tension deliberately.
