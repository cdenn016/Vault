# How and Why Consciousness Arises: Some Considerations from Physics and Physiology

> [!info] Citation
> Solms, Mark & Friston, Karl J. (2018). "How and why consciousness arises: some considerations from physics and physiology." *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 25(5–6), 202–238.

## TL;DR

Solms and Friston offer a Free-Energy-Principle (FEP) route into Chalmers's hard problem. They argue that consciousness is fundamentally **endogenous and affective** — rooted in homeostatic/interoceptive regulation rather than exteroceptive cognition — and that the minimal thermodynamic conditions for being a self-organizing (Markov-blanketed) system that resists dissipation specify *sufficient conditions for feeling*. On this view, the felt quality of experience is tied to the precision-weighted, free-energy-minimizing dynamics by which a living system maintains its existence; to *be* such a system is, at the relevant level, to *feel*.

## What it establishes

The paper couples two ingredients: (i) the endogenous, affect-first picture of consciousness from neuropsychology (consciousness as arising from brainstem-mediated homeostatic affect, not cortical representation), and (ii) the FEP/active-inference account of self-organizing systems possessing Markov blankets and minimizing variational free energy. The synthesis proposes that feeling is what it is like, from the inside, to be a free-energy-minimizing system maintaining its expected states — an identity-style claim that grounds phenomenal experience in the physics and physiology of self-evidencing. It is a major statement of how the FEP bears on consciousness, and is very likely the manuscript's "Friston2018" citation.

## Why the project cites it

This is the FEP-identity theory that the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] shares machinery with but **differs from**, and pinning down that difference is the point of the citation. Both PIFB and Solms–Friston build on [[Variational free energy]] and active inference (see [[friston-2010-free-energy-principle]], [[parr-2022-active-inference]], [[seth-2021-being-you]]); both treat the self as a free-energy-minimizing, self-evidencing system. The divergence is metaphysical. Solms and Friston make a near-*identity* claim — feeling *is* what it is like to be a free-energy-minimizing system — whereas PIFB takes a panprotopsychist, participatory stance (via [[chalmers-2013-panpsychism]], [[strawson-2006-realistic-monism]]): the free-energy/gauge structure supplies the dispositional roles, but the experiential character is the protophenomenal occupant of those roles, and the residual [[Explanatory gap]] is relocated rather than identified away. The contrast is exactly the one PIFB needs to state its position: it is not the claim that minimizing $F$ just *is* feeling, but that frame-relative registered content within the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] ontology is where experiential character is constituted. This places the note at the junction of the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] thread and the project's [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] core, and it links the affect-first, homeostatic reading to the project's [[Belief inertia]] / [[Mass as Fisher information]] picture of a self as a model that resists perturbation.
