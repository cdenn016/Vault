# Realistic Monism: Why Physicalism Entails Panpsychism

> [!info] Citation
> Strawson, Galen (2006). "Realistic Monism: Why Physicalism Entails Panpsychism." *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, 13(10–11), 3–31. (Reprinted in A. Freeman (Ed.), *Consciousness and Its Place in Nature*, Imprint Academic / OUP, 2006; chapter DOI [10.1093/oso/9780199267422.003.0001](https://doi.org/10.1093/oso/9780199267422.003.0001).)

## TL;DR

Strawson argues that a genuinely *realistic* physicalism — one that does not deny the obvious reality of conscious experience — is committed to panpsychism. His move is that experience is concretely real and is wholly physical (the realistic physicalist's premise), yet experiential being cannot be wholly constituted by, or emerge brutely from, the *non*-experiential. If everything is physical and experience is real and not radically emergent from the non-experiential, then the physical must be experiential "all the way down": fundamental physical entities have experiential nature. Panpsychism is thus the consequence, not the rejection, of taking physicalism seriously.

## What it establishes

The essay sharpens the **anti-(brute-)emergence** argument: there is no intelligible route from wholly non-experiential ingredients to experiential being, so experience cannot be a brute emergent and must be present at the base. Combined with realistic physicalism, this yields micropsychism/panpsychism. The paper is the lead target article of an influential *JCS* special issue (with seventeen commentaries) and is, alongside Russell and Chalmers, a foundational statement of the modern panpsychist case framed in Russellian-monist terms.

## Why the project cites it

Strawson supplies the **anti-emergence pressure** that motivates the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]]'s panprotopsychist commitment (via [[chalmers-2013-panpsychism]]) rather than an emergentist one. His argument is that experiential character cannot appear by brute emergence from the wholly non-experiential; if PIFB took the line that consciousness simply *emerges* at some scale of belief-coupling, Strawson is the standing objection. The project answers in the Russellian-structuralist idiom it shares with [[russell-1927-analysis-of-matter]] and [[goff-2017-consciousness-fundamental-reality]]: protophenomenal nature occupies the categorical roles of the gauge structure at the base, so higher-scale experience is *constituted* (via [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]]) rather than brutely emergent — which is exactly why the project owes an account of the combination problem ([[chalmers-2016-combination-problem]]). This places the note within the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] and [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] threads and clarifies what kind of theory PIFB is: constitutive panprotopsychism, defended against brute emergence on Strawson's grounds.
