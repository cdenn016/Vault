# Cool Horizons for Entangled Black Holes (ER = EPR)

> [!info] Citation
> Juan Maldacena and Leonard Susskind (2013). "Cool horizons for entangled black holes." *Fortschritte der Physik* 61(9): 781–811. DOI: [10.1002/prop.201300020](https://doi.org/10.1002/prop.201300020). Preprint: [arXiv:1306.0533](https://arxiv.org/abs/1306.0533).

## TL;DR

Maldacena and Susskind propose the ER=EPR conjecture: an Einstein–Rosen bridge (ER, a wormhole connecting two black holes) is the geometric manifestation of quantum entanglement (EPR) between them. Any entangled pair — down to a single Bell pair — is, in this view, connected by a (possibly Planckian, non-traversable) wormhole. The conjecture welds the geometric and informational descriptions of spacetime: entanglement *is* a geometric connection, the most literal statement of "spacetime is built from entanglement."

## What it establishes

- A proposed identity between entanglement (EPR) and wormhole geometry (ER): entangled systems are spatially connected through the bulk.
- A resolution route for the black-hole firewall (AMPS) paradox, since the interior is encoded in the entanglement with the exterior/partner.
- The sharpest conceptual fusion of the entanglement-geometry program: it elevates [[vanraamsdonk-2010-entanglement-spacetime]]'s "entanglement glues geometry" to "entanglement *is* a geometric bridge."

## Why the project cites it

PIFB ([[participatory-it-from-bit]]) cites ER=EPR as the most extreme and vivid instance of its governing idea that *relational/informational* structure constitutes geometric/spatial structure. Where the program models inter-agent relations as gauge transport and [[Holonomy]] between [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], ER=EPR models inter-system entanglement as literal spatial connection — both assert that "how strongly two parts are related" is the same datum as "how they are geometrically connected." This is the participatory claim in its strongest physics form, anchoring the [[Emergent spacetime and holography]] theme and the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] thread, and it pairs with [[susskind-2014-complexity-black-hole-horizons]] to extend the picture from entanglement to computation.
