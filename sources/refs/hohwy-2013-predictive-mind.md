# The Predictive Mind

> [!info] Citation
> Hohwy, Jakob (2013). *The Predictive Mind*. Oxford: Oxford University Press. ISBN: 9780199682737.

## TL;DR

Hohwy's book is the other foundational philosophical statement of **predictive processing**, developed from the Free-Energy Principle. The mind is a hierarchical Bayesian prediction-error-minimizing engine: perception is the brain's best hypothesis about the hidden causes of sensory input, learning tunes the generative model, and attention is the optimization of precision (expected error reliability). Hohwy presses a comparatively *internalist* reading: because the brain is separated from the world by a sensory/statistical boundary (a Markov-blanket-like "evidentiary boundary"), the mind is in a sense **secluded**, inferring its world from behind a veil of sensory effects rather than reaching out to grasp it directly.

## What it establishes

The book systematizes prediction-error minimization as a complete account of perception, attention, and the structure of conscious experience, emphasizing the inferential-isolation consequence: what we experience is the model, and the world is accessed only via its sensory consequences. It develops precision-weighting as the mechanism of attention and offers PP-based readings of illusions, binding, and the unity of consciousness. With Clark's *Surfing Uncertainty*, it anchors the philosophical predictive-processing literature, while taking a more skull-bound, less embodied stance.

## Why the project cites it

Hohwy's **secluded/inferentially-isolated mind** is the cleanest philosophical statement of the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]]'s premise that an agent never touches a pre-given world but only its own model of it. The "evidentiary boundary" is the cognitive-science analogue of the project's frame-relative registration: an agent reads its world only through sensory effects, in its own local frame, exactly as PIFB models content as frame-relative and transported ([[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], [[Gauge transformation]]). The project formalizes Hohwy's [[Prediction error]] and [[Precision weighting]] in the [[Variational free energy]] functional and its precision-weighted couplings ([[Multi-agent variational free energy]]). Hohwy pairs with [[clark-2016-surfing-uncertainty]] (the embodied counterpart), [[seth-2021-being-you]] (controlled hallucination), and [[hoffman-2019-case-against-reality]] (the interface/pullback) as the predictive-processing foundation for [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] and the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] thread. The internalist seclusion thesis is the one PIFB leans on most directly for its noumenal-pullback claim.
