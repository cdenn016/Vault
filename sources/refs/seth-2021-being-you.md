# Being You: A New Science of Consciousness

> [!info] Citation
> Seth, Anil K. (2021). *Being You: A New Science of Consciousness*. London: Faber & Faber (UK) / New York: Dutton (US). ISBN 978-1524742874.

## TL;DR

Seth's *Being You* develops the "beast machine" view of consciousness, in which perception is a process of **controlled hallucination**: the brain is a prediction engine that continuously generates and tests Bayesian best-guesses about the hidden causes of its sensory signals. Crucially, the experience of *being a self* — of having a body, of being alive — is itself a kind of controlled hallucination grounded in predictive (interoceptive) regulation of the organism, not a passive readout of an external world.

## What it establishes

The book is a broad, accessible synthesis built on the predictive-processing / active-inference research program, organized around several claims:

- **Perception as inference.** What we experience is not the world as it is but the brain's *generative model* of the world, updated by [[Prediction error]] and weighted by [[Precision weighting]]. Seth popularizes the slogan that perception is "controlled hallucination" — the percept is the prediction, corrected by sensory evidence rather than constructed from it.
- **The self as a perception.** Selfhood (bodily, perspectival, narrative, and especially interoceptive "being alive") is cast as another inferential construction the brain makes about itself, tied to homeostatic regulation of the body.
- **Beast machine.** Consciousness is rooted in the biological imperative to stay alive; experience is "of and for the body" rather than primarily for abstract cognition — a deflation of the disembodied, computationalist picture of mind.
- **Real problem vs. hard problem.** Seth reframes the agenda as the *real problem*: explaining, predicting, and controlling the phenomenological properties of conscious experience in terms of physical mechanisms, rather than first solving the metaphysical hard problem.

Seth grounds these positions in the **Free Energy Principle** and active inference (Friston and colleagues), which the book presents informally rather than formally.

## Why the project cites it

This reference anchors the **participatory / observer-centric** side of the program (cluster/participatory). Its relevance is conceptual and motivational rather than technical:

- **From free energy to phenomenology.** *Being You* is the trade-book articulation of the same generative-model machinery the project formalizes through [[Variational free energy]] and the [[Free-energy principle active inference]] method. Where Seth argues verbally that an agent's experienced world is a prediction-error-minimizing best guess, the project gives that guess explicit geometric structure: beliefs as sections of a bundle ([[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]]) updated under [[Prediction error]] and [[Precision weighting]].
- **The observer constructs its world (participatory realism).** Seth's "controlled hallucination" — the claim that the perceived world is generated by the perceiving system — is a neuroscientific cousin of [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]]. It supplies an empirically grounded motivation for treating the agent not as a passive measurer of a pre-given world but as a participant whose model *is* its accessible reality, complementing the physics-side sources on participatory realism.
- **Multi-agent extension.** Each agent in the [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] setting is a Seth-style inferential self maintaining its own controlled hallucination; the social/coupled dynamics the project studies arise when many such self-models interact, connecting to the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]].
- **Self as homeostatic inference.** The "beast machine" emphasis on interoceptive, life-preserving inference resonates with the project's reading of inertial and dynamical structure on beliefs ([[Belief inertia]], [[Mass as Fisher information]]): a self is a model that resists perturbation because maintaining it is what keeps the agent viable.

Quantitative claims in the project's manuscripts should be cited to the primary active-inference and information-geometry sources, not to this book.
