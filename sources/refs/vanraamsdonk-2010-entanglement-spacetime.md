# Building up Spacetime with Quantum Entanglement

> [!info] Citation
> Mark Van Raamsdonk (2010). "Building up spacetime with quantum entanglement." *General Relativity and Gravitation* **42**(10), 2323–2329. (First Award, 2010 Gravity Research Foundation Essay Competition.) arXiv:1005.3035, DOI:10.1007/s10714-010-1034-0.

## TL;DR

Van Raamsdonk argues that the classical connectivity of spacetime is built out of the quantum entanglement of the underlying degrees of freedom. Working within the AdS/CFT correspondence, he shows that gradually disentangling the degrees of freedom describing two regions causes those regions to pull apart and ultimately pinch off, so that geometric proximity is a manifestation of how strongly the boundary theory's parts are entangled.

## What it establishes

Using the holographic (AdS/CFT) dictionary, the essay makes the case that spatial geometry is not fundamental but *emergent* from a more basic informational quantity — entanglement. The central claims are:

- For a boundary state describing a connected bulk spacetime, the entanglement entropy between a region and its complement (via the Ryu–Takayanagi area prescription) tracks the geometric area separating them.
- Continuously reducing the entanglement between two sets of boundary degrees of freedom corresponds to the dual spatial regions moving apart; in the limit of zero entanglement the regions disconnect entirely.
- Therefore the "amount of geometry" connecting two parts of space is a measure of how entangled those parts are: *it from (qu)bit*, with entanglement as the binding agent of spacetime.

> [!note] Editorial: This is a short essay (a Gravity Research Foundation award piece), so it advances a conceptual thesis with illustrative holographic arguments rather than a fully formalized theorem. The note above reflects its argued claims, not derived proofs.

## Why the project cites it

The project's participatory thread treats geometric and physical structure as constructed from information rather than presupposed. Van Raamsdonk supplies a concrete, physics-grounded instance of that stance: a *metric* relation (spatial distance / connectivity) is reconstructed from an *informational* relation (entanglement). This resonates directly with the project's reading of [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] and its citation of [[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]] — geometry is "it," entanglement is "bit."

The deeper methodological hook for this work is that an *information-theoretic* quantity organizes geometry. In the project's own machinery, the relevant informational geometry is the [[Fisher information metric]] over belief states, which underwrites the [[Natural gradient]] and motivates the identification of [[Mass as Fisher information]]. Van Raamsdonk's entanglement-builds-geometry picture is the participatory analogue used to argue that the metric structure agents act within can itself be emergent from the relational/informational content of their beliefs rather than externally imposed.

It also reinforces the relational and emergent commitments elsewhere in the cluster: that macroscopic structure assembles from finer informational degrees of freedom connects to [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]], and the entanglement-as-glue motif sits alongside the relational and QBist readings of physics in [[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]] and [[fuchs-2014-qbism]]. Within the manuscript layer it backstops the participatory positioning of [[participatory-it-from-bit]].
