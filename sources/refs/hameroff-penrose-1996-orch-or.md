# Orchestrated Reduction of Quantum Coherence in Brain Microtubules (Orch-OR)

> [!info] Citation
> Hameroff, Stuart & Penrose, Roger (1996). "Orchestrated reduction of quantum coherence in brain microtubules: A model for consciousness." *Mathematics and Computers in Simulation*, 40(3–4), 453–480. DOI: [10.1016/0378-4754(96)80476-9](https://doi.org/10.1016/0378-4754(96)80476-9).

## TL;DR

Orch-OR proposes that consciousness arises from quantum computations in neuronal microtubules, terminated by Penrose's gravity-induced "objective reduction" of the wavefunction. Each orchestrated reduction is posited to be a discrete conscious moment, making consciousness a quantum-gravitational rather than purely computational phenomenon.

> [!note] Editorial: Highly contested; cited by the project as one representative of the quantum-coherence-in-the-brain family of approaches, not endorsed. The DOI above is from one of the standard reprints; the project lists Orch-OR among unresolved attempts on the hard problem.

## Why the project cites it

The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] lists Orch-OR among the **unresolved attempts** to explain consciousness, as a foil that takes a quantum route the project does not. Where Orch-OR locates experience in objective wavefunction collapse, PIFB relocates the [[Explanatory gap]] within a participatory, panprotopsychist, gauge-theoretic framework ([[Participatory realism (it from bit)]], [[chalmers-2013-panpsychism]]) that does not require exotic quantum-gravitational physics. The contrast situates PIFB within the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] landscape of candidate theories.
