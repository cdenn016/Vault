# Differential Geometry: Connections, Curvature, and Characteristic Classes

> [!info] Citation
> Loring W. Tu (2017). *Differential Geometry: Connections, Curvature, and Characteristic Classes*. Graduate Texts in Mathematics, vol. 275. Springer. DOI 10.1007/978-3-319-55084-8.

## TL;DR

Tu provides the direct mathematical route from smooth manifolds to vector and principal bundles, connections, curvature, Chern–Weil theory, and characteristic classes. It is the central geometry text for distinguishing a local frame change from a connection and a connection from its curvature.

## What it establishes

The book develops covariant differentiation on bundles, connection forms, curvature forms, parallel transport, and global topological invariants. It explains how local representatives glue into global geometric objects and why curvature is an obstruction to path-independent transport.

## Relevance to this research

Gauge covariance in the program becomes mathematically meaningful only after the base space, fibers, transition functions, connection, and group action are stated. Tu supplies that language and supports [[Gauge transformation]], [[Parallel transport]], [[Holonomy]], [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], and [[Non-flat connection and the photon analogy]]. It is the main corrective against treating every matrix-valued pairwise map as a nontrivial gauge field.
