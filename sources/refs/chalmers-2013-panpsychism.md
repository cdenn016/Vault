# Panpsychism and Panprotopsychism

> [!info] Citation
> Chalmers, David J. (2013). "Panpsychism and Panprotopsychism." *The Amherst Lecture in Philosophy*, 8, 1–35. Available at <https://amherstlecture.org/chalmers2013/>. A revised version appears in G. Brüntrup & L. Jaskolla (Eds.), *Panpsychism: Contemporary Perspectives* (Oxford University Press, 2017).

## TL;DR

Chalmers presents panpsychism and panprotopsychism as the synthesis in a Hegelian dialectic whose thesis is materialism and antithesis is dualism. **Panpsychism** holds that fundamental physical entities are themselves conscious; **panprotopsychism** holds, more weakly, that they have *protophenomenal* properties — not themselves experiential, but the right kind of basis from which experience can be constituted. Both are framed as forms of **Russellian monism**: physics characterizes only the dispositional/structural roles of fundamental entities, leaving their intrinsic (categorical) natures open, and (proto)phenomenal properties fill those intrinsic-nature slots. The view thereby accommodates both the causal argument for materialism and the conceivability argument for dualism. Its standing antithesis is the combination problem.

## What it establishes

The lecture defines the panpsychism/panprotopsychism distinction carefully, situates both within Russellian monism (so that experience or protoexperience is the categorical ground of physical structure), and argues this is the most promising route to a positive theory of consciousness given the failures of pure materialism and pure dualism. It catalogs varieties (constitutive vs. emergent, micro vs. cosmo) and identifies the **combination problem** — how micro-level (proto)experiences combine into macro-level subjects — as the central obstacle, setting up the dedicated treatment in [[chalmers-2016-combination-problem]].

## Why the project cites it

The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] **self-classifies as panprotopsychist in Chalmers's exact sense**, and this lecture is the definitional source for that label. The project does not claim its gauge frames or beliefs are themselves conscious; it claims the frame-relative informational structure provides the protophenomenal basis from which experiential character is constituted — precisely panprotopsychism, not panpsychism. The Russellian-monist framing is doubly load-bearing: it lets PIFB say that the project's physics (gauge structure, transport, [[Variational free energy]]) specifies dispositional/structural roles while the intrinsic natures filling those roles are protophenomenal, which is the relocation strategy for the [[Explanatory gap]] stated in the right metaphysical idiom. This connects forward to the project's central open question — the cross-scale [[combination problem]] (see [[chalmers-2016-combination-problem]], [[goff-2017-consciousness-fundamental-reality]]), which the project's [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]] machinery is the candidate answer to — and back to [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] and [[Mathematical consciousness science]]. The Russellian structuralism Chalmers invokes is the one PIFB defines itself against and partly with (cf. [[russell-1927-analysis-of-matter]], [[strawson-2006-realistic-monism]]).
