# Quantum Theory, Groups and Representations: An Introduction

> [!info] Citation
> Woit, P. (2017). *Quantum Theory, Groups and Representations: An Introduction*. Springer. ISBN 978-3-319-64610-7. DOI: 10.1007/978-3-319-64612-1.

## TL;DR

A textbook developing quantum mechanics **through the lens of Lie groups and their unitary representations**: symmetries as group actions, observables as Lie-algebra elements, and the central examples ($U(1)$, $SU(2)$/$SO(3)$, the Heisenberg group, the harmonic oscillator, the Euclidean and Poincaré groups) built explicitly. It emphasizes how representation theory organizes quantum structure — angular momentum as $SU(2)$ irreps, the Stone–von Neumann theorem, and the role of the moment/momentum map.

## What it establishes

A representation-theoretic account of QM: unitary representations of symmetry groups, the correspondence between self-adjoint observables and Lie-algebra generators, [[Clebsch-Gordan coefficients|Clebsch–Gordan]] decomposition of angular momenta, and the [[Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula|exponential map]] linking algebra to group. Substantial coverage of $SU(2)$ and the rotation group as the worked prototype of [[Irreducible representation|irreducible-representation]] theory.

## Why the project cites it

A complementary representation-theory reference, overlapping [[hall-2015-lie-groups|Hall]] but pitched toward the physics of symmetry and observables. The program's gauge structure ([[Gauge transformation]], the block-$GL(K)$ attention of the [[VFE Transformer Program]]) and its equivariance machinery ([[Group equivariance]]) rest on exactly this Lie-group/representation vocabulary, and Woit is a readable secondary source for the $SU(2)$/$SO(3)$ irrep and exponential-map material, with a clean treatment of how a momentum map arises from a group action.

> [!note] Editorial: A secondary/complementary reference for Lie-group representation theory in a quantum setting; overlaps Hall and Fulton–Harris already in the vault, included for its physics-of-symmetry emphasis.
