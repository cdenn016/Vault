# Every Thing Must Go: Metaphysics Naturalized

> [!info] Citation
> Ladyman, James, and Ross, Don (with David Spurrett and John Collier) (2007). *Every Thing Must Go: Metaphysics Naturalized*. Oxford: Oxford University Press. DOI: [10.1093/acprof:oso/9780199276196.001.0001](https://doi.org/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780199276196.001.0001).

## TL;DR

The book-length, naturalized defense of *ontic structural realism* (OSR): the only metaphysics worth having is one continuous with current fundamental science, and that science tells us reality is *structure* — relations, symmetries, patterns — all the way down, with self-subsistent individual objects eliminated or radically demoted ("there are no things; structure is all there is"). It couples OSR about physics with *rainforest realism* about the special sciences (real patterns at multiple scales) and a fierce critique of a priori, intuition-driven "domesticated" analytic metaphysics.

## What it establishes

- **Ontic structural realism, naturalized.** Fundamental ontology is relational structure; objects are not the bedrock. Metaphysics must answer to science, not to common sense or a priori intuition.
- **Rainforest realism / real patterns.** The special sciences track genuine, scale-relative patterns (after Dennett's "real patterns"); reality is layered structure, not a reducible hierarchy of little things.
- **"Information-theoretic" ontology.** Physical structure is articulated in terms of modal/relational and information-theoretic notions rather than substantival objects, harmonizing with the *it-from-bit* tradition.
- **Anti-domestication.** A methodological mandate: reject metaphysics disconnected from science.

## Why the project cites it

This is the framework's named OSR ontology and the anchor of [[Structural realism]] for the program. The manuscript's pan-agentic, relations-first metaphysics — modeling [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] with no intrinsic state, individuated only by their relations and the connection ([[Gauge transformation]]) among them — is OSR in the Ladyman–Ross sense applied to agents rather than particles. Their information-theoretic articulation of structure directly supports the bridge from Wheeler's *it from bit* ([[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]]) to the project's [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]]: what is real is invariant relational/structural content, and "things" are derivative nodes. The "real patterns" idea also resonates with the program's cross-scale, coarse-graining architecture (meta-agents as higher-scale patterns over clusters of agents). It builds on the journal-article OSR of [[french-ladyman-2003-remodelling]] and is refined toward the project's setting by the fibre-bundle moderate OSR of [[esfeld-lam-2008-moderate-osr]]. Project/transformer tag too: structural-realist, relations-first ontology is shared core math/metaphysics for both the multi-agent and transformer programs.
