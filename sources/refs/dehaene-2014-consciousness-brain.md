# Consciousness and the Brain: Deciphering How the Brain Codes Our Thoughts

> [!info] Citation
> Dehaene, Stanislas (2014). *Consciousness and the Brain: Deciphering How the Brain Codes Our Thoughts*. New York: Viking. ISBN: 9780670025435.

## TL;DR

Dehaene's trade book synthesizes the **global neuronal workspace** theory of consciousness: a stimulus becomes conscious when it is amplified and broadcast across a wide fronto-parietal network ("ignition"), making the information globally available to many specialized processors. The book reviews the experimental signatures of conscious access (the "four signatures") and the contrast between subliminal and conscious processing.

> [!note] Editorial: General-audience monograph; cited for the global-workspace framing of conscious access, not for technical results.

## Why the project cites it

The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] cites Dehaene for the **global-workspace** account of conscious access, one of the major empirical theories PIFB situates itself among. Global broadcast/ignition — information becoming widely available across a network — has a structural echo in the project's multi-agent coupling, where coherent collective beliefs propagate across agents ([[Multi-agent variational free energy]], [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]]). It pairs with [[mashour-2020-global-workspace]] (the primary-literature statement) and sits in the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] thread alongside integration-based ([[tononi-2016-iit]]) and predictive ([[clark-2016-surfing-uncertainty]]) theories.
