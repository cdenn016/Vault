# Information, Physics, Quantum: The Search for Links (It from Bit)

> [!info] Citation
> Wheeler, J. A. (1990). "Information, Physics, Quantum: The Search for Links." In W. H. Zurek (Ed.), *Complexity, Entropy, and the Physics of Information* (pp. 309–336). Redwood City, CA: Addison-Wesley.

## TL;DR

Wheeler's essay argues that physical reality is, at bottom, informational: "every *it* — every particle, every field of force, even the spacetime continuum itself — derives its function, its meaning, its very existence" from binary answers to yes-or-no questions — bits. The slogan *it from bit* couples this informational ontology to a *participatory* view of physics, in which the act of observation (posing and registering an answer to a question) is constitutive of the phenomena it records rather than merely revealing a pre-existing fact.

## What it establishes

The essay advances a programmatic thesis rather than a formal result. Its central claims, as Wheeler frames them, are:

- **It from bit.** Every physical quantity, every *it*, draws its ultimate significance from bits — from the apparatus-elicited answers to yes-or-no questions. Information is taken as ontologically prior to "things."
- **The participatory universe.** Observers are not passive spectators. By choosing which question to ask and registering the answer, the observer participates in bringing the observed phenomenon into definite being — Wheeler's "no phenomenon is a phenomenon until it is an observed (registered) phenomenon."
- **"No tower of turtles."** Wheeler rejects an infinite regress of underlying mechanisms, locating the bottom layer in elementary acts of distinction (questions and answers) rather than in a continuum substrate.
- A set of guiding questions and aphorisms ("How come existence?", "How come the quantum?", "How come 'one world' out of many observer-participants?") posed as a research agenda linking information theory, quantum measurement, and cosmology.

> [!note] Editorial: This is a conceptual/foundational essay. It does not derive theorems or report numerical results; the contribution is the framing of an informational, participatory ontology.

## Why the project cites it

The project's [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] concept takes its name and motivating stance directly from this essay. Wheeler's claim that *its* arise from registered answers to questions supplies the philosophical backdrop for treating an agent's state not as a passive readout of a fixed world but as the product of an inferential, observation-driven process — the move from "bit" to "belief."

Concretely, the gauge-theoretic multi-agent program reinterprets Wheeler's bits as the data that drive belief updating under the [[Variational free energy]] functional. Where Wheeler speaks of observer-participancy, the project formalizes participation as active inference: an agent acts to elicit observations and minimizes [[Prediction error]] (with [[Precision weighting]]) so that its [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] decreases. The "question/answer" elementary act maps onto a prediction and its sensory confirmation or violation.

The participatory thread also connects to the model's geometric content. Modeling [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] makes each agent's "answer" a frame-dependent, locally registered quantity, with [[Gauge transformation]] and [[Holonomy]] encoding how registered information transforms and accumulates across observer-participants — a structural counterpart to Wheeler's emphasis that observation is always situated. In this way the note sits alongside the project's other [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] sources (e.g. [[fuchs-2014-qbism]], [[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]]) as the originating statement of the informational, observer-centric ontology the program seeks to make quantitative through [[Variational free energy]] and information geometry.
