# Conscious Processing and the Global Neuronal Workspace Hypothesis

> [!info] Citation
> Mashour, G. A., Roelfsema, P., Changeux, J.-P., & Dehaene, S. (2020). "Conscious processing and the global neuronal workspace hypothesis." *Neuron*, 105(5), 776–798. DOI: [10.1016/j.neuron.2020.01.026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2020.01.026).

## TL;DR

This review states the contemporary **global neuronal workspace** (GNW) hypothesis: conscious access occurs when a non-linear "ignition" of recurrent fronto-parietal activity amplifies and sustains a representation, broadcasting it for global access by otherwise local processors. The paper lays out the neuroanatomical basis, the empirical signatures of ignition, and how GNW accounts for the subliminal/conscious distinction.

> [!note] Editorial: Primary-literature companion to Dehaene's trade book; cited for the GNW mechanism of conscious access.

## Why the project cites it

The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] cites this as the **authoritative statement of the global-workspace theory**, one of the major neuroscientific theories of consciousness PIFB positions itself among. GNW's ignition-and-broadcast mechanism — local content becoming globally available — parallels the project's account of coherent collective belief propagation across coupled agents ([[Multi-agent variational free energy]], [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]]). It pairs with [[dehaene-2014-consciousness-brain]] and sits in the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] landscape alongside [[tononi-2016-iit]] and the predictive-processing sources.
