# Structural Realism: The Best of Both Worlds?

> [!info] Citation
> Worrall, John (1989). "Structural Realism: The Best of Both Worlds?" *Dialectica* **43**(1–2): 99–124. DOI: [10.1111/j.1746-8361.1989.tb00933.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1746-8361.1989.tb00933.x).

## TL;DR

The paper that launched modern structural realism. Worrall seeks a position that secures the realist's best argument — the *no-miracles* intuition that science's predictive success would be inexplicable unless theories were latching onto reality — while surviving the realist's worst problem — the *pessimistic meta-induction* from the historical discarding of theoretical ontologies. His resolution: across theory change, *mathematical structure* is retained and accumulates (Fresnel's equations survive the demise of the elastic-ether ontology), even though the posited objects/mechanisms are abandoned. So the realist should commit to *structure*, not to the nature of the entities.

## What it establishes

- **Epistemic structural realism.** We can know the relational/mathematical structure of the world but not the intrinsic nature of its objects; structure, not objects, is the locus of realist commitment.
- **Fresnel example.** The mathematical equations of optics carried over from elastic-ether to electromagnetic theory while the ontology was overturned — the paradigm case of structural continuity across revolutions.
- **Best of both worlds.** Structural commitment explains predictive success (no-miracles) yet is immune to the pessimistic meta-induction, since it never committed to the discarded objects.

## Why the project cites it

Worrall is the founding modern source for [[Structural realism]] and supplies the project's core slogan that *what is preserved and real is structure, not object-identity*. For the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]], this is the philosophy-of-science justification for treating the invariant, gauge-relational content as the objective content: across changes of frame ([[Gauge transformation]]) or even paradigm ([[Kuhn 1962|kuhn-1962-structure]]), it is the structural/relational pattern that is conserved, while any agent's local object-readout is frame-dependent and dispensable. Worrall's *epistemic* version (objects exist behind the structure) is the foil that the project's stronger *ontic* commitments ([[french-ladyman-2003-remodelling]], [[ladyman-ross-2007-every-thing-must-go]], [[esfeld-lam-2008-moderate-osr]]) build past, so this note is the entry point of the whole structural-realism lineage anchoring the program's pan-agentic ontology and [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]]. Project/transformer tag too: the structure-over-object principle is shared core for both programs.
