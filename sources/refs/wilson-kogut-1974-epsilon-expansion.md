# The Renormalization Group and the Epsilon Expansion

> [!info] Citation
> Kenneth G. Wilson and John Kogut (1974). "The renormalization group and the epsilon expansion." *Physics Reports* **12**(2), 75–199. DOI: [10.1016/0370-1573(74)90023-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/0370-1573(74)90023-4).

## TL;DR

The canonical long-form review of Wilson's renormalization group and the $\epsilon = 4 - d$ expansion. It develops the RG as a flow generated by integrating out momentum shells, derives the effective action / effective Hamiltonian at each scale, and uses the expansion about the upper critical dimension $d = 4$ to compute critical exponents perturbatively in $\epsilon$. It is the foundational source for the *effective-action* machinery and the systematic expansion technique the project leans on for its scale-by-scale belief coarse-graining.

## What it establishes

Wilson and Kogut formalize the RG transformation as repeated coarse-graining plus rescaling, producing a flow of the effective Hamiltonian whose fixed points govern critical behavior. The central technical contribution is the $\epsilon$-expansion: near four dimensions the non-trivial Wilson-Fisher fixed point is reachable perturbatively, so critical exponents can be computed order by order in $\epsilon$. Along the way the review lays out the effective-action formalism — how integrating out fast modes produces a renormalized action for the slow modes — and the relevant/irrelevant operator bookkeeping in concrete calculational form. It is the methods companion to Wilson's earlier conceptual papers.

## Why the project cites it

The project's [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]] is built on producing an *effective action* for coarse beliefs by integrating out fine-scale agent fluctuations; Wilson-Kogut is the reference that defines this effective-action machinery and the perturbative expansion that makes it computable. When the PIFB manuscript argues that meta-agent formation ([[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]]) generates a controlled flow on the space of [[Variational free energy]] functionals, the epsilon-expansion is the prototype of a systematic small-parameter expansion around a fixed point that such a claim would need to be made quantitative. It pairs with [[cardy-1996-scaling-renormalization]] (pedagogy), [[wilson-1971-rg-critical-phenomena]] (the relevant/irrelevant taxonomy), and [[berges-tetradis-wetterich-2002-nonperturbative-rg]] (the non-perturbative functional-RG generalization).
