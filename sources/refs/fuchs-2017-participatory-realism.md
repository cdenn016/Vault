# On Participatory Realism

> [!info] Citation
> Fuchs, C. A. (2017). "On Participatory Realism." In I. T. Durham & D. Rickles (Eds.), *Information and Interaction: Eddington, Wheeler, and the Limits of Knowledge* (pp. 113–134). Springer. Preprint: arXiv:1601.04360.

## TL;DR

Fuchs coins and defends *participatory realism* as the umbrella stance shared by the agent-centered interpretations of quantum theory — QBism, relational quantum mechanics, and Wheeler's observer-participancy. The common thread is that a first-person perspective is ineliminable from physics: the formalism is irreducibly about an agent's engagement with the world, not a view from nowhere. Fuchs traces the lineage from Wheeler's "law without law" and "it from bit" to QBism, arguing that quantum theory is telling us the world is *participatory* — agents are not passive spectators reading off pre-existing facts but contributors whose actions help bring outcomes into being. The essay is the explicit naming of the interpretive family that the project adopts.

## What it establishes

- **A named umbrella.** "Participatory realism" groups QBism, RQM, and Wheeler-style observer-participancy under one heading defined by the indispensability of the agent's perspective.
- **Realism without spectator-objectivity.** The view is realist — there is a world — but denies that the world consists of agent-independent facts waiting to be discovered; reality is in part *made* in the agent's participation.
- **Wheeler lineage.** Fuchs locates the roots in Wheeler's "law without law" and "it from bit," presenting QBism as their disciplined modern descendant.

## Why the project cites it

This essay names the project's own interpretive stance. The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] takes its title and orientation from precisely this participatory-realist family, and Fuchs 2017 is the source that christens it and articulates the lineage from [[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]] through QBism ([[fuchs-2014-qbism]], [[fuchs-schack-2013-bayesian-coherence]]) to relational accounts ([[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]]). The project's distinctive contribution is to make participatory realism *constructive and multi-agent*: where Fuchs emphasizes the single agent's participation, the project adds the structure by which many participating agents reach (partial) agreement — beliefs as variational posteriors coupled through [[Multi-agent variational free energy]], compared across frames by [[Parallel transport]] of [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], with [[Holonomy]] under a [[Gauge transformation]] measuring irreducible perspectival disagreement.
