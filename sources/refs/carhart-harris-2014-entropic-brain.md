# The Entropic Brain

> [!info] Citation
> Carhart-Harris, R. L., Leech, R., Hellyer, P. J., Shanahan, M., Feilding, A., Tagliazucchi, E., Chialvo, D. R., & Nutt, D. (2014). "The entropic brain: a theory of conscious states informed by neuroimaging research with psychedelic drugs." *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 8, 20. DOI: [10.3389/fnhum.2014.00020](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2014.00020).

## TL;DR

The entropic-brain hypothesis proposes that the richness and character of a conscious state track the *entropy* of its underlying brain dynamics. Normal waking cognition sits near a critical point; "primary" states (e.g. the psychedelic state, REM, early psychosis) involve elevated neural entropy and a corresponding loosening of the brain's ordinarily constrained, hierarchically organized dynamics.

> [!note] Editorial: Cited as a brief reference to the entropy/consciousness link; the project does not adopt the psychedelic-specific claims.
