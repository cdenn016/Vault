# Consciousness and Fundamental Reality

> [!info] Citation
> Goff, Philip (2017). *Consciousness and Fundamental Reality*. New York: Oxford University Press. DOI: [10.1093/oso/9780190677015.001.0001](https://doi.org/10.1093/oso/9780190677015.001.0001). ISBN: 9780190677015.

## TL;DR

Goff's monograph is a sustained, contemporary defense of **Russellian monism** and ultimately of **cosmopsychism**. Part I argues that physicalism cannot account for consciousness (developing the conceivability and knowledge arguments into a rigorous anti-physicalist case); Part II surveys forms of Russellian monism — the view that physics describes only structural/dispositional roles while consciousness or protoconsciousness supplies the intrinsic categorical natures — and argues that the constitutive micro-panpsychist version founders on the combination problem. Goff's preferred resolution inverts the direction of constitution: cosmopsychism, on which the cosmos as a whole is the fundamental conscious subject and individual subjects are derivative.

## What it establishes

The book gives the canonical recent statement of the **combination problem** as the decisive obstacle for constitutive micropsychism, and argues that taking the whole (cosmos) rather than the parts (micro-subjects) as fundamental is the most defensible response. It carefully develops Russellian monism as the synthesis that honors both the explanatory virtues of physicalism and the reality of phenomenal consciousness, and it sharpens the subject-summing difficulty: many small subjects do not obviously constitute one larger subject. It is, alongside [[chalmers-2016-combination-problem]], the reference statement of the combination problem in the current literature.

## Why the project cites it

The manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] cites Goff for the **canonical combination-problem statement** its cross-scale account must answer. The project's panprotopsychism (via [[chalmers-2013-panpsychism]]) inherits exactly Goff's challenge: how do constituent protophenomenal/experiential structures compose into a higher-scale subject? Two of Goff's moves bear directly on the project. First, his Russellian-monist framing — physics gives structure, consciousness gives intrinsic nature — is the metaphysics PIFB operates within, where the gauge-theoretic structure ([[Gauge transformation]], [[Holonomy]], [[Variational free energy]]) specifies dispositional roles and the protophenomenal fills the categorical slots (cf. [[russell-1927-analysis-of-matter]], [[strawson-2006-realistic-monism]]). Second, Goff's *cosmopsychist inversion* — the whole as fundamental, parts as derived — is suggestive for the project's [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]], where the apex scale folds back over all scales below (the system observes itself and feeds collective beliefs down). PIFB's [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] coarse-graining is the candidate constitutive mechanism, and Goff's argument is precisely the warning that *structural* composition may not deliver *subject* composition. This anchors the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] and [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] threads.
