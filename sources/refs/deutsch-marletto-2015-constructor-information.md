# Constructor Theory of Information

> [!info] Citation
> Deutsch, D., & Marletto, C. (2015). "Constructor theory of information." *Proceedings of the Royal Society A* **471**(2174), 20140540. DOI: 10.1098/rspa.2014.0540.

## TL;DR

Deutsch and Marletto formulate information physically by expressing it solely in terms of which physical transformations are *possible* and which are *impossible* — the "constructor-theoretic" mode of explanation. Rather than treating information as an a priori mathematical or logical notion, they derive its properties (copyability, distinguishability, computability) from conjectured exact laws of physics governing what tasks a "constructor" can perform with arbitrarily high accuracy, indefinitely repeatable. Classical and quantum information then appear as distinct regimes characterized by which sets of attributes can be cloned and measured. The program aims to ground information science in physics at a level more fundamental than the dynamical laws themselves.

## What it establishes

- **Information from possibility/impossibility.** A substrate carries information if it admits certain reversible, repeatable transformations; the laws of physics, expressed as constraints on possible tasks, fix what information is.
- **Constructor-theoretic principles.** Exact "principles" (rather than initial-conditions-plus-dynamics laws) express the regularities that make information physically instantiable, including an interoperability principle linking distinct information media.
- **Classical vs. quantum as task regimes.** The classical/quantum distinction is recast as a difference in which sets of attributes can be jointly copied and measured, not as a difference in underlying ontology.

## Why the project cites it

Constructor theory is the *contrast class* the project explicitly defines itself against. Both programs are "information-first," but they differ sharply on what grounds information: Deutsch and Marletto ground it in *objective, agent-independent* possibility/impossibility facts about physical transformations, whereas [[participatory-it-from-bit]] grounds objectivity in *consensus among coupled agents* — information is fundamentally perspectival, and shared structure is an achievement of inter-agent [[Parallel transport]] rather than a pre-given physical invariant. Citing Deutsch-Marletto lets the manuscript stake out its participatory position: it accepts the "it from bit" priority of information ([[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]]) but rejects the constructor-theoretic move of locating information in observer-independent physical possibility, siding instead with relational and QBist accounts ([[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]], [[fuchs-2014-qbism]], [[adlam-rovelli-2022-cross-perspective]]). The contrast clarifies the project's distinctive commitment within [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]]: objectivity is not read off the laws but built between [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], with [[Holonomy]] measuring where that construction fails to globalize.
