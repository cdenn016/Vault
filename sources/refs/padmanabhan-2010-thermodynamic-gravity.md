# Thermodynamical Aspects of Gravity: New Insights

> [!info] Citation
> Thanu Padmanabhan (2010). "Thermodynamical aspects of gravity: new insights." *Reports on Progress in Physics* 73(4): 046901. DOI: [10.1088/0034-4885/73/4/046901](https://doi.org/10.1088/0034-4885/73/4/046901). Preprint: [arXiv:0911.5004](https://arxiv.org/abs/0911.5004).

## TL;DR

Padmanabhan develops a comprehensive thermodynamic reformulation of gravity that goes beyond Jacobson's horizon-Clausius argument. He shows that the gravitational field equations (for Einstein gravity and for the broader Lanczos–Lovelock class) can be derived by extremizing an entropy functional associated with null surfaces, that the action splits into a bulk and a surface term related by a holographic equipartition, and that observers perceive horizon thermodynamics in a way that makes the field equations read like a local entropy-balance law. Gravity is "emergent" in the strong sense that its equations *are* thermodynamic identities of spacetime's microscopic degrees of freedom.

## What it establishes

- Field equations of a large class of gravity theories follow from extremizing a surface/entropy functional rather than from a metric action principle taken as fundamental.
- A holographic equipartition relates the number of bulk and surface degrees of freedom; the difference drives cosmic expansion in his later "emergent universe" work.
- Provides a complementary and more general thermodynamic route to emergent gravity than [[jacobson-1995-thermodynamics-spacetime]], with explicit higher-curvature generalizations.

## Why the project cites it

Padmanabhan gives PIFB ([[participatory-it-from-bit]]) a second, independent demonstration that gravity's *equations of motion* are the extremum condition of an entropy/information functional — the same structural claim PIFB makes for the [[Variational free energy]]. Where Jacobson uses a Clausius relation and Verlinde an entropic force, Padmanabhan uses an explicit variational principle over an entropy functional of null surfaces, which is the closest in spirit to PIFB's stationarity-of-$F$ formulation and to its emergent-metric program built on the pullback [[Fisher information metric]].
