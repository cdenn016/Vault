# Foundations of Differential Geometry, Volume I

> [!info] Citation
> Kobayashi, S., & Nomizu, K. (1963). *Foundations of Differential Geometry, Volume I*. Interscience Tracts in Pure and Applied Mathematics, No. 15. New York: Interscience Publishers (John Wiley & Sons).

## TL;DR

The canonical rigorous treatment of connections on principal and associated fibre bundles, developing parallel transport, curvature, and holonomy from the ground up. Volume I builds the bundle-theoretic language — principal bundles, connection forms, the structure equation, and the holonomy group — that is the mathematical substrate for any gauge theory. It is the reference the project leans on whenever a claim about belief geometry needs to be cashed out in terms of bundles, connections, or transport.

## What it establishes

Volume I develops differential geometry in the modern, coordinate-free, bundle-theoretic style. Its load-bearing contributions for this project are:

- **Principal fibre bundles and structure groups.** A principal bundle $P(M, G)$ over a base manifold $M$ with structure (Lie) group $G$ acting freely on the fibres, together with associated bundles built from a representation of $G$. This is the formal home of the [[Gauge transformation]] (a vertical automorphism of $P$) and of the [[Irreducible representation]] that defines an associated bundle.
- **Connections and the connection form.** A connection is a splitting of the tangent space of $P$ into horizontal and vertical subspaces, equivalently a $\mathfrak{g}$-valued connection 1-form $\omega$. This object is precisely the gauge field / potential of physics.
- **Parallel transport and covariant differentiation.** Horizontal lifts of curves define [[Parallel transport]], and the induced covariant derivative on associated bundles. Transport is path-dependent exactly when the connection has curvature.
- **Curvature and the structure equation.** The curvature 2-form $\Omega = d\omega + \tfrac{1}{2}[\omega,\omega]$, the structure equation, and the Bianchi identity. Curvature measures the infinitesimal failure of parallel transport to commute.
- **The holonomy group and the Ambrose–Singer theorem.** [[Holonomy]] — the group of transformations generated by parallel transport around closed loops — is characterized, and the Ambrose–Singer theorem identifies the holonomy Lie algebra with the span of curvature values. Volume I also treats reduction of the structure group and the relation between holonomy and reducibility.
- **Linear and affine connections, geodesics, and the exponential map**, specializing the general theory to the frame bundle, plus the standard apparatus of Lie groups and homogeneous spaces underlying $G$.

## Why the project cites it

The project's central conceit is that beliefs are sections of a fibre bundle and that inference is geometry on that bundle, so the bundle-and-connection formalism of Kobayashi–Nomizu is the literal mathematical backbone rather than a decorative analogy.

- **Agents as bundle sections.** The picture of [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] is given precise content by the principal/associated bundle construction here: an agent's belief is a section of an associated bundle over a base of contexts or positions, with the structure group encoding the gauge freedom in how local beliefs are framed.
- **Gauge structure of belief dynamics.** The [[Gauge transformation]] as a vertical bundle automorphism, and a connection 1-form as the gauge field, supply the formal objects behind the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]]. Changing an agent's local frame should leave physical content invariant, which is exactly the bundle-automorphism notion defined in Volume I.
- **Parallel transport of beliefs and holonomy of disagreement.** [[Parallel transport]] is the mechanism by which one agent's belief is carried to another's frame for comparison; the path-dependence captured by [[Holonomy]] (via Ambrose–Singer, curvature = infinitesimal holonomy) formalizes the idea that propagating beliefs around a social loop can fail to return them unchanged. This grounds the curvature/holonomy diagnostics used across the [[Gauge-Theoretic Multi-Agent VFE Model]] and the [[belief-inertia]] manuscript.
- **Bridge to information geometry.** When the associated bundle's fibre is a statistical manifold, the connection interacts with the [[Fisher information metric]] and the structures studied under [[Mass as Fisher information]]; Kobayashi–Nomizu provides the connection-theoretic side of that pairing that information geometry (e.g. Amari) supplies the metric side of.
- **Equivariance backbone for the transformer.** The same principal-bundle connection theory underlies gauge-equivariant deep learning ([[Gauge equivariant CNN]], [[Group equivariance]]) used in the [[VFE Transformer Program]], so this text is the shared foundation linking the geometric-deep-learning machinery to the multi-agent belief model.

> [!note] Editorial: This note summarizes the standard, well-known content of Volume I (principal bundles, connections, curvature, holonomy). The project-specific interpretations above are the author's framing; only the differential-geometric claims are attributable to Kobayashi–Nomizu itself.
