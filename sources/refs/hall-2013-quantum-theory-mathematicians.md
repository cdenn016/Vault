# Quantum Theory for Mathematicians

> [!info] Citation
> Hall, B. C. (2013). *Quantum Theory for Mathematicians*. Graduate Texts in Mathematics, Vol. 267. Springer, New York. ISBN 978-1-4614-7115-8. DOI: 10.1007/978-1-4614-7116-5.

## TL;DR

A mathematically rigorous development of quantum mechanics for a mathematics audience: Hilbert spaces, unbounded self-adjoint operators and the spectral theorem, Stone's theorem, canonical quantization, the semiclassical limit, and a substantial treatment of **Lie groups and Lie algebras in quantum mechanics** (angular momentum / $SU(2)$, the Wigner–Eckart theorem) and **geometric quantization**. A companion in rigor and notation to Hall's Lie-groups text ([[hall-2015-lie-groups]]).

## What it establishes

The functional-analytic backbone of QM done carefully (domains, self-adjointness, the spectral theorem, Stone's theorem on one-parameter unitary groups), followed by the group-theoretic structure of quantum systems and an introduction to geometric quantization — the construction of a quantum Hilbert space from a classical symplectic manifold with a chosen polarization.

## Why the project cites it

A peripheral but coherent reference for the **rigorous operator and Lie-group foundations** behind the program's quantum-foundations and representation-theoretic threads. Two contact points justify keeping it: the geometric-quantization material connects symplectic/[[Hamiltonian belief dynamics|Hamiltonian]] structure to a Hilbert-space representation (the same classical→quantum passage the [[Participatory realism and quantum foundations]] theme circles), and the $SU(2)$/angular-momentum and [[Irreducible representation|irreducible-representation]] development is a rigorous companion to the gauge/representation machinery ([[Group equivariance]], [[Gauge transformation]]) the program uses. It is the most marginal of the gauge/representation references here, included for rigor and cross-reference rather than for any directly reused result.
