# The Holographic Principle

> [!info] Citation
> Raphael Bousso (2002). "The holographic principle." *Reviews of Modern Physics* 74(3): 825–874. DOI: [10.1103/RevModPhys.74.825](https://doi.org/10.1103/RevModPhys.74.825). Preprint: [arXiv:hep-th/0203101](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0203101).

## TL;DR

Bousso's review consolidates the holographic principle — the claim that the maximum information (entropy) in a spacetime region is bounded by the area of an appropriate bounding surface in Planck units, not by its volume. He develops the *covariant entropy bound* (the Bousso bound), which formulates the principle generally on light-sheets and remains valid in dynamical, cosmological settings where earlier area bounds fail. The article is the standard reference for the precise statement of holography as an information bound.

## What it establishes

- A general, covariant entropy bound: the entropy passing through a light-sheet of a surface is at most one quarter its area.
- A unified information-theoretic formulation of holography subsuming Bekenstein's bound, 't Hooft–Susskind dimensional reduction, and the area scaling of black-hole entropy.
- The benchmark formulation against which "it from bit" information bounds are stated quantitatively.
