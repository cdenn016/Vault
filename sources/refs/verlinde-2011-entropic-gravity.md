# On the Origin of Gravity and the Laws of Newton

> [!info] Citation
> Erik P. Verlinde (2011). "On the origin of gravity and the laws of Newton." *Journal of High Energy Physics* 2011(4): 29. DOI: [10.1007/JHEP04(2011)029](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP04(2011)029). Preprint: [arXiv:1001.0785](https://arxiv.org/abs/1001.0785).

## TL;DR

Verlinde argues that gravity is not a fundamental interaction but an *entropic force*: a statistical tendency of a system to maximize entropy, analogous to the restoring force of a stretched polymer. Combining the holographic storage of information on screens with the equipartition of energy and the Unruh relation between temperature and acceleration, he recovers Newton's $F = ma$, Newton's inverse-square law, and (relativistically) the Einstein equations, with mass and inertia emerging from changes in the information content of holographic screens.

## What it establishes

- Newtonian gravity arises as an entropic force from the displacement of matter relative to information-bearing holographic screens, with $\Delta S$ set by displacement and the screen's bit count fixed by its area.
- Inertia and the equivalence principle are reframed thermodynamically; the Einstein equations follow in the relativistic generalization.
- Gravity is fully emergent and informational — perhaps the most aggressive "gravity = information dynamics" claim in the literature, and correspondingly the most debated (see [[carlip-2014-challenges-emergent-gravity]]).
