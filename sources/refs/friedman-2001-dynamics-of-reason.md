# Dynamics of Reason

> [!info] Citation
> Friedman, Michael (2001). *Dynamics of Reason: The 1999 Kant Lectures at Stanford University*. Stanford, CA: CSLI Publications.

## TL;DR

Friedman revives and updates the *relativized a priori* (originating with Reichenbach) to give a rationalist answer to Kuhnian incommensurability. He distinguishes three layers in a scientific framework: empirical laws, the *constitutive a priori* principles (e.g. the mathematics and coordinating principles that first make those laws empirically meaningful), and philosophical meta-frameworks that guide and rationalize the transition between successive constitutive frameworks. Paradigm change is real and deep, but it is *rationally bridgeable* through this philosophical-meta-framework layer, so revolutions are not the arational gestalt-switches Kuhn sometimes suggested.

## What it establishes

- **Relativized, constitutive a priori.** Each framework has a priori constitutive principles that are framework-relative and revisable, not eternal — see [[Relativized a priori]].
- **Three-tiered structure of science.** Empirical / constitutive-a-priori / meta-framework; objectivity and meaning are constituted at the middle tier.
- **Rational inter-paradigm transitions.** The meta-framework layer supplies continuity and rational adjudication across revolutions, tempering Kuhnian incommensurability.

## Why the project cites it

Friedman is the manuscript's proximate source for the [[Relativized a priori]] and constitutive-frameworks idea, mediating between [[kant-1781-critique-pure-reason|Kant 1781]] (constitutive but absolute a priori), its relativization in [[reichenbach-1920-relativity-apriori]], and the framework dynamics of [[kuhn-1962-structure|Kuhn 1962]]. For the program, Friedman's constitutive principles are read as *gauge/framework choices*: the conventions that fix the meaning of an agent's observations within a paradigm, framework-relative yet revisable. His account of *rational* transitions between frameworks is exactly what the manuscript needs to make "Kuhnian revolutions as collective gauge transformations" more than a metaphor — a re-gauging across the community of [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] that, like Friedman's meta-framework, preserves enough structure to be intelligible across the shift. This supports the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] thesis that objectivity is framework-constituted, not given.
