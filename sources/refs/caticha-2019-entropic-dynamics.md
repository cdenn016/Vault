# The Entropic Dynamics Approach to Quantum Mechanics

> [!info] Citation
> Ariel Caticha (2019). "The Entropic Dynamics Approach to Quantum Mechanics." *Entropy* 21(10): 943. DOI: [10.3390/e21100943](https://doi.org/10.3390/e21100943). Preprint: [arXiv:1908.04693](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.04693).

## TL;DR

Caticha derives quantum mechanics not as a fundamental theory but as an application of *entropic inference*: the dynamics of the wave function follow from the principle of maximum entropy applied to probabilities over configuration space, with a constraint enforced through a drift potential. The geometry that organizes this construction is the [[Fisher information metric]] on the statistical manifold of beliefs, so that the Schrödinger equation emerges as an information-geometric flow rather than a postulate.

## What it establishes

The paper is a review and synthesis of the Entropic Dynamics (ED) program. Its core claims, all grounded in the entropic-inference machinery, are:

- **Dynamics from inference.** Time evolution is generated by maximizing entropy subject to constraints, so each infinitesimal update of the probability distribution is itself a Bayesian/entropic inference step. There is no separate dynamical postulate; motion is "inference in progress."
- **Information geometry as the substrate.** The space of probability distributions carries the [[Fisher information metric]] as its natural Riemannian structure. The drift, fluctuations, and the emergent Hamiltonian flow are read off this geometry, tying the construction to the broader program of information geometry and the [[Natural gradient]] family of updates.
- **Quantum mechanics as a derived special case.** With an appropriate constraint (a global phase / drift potential) and a notion of entropic time, the Schrödinger equation is recovered. Probability is fundamental; the wave function's phase is an epistemic bookkeeping device for the constraints, not an independent ontic field.
- **Participatory / epistemic stance.** ED treats the quantum state as a representation of an agent's information, aligning the framework with epistemic and [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] readings of quantum theory rather than with hidden-variable realism.

> [!note] Editorial: This note summarizes the ED program at the level of its conceptual architecture (entropy maximization, Fisher geometry, entropic time). It does not reproduce the paper's detailed derivations or specific equations.

## Why the project cites it

The project's manuscripts build dynamics for *beliefs* — probability distributions held by agents — out of variational and information-geometric principles, and Caticha's ED is the cleanest physics precedent for "dynamics as inference." The connections are concrete:

- **Inference-generated dynamics for beliefs.** ED's central move — deriving time evolution from successive maximum-entropy updates — is the template behind the project's [[Hamiltonian belief dynamics]] and [[Belief inertia]]: belief trajectories are not imposed but fall out of an entropic/variational update rule, mirroring the way [[Variational free energy]] minimization drives [[Multi-agent variational free energy]] models.
- **Fisher geometry as the metric of belief space.** ED elevates the [[Fisher information metric]] to the role of the dynamical metric on the statistical manifold. The project reuses exactly this identification — most directly in [[Mass as Fisher information]], where inertial "mass" in the belief dynamics is the Fisher information, the same quantity that sets the metric and fluctuation scale in ED.
- **Information geometry and natural-gradient flow.** Because ED phrases evolution as motion governed by the Fisher metric, it sits squarely in the [[Information geometry and natural gradient]] theme and connects to the [[Natural gradient]] used throughout the project's inference machinery. See companion references [[amari-2016-information-geometry-applications]] and [[ay-2017-information-geometry]].
- **Entropy-first, participatory ontology.** ED's reading of the quantum state as encoded information supports the project's [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] thread (manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]]) and its use of [[Meta-entropy]] as an organizing quantity, complementing references such as [[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]] and [[fuchs-2014-qbism]].
- **Bridge to the gauge picture.** Treating agents as carriers of beliefs evolving by entropic inference under constraints connects to the project's geometric framing of [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]]: the constraint (drift) potential in ED plays a role analogous to a connection guiding belief transport, linking entropic dynamics to [[Gauge transformation]] and [[Parallel transport]] in the multi-agent VFE model.
