# Reconstructing Scientific Revolutions: Thomas S. Kuhn's Philosophy of Science

> [!info] Citation
> Hoyningen-Huene, Paul (1993). *Reconstructing Scientific Revolutions: Thomas S. Kuhn's Philosophy of Science*, trans. Alexander T. Levine. Chicago: University of Chicago Press. (Foreword by Thomas S. Kuhn.)

## TL;DR

The standard systematic reconstruction of Kuhn's philosophy of science, organizing the scattered and evolving claims of *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* into a coherent framework. Hoyningen-Huene foregrounds Kuhn's neo-Kantian, "world-constitution" reading: paradigms function as constitutive frameworks that structure the phenomenal world of practitioners, so the "different worlds" of incommensurable paradigms are read constitutively rather than as mere rhetoric.

## Why the project cites it

This is the rigorous companion to [[kuhn-1962-structure|Kuhn 1962]] that the manuscript pairs with Kuhn to support its "Kuhnian revolutions as collective gauge transformations" subsection. Hoyningen-Huene's neo-Kantian, world-constituting reading of paradigms is exactly the interpretation the project needs: it makes paradigms *constitutive frameworks* (akin to [[Relativized a priori]] choices, [[friedman-2001-dynamics-of-reason]], [[reichenbach-1920-relativity-apriori]]) rather than sociological labels, licensing the formal analogy in which a paradigm is a shared frame/gauge and a revolution a collective re-gauging across [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] under a global [[Gauge transformation]]. It thereby strengthens the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] reading of scientific change in [[participatory-it-from-bit]].
