# Introduction to Mechanics and Symmetry

> [!info] Citation
> Marsden, J. E. & Ratiu, T. S. (1999). *Introduction to Mechanics and Symmetry: A Basic Exposition of Classical Mechanical Systems*, 2nd ed. Texts in Applied Mathematics, Vol. 17. Springer, New York. ISBN 978-0-387-98643-2.

## TL;DR

The modern reference for **geometric mechanics**: Lagrangian and Hamiltonian dynamics built on symplectic and Poisson manifolds, with symmetry handled intrinsically through **momentum maps** and **symplectic / Poisson reduction**. Where [[arnold-1989-classical-mechanics|Arnold]] gives the classical symplectic foundation, Marsden–Ratiu centers the role of Lie-group symmetry: Noether's theorem becomes the equivariance of a momentum map, and reducing a system by its symmetry group (Marsden–Weinstein reduction) is the systematic way to pass to the physically meaningful, gauge-fixed phase space. It is the canonical bridge between Hamiltonian dynamics and Lie-group / gauge structure.

## What it establishes

The book develops mechanics coordinate-freely and organizes everything around symmetry:

- **Symplectic and Poisson manifolds** as the state spaces of Hamiltonian systems; Hamilton's equations as a vector field generated by $H$ through the symplectic form.
- **Momentum maps** $J: P \to \mathfrak{g}^*$ encoding the conserved quantities associated with a Lie-group action (linear/angular momentum as the momentum map of translation/rotation); Noether's theorem as the statement that $J$ is conserved along the flow when $H$ is $G$-invariant.
- **Reduction** — quotienting a symmetric phase space by its group action (symplectic/Marsden–Weinstein and Poisson reduction) to obtain the reduced dynamics on a lower-dimensional space, the rigorous form of "fixing the gauge / dividing out the redundancy."
- Worked geometric treatments of the rigid body, fluids, and other systems where the configuration space is itself a Lie group and the dynamics is governed by Euler–Poincaré / Lie–Poisson equations.

## Why the project cites it

This is the program's reference for putting **belief momentum on a rigorous geometric footing**. The [[Hamiltonian belief dynamics|Hamiltonian reading of belief dynamics]] in [[belief-inertia]] posits a second-order equation of motion $M_i\ddot{\mu}_i + \gamma_i\dot{\mu}_i + \nabla_{\mu_i}\mathcal{F} = 0$ with an inertial [[Mass as Fisher information|mass given by the Fisher/precision tensor]]; Marsden–Ratiu supplies the symplectic/Poisson framework in which such a system lives, the conditions under which a momentum is genuinely conserved, and — crucially — the **reduction** machinery for handling the symmetry the program builds in. The program's belief space carries a [[Gauge transformation|gauge action]] (the local frames $\phi_i$ and the transport $\Omega_{ij}$), so the physically meaningful dynamics is the *reduced* dynamics after dividing out that redundancy, which is exactly Marsden–Weinstein reduction; and the program's claim of a conserved momentum current under reciprocal symmetric coupling is, in this language, the equivariance of a momentum map. The book is therefore the natural toolset both for making the inertial ansatz precise and for connecting the program's [[Killing form|Lie-algebraic]] gauge structure to its Hamiltonian one.
