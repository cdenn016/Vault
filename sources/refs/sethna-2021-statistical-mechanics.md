# Statistical Mechanics: Entropy, Order Parameters, and Complexity

> [!info] Citation
> James P. Sethna (2021). *Statistical Mechanics: Entropy, Order Parameters, and Complexity*, 2nd edition. Oxford University Press. DOI 10.1093/oso/9780198865247.001.0001.

## TL;DR

Sethna develops statistical mechanics around entropy, free energies, order parameters, fluctuations, symmetry, collective behavior, and renormalization. It is the most pedagogical bridge from probabilistic inference to configuration Gibbs measures and multi-scale population behavior.

## What it establishes

The book builds canonical and grand-canonical ensembles, thermodynamic potentials, mean-field reasoning, phase transitions, critical phenomena, and renormalization through problems that emphasize universality and emergence. It distinguishes exact ensemble definitions from saddle-point, mean-field, and scaling approximations.

## Relevance to this research

This is the primary statistical-mechanics background for [[Meta-entropy]], [[Multi-agent variational free energy]], and [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]]. It explains what is required to define a genuine Gibbs distribution over belief configurations and why a local energy sum is not by itself a thermodynamic ensemble. It should precede [[cardy-1996-scaling-renormalization]] when the program moves from state-level VFE to configuration-level or RG claims.
