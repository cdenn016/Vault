# Feynman Diagrams for the Yang-Mills Field

> [!info] Citation
> L. D. Faddeev and V. N. Popov (1967). “Feynman Diagrams for the Yang-Mills Field.” *Physics Letters B* **25**(1), 29–30. DOI: [10.1016/0370-2693(67)90067-6](https://doi.org/10.1016/0370-2693(67)90067-6).

## TL;DR

Faddeev and Popov give the functional-integral prescription that removes repeated integration over gauge-equivalent field configurations by imposing a gauge condition and including the associated determinant. The determinant can be represented by ghost fields in perturbative field theory. For gauge-VFE configuration thermodynamics, the transferable principle is measure correction on redundant coordinates, not the literal importation of quantum ghosts into the finite-dimensional model.

## What it establishes

Gauge invariance makes a naive functional integral overcount every gauge orbit and leaves a singular quadratic operator. A gauge condition selects representatives, while the Faddeev–Popov determinant accounts for the change of variables from redundant fields to gauge-fixed fields and orbit directions. Physical results are required to be independent of the auxiliary gauge choice when the construction is valid.

## Why the project cites it

The configuration integral in [[participatory-it-from-bit]] must choose between direct integration on a gauge quotient and a redundant-coordinate integral with gauge fixing and its Jacobian. This source supports the second route. It does not justify dividing by an infinite noncompact gauge volume without an explicit slice, determinant, boundary treatment, and integrability proof. Those restrictions are part of [[Meta-entropy]] and the local background-field construction.
