# A Relativistic Theory of Consciousness

> [!info] Citation
> Lahav, Nir & Neemeh, Zachariah A. (2022). "A Relativistic Theory of Consciousness." *Frontiers in Psychology*, 12, 704270. DOI: [10.3389/fpsyg.2021.704270](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2021.704270).

## TL;DR

Lahav and Neemeh propose that phenomenal consciousness is neither private nor illusory but **relativistic** — observer-frame-dependent in a sense modeled on special relativity. A cognitive system either has or lacks phenomenal consciousness *relative to a given frame of reference*: in the system's own cognitive frame, consciousness is observable (the first-person perspective); in other frames it is not (the third-person perspective). Both descriptions are correct, just as inertial observers can disagree about who is at rest. The hard problem, on this view, is dissolved by recognizing that the apparent contradiction between physical brain processes and felt experience is a frame-of-reference confusion.

## What it establishes

The paper argues, by analogy with the relativity of simultaneity and length, that the first-person/third-person divide reflects two legitimate frames rather than two incompatible kinds of fact. It sketches a "cognitive frame of reference" and claims that if the dynamics of one brain could be transformed to match another's, the two systems would share a frame and have identical experience — making experience frame-covariant rather than frame-absolute. The authors gesture at a transformation between frames as the missing formal ingredient, the way Lorentz transformations relate inertial frames in physics.

## Why the project cites it

This is the highest-priority cited consciousness source: the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] devotes a full subsection to *writing the transformation law that Lahav and Neemeh leave unspecified*. They assert a relativity of consciousness across cognitive frames but supply no explicit operator carrying experiential content from one frame to another; PIFB identifies that operator with the project's gauge transport. A cognitive frame becomes a local frame $\phi_i$ in the gauge bundle, and the change between frames is the transport $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ acting on belief content ([[Gauge transformation]], [[Holonomy]], [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]]). What was an analogy to relativity in Lahav and Neemeh becomes an actual covariant structure: the first-person/third-person distinction is the difference between reading a section in its own frame versus a transported frame, and frame-relative registered content is exactly the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] move from bit to belief. This also connects to the [[Mathematical consciousness science]] thread — the project's contribution is precisely to make their relativistic claim a *theory with a transformation group* rather than a metaphor — and it complements the controlled-hallucination reading of [[seth-2021-being-you]].

> [!note] Editorial: The correspondence is interpretive — Lahav and Neemeh use "relativistic" by analogy to physics, and the project supplies a definite (gauge-theoretic) transformation law they did not state. This is a constructive completion of their proposal, not a result they prove; the manuscript should present it as such.
