# Von Neumann Algebra Automorphisms and Time–Thermodynamics Relation (Thermal Time Hypothesis)

> [!info] Citation
> Alain Connes and Carlo Rovelli (1994). "Von Neumann algebra automorphisms and time–thermodynamics relation in generally covariant quantum theories." *Classical and Quantum Gravity* 11(12): 2899–2917. DOI: [10.1088/0264-9381/11/12/007](https://doi.org/10.1088/0264-9381/11/12/007). Preprint: [arXiv:gr-qc/9406019](https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9406019).

## TL;DR

Connes and Rovelli propose the *thermal time hypothesis*: in a generally covariant theory there is no preferred external time, so the flow of time is not fundamental but emerges from a state. Given a statistical (thermal) state on the algebra of observables, the modular (Tomita–Takesaki) automorphism group of the von Neumann algebra defines a one-parameter flow — the "thermal time" — that the observer experiences as physical time. Time is thus a state-dependent, observer-relative, thermodynamic notion rather than a background parameter.

## What it establishes

- The Tomita–Takesaki modular flow associated with a state on an observable algebra provides a canonical, state-determined notion of time evolution, even when no background time exists.
- Different thermal states give different modular flows, so time becomes relative to the observer's (statistical) state — a precise, algebraic version of relational/thermodynamic time.
- Connects the absence of fundamental time in quantum gravity to a derived, entropy-linked temporal flow.
