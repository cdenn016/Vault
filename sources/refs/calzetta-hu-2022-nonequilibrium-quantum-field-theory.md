# Nonequilibrium Quantum Field Theory

> [!info] Citation
> Esteban A. Calzetta and Bei-Lok B. Hu (2022). *Nonequilibrium Quantum Field Theory*. Open-access reissue. Cambridge University Press. DOI 10.1017/9781009290036. The original edition appeared in 2008.

## TL;DR

Calzetta and Hu develop nonequilibrium quantum field theory using closed-time-path methods, influence functionals, effective actions, and correlation dynamics. For this program, its specialized value is the explicit construction and interpretation of two-particle-irreducible effective actions.

## What it establishes

The book derives generating functionals and Legendre-transform effective actions, then develops dynamics for mean fields and correlation functions. The 2PI construction promotes the one-point field and two-point function to independent variational arguments subject to stationarity equations. Approximation quality and conservation properties depend on how the effective action is truncated.

## Relevance to this research

This is not a prerequisite for an ordinary ELBO, gauge-covariant Gaussian belief, or natural-gradient update. It becomes relevant only when the configuration-level theory introduces connected correlators and a genuine 2PI effective action. It should be read after [[sethna-2021-statistical-mechanics]], [[cardy-1996-scaling-renormalization]], and [[cornwall-jackiw-tomboulis-1974-2pi-effective-action]]. It supports [[Meta-entropy]] and [[Renormalization-group flow of beliefs]] while guarding against calling an arbitrary covariance correction “2PI.”
