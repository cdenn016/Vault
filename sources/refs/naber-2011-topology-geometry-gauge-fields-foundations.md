# Topology, Geometry and Gauge Fields: Foundations

> [!info] Citation
> Gregory L. Naber (2011). *Topology, Geometry and Gauge Fields: Foundations*, 2nd edition. Texts in Applied Mathematics, vol. 25. Springer. DOI 10.1007/978-1-4419-7254-5.

## TL;DR

Naber is the pedagogical bridge between abstract bundle geometry and gauge-field practice. It develops the topology and differential geometry required for principal bundles and then connects those structures to gauge potentials, field strengths, and physical examples.

## What it establishes

The book motivates manifolds, homotopy, bundles, connections, and curvature through problems originating in mathematical physics. Its value is not that it replaces a systematic geometry text, but that it shows how local gauge potentials and global bundle data represent the same connection in different trivializations.

## Relevance to this research

Read Naber after [[lee-2012-smooth-manifolds]], [[hall-2015-lie-groups]], and alongside [[tu-2017-differential-geometry-connections-curvature]]. It connects the project's frame variables and transport matrices to standard gauge language while making clear which constructions are flat, which have curvature, and which remain analogies. It supports [[Gauge transformation]], [[Parallel transport]], [[Holonomy]], and [[GL(K) gauge group]].
