# Information is Physical: Cross-Perspective Links in Relational Quantum Mechanics

> [!info] Citation
> Adlam, E., & Rovelli, C. (2022). "Information is physical: cross-perspective links in relational quantum mechanics." *Philosophy of Physics* (2023). Preprint: arXiv:2203.13342.

## TL;DR

Adlam and Rovelli identify and repair a tension in relational quantum mechanics (RQM): RQM insists information is physical, yet in its standard formulation the facts one observer holds are inaccessible in principle to any other, which makes inter-observer agreement mysterious. They add a new postulate — **cross-perspective links** — requiring that whenever an observer has obtained a definite value for a variable, that information is stored in physical variables and is therefore *accessible by measurement to other observers*, who will find consistent values. This restores a controlled, physically grounded form of intersubjectivity to RQM without reintroducing observer-independent absolute facts: agreement is achieved through physical interaction, not posited a priori.

## What it establishes

- **The accessibility postulate.** Cross-perspective links demand that a fact relative to one observer, once stably recorded, can be read by another observer through physical interaction, yielding consistent records. This is RQM's mechanism for *building* inter-observer agreement.
- **Information is genuinely physical.** The postulate makes precise the RQM slogan that information must be carried by physical variables, closing the gap between "facts are relational" and "yet observers manifestly agree."
- **No absolute facts required.** Consistency across perspectives is secured without a frame-independent ledger; agreement is a physical achievement, compatible with the no-go results of [[brukner-2018-no-go-observer-facts]] and [[frauchiger-renner-2018-no-self-description]].
