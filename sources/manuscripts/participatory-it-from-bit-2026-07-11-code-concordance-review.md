## High-severity findings

### The active frame update is not equivariant under the advertised common-right GL+(K) action

The manuscript states that natural-gradient frame updates preserve the orbit generated by $U_i\mapsto U_i g$. The active Frobenius path forms

$$
B=U^\top\nabla_U F,\qquad U^+=U\exp(-\eta B).
$$

For a scalar objective invariant under common-right translation, the Euclidean gradient transforms as $\nabla_{Ug}F=\nabla_UF g^{-\top}$, so the implemented body covector transforms as $B'=g^\top B g^{-\top}$. Equivariance of the right retraction instead requires $B'=g^{-1}Bg$. Those laws agree on restricted transformations such as orthogonal $g$ but not on generic nonorthogonal elements of $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$.

A two-frame Gaussian-KL fixture preserved the pre-update scalar loss under a nonorthogonal common-right transformation. After one active update with $\eta=10^{-3}$, the relative transport residual was $1.4779626631\times10^{-2}$ and the post-update losses were $0.9264730017$ and $0.9221688151$. Gradient clipping and the spectral bound were inactive. This result does not refute common-pushforward invariance of Gaussian KL. It refutes the stronger claim that the reached optimizer follows full-$\mathrm{GL}^+$ gauge-equivalent trajectories.

The theory and implementation must choose one contract: restrict orbit-preservation claims to a right-isometric subgroup, state an explicit gauge fixing, or implement and prove a right-equivariant update. This limits the optimizer claim summarized in [[GL(K) gauge group]] and [[Gauge equivariance and geometric deep learning]].

### The hierarchy is not closed on the domain required by its frame logarithm

Initialization uses one matrix exponential, while later active updates multiply the frame by additional exponentials. A product of real exponentials belongs to $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ but need not itself possess a real logarithm. Meta-agent formation nevertheless calls `matrix_log_principal` unconditionally when pooling the reached belief and model frames.

For $K=7$, the exact fixture

$$
H=\operatorname{diag}(-1,-2,1,1,1,1,1),
$$

has positive determinant, singular values in $[1,2]$, and passes the active determinant and conditioning guards. It has no real logarithm because each of its two distinct negative eigenvalues has an odd number of Jordan blocks. It is reachable as a product of legal active-form increments,

$$
H=\exp(\pi J)\exp\left(\operatorname{diag}(0,\log 2,0,\ldots,0)\right),
$$

where $J_{01}=-1$ and $J_{10}=1$. Splitting the rotation into seven steps and the dilation into two keeps the maximum step spectral norm below $0.5$. The reproduced trajectory reached $H$ with relative residual $6.29\times10^{-16}$, after which `matrix_log_principal` raised a `ValueError` with worst round-trip error $0.7071$.

The hierarchy therefore needs a monitored log-safe invariant domain, a restricted operative group closed under its chart and updates, or a log-free group-level barycenter. The result constrains the pooling claims on [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]] and the frame-domain account on [[GL(K) gauge group]].

### The reached Gibbs temperature does not support the covariance-truncation bound

The manuscript uses a reference temperature $\tau_q=0.05$ and a membership threshold $\Gamma_{\min}=0.5$ to obtain $\overline{\mathrm{KL}}<\tau_q\log 2\approx0.035$ nats, then invokes that value to control the between-child mean-dispersion term omitted by the parent covariance pool. The checked-in configuration omits explicit detector temperatures and sets `kl_threshold=14`. The loader therefore makes $\tau_q=\tau_s=14$. The corresponding pass band is

$$
\overline{\mathrm{KL}}<14\log 2\approx9.704\text{ nats},
$$

while the reached `covariance_collapse="moment"` path still omits between-child mean dispersion. The claimed $0.035$-nat control does not apply to this executable arm. The implementation must use and verify the stated temperature, retain dispersion through a matching collapse, or report a different measured bound.

### The prospective primary arm does not execute the mandatory belief-shadow relation

The manuscript treats $p_i^{(s)}=\Omega_{iI}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ and the corresponding model shadow as structural cross-scale relations. The reached primary arm has both top-down propagation flags disabled. Descendant belief priors remain independently learned parameters. Its T6 ancestor contribution is a model-fiber alignment penalty, not an assignment of the parent belief into $p_i$, and the enabled apex closure does not supply lower-scale belief shadows.

The prospective protocol therefore cannot test the relation it calls operative. It needs a declared arm with top-down belief propagation enabled and recorded, or the equations must be scoped as formal or opt-in branches. Model-shadow assignment, T2, and T6 also require a non-duplicative accounting rule. This executable-status correction is reflected in [[Ouroboros multi-scale dynamics]] and [[Meta-agents and hierarchical emergence]].

### One-shot topology makes hierarchy persistence non-independent

The prospective validation protocol requests stable identifiers, creation and retirement events, constituent membership, and a predeclared persistence window. The reached tower creates a next scale only when it does not yet exist. After formation, newly detected parents are discarded and the parent-child maps are not reconciled, replaced, or retired during the live run. A formed hierarchy therefore persists by construction.

Persistence cannot discriminate stable emergence from immutable bookkeeping on this path. Either the implementation needs time-indexed membership reconciliation, replacement, retirement, and identity rules, or the paper must call the structure a one-shot genealogy and remove persistence as independent evidence.

## Medium- and low-severity scope corrections

Six additional discrepancies require local correction. First, neither emitted nonequilibrium proxy equals the manuscript's declared normalized statistic: one substitutes energy variance across scales for gradient variance across agents, while the other omits information flux, and the fixed observation target supplies a static constraint rather than time-dependent work. Second, the reached base is $S^2$ with nonuniform volume weights, not flat $\mathbb{R}^2$. Third, the active study contains the `lambda_smooth=0.01` frame regularizer despite an alignment-only claim. Fourth, the current recorder receives only the base system and cannot emit the requested full tower state, dynamic lineage, complete environment metadata, source-diff hashes, and file hashes. Fifth, stationary-point equivalence between the Cholesky-Euclidean and Fisher covariance paths holds only in the unsaturated interior chart; the active absolute value, diagonal clamps, and projected update admit boundary fixed points. Sixth, the universal derivative-validation statement exceeds the finite-difference test's named kernel coverage and its actual `atol=10^{-4}` and `rtol=10^{-3}` acceptance thresholds.

The low-severity correction concerns attention temperature. The checked-in MAgent arm stores separate belief and model temperatures with `learnable_kappa=False` and a zero free-energy-parameter learning rate. It does not execute the single learned shared scalar described for the working implementation; that description belongs only to a suitably configured companion transformer.

## Surfaces that survived review

The review confirmed common-pushforward invariance of Gaussian KL and found the inspected covariance transports using the required sandwich $\Omega\Sigma\Omega^\top$. The canonical entropy-retaining attention scalar and its envelope gradient matched the code. The exact renormalization-group pushforward identities remained correct under their stated assumptions, and the repaired conditional-Gaussian closure theorem kept the operational anharmonic remainder outside its exact branch.

The higher-scale continuum $R_2$ term is reachable when a scale has a grid, the sphere contraction uses the inverse metric, and the manuscript rejects the parent-stiffness mechanism. The corrected Role-A/Role-B gauge language, regulator obstruction, holonomy statement, Gamma normalization, Fenichel scope, maximum-scale convention, deterministic-CUDA caveat, and absence of historical emergence evidence also survived this pass.

Verification included an 86-test focused gate, independent reproduction of the common-right and real-log fixtures, a complete LaTeX and BibTeX build, and a final repository suite of 1,319 passing tests with 5 skips. The generated PDF had 164 pages and no unresolved references, citations, rerun request, overfull box, fatal error, or bibliography warning. These checks establish the review's internal evidence; they do not convert the manuscript's prospective experiment into completed empirical evidence.

## Required correction order

The first correction should make the frame-space theorem, frame metric, regularizer, update, and admitted subgroup describe the same action. The hierarchy must then be made closed on that frame domain, either through a log-safe invariant or a log-free pooling construction.

The second correction should align formation and feedback semantics. Detector temperature and covariance pooling need a paired contract. Belief-shadow propagation must be enabled in a declared arm or removed from the active ontology, and parent pulls from assignments and penalties must not be counted twice.

The third correction should make validation independent and reproducible. Dynamic membership or an explicit one-shot genealogy is required before persistence can carry evidentiary weight. The declared nonequilibrium observable, a time-dependent observation drive, a tower-aware recorder, lineage events, and a provenance manifest must exist before the prospective protocol can test its assigned claims.
