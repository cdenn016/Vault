# Consciousness in Action

> [!info] Citation
> Hurley, Susan L. (1998). "Consciousness in Action." Harvard University Press. https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674007963

## TL;DR
Hurley critiques the 'classical sandwich' model of mind (perception in, cognition central, action out) and develops a dynamic, sensorimotor account in which perception and action are deeply interdependent. The book entertains denial of strong unity-of-consciousness theses, which Bayne and Chalmers cite as evidence that the Phenomenal Unity Thesis is non-trivial.

## Relevance to this research
Cited by Bayne-Chalmers as a philosopher who explicitly entertains denying phenomenal unity, establishing the thesis's substantive content; its sensorimotor, action-coupled view of consciousness resonates with the active-inference grounding of the VFE program.
