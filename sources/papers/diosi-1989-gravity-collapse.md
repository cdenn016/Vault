# Models for Universal Reduction of Macroscopic Quantum Fluctuations

> [!info] Citation
> Diósi, Lajos (1989). "Models for Universal Reduction of Macroscopic Quantum Fluctuations." Physical Review A 40, 1165. https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevA.40.1165

## TL;DR
Diósi proposes a stochastic, nonlinear modification of the Schrödinger equation in which a universal gravity-related noise localizes the wavefunction in position, suppressing macroscopic quantum fluctuations. The model yields a mass- and size-dependent decoherence/collapse rate set by the Newtonian gravitational self-energy, providing a concrete dynamical realization of objective state reduction (later combined with Penrose's ideas into the Penrose-Diósi model).

## Relevance to this research
Supplies the explicit objective-collapse decoherence rate that experiments such as the Marshall mirror superposition and levitated optomechanics seek to test, and grounds the gravity-induced measurement/observer thread feeding the participatory it-from-bit (PIFB) program.
