# Irreversibility and Heat Generation in the Computing Process

> [!info] Citation
> Landauer, Rolf (1961). "Irreversibility and Heat Generation in the Computing Process." IBM Journal of Research and Development. https://doi.org/10.1147/rd.53.0183

## TL;DR
Establishes Landauer's principle: logically irreversible operations — notably erasing one bit of information — necessarily dissipate at least k_B T ln 2 of heat, tying information to thermodynamics. Reversible computation can in principle avoid this cost; only the destruction of information has an unavoidable energy price.

## Relevance to this research
Landauer's principle is a cornerstone of physics-from-information and the thermodynamics of computation underlying Lloyd's limits on the computational capacity of the universe; it grounds the it-from-bit thesis that information is physical and connects entropy/free-energy bookkeeping to the cost of belief updating.
