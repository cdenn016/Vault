# Compressed Sensing, Sparsity, and Dimensionality in Neuronal Information Processing and Data Analysis

> [!info] Citation
> Ganguli, Surya, Sompolinsky, Haim (2012). "Compressed Sensing, Sparsity, and Dimensionality in Neuronal Information Processing and Data Analysis." Annual Review of Neuroscience. https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-neuro-062111-150410

## TL;DR
Reviews how compressed sensing and sparsity let high-dimensional neural signals be recovered from few measurements, and how the curse of dimensionality shapes both neural information processing and the analysis of neural data. Argues that low effective dimensionality and sparse structure are pervasive in neuronal representations.

## Relevance to this research
Theoretical backbone for the claim that neural (and belief) representations occupy a low-dimensional, sparse manifold, supporting the program's dimensionality-reduction analyses of learned belief geometry and the intrinsic low-dimensionality of VFE representations.
