# #Republic: Divided Democracy in the Age of Social Media

> [!info] Citation
> Sunstein, C. R. (2017). *#Republic: Divided Democracy in the Age of Social Media*. Princeton University Press. ISBN 9780691175515. DOI: [10.1515/9781400884711](https://doi.org/10.1515/9781400884711).

## TL;DR
A book-length argument that personalized information environments — the self-curated "Daily Me" — combine with social media to fragment the shared public sphere. Sunstein contends that filtering, echo chambers, and cybercascades reduce serendipitous and cross-cutting encounters, intensify group polarization, and erode the common experiences a deliberative democracy requires. He proposes design and norm remedies that engineer serendipity and exposure to opposing views, framing a well-functioning information commons as a precondition for self-government.

## What it establishes
The book is conceptual and normative rather than a formal model. Its core constructs are the "Daily Me" (an information diet tailored to pre-existing preferences), echo chambers (enclaves of like-minded exposure), cybercascades (rapid social propagation of beliefs along homophilic ties), and "polarization entrepreneurs" who exploit fragmentation. It connects these to the group-polarization law — enclave deliberation drives members toward extremes — and argues that architecture choices in information systems, not just individual psychology, determine whether a public stays connected or splinters.

## Relevance to this research
This is the canonical conceptual statement of the echo-chamber thesis that the program seeks to formalize mechanistically. It supplies the qualitative phenomenology — enclave deliberation, cascades, the Daily Me, polarization entrepreneurs — that the VFE dynamics aim to reproduce from a single free-energy functional, with homophilic attention coupling standing in for the Daily Me and within-cluster extremization for enclave deliberation. Honestly, this is adjacent-to-strong: framing and motivation rather than a quantitative model the functional uses. Its value is naming the targets the model must hit and the design levers (serendipity, cross-cutting exposure) the model could be used to evaluate.
