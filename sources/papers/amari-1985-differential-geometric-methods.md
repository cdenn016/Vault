# Differential-Geometrical Methods in Statistics

> [!info] Citation
> Amari, Shun-ichi (1985). "Differential-Geometrical Methods in Statistics." Lecture Notes in Statistics, vol. 28, Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4612-5056-2

## TL;DR
Amari's foundational monograph establishing information geometry: the statistical manifold of a parametric model equipped with the Fisher metric and the one-parameter family of alpha-connections (dual exponential/mixture connections). It develops the dually-flat structure, alpha-divergences, and the geometric theory of statistical estimation and higher-order asymptotics.

## Relevance to this research
The geometric backbone of the program: the Fisher metric and dual alpha-connections underwrite natural-gradient belief updates and the SPD/statistical-manifold geometry used throughout the VFE transformer and multi-agent models.
