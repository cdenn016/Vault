# Probability Theory: The Logic of Science

> [!info] Citation
> Jaynes, Edwin T. (2003). "Probability Theory: The Logic of Science." Cambridge University Press. https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511790423

## TL;DR
Jaynes's posthumous treatise develops probability theory as an extension of Aristotelian logic to reasoning under uncertainty, following Cox's theorem and the maximum-entropy principle. It argues that probabilities are objective-but-epistemic degrees of plausibility and grounds Bayesian inference, the MaxEnt method, and the role of prior information.

## Relevance to this research
The canonical statement of the Bayesian/MaxEnt epistemic view of probability that underpins the VFE program's treatment of beliefs and the participatory-realism reading of probability as degree of belief. The MaxEnt principle connects to variational free energy and information geometry across the program.
