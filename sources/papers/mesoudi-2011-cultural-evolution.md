# Cultural Evolution: How Darwinian Theory Can Explain Human Culture and Synthesize the Social Sciences

> [!info] Citation
> Mesoudi, A. (2011). *Cultural Evolution: How Darwinian Theory Can Explain Human Culture and Synthesize the Social Sciences*. University of Chicago Press, Chicago. ISBN 978-0-226-52044-5.

## TL;DR
A unifying textbook arguing that human culture is a genuine Darwinian process — exhibiting variation, competition/selection, and inheritance — and that the empirical and theoretical toolkit of evolutionary biology can therefore be ported wholesale to study it. Mesoudi makes the case that cultural evolution can serve as an integrating framework for the otherwise fragmented social sciences, reviewing the macroevolutionary (phylogenetic, archaeological) and microevolutionary (experimental, modeling) methods that make the analogy operational rather than merely metaphorical.

## What it establishes
The book systematizes the field around the Darwinian triad applied to culture: variation among cultural variants, biased and unbiased transmission acting as the analog of selection and drift, and social learning as the inheritance channel. It surveys the population-thinking models of Cavalli-Sforza–Feldman and Boyd–Richerson, the menu of transmission biases (content, conformist, prestige), and — pivotally for empirical work — the *transmission-chain* and *replacement* experimental paradigms, in which information passed serially through a chain of participants reveals how social transmission distorts, stabilizes, or amplifies content. It also addresses microevolution–macroevolution links via cultural phylogenetics and the use of historical and cross-cultural datasets.

## Relevance to this research
This is the clearest single-volume map of the cultural-evolution field and its methods, and a natural anchor textbook for the "social learning is belief updating" premise underlying the VFE coupling. The experimental transmission-chain paradigm it foregrounds is precisely an operationalization of belief updating from others — serial reconstruction under cognitive and social biases — which is the empirical phenomenon the pairwise coupling $\beta_{ij}\,\mathrm{KL}(q_i\,\|\,\Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ models at the individual level and which transmission-chain data could be used to test. The relevance is orienting and methodological rather than machinery-supplying: it locates the program within an existing Darwinian-cultural research tradition and points to the experiments that bear on it.
