# The Emperor's New Mind: Concerning Computers, Minds, and the Laws of Physics

> [!info] Citation
> Penrose, Roger (1989). "The Emperor's New Mind: Concerning Computers, Minds, and the Laws of Physics." Oxford University Press. https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-emperors-new-mind-9780198784920

## TL;DR
Penrose's influential book argues that human consciousness and mathematical understanding are non-algorithmic and cannot be captured by any Turing-computable process. He invokes Goedelian incompleteness to claim that mathematical insight transcends formal computation, and proposes that an as-yet-undiscovered theory of quantum gravity (objective state reduction) underlies mind, laying groundwork for the later Orch-OR proposal.

## Relevance to this research
A foundational anti-computationalist position on mind that the participatory-realism cluster engages with: it frames the observer/consciousness role in physics that PIFB and the multi-agent VFE program reinterpret information-theoretically, and connects to the Penrose gravitational-collapse line (Diosi-Penrose, Orch-OR) already in the vault.
