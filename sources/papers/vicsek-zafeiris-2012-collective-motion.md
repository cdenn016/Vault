# Collective motion

> [!info] Citation
> Vicsek, T., & Zafeiris, A. (2012). *Collective motion*. Physics Reports, 517(3–4), 71–140. DOI 10.1016/j.physrep.2012.03.004.

## TL;DR
The comprehensive review of collective motion across all scales of biological and physical organization, from molecular motors, bacteria, and cells through insect swarms, fish schools, and bird flocks, up to human crowds and traffic. It surveys the experiments, the agent-based and continuum models, and the unifying theoretical themes — order parameters, symmetry breaking, nonequilibrium phase transitions, and the universality of simple alignment rules — that organize the field.

## What it establishes
The review provides the taxonomy of the field. It classifies models by ingredients (self-propulsion, alignment, attraction/repulsion, noise, topological vs metric neighborhoods) and by description level (microscopic agent-based, mesoscopic kinetic, macroscopic hydrodynamic). It catalogues the canonical order parameter $\varphi = \frac{1}{N v_0}|\sum_i \mathbf{v}_i|$ and the order–disorder transitions it diagnoses, and reviews continuum (Toner–Tu-style) theories of polar active matter. It surveys empirical evidence — including the topological-interaction finding in starling flocks — and connects collective motion to applications in biology, robotics, and social systems. It is the natural overview reference for situating any specific flocking result.

## Relevance to this research
This is the single most useful survey for situating the whole flocking / collective-motion field. It supplies the taxonomy of alignment models, order parameters, and phase transitions that the program's consensus dynamics recapitulate in belief space, and serves as the overview reference card that frames where any individual model (Vicsek, Toner–Tu, Couzin, Ballerini) sits.
