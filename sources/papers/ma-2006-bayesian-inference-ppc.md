# Bayesian inference with probabilistic population codes

> [!info] Citation
> Ma, W. J., Beck, J. M., Latham, P. E., Pouget, A. (2006). "Bayesian inference with probabilistic population codes." Nature Neuroscience. https://doi.org/10.1038/nn1790

## TL;DR
Shows that neural populations with Poisson-like (exponential-family) variability can represent probability distributions such that optimal Bayesian cue combination is implemented by simply summing the population activities. The gain (total spike count) of a probabilistic population code (PPC) encodes the precision/certainty of the represented distribution, so reliability-weighted integration falls out for free.

## Relevance to this research
A foundational probabilistic-population-coding result underpinning precision-weighted neural inference; it grounds the program's identification of neural gain with precision (Fisher information) and motivates precision-weighted attention as Bayesian cue combination.
