# Interoceptive inference, emotion, and the embodied self

> [!info] Citation
> Seth, Anil K. (2013). "Interoceptive inference, emotion, and the embodied self." Trends in Cognitive Sciences. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2013.09.007

## TL;DR
Seth recasts emotion and selfhood as the product of predictive (active-inference) processing applied to interoceptive signals — the brain's Bayesian estimation of the internal physiological state of the body. Subjective feeling states and the experience of body ownership emerge from interoceptive prediction error minimization, extending the free-energy / predictive-coding framework from exteroception to the visceral domain.

## Relevance to this research
Provides a free-energy / active-inference grounding for embodied selfhood and emotion, connecting the VFE program's belief-updating machinery to interoception and the construction of the experiencing self, relevant to the participatory and multi-agent threads.
