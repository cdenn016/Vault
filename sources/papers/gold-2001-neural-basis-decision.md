# Neural computations that underlie decisions about sensory stimuli

> [!info] Citation
> Gold, Joshua I., Shadlen, Michael N. (2001). "Neural computations that underlie decisions about sensory stimuli." Trends in Cognitive Sciences. https://doi.org/10.1016/S1364-6613(00)01567-9

## TL;DR
Reviews how neural circuits compute perceptual decisions by accumulating noisy sensory evidence toward a threshold, linking the log-likelihood-ratio (sequential probability ratio) framework to firing rates in areas such as LIP. Argues decision formation is the temporal integration of difference signals between competing sensory pools.

## Relevance to this research
Provides the neural-mechanistic grounding for sequential-sampling / drift-diffusion accounts of inference, connecting evidence accumulation and decision thresholds to the program's treatment of belief updating and the speed-accuracy tradeoff.
