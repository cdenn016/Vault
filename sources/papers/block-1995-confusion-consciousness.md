# On a Confusion About a Function of Consciousness

> [!info] Citation
> Block, Ned (1995). "On a Confusion About a Function of Consciousness." Behavioral and Brain Sciences. https://doi.org/10.1017/S0140525X00038188

## TL;DR
Block's landmark paper introducing the distinction between phenomenal consciousness (P-consciousness, the subjective feel) and access consciousness (A-consciousness, content poised for reasoning, report, and behavioral control), arguing that conflating them produces a 'confusion about a function of consciousness'. The access/phenomenal distinction became foundational to the philosophy and science of consciousness.

## Relevance to this research
Supplies the access/phenomenal distinction that Bayne and Chalmers cross-classify with their unity taxonomy, and which the VFE program maps onto local-accessibility versus the global free-energy minimum.
