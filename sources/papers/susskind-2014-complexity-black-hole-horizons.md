# Computational Complexity and Black Hole Horizons

> [!info] Citation
> Leonard Susskind (2014/2016). "Computational complexity and black hole horizons." *Fortschritte der Physik* 64(1): 24–43. DOI: [10.1002/prop.201500092](https://doi.org/10.1002/prop.201500092). Preprint: [arXiv:1402.5674](https://arxiv.org/abs/1402.5674).

## TL;DR

Susskind argues that the *computational complexity* of a quantum state — roughly, the minimal number of elementary gates needed to prepare it — is the boundary quantity dual to the growth of geometric features behind a black-hole horizon (the volume or length of the Einstein–Rosen bridge). Whereas entanglement saturates quickly, the wormhole keeps growing for an exponentially long time, tracking the continued growth of complexity. This launches the "complexity = geometry" program (later sharpened to complexity = volume and complexity = action).

## Problem & setting

Within the ER=EPR picture, the interior of an eternal black hole grows for far longer than entanglement entropy can explain. Susskind proposes that a different information-theoretic quantity — circuit complexity — is what the growing bulk geometry encodes.

## Method

Heuristic correspondence and scaling arguments relating the late-time linear growth of ERB volume to the expected linear-then-saturating growth of quantum circuit complexity, framed within AdS/CFT and the ER=EPR conjecture.

## Key results

The conjecture that bulk geometric growth behind horizons is dual to boundary computational complexity, opening a computational-information reading of spacetime that goes beyond entanglement entropy.
