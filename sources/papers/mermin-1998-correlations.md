# What Is Quantum Mechanics Trying to Tell Us?

> [!info] Citation
> Mermin, N. D. (1998). "What is quantum mechanics trying to tell us?" *American Journal of Physics* **66**(9), 753–767. DOI: 10.1119/1.18955. Preprint: arXiv:quant-ph/9801057.

## TL;DR

Mermin proposes that the proper subject matter of quantum physics is *correlation and only correlation* — his "Ithaca interpretation," summarized by the slogan **correlations without correlata**. Quantum theory tells us about the correlations between subsystems while remaining silent about the absolute, correlate properties of the subsystems themselves; the relations are physical and definite even when the relata are not. This dissolves much of the measurement puzzle by relocating physical content from intrinsic system properties to the relational structure among them, and it explicitly bridges the relational ([[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]]) and emerging QBist ([[fuchs-2014-qbism]]) lines of thought.

## Relevance to this research

"Correlations without correlata" is a compact slogan for the project's relational stance in [[participatory-it-from-bit]]: what is objective is the *relation between agents' beliefs*, not any agent's intrinsic, absolute belief content. In the gauge-theoretic picture this is the statement that physically meaningful quantities are the frame-independent relations recovered by [[Parallel transport]] between [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]], with the residual non-relational ambiguity (the absence of canonical correlata) reflected in [[Holonomy]] under a [[Gauge transformation]]. Mermin's view sits at the hinge between [[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]] and the QBist program and supplies the participatory cluster with its sharpest one-line thesis ([[Participatory realism (it from bit)]]).
