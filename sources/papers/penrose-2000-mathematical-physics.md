# Wavefunction Collapse as a Real Gravitational Effect

> [!info] Citation
> Penrose, Roger (2000). "Wavefunction Collapse as a Real Gravitational Effect." Mathematical Physics 2000 (Imperial College Press). https://doi.org/10.1142/9781848160224_0013

## TL;DR
Penrose's contribution to the Mathematical Physics 2000 volume develops his proposal that the reduction of the quantum state is a genuine, objective physical process driven by gravity. He argues that a superposition of two mass distributions is unstable with a decay timescale tau ~ hbar / E_G, where E_G is the gravitational self-energy of the difference of the two mass distributions, predicting spontaneous collapse at mesoscopic scales without any conventional environmental decoherence.

## Relevance to this research
Provides the theoretical objective-collapse model (Penrose-Diosi) that the mirror-superposition and levitated-optomechanics experiments aim to test, and underpins the observer-dependent, gravity-coupled ontology discussed in the PIFB participatory it-from-bit manuscript.
