# Optimal Information Processing and Bayes's Theorem

> [!info] Citation
> Zellner, Arnold (1988). "Optimal Information Processing and Bayes's Theorem." The American Statistician. https://doi.org/10.1080/00031305.1988.10475585

## TL;DR
Zellner shows that Bayes's theorem is the optimal information-processing rule: it is the unique update that minimizes the difference between the information in the inputs (prior plus data/likelihood) and the information in the output (posterior), i.e. it is 100% efficient, adding no information and losing none. This casts Bayesian updating as an information-conservation / free-energy variational principle.

## Relevance to this research
Provides the variational/information-theoretic justification for Bayesian updating that generalized-Bayes and Gibbs-posterior work (Bissiri et al.) builds on, and underwrites the program's view of belief updates as free-energy-optimal information processing.
