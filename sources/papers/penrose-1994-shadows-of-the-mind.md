# Shadows of the Mind: A Search for the Missing Science of Consciousness

> [!info] Citation
> Penrose, Roger (1994). "Shadows of the Mind: A Search for the Missing Science of Consciousness." Oxford University Press. https://global.oup.com/academic/product/shadows-of-the-mind-9780195106466

## TL;DR
Penrose argues, via a Gödelian incompleteness argument, that human mathematical understanding is non-computable and therefore cannot arise from any algorithmic process. He proposes that consciousness depends on non-computable physics, specifically gravitationally induced objective reduction (OR) of the quantum state, situated physically in neuronal microtubules — the conceptual basis later developed with Hameroff into Orch OR.

## Relevance to this research
The primary source for the objective-reduction / non-computability hypothesis cited by the Orch OR paper; relevant to the PIFB / participatory-realism thread on observer-dependent versus objective collapse.
