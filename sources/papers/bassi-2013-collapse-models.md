# Models of Wave-function Collapse, Underlying Theories, and Experimental Tests

> [!info] Citation
> Bassi, Angelo, Lochan, Kinjalk, Satin, Seema, Singh, Tejinder P., Ulbricht, Hendrik (2013). "Models of Wave-function Collapse, Underlying Theories, and Experimental Tests." Reviews of Modern Physics. https://arxiv.org/abs/1204.4325

## TL;DR
A comprehensive review of dynamical wave-function collapse models (GRW, CSL, gravity-induced collapse) that modify the Schrodinger equation with stochastic nonlinear terms to suppress macroscopic superpositions and thereby solve the quantum measurement problem. It surveys the underlying theory, possible physical origins of the collapse noise, and the experimental tests (interferometry, heating, spontaneous radiation) that can constrain or falsify these models.

## Relevance to this research
Collapse models are a foil for the participatory it-from-bit (PIFB) reading of measurement: where collapse models add objective physical dynamics to dissolve superpositions, the participatory/QBist view treats measurement as observer-dependent belief update. Cited in nanoparticle-interferometry work bounding collapse parameters.
