# Quantum Mechanics as Quantum Information, Mostly

> [!info] Citation
> Fuchs, Christopher A. (2002). "Quantum Mechanics as Quantum Information, Mostly." Journal of Modern Optics. https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/0205039

## TL;DR
Fuchs's foundational QBism manifesto, arguing that quantum states are not objective properties of systems but Bayesian (subjective) degrees of belief held by an agent, and that the quantum formalism is largely a theory of consistent information-gathering and belief-updating about measurement outcomes. It develops the program of stripping quantum mechanics down to its irreducibly 'law of nature' core by recasting the rest as information theory.

## Relevance to this research
A primary reference for the participatory-realism / QBism strand of the VFE program, grounding the view that beliefs (the transformer's Gaussian tuples) constitute an agent's epistemic state rather than mirror objective reality. Directly supports the PIFB manuscript's it-from-bit / participatory interpretation.
