# A synergistic core for human brain evolution and cognition

> [!info] Citation
> Luppi, Andrea I., Mediano, Pedro A. M., Rosas, Fernando E., Holland, Negin, Fryer, Tim D., O'Brien, John T., Rowe, James B., Menon, David K., Bor, Daniel, Stamatakis, Emmanuel A. (2022). "A synergistic core for human brain evolution and cognition." Nature Neuroscience 25 (2022) 771-782. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41593-022-01070-0

## TL;DR
Uses information decomposition (synergy vs. redundancy) to identify a 'synergistic core' of brain regions whose interactions carry higher-order, irreducibly joint information. Shows this synergistic workspace is disproportionately expanded in human cortical evolution and supports integrative cognition, distinguishing it from redundant sensorimotor coupling.

## Relevance to this research
Supplies the higher-order information-decomposition framework (synergy/redundancy) connecting integrated-information and entropy-of-consciousness measures to the multi-agent program's interest in emergent collective structure and meta-agent formation.
