# The False Consensus Effect: An Egocentric Bias in Social Perception and Attribution Processes

> [!info] Citation
> Ross, L., Greene, D., House, P. (1977). "The False Consensus Effect: An Egocentric Bias in Social Perception and Attribution Processes." Journal of Experimental Social Psychology. https://doi.org/10.1016/0022-1031(77)90049-X

## TL;DR
Introduces the false consensus effect: people overestimate the extent to which others share their own beliefs, preferences, and behaviors, treating their own response as relatively common and appropriate while seeing alternative responses as uncommon and deviant. Demonstrated across several studies including the classic 'sandwich board' experiment.

## Relevance to this research
A foundational egocentric social-perception bias relevant to opinion-dynamics and belief-inertia models, where an agent's overweighting of self-consistent evidence and assumed social agreement shapes consensus formation and resistance to updating.
