# Helgoland: Making Sense of the Quantum Revolution

> [!info] Citation
> Rovelli, Carlo (2021). *Helgoland: Making Sense of the Quantum Revolution*, trans. Erica Segre and Simon Carnell. New York: Riverhead Books (orig. *Helgoland*, Milan: Adelphi, 2020).

## TL;DR

Rovelli's popular-yet-substantive development of the relational interpretation of quantum mechanics (RQM): physical properties are not absolute but exist only relative to other systems, so the world is a web of relations and interactions rather than a collection of objects with intrinsic states. The book extends RQM into an explicitly relational and structuralist metaphysics, bridging quantum foundations to a broader anti-substantialist worldview in which "things" are nodes where relations meet.

## Problem & setting

The book is a non-technical companion to Rovelli's RQM program (cf. [[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]]), aimed at making sense of quantum theory's measurement puzzle without collapse mysticism, hidden variables, or many worlds. The organizing claim is that the quantum formalism is telling us something ontological: properties are relational, manifested only in interactions, and there is no observer-independent fact about a system's state in isolation.

## Method

Conceptual and historical exposition rather than new formalism. Rovelli reconstructs Heisenberg's Helgoland breakthrough, develops the relational reading of state and measurement (every physical interaction is a "measurement" relative to the interacting systems), and connects it to anti-substantialist currents in philosophy (Nāgārjuna's emptiness, Mach, the Vienna empiricists). The throughline is that relations are prior to relata.

## Key results

- **Relational properties.** A system's properties are defined only relative to another system; there is no absolute, observer-free state.
- **No intrinsic objects.** Reality is a network of interactions; "objects" are derivative, echoing the structural-realist relations-first metaphysics.
- **Participation without solipsism.** Relativity of facts to systems generalizes the participatory move beyond conscious observers — any interaction instantiates it.

## Relevance to this research

Helgoland bridges the project's two foundational pillars: quantum-foundations participation ([[Participatory realism (it from bit)]], [[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]], [[fuchs-2014-qbism]]) and relations-first [[Structural realism]]. Its claim that properties exist only relative to other systems is the foundational-physics analogue of the manuscript's insistence that an agent's belief is meaningful only in relation to other agents, expressed through the connection that transports between [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] under a [[Gauge transformation]]. RQM thus supplies the metaphysical reading under which the program's gauge structure is not mere formalism but ontology: no agent holds a view from nowhere, and the objective content is the invariant relational web. It develops the technical [[rovelli-1996-relational-qm]] into the broad anti-substantialist stance the manuscript [[participatory-it-from-bit]] adopts.
