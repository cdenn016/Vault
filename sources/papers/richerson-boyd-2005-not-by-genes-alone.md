# Not by Genes Alone: How Culture Transformed Human Evolution

> [!info] Citation
> Richerson, P. J., & Boyd, R. (2005). *Not by Genes Alone: How Culture Transformed Human Evolution*. University of Chicago Press, Chicago. ISBN 978-0-226-71284-0.

## TL;DR
The accessible, prose synthesis of the dual-inheritance program first formalized in *Culture and the Evolutionary Process* (1985). Richerson and Boyd argue that culture is a second, genuinely Darwinian inheritance system — information stored in brains and transmitted by social learning — with its own selective and biased-transmission dynamics that coevolve with genes and cannot be reduced to them. The book translates the recursion-equation models into a verbal account of why human-scale cooperation, rapid cumulative adaptation, and maladaptive runaways all follow from the population dynamics of socially learned variants.

## What it establishes
Without equations, the book lays out the conceptual model behind the formalism: variants spread by *unbiased* transmission (copying at random), by *content/direct bias* (adopting intrinsically attractive variants), by *frequency-dependent bias* (conformity, adopting the common variant disproportionately), and by *model-based bias* (prestige, copying successful or admired individuals). It argues these biases are adaptive shortcuts for acquiring locally good behavior cheaply, explains how conformity and limited migration sustain stable between-group differences that enable cultural group selection, and accounts for "maladaptive" culture as the predictable byproduct of otherwise adaptive learning heuristics. The thesis is that gene–culture coevolution, not genes alone, shaped the human social psychology that makes large-scale cooperation possible.

## Relevance to this research
This is the narrative companion to the 1985 formalism; it lays out the verbal model of population-level belief dynamics — why conformism, prestige bias, and content bias are adaptive — that the VFE functional aims to subsume. It is useful conceptual scaffolding rather than machinery the equations directly use: the book supplies the menu of biases and the rationale for them, while the gauge-theoretic functional is where those biases would have to be recovered as limiting behaviors of the coupling kernel $\beta_{ij}$ and the precision-weighted update. As context for the overdamped-recovery claim and the polarization phenomenology, its value is framing and motivation. See [[Cultural evolution and social learning]].
