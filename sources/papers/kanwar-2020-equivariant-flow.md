# Equivariant Flow-Based Sampling for Lattice Gauge Theory

> [!info] Citation
> Kanwar, Gurtej, Albergo, Michael S., Boyda, Denis, Cranmer, Kyle, Hackett, Daniel C., Racaniere, Sebastien, Rezende, Danilo Jimenez, Shanahan, Phiala E. (2020). "Equivariant Flow-Based Sampling for Lattice Gauge Theory." Physical Review Letters. https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.06413

## TL;DR
Introduces gauge-equivariant normalizing flows that sample from lattice gauge theory distributions while exactly respecting the local gauge symmetry, building flows on the U(1) link variables that preserve the gauge-invariant measure. Demonstrates efficient, asymptotically exact sampling for a 2D U(1) lattice gauge theory, addressing critical-slowing-down in lattice QCD-style simulations.

## Relevance to this research
A concrete instance of building gauge-equivariant deep generative models, directly relevant to the program's gauge-theoretic neural architectures and the use of symmetry-respecting flows over gauge frames.
