# Black Hole Entropy is the Noether Charge

> [!info] Citation
> Robert M. Wald (1993). "Black hole entropy is the Noether charge." *Physical Review D* 48(8): R3427–R3431. DOI: [10.1103/PhysRevD.48.R3427](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.48.R3427). Preprint: [arXiv:gr-qc/9307038](https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/9307038).

## TL;DR

Wald shows that black-hole entropy, for any diffeomorphism-invariant theory of gravity, equals a Noether charge associated with the horizon-generating Killing symmetry, integrated over the horizon cross-section. For Einstein gravity this reproduces $S = A/4$, but the construction generalizes the area law to arbitrary (e.g. higher-curvature) Lagrangians, identifying horizon entropy as a *symmetry* charge rather than merely an area.

## Problem & setting

Bekenstein–Hawking entropy is specific to Einstein gravity. Wald asks for the entropy of a stationary black hole in a general diffeomorphism-invariant theory, deriving it from the structure of the action and its symmetries rather than from horizon thermodynamics case by case.

## Method

Using the covariant phase-space / Noether-charge formalism, the first law of black-hole mechanics is derived from diffeomorphism invariance; entropy emerges as the Noether charge of the horizon Killing field, expressed as a local geometric integral over the bifurcation surface.

## Key results

The Wald entropy formula, a symmetry-based generalization of the area law valid for any covariant gravitational Lagrangian; it underpins higher-curvature extensions of holographic entanglement entropy.
