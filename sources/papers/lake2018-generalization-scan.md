# Generalization without Systematicity: On the Compositional Skills of Sequence-to-Sequence Recurrent Networks

> [!info] Citation
> Lake, B. and Baroni, M. (2018). "Generalization without Systematicity: On the Compositional Skills of Sequence-to-Sequence Recurrent Networks." Proceedings of the 35th International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), Stockholm, Sweden, PMLR 80. arXiv:1711.00350.

## TL;DR
This paper introduces SCAN, a controlled benchmark of compositional navigation commands, and demonstrates that modern seq2seq RNNs (LSTMs, GRUs) generalize well under distributional overlap between training and test but fail catastrophically when test requires systematic compositional rules — for example, applying a known modifier to a new verb seen only in isolation during training. The gap between human-like algebraic systematicity and neural associative generalization is the central finding.

## Problem & setting
Human language understanding is characterized by systematic compositionality: knowing "dax" and knowing "run twice" immediately licenses understanding "dax twice," without further training. Fodor and colleagues argued in the 1980s–2000s that neural networks, as associative devices, cannot capture this algebraic property. This paper revisits that claim in the era of modern seq2seq architectures. Prior work on this question used older, simpler networks; no study had tested systematicity rigorously in contemporary seq2seq models.

## Method
The authors construct SCAN (Simplified version of the CommAI Navigation tasks), a finite but large (20,910 commands) dataset generated by a phrase-structure grammar. Commands are paired unambiguously with action sequences via a compositional interpretation function, making the benchmark ideal for probing zero-shot generalization. Four experiments vary the train/test split to isolate different forms of generalization: (1) random 80/20 split (mix-and-match), (2) train on short action sequences, test on longer ones, (3) train on a primitive verb in isolation, test on its full compositional paradigm, and (4) a proof-of-concept machine translation experiment with a new nonce word. The seq2seq models tested span SRNs, LSTMs, GRUs, with and without Bahdanau attention; a hyperparameter search covers 180 architectures across 5 random seeds each.

## Key results
In Experiment 1 (random split), the best LSTM achieves 99.8% test accuracy, demonstrating strong mix-and-match generalization. In Experiment 2 (longer action sequences), the best model reaches only 20.8% — a sharp drop showing failure to extrapolate length. In Experiment 3 (primitive generalization for "jump"), the overall-best model reaches 0.08% accuracy, while the same architecture generalizes to 90.0% for "turn left" (where the output action LTURN appears in many training contexts). The asymmetry is traced to representational isolation: "jump twice" has no near neighbors in encoder space when "jump" is seen only in primitive context. Adding up to 8 composed "jump" examples at training yields only 38.3% generalization; 32 composed examples reach 88.4%, confirming that generalization is achievable but requires many positive examples rather than rule extraction. In machine translation (Experiment 4), a network trained on "I am daxy" gets 1/8 novel constructions correct, versus 8/8 for a well-attested adjective.

## Relevance to this research
This paper is primarily a cognitive science / NLP benchmark paper with limited direct relevance to the VFE transformer's gauge-theoretic or variational architecture. However, it is relevant as a contrast case for attention mechanisms: the Bahdanau attention used here is a purely associative softmax over encoder hidden states, carrying no structural inductive bias toward compositionality. The VFE framework's attention weights beta_ij emerge from KL divergence between transported Gaussian beliefs, which in principle encodes relational structure among tokens rather than surface co-occurrence statistics. If the VFE transformer's belief-geometric attention imposes more compositional structure on the representation, the SCAN benchmark would be a natural probe. Additionally, the failure mode diagnosed here — distributional shift in latent representational geometry — connects to the SPD belief geometry and information-geometric distances used in the VFE framework, where the geometry of the belief manifold determines generalization structure.
