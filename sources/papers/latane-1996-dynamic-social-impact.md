# Dynamic Social Impact: The Creation of Culture by Communication

> [!info] Citation
> Latané, Bibb (1996). "Dynamic Social Impact: The Creation of Culture by Communication." Journal of Communication. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1460-2466.1996.tb01501.x

## TL;DR
Extends static Social Impact Theory into a dynamic, spatially-distributed model: agents arranged on a grid repeatedly influence neighbours via strength, immediacy, and number, and the model is run to a self-organized macro-state. Predicts and exhibits four robust emergent phenomena — consolidation (reduction of minority size), clustering (spatial coherence of opinions), correlation (emergent association of initially independent attributes), and continuing diversity (minorities survive in clusters rather than vanishing).

## Relevance to this research
Dynamic Social Impact is a direct classical antecedent of the multi-agent VFE program's spatial coupling dynamics: clustering and continuing diversity are the echo-chamber/polarization phenomenology the overdamped limit of multi-agent free energy must reproduce, and consolidation maps onto precision-weighted opinion relaxation.
