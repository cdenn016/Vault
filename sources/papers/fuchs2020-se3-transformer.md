# SE(3)-Transformers: 3D Roto-Translation Equivariant Attention Networks

> [!info] Citation
> Fuchs, F. B., Worrall, D. E., Fischer, V., & Welling, M. (2020). "SE(3)-Transformers: 3D Roto-Translation Equivariant Attention Networks." NeurIPS 2020. arXiv:2006.10503.

## TL;DR
The SE(3)-Transformer is a self-attention module for 3D point clouds and graphs that is equivariant under continuous 3D roto-translations. It combines the expressiveness and data-adaptivity of self-attention with the geometric guarantees of tensor field networks (TFN), achieving SE(3)-equivariant attention weights and value messages by embedding features as type-$\ell$ vectors (Wigner-D irreps of SO(3)) and constructing TFN-type query/key/value projections. The model outperforms both non-equivariant attention baselines and equivariant models without attention on N-body simulation, 3D object classification, and molecular property prediction tasks.

## Problem & setting
Standard self-attention mechanisms discard the geometric symmetries of 3D point cloud and graph data: two equivalent point clouds related by a rotation or translation will produce different outputs, requiring data augmentation at training time and offering no robustness guarantee. Prior equivariant architectures such as Tensor Field Networks (TFN) are purely convolutional and lack the ability to perform data-dependent aggregation, leading to overly constrained angular filters. The paper asks whether equivariance and the expressiveness of self-attention can be combined in a single architecture.

## Method
The SE(3)-Transformer operates on point clouds converted to neighbourhood graphs. Each layer computes:

$$f^\ell_{\text{out},i} = W^{\ell\ell}_V f^\ell_{\text{in},i} + \sum_{k \geq 0} \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i \setminus i} \alpha_{ij} \, W^{\ell k}_V(x_j - x_i) f^k_{\text{in},j}$$

where $f^\ell$ denotes a type-$\ell$ feature vector transforming under the $\ell$-th Wigner-D irrep $D_\ell$ of SO(3), and $W^{\ell k}_V(x_j - x_i)$ is a TFN-type equivariant kernel built from spherical harmonics and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The scalar attention weights $\alpha_{ij}$ are SE(3)-invariant, computed via dot-product attention between TFN-type query and key embeddings; invariance follows from orthonormality of SO(3) representations ($q^\top S_g^\top S_g k = q^\top k$). The architecture also introduces an "attentive self-interaction" layer in which per-point channel-mixing weights are generated by an MLP applied to invariant inner products of features, providing a data-dependent nonlinearity. Spherical harmonics are implemented in PyTorch with 10--1000x GPU speedup over SciPy.

## Key results
On a 5-body electrostatic particle simulation (equivariant regression): SE(3)-Transformer achieves position MSE of 0.0076 vs. 0.0151 for TFN and 0.0139 for Set Transformer, with equivariance error $\Delta_{\mathrm{EQ}} = 3.2 \times 10^{-7}$ confirming near-exact equivariance. On ScanObjectNN object classification (128 input points), the model reaches 72.8% accuracy, rising to 85.0% with a symmetry-breaking z-axis coordinate input (SE(3)-Transformer +z), competitive with DGCNN (86.2%) which uses 1024 points and no equivariance guarantee. On QM9 molecular property regression the model offers competitive but not state-of-the-art performance versus Cormorant and LieConv. In all settings, adding equivariance constraints to attention consistently improves over non-equivariant attention, and adding attention consistently improves over equivariant convolution alone.

## Relevance to this research
The SE(3)-Transformer is the canonical precedent for combining group-equivariant representation theory with attention mechanisms — the same synthesis at the core of the GL(K) gauge-equivariant attention developed in this research program. Several structural parallels are direct: (1) both architectures decompose features into irreps of a symmetry group and design attention weights to be invariant while value messages are equivariant; (2) the Clebsch-Gordan coupling used here to build the equivariant TFN kernels is exactly the mechanism explored under `use_cg_coupling=True` in the VFE transformer's irrep-tower variants; (3) the key-query inner product yielding an invariant attention scalar mirrors the GL(K) construction where $\beta_{ij}$ is derived from a KL divergence (a transport-invariant quantity) between GL(K)-transformed beliefs. The primary difference is the symmetry group: SE(3) acts on Euclidean point clouds via its defining geometric action, whereas GL(K) acts on the space of Gaussian beliefs via pushforward, making the VFE framework probabilistically rather than geometrically grounded. The "attentive self-interaction" layer here (MLP on invariant inner products) is conceptually analogous to the VFE prior-bank decode, which also produces scalars from invariant quantities. The paper's Wigner-D / spherical harmonic decomposition and the derivation of the kernel constraint (Eq. 35) are the SO(3) analog of the GL(K) gauge-equivariance constraint on transport kernels.
