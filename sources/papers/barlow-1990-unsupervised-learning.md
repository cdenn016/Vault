# Unsupervised Learning

> [!info] Citation
> Barlow, Horace B. (1989). "Unsupervised Learning." Neural Computation. https://doi.org/10.1162/neco.1989.1.3.295

## TL;DR
Barlow's classic argument that the goal of unsupervised cortical learning is to discover the statistical regularities and redundancy of sensory input, building efficient, factorial representations (the redundancy-reduction / efficient-coding hypothesis). It frames perception as learning the suspicious coincidences and independent causes underlying sensory data.

## Relevance to this research
Efficient coding and redundancy reduction are precursors to the free-energy / predictive-coding view of perception and to adaptation phenomena; cited by Webster (2015) on visual adaptation as an instance of the brain recalibrating to input statistics, relevant to the program's predictive-coding foundations.
