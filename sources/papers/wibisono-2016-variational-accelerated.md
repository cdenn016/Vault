# A Variational Perspective on Accelerated Methods in Optimization

> [!info] Citation
> Wibisono, Andre, Wilson, Ashia C., Jordan, Michael I. (2016). "A Variational Perspective on Accelerated Methods in Optimization." Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS) 113(47). https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.04245

## TL;DR
Introduces the Bregman Lagrangian, a single variational family whose Euler-Lagrange equations generate the continuous-time limits of accelerated gradient methods (Nesterov and beyond). Shows that a broad class of accelerated methods arise from this Lagrangian by discretization, with convergence rates governed by the choice of generator and damping schedule.

## Relevance to this research
Provides the variational/Lagrangian foundation that the Diakonikolas-Jordan Hamiltonian view of momentum methods builds on, connecting accelerated optimization to the information-geometric Bregman/Hamiltonian structure used in the VFE program's natural-gradient and belief-dynamics analyses.
