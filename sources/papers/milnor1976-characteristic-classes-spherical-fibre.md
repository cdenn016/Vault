# On Characteristic Classes for Spherical Fibre Spaces

> [!info] Citation
> Milnor, J. (1968). "On Characteristic Classes for Spherical Fibre Spaces." *Commentarii Mathematici Helvetici*, Vol. 43. https://doi.org/10.5169/seals-32907

## TL;DR

Milnor investigates the singular cohomology of the classifying space BG(n), where G(n) is the H-space of homotopy self-equivalences of S^{n-1}. The central result (Theorem 4) shows that, in dimensions below 2p(p-1), the mod-p cohomology ring H*(BG; Z_p) is free commutative, generated by Wu characteristic classes q_i and their Bockstein coboundaries beta q_i. The paper also establishes that BG carries a homotopy-associative, homotopy-commutative H-space structure via the Whitney join operation on spherical fibre spaces.

## Problem & setting

For an (n-1)-spherical Hurewicz fibre space over a CW-complex B, Thom's isomorphism relates H^k(B) to H^{k+n}(B, E). By applying Steenrod operations P^i to the fundamental class u in H^n(B, E; Z_p), one obtains Wu characteristic classes q_i in H^{2i(p-1)}(B; Z_p). The paper asks: what is the full cohomology ring structure of the universal base space BG classifying all such spherical fibrations? Prior partial results by Gitler-Stasheff and Browder-Liulevicius-Peterson had addressed special cases (mod-2 coefficients, rational coefficients), but the odd-prime structure was incomplete.

## Method

The Wu classes q_i are defined via the fundamental construction: any cohomology operation theta acting on H*(B, E; Z_p) yields a characteristic class Phi^{-1} theta Phi via the Thom isomorphism Phi. Applying the Steenrod operation P^i gives q_i = Phi^{-1} P^i Phi(1). Milnor proves that the Steenrod operations P^h act on Wu classes by polynomials in the q_j (Theorem 1), using Adem relations and induction. The Hopf algebra structure of H*(BG; Z_p), arising from the Whitney join H-space structure on BG (Theorem 2), is then analysed via Borel's theorem. The Postnikov system of BSG is built inductively in dimensions below 2p(p-1), with k-invariants in dimensions mr+1 (r = 2(p-1)) computed from Toda's calculation of the p-primary homotopy groups of BSG. Theorem 3 identifies H*(BSG^{[0,mr-1]}; Z_p) inductively as free commutative on P^i q_1 and their Bocksteins, with the k-invariant km satisfying the explicit relation ((m+1)P^{p-m} - p P^1) k_m = 0.

Key equations: the Cartan-formula identities (1.5)-(1.8), the Adem-relation computations (1.9)-(1.11), and the diagonal map formula for the Hopf algebra:

  psi*(q_i) = sum_{j} (q_{i-j} tensor P^j 1 + q_{i-j} tensor beta q_j)   [equations (2.4)-(2.5)]

## Key results

Theorem 4: In dimensions below 2p(p-1), H*(BG; Z_p) is isomorphic as a graded algebra to the tensor product of a polynomial algebra freely generated by the Wu classes q_i and a Grassmann algebra freely generated by their Bockstein coboundaries beta q_i; the algebra is "free commutative" in this range.

Theorem 5: With rational localization at the integers, the cohomology of the analogous oriented classifying space is free commutative on a single generator p_t in dimension 4t (for t >= 1), corresponding to the t-th Pontryagin class in H^{4t}(BO(2t); Q).

Corollary 1: BG has the homotopy type of BSG x P(R) (infinite real projective space), from which Corollary 2 follows: for p odd, H*(BG; Z_p) is isomorphic to H*(BSG; Z_p).

Corollary 3: The subalgebra of H*(BG; Z_p) generated by q_i and beta q_i is free commutative, with all q_i and some subset of beta q_i as independent generators.

Stasheff's result (Theorem 2) that BG is a homotopy-associative, homotopy-commutative H-space is proved rigorously using a finiteness lemma (Lemma 2) exploiting the fact that all homotopy groups of BG are finite.
