# Geometric Quantum Mechanics

> [!info] Citation
> Dorje C. Brody and Lane P. Hughston (2001). "Geometric Quantum Mechanics." *Journal of Geometry and Physics* 38(1): 19–53. DOI: [10.1016/S0393-0440(00)00052-8](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0393-0440(00)00052-8).

## TL;DR

Reformulates quantum mechanics as classical Hamiltonian dynamics on the complex projective space of rays, equipped with the Fubini–Study metric. The Schrödinger equation becomes Hamiltonian flow on this Kähler manifold; the symplectic structure gives the dynamics, while the Riemannian (Fubini–Study / [[Fisher information metric|Fisher–Rao]]) part gives uncertainty and distinguishability. Quantum mechanics is thereby presented as a geometric theory whose state space carries both a symplectic and a compatible metric structure.
