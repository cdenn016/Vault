# QBism, the Perimeter of Quantum Bayesianism

> [!info] Citation
> Fuchs, Christopher A. (2010). "QBism, the Perimeter of Quantum Bayesianism." arXiv:1003.5209. https://arxiv.org/abs/1003.5209

## TL;DR
Fuchs's manifesto-style survey introducing the term 'QBism' and laying out quantum Bayesianism's core stance: quantum states are an agent's personalist (subjective) degrees of belief, the Born rule is a normative coherence constraint on those beliefs, and measurement outcomes are personal experiences rather than observer-independent facts. It frames quantum mechanics as a single-agent decision theory about the consequences of acting on the world.

## Relevance to this research
A primary source for the participatory-realism / it-from-bit (PIFB) thread: QBism's agent-centric, belief-based reading of quantum states is the philosophical anchor for treating physics as participatory inference. Cited by fuchs2014-qbism-locality.
