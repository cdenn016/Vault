# Connecting Information Geometry and Geometric Mechanics

> [!info] Citation
> Melvin Leok and Jun Zhang (2017). “Connecting Information Geometry and Geometric Mechanics.” *Entropy* 19(10), 518. DOI: [10.3390/e19100518](https://doi.org/10.3390/e19100518).

## TL;DR

Leok and Zhang establish a formal bridge between divergence functions in information geometry and discrete Lagrangians in geometric mechanics. Both induce differential-geometric structure on $Q\times Q$, and Legendre duality connects tangent- and cotangent-bundle formulations. The bridge is precise but not automatic evidence that any selected divergence uniquely determines a continuous kinetic energy.

## Problem & setting

Information geometry commonly starts from a divergence between nearby points, whereas geometric mechanics starts from a Lagrangian and a variational principle. The paper asks how these constructions relate and under what conditions one can move rigorously between the information-geometric structure induced by a divergence and the mechanical structure induced by a discrete Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors compare the geometry generated by a divergence function with that generated by a discrete Lagrangian on the product manifold. They use Legendre transforms to relate tangent-bundle and cotangent-bundle descriptions and apply variational error analysis to compare a divergence with the exact discrete Lagrangian associated with an underlying continuous system.

## Key results

The paper shows that the divergence function agrees with the exact discrete Lagrangian through third order precisely when the configuration space is a Hessian manifold. This gives a mathematically controlled information-geometry/mechanics connection and identifies the geometric condition under which the approximation has the stated order. The result does not say that a divergence, by itself, selects a unique mass tensor, continuous Lagrangian, or kinetic energy.

## Relevance to this research

This source strengthens the formal foundation for relating [[Fisher information metric]] geometry to [[Hamiltonian belief dynamics]], while restricting what may be claimed. A divergence can induce metric and connection data and can approximate a discrete action under specific conditions; it does not automatically choose the kinetic postulate used by [[Belief inertia]]. The manuscript should therefore distinguish the intrinsic information geometry, the loss curvature, and the separately adopted kinetic metric rather than collapsing them into one object.
