# Quantum Darwinism

> [!info] Citation
> Zurek, Wojciech Hubert (2009). "Quantum Darwinism." Nature Physics. https://doi.org/10.1038/nphys1202

## TL;DR
Zurek's focused review of Quantum Darwinism: classical objectivity emerges because pointer states selected by einselection imprint redundant copies of their information across many fragments of the environment. Many observers can independently access the same information from disjoint fragments without perturbing the system, so a few selected states acquire objective, classical existence. The paper develops redundancy and mutual-information measures quantifying this proliferation of records.

## Relevance to this research
Provides the quantitative account of how shared environmental records create observer-independent objectivity, mapping onto the multi-agent VFE setting where shared coupling signals drive consensus over pointer-like belief states; central to the participatory-realism (PIFB) thread on observer-relative yet intersubjectively binding facts.
