# Gravitationally Induced Entanglement between Two Massive Particles is Sufficient Evidence of Quantum Effects in Gravity

> [!info] Citation
> Marletto, Chiara, Vedral, Vlatko (2017). "Gravitationally Induced Entanglement between Two Massive Particles is Sufficient Evidence of Quantum Effects in Gravity." Physical Review Letters 119, 240402. https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.06036

## TL;DR
Companion to Bose et al. (2017): argues, using constructor-theory / quantum information principles, that gravitationally induced entanglement between two masses is sufficient evidence that the gravitational field is non-classical, because a classical mediator cannot entangle two quantum systems.

## Relevance to this research
Information-theoretic (constructor-theory) argument that gravity must be quantum if it can entangle masses; ties the participatory information-first program to gravity and to Deutsch-Marletto constructor theory.
