# Remodelling Structural Realism: Quantum Physics and the Metaphysics of Structure

> [!info] Citation
> French, Steven, and Ladyman, James (2003). "Remodelling Structural Realism: Quantum Physics and the Metaphysics of Structure." *Synthese* **136**(1): 31–56. DOI: [10.1023/A:1024156116636](https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1024156116636).

## TL;DR

This is the canonical statement of *ontic* structural realism (OSR), the position that structure — the network of relations among entities — is metaphysically primary, while individual objects are at best derivative and at worst dispensable. Against John Worrall's *epistemic* structural realism (we can know only structure, but objects still exist behind it; see [[worrall-1989-structural-realism]]), French and Ladyman argue from the underdetermination of object-metaphysics by physics (especially the metaphysical indeterminacy of particle individuality in quantum theory) that the realist's commitment should be to relational structure itself, not to relata conceived as self-subsistent things.

## Problem & setting

Worrall's structural realism promised "the best of both worlds": it explained the success of science (preserved mathematical structure across theory change) while dodging the pessimistic meta-induction (discarded objects/mechanisms were never the realist's real commitment). French and Ladyman press the question of what *grounds* this structure. They argue that quantum mechanics leaves the individuality of particles metaphysically underdetermined — the formalism is equally compatible with particles as individuals and as non-individuals — so a realism that rests on objects with determinate identity conditions is built on sand. The natural response is to relocate the realist commitment from objects to structure.

## Method

The argument is metaphysical and philosophy-of-physics analysis rather than formal proof. It combines (i) the permutation-invariance and (anti)symmetrization arguments from quantum statistics to establish metaphysical underdetermination of particle individuality, (ii) a critique of the "hidden natures" left untouched by epistemic structural realism, and (iii) a positive proposal that relational structure can be ontologically prior — relations need not be parasitic on independently identifiable relata. They contend that group-theoretic structure (symmetry groups, their representations) is the appropriate locus of ontological commitment in modern physics.

## Key results

- **Ontic structural realism.** Structure is primary; objects are reconceived as nodes in, or derivative of, relational structure rather than as ontological bedrock.
- **Underdetermination argument.** Quantum statistics leave particle individuality metaphysically undetermined, undercutting object-based realism and motivating the shift to structure.
- **Relations without prior relata.** A coherent metaphysics can take relations as fundamental, dissolving the assumption that every relation presupposes independently constituted things — the most radical and influential thesis of the paper.

## Relevance to this research

This paper is the backbone of [[Structural realism]] as the project deploys it, and it is the precise philosophical license for the manuscript's pan-agentic, relations-first metaphysics. The program treats [[Agents as fibre-bundle sections]] that have no intrinsic, frame-independent state: an agent is constituted by its relations to other agents and by the [[Gauge transformation]] / connection that transports beliefs between them. That is OSR almost literally — relata (agents) fixed by structure (the bundle, its connection, its symmetry group) rather than the reverse. The "structure prior to relata" thesis underwrites the move from Wheeler's *it from bit* ([[wheeler-1990-it-from-bit]]) to a *participatory* ontology in which what is objective is the invariant relational content, not any agent's local readout (cf. [[cassirer-1910-substance-function|Cassirer 1910]]'s function-over-substance). It pairs directly with the project's named OSR anchor [[ladyman-ross-2007-every-thing-must-go]] and with the spacetime-on-a-bundle version in [[esfeld-lam-2008-moderate-osr]].
