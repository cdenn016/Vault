# The physics of optimal decision making: A formal analysis of models of performance in two-alternative forced-choice tasks

> [!info] Citation
> Bogacz, Rafal, Brown, Eric, Moehlis, Jeff, Holmes, Philip, Cohen, Jonathan D. (2006). "The physics of optimal decision making: A formal analysis of models of performance in two-alternative forced-choice tasks." Psychological Review. https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-295X.113.4.700

## TL;DR
Unifies a family of two-alternative forced-choice decision models (drift-diffusion, Ornstein-Uhlenbeck, leaky competing accumulator, mutual-inhibition) and shows that, under reduction, they map onto the drift-diffusion model, which implements the optimal sequential probability ratio test. Derives the optimal threshold setting that maximizes reward rate, formalizing the speed-accuracy tradeoff.

## Relevance to this research
Establishes the optimality of sequential evidence accumulation and the reward-rate-optimal decision threshold, grounding the program's view of bounded, precision-weighted inference and the speed-accuracy tradeoff in a physics-of-decisions framework.
