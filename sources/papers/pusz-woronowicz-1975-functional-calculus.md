# Functional Calculus for Sesquilinear Forms and the Purification Map

> [!info] Citation
> Pusz, W., Woronowicz, S. L. (1975). "Functional Calculus for Sesquilinear Forms and the Purification Map." Reports on Mathematical Physics. https://doi.org/10.1016/0034-4877(75)90061-0

## TL;DR
Pusz and Woronowicz developed a functional calculus for pairs of positive sesquilinear forms and introduced the 'purification map', supplying the operator-theoretic machinery (the operator geometric mean and related means) that Uhlmann used to define the transition probability between states. It is a foundational ingredient of Kubo-Ando operator-mean theory.

## Relevance to this research
Cited by Uhlmann (1976) for the operator-mean / functional-calculus tools behind the transition probability (fidelity); operator means via this calculus also underpin the operator-monotone classification of monotone quantum Fisher metrics used in the program.
