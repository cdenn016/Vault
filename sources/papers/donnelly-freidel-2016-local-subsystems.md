# Local subsystems in gauge theory and gravity

> [!info] Citation
> Donnelly, William and Freidel, Laurent (2016). "Local subsystems in gauge theory and gravity." *JHEP* 09 (2016) 102. doi:10.1007/JHEP09(2016)102. arXiv:1601.04744.

## TL;DR
This paper develops a general covariant-canonical formalism for associating a gauge-invariant extended phase space to a spatial region with a boundary in Yang-Mills theory and general relativity. The key move is to introduce additional boundary degrees of freedom — a bundle trivialization field in Yang-Mills and a surface-location embedding field in gravity — that restore gauge invariance of the presymplectic potential on regions with boundaries. The result is a precise classical analog of the extended Hilbert space construction, yielding a continuum generalization of lattice gauge theory's entanglement entropy framework and identifying new physical surface symmetry groups whose Hamiltonian generators are the gravitational and gauge-theoretic surface charges.

## Problem & setting
The problem of defining entanglement entropy in gauge theories and gravity is obstructed by the absence of a natural tensor-product factorization of the Hilbert space (H = H_Sigma x H_Sigma-bar) for gauge-constrained theories: the constraints (Gauss law in Yang-Mills, diffeomorphism constraints in gravity) couple the two regions through their shared boundary, and compactly supported gauge-invariant operators do not generically exist in gravity. The paper aims to construct a gauge-invariant extended phase space for a spatial slice with boundary by augmenting the bulk phase space with new boundary degrees of freedom, providing the classical underpinning of the extended Hilbert space construction used on the lattice.

## Method
For Yang-Mills with gauge group G, the bulk presymplectic potential integrated over a Cauchy surface Sigma with boundary S = dSigma fails to be gauge-invariant (it shifts by a boundary term under gauge transformations). The authors introduce a G-valued trivialization field phi: S -> G as an additional phase-space variable and add a compensating boundary term to the presymplectic potential, yielding a fully gauge-invariant extended presymplectic potential:

    Theta[A, deltaA, phi, deltaphi] = -int_Sigma Tr[deltaA ^ *F] + oint_S Tr[*F delta-phi]

where delta-phi := phi^{-1} delta-phi. This construction distinguishes two transformation types: gauge symmetries (acting on the right of phi, with vanishing on-shell generator H[alpha]) and surface symmetries (acting on the left of phi, with nonvanishing generator E[alpha] = oint_S Tr[*(phi F phi^{-1}) alpha] — the dressed nonabelian electric field). The Poisson brackets of E recover the current algebra {E^a(x), E^b(y)} = delta(x-y) f^{abc} E^c(x), identifying E as the nonabelian surface charge. Phase spaces of adjacent regions are combined by a fusion product P_{Sigma cup Sigma-bar} = P_Sigma x_{G_S} P_{Sigma-bar}, quotienting by the common surface symmetry group.

For gravity, the same structure applies with an embedding field X^a: R^D -> M replacing phi to specify the location of the codimension-2 entangling surface S. The surface-preserving symmetry group is identified as the semidirect product G_S = Diff(S) x SL(2,R)^S, where diffeomorphisms of S are generated by the curvature of the normal connection and SL(2,R) surface boosts are generated by the conformal components of the normal metric. These generators together form the gravitational surface charges for the entangling surface.

## Key results
The main theorem identifies the classical extended phase space with a gauge-invariant presymplectic structure for bounded spatial regions in both Yang-Mills and gravity, extending the lattice construction to the continuum. The surface symmetry group for gravity is G_S = Diff(S) x SL(2,R)^S, with the SL(2,R) Casimir equal to the area element of S — so representation theory singles out the area of the entangling surface as the label for irreducible representations. The Hamiltonian generators of surface symmetries are the conformal normal metric and the curvature of the normal connection (eq. 1.13). The entangling product at the quantum level takes the form H_{Sigma cup Sigma-bar} = H_Sigma otimes_{G_S} H_{Sigma-bar}, a tensor product of G_S-modules that projects onto singlets under the diagonal surface symmetry action. The construction provides a field-theoretic basis for the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy as arising from the different ways of gluing partial Cauchy surfaces at a horizon cross-section.

## Relevance to this research
This paper is directly relevant to the gauge-theoretic foundations of the VFE transformer and GL(K) attention framework. The core construction — introducing boundary degrees of freedom (the trivialization phi) to restore gauge invariance of a variational structure on a region with boundary — is a precise classical field-theory analog of the gauge-equivariance conditions imposed in the GL(K) attention mechanism, where beliefs must transform covariantly under local group actions. The distinction between gauge symmetry (acting on the right of phi, generating constraints that vanish on-shell) and surface symmetry (acting on the left, generating physical charges) maps cleanly onto the distinction between gauge redundancy and physical gauge-equivariant observables in the VFE attention framework. The fusion product / entangling product P_Sigma x_{G_S} P_Sigma-bar is a field-theoretic instance of the same symplectic reduction / quotient by gauge group that underlies the GL(K) variational free energy's gauge-invariant belief-coupling term. The identification of SL(2,R) as the boundary boost algebra, and its Casimir as the area, is suggestive for the role of the GL(K) Cartan subalgebra in determining information-geometric "area" (entropy). The extended phase space / Hilbert space construction is also the cleanest field-theory precursor to boundary degrees of freedom in multi-agent active inference networks where agents share a boundary.
