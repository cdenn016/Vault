# On the Quantum Measurement Problem

> [!info] Citation
> Brukner, Caslav (2016). "On the Quantum Measurement Problem." Quantum [Un]Speakables II (R. Bertlmann & A. Zeilinger, eds.), Springer, pp. 95-117. https://arxiv.org/abs/1507.05255

## TL;DR
Brukner reframes the quantum measurement problem through extended Wigner's-friend scenarios, arguing that taking the observed friend's measurement outcome as an objective fact for all observers leads to contradictions. The essay is the precursor analysis to his 2018 no-go theorem against observer-independent facts.

## Relevance to this research
Foundational input to the participatory-it-from-bit (PIFB) line: it grounds the observer-dependent-facts argument that the program uses to motivate agent-relative, measurement-as-inference readings of belief states.
