# A free energy principle for the brain

> [!info] Citation
> Friston, Karl, Kilner, James, Harrison, Lee (2006). "A free energy principle for the brain." Journal of Physiology-Paris. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jphysparis.2006.10.001

## TL;DR
The foundational paper introducing the free-energy principle: the brain minimizes a variational free-energy bound on surprise (negative log model evidence) to resist the dispersive effects of its environment. It frames perception, learning, and action as gradient descent on the same free-energy functional, unifying predictive coding and Bayesian inference in the brain.

## Relevance to this research
This is the origin of the free-energy principle that the entire VFE research program generalizes; the gauge-theoretic VFE transformer and multi-agent model both descend from minimizing this variational free-energy bound.
