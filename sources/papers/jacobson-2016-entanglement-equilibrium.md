# Entanglement Equilibrium and the Einstein Equation

> [!info] Citation
> Ted Jacobson (2016). "Entanglement equilibrium and the Einstein equation." *Physical Review Letters* 116(20): 201101. DOI: [10.1103/PhysRevLett.116.201101](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.116.201101). Preprint: [arXiv:1505.04753](https://arxiv.org/abs/1505.04753).

## TL;DR

Jacobson reframes his own 1995 thermodynamic derivation of gravity as a *stationarity* (equilibrium) condition. The hypothesis is that the vacuum is a state of maximal entanglement entropy at fixed volume: for any small causal diamond, the entanglement entropy of the matter inside, plus the area term, is extremized in the vacuum. Imposing that this entanglement entropy be stationary under small perturbations of the geometry and the quantum state — to first order, at fixed volume — yields the full nonlinear Einstein equation with the correct cosmological constant. Gravity becomes the statement that spacetime sits at an entanglement extremum.

## Problem & setting

The 1995 "Einstein equation of state" derivation ([[jacobson-1995-thermodynamics-spacetime]]) used a Clausius relation $\delta Q = T\,\delta S$ across local Rindler horizons. The 2016 paper recasts the same physics in a cleaner variational language that meshes with the entanglement-entropy program of [[ryu-takayanagi-2006-holographic-entanglement-entropy]] and [[faulkner-2014-gravitation-from-entanglement]], avoiding some interpretational issues of the heat-flow argument.

## Method

For a geodesic ball of small radius, the variation of the generalized (area + matter entanglement) entropy is computed. Maximality of the vacuum entanglement at fixed volume forces a relation between the conformal-Killing-energy of matter and the curvature, which integrates to the Einstein equation.

## Key results

Stationarity of entanglement entropy of small causal diamonds is equivalent to the Einstein equation; the cosmological constant appears as a Lagrange multiplier enforcing the fixed-volume constraint. This is arguably the cleanest "gravity is an equilibrium/extremum of an information functional" statement in the literature.

## Relevance to this research

This is the single closest external precedent for PIFB's central methodological thesis. PIFB ([[participatory-it-from-bit]]) proposes that physics — including emergent geometric structure — falls out of the *stationarity* of an information functional, the [[Variational free energy]], over belief fibres. Jacobson does exactly this for gravity: the field equation is not postulated but is the Euler–Lagrange/extremum condition of an entanglement-entropy functional. The mapping is structural and tight: PIFB's "stationarity of $F$" plays the role of Jacobson's "stationarity of entanglement entropy," and PIFB's emergent metric from the pullback [[Fisher information metric]] plays the role of the geometry that the extremum condition constrains. It is the keystone of the [[Emergent spacetime and holography]] theme and the most direct support for the [[Participatory realism (it from bit)]] reading of the program.
