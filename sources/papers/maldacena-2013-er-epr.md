# Cool Horizons for Entangled Black Holes

> [!info] Citation
> Maldacena, Juan and Susskind, Leonard (2013). "Cool Horizons for Entangled Black Holes." *Fortschritte der Physik*, 61(9), 781–811. arXiv:1306.0533 [hep-th].

## TL;DR
Maldacena and Susskind propose that Einstein-Rosen (ER) bridges — wormhole solutions connecting two distant black holes through their interiors — are equivalent to, and generated by, Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen (EPR) entanglement between those black holes. The ER=EPR conjecture asserts that any entangled pair of quantum systems is connected by some form of Einstein-Rosen bridge, even if that bridge is a highly quantum object with no classical geometric description. This connection is used to resolve versions of the AMPS firewall paradox by showing that maximally entangled black holes have smooth, cool horizons precisely because they are connected by a wormhole.

## Problem & setting
The firewall paradox (AMPS/AMPSS) posed a conflict between the equivalence principle (smooth horizon for an infalling observer), black hole unitarity (Hawking radiation purifies the black hole), and monogamy of entanglement (a Hawking mode cannot be maximally entangled with both the black hole interior and the early radiation simultaneously). Prior work established separately that (i) two-sided eternal black holes in AdS/CFT are dual to the thermofield double state of two CFTs and share an ER bridge, and (ii) EPR correlations cannot send superluminal signals. The paper points out these two seemingly distinct non-locality phenomena share a common origin.

## Method
The core argument proceeds in AdS/CFT: the eternal AdS-Schwarzschild black hole is described by the thermofield double state
$$|\Psi\rangle = \sum_n e^{-\beta E_n/2}|n,n\rangle$$
which is both a maximally entangled state of two CFTs (EPR) and dual to a geometry with an Einstein-Rosen bridge (ER). The authors generalise this identification beyond the maximally entangled thermofield double: different entangled states in the family $|\Psi_t\rangle \sim \sum_n e^{-\beta E_n/2} e^{-2iE_n t}|n,n\rangle$ correspond to geometrically distinct bridges, showing that the bridge is a property of the precise entanglement structure, not merely its degree. The time evolution of the bridge is modelled by qubit scrambling: after the scrambling time $\sim \log S$, entanglement grows between northern and southern sub-regions, manifesting geometrically as bridge stretching. The AMPS paradox is then reformulated as a question about what an observer (Alice) can do with the Hawking radiation on her side to affect the horizon geometry experienced by Bob on the other side.

## Key results
The ER=EPR conjecture: the Einstein-Rosen bridge connecting two black holes is created by and equivalent to EPR entanglement between them. Distinct entangled states correspond to distinct bridges: even states with the same reduced density matrix on each side but different inter-copy unitaries produce different geometric interiors. Black hole pair creation in a magnetic field naturally produces pairs in precisely this maximally entangled state, with a pair-creation rate carrying an overall factor $e^S$ consistent with the entanglement interpretation. Alice can create a firewall at Bob's horizon by acting on the radiation with a quantum computer, but if she does not, the bridge remains smooth; the firewall paradox is thus reinterpreted as a question about quantum operations on one side of an entangled pair. The bridge grows in spatial length under time evolution — its interior stretches at a rate no signal can outrun — while the reduced density matrices on each side remain unchanged.

## Relevance to this research
The ER=EPR paper is directly relevant to the participatory-realism / "It from Bit" theoretical programme in this research program. The identification of geometric connectivity (wormholes) with quantum informational connectivity (entanglement) is a concrete realisation of participatory realism: the spacetime structure is not a background but is constituted by relational quantum information. This resonates with the VFE transformer's gauge-theoretic treatment of belief coupling — the attention weights $\beta_{ij}$ encode relational entanglement-like correlations between belief states, and the GL(K) gauge transport $\Omega_{ij}$ plays a role analogous to the interior geometry threading the bridge. The idea that different entangled states (different unitaries relating the two sides) produce different bridges maps loosely onto the idea that different gauge connections produce different transported beliefs in the VFE attention mechanism. The growth of bridge complexity under time evolution connects to the complexity-equals-action or complexity-equals-volume proposals, and more broadly to the question of how relational information structures grow under iterative VFE minimisation. For the multi-agent active inference programme, the ER=EPR non-locality-without-signalling principle parallels the VFE result that coupled agents can share information through belief transport without violating the free-energy bound on inference.
