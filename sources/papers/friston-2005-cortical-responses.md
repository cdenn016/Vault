# A Theory of Cortical Responses

> [!info] Citation
> Friston, K. (2005). "A Theory of Cortical Responses." Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B 360(1456), 815-836. https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2005.1622

## TL;DR
Friston casts perception as empirical Bayesian inference in a hierarchical generative model of the cortex, in which higher levels predict the activity of lower levels and only prediction errors propagate forward. Minimizing free energy (a bound on surprise) by recognition (state estimation) and learning (parameter updating) yields predictive coding as the implementation, and this paper is an early statement of the free-energy principle for cortical function.

## Relevance to this research
A foundational free-energy-principle / predictive-coding reference for the VFE program: it establishes free-energy minimization over hierarchical generative models as the account of cortical inference, the conceptual root of the variational-free-energy transformer's layered belief updating.
