# Statistical Physics of Human Cooperation

> [!info] Citation
> Perc, M., Jordan, J. J., Rand, D. G., Wang, Z., Boccaletti, S., & Szolnoki, A. (2017). *Statistical Physics of Human Cooperation*. Physics Reports 687:1–51. DOI: [10.1016/j.physrep.2017.05.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physrep.2017.05.004).

## TL;DR
This comprehensive review recasts the evolution of cooperation in structured populations as a problem in statistical physics. It surveys how Monte Carlo methods, order parameters, phase diagrams, and the theory of phase transitions illuminate evolutionary games on lattices and complex networks, treating the cooperator fraction as an order parameter that undergoes continuous or discontinuous transitions as game and structural parameters vary. The review integrates spatial reciprocity, coevolution of strategy and structure, reward and punishment, group interactions (public-goods games), and behavioral-experiment findings on human cooperation, arguing that the macroscopic patterns of cooperation are emergent collective phenomena best understood with the conceptual apparatus of critical phenomena and pattern formation.

## What it establishes
The organizing claim is that the equilibrium fraction of cooperators $\langle \rho_C \rangle$ behaves like an order parameter, with phase transitions (including critical points in the Ising/directed-percolation universality classes for some spatial games) separating cooperative, mixed, and defective phases as a function of the temptation to defect and the update noise (an effective "temperature"). The review catalogues mechanisms — network reciprocity, heterogeneous and multilayer networks, coevolutionary rules, costly punishment — through the lens of how each shifts these transitions and the associated spatial patterns (domain growth, clustering, percolation), supplying a statistical-mechanical vocabulary for collective cooperation.

## Relevance to this research
This is the most direct bridge from evolutionary game theory to the SocialPhysics program: it explicitly treats collective cooperation with statistical-physics tools — order parameters, phase transitions, critical phenomena — the same lens the program applies to belief dynamics and to its meta-entropy / thermodynamic-limit arguments. It is a strong reference, arguably the best single EGT-to-social-physics interface for the vault, because it legitimizes and exemplifies the program's core methodological stance (collective behavior as emergent critical phenomena) even though its microscopic dynamics are stochastic strategy updates rather than VFE gradient flow.
