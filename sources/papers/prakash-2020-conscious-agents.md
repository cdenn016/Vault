# Fact, Fiction, and Fitness (Conscious Agents and the Interface Theory)

> [!info] Citation
> Prakash, Chetan, Fields, Chris, Hoffman, Donald D., Prentner, Robert, Singh, Manish (2020). "Fact, Fiction, and Fitness (Conscious Agents and the Interface Theory)." Entropy. https://doi.org/10.3390/e22050514

## TL;DR
Develops the conscious-agents formalism underpinning Hoffman's interface theory of perception: reality is modeled as a network of interacting conscious agents whose Markovian dynamics give rise to the perceived spacetime interface, which is shaped by evolutionary fitness rather than veridical truth. Argues fitness-driven perception systematically diverges from objective structure.

## Relevance to this research
Provides the agent-network / observer-relative substrate that connects the multi-agent VFE model and participatory-realism strand: perception as a fitness-tuned interface aligns with belief-driven, observer-dependent inference in the program.
