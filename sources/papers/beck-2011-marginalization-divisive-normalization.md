# Marginalization in neural circuits with divisive normalization

> [!info] Citation
> Beck, J. M., Latham, P. E., Pouget, A. (2011). "Marginalization in neural circuits with divisive normalization." Journal of Neuroscience. https://doi.org/10.1523/JNEUROSCI.4126-11.2011

## TL;DR
Argues that the canonical cortical computation of divisive normalization implements approximate marginalization (a near-optimal nonlinear operation) over nuisance variables within the probabilistic-population-code framework. Linear combination of population codes handles cue integration; divisive normalization handles marginalization, together giving a near-complete neural toolkit for Bayesian inference.

## Relevance to this research
Connects divisive normalization — the operation behind softmax/attention normalization — to approximate Bayesian marginalization in PPCs, supporting the program's view of attention normalization as precision-weighted probabilistic inference.
