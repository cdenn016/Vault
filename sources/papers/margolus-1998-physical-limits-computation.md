# The Maximum Speed of Dynamical Evolution

> [!info] Citation
> Margolus, Norman, Levitin, Lev B. (1998). "The Maximum Speed of Dynamical Evolution." Physica D 120 (1998) 188-195. https://arxiv.org/abs/quant-ph/9710043

## TL;DR
Derives the Margolus-Levitin theorem: a quantum system with average energy E above its ground state needs at least time h/(4E) to evolve to an orthogonal (distinguishable) state. This sets a fundamental energy-time bound on the maximum rate of distinct logical operations, complementing the Heisenberg uncertainty bound.

## Relevance to this research
Provides one of the physical limits on computation invoked by Lloyd (2002) to bound the information-processing capacity of physical systems, anchoring the it-from-bit / participatory view that information dynamics are constrained by physical law.
